## What this business is
Wheaton Precious Metals is the largest precious-metals streaming company. **[Fact]** It provides upfront capital to miners under precious metal purchase agreements (PMPAs) and receives a long-lived share of gold, silver, and other metal production at low cash costs, without operating mines. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$2.3 billion**, net earnings **$1.5 billion**, and operating cash flow **$1.9 billion** (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/EX99_1_2025_results_202602.htm`). **[Fact]** Operating cash flow per share (basic) was **$4.197** in 2025 versus **$2.266** in 2024 (same source). **[Fact]** The portfolio spans **23 operating mines** and **25 development and other projects** (EX-99.1). **[Fact]** Wheaton is Canadian-domiciled, listed on NYSE, TSX, and LSE.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock may trade as fully priced precious-metals beta after a record 2025, while the contract book is still compounding through Antamina, new mine ramps, and a filed path to **~1.2 million** gold-equivalent ounces (GEOs) by 2030. **[Inference]** Index investors often bucket WPM with spot gold; the economics are closer to a diversified toll on global production with limited operating cost on Wheaton's books. **[Inference]** At roughly **$125** per share, the market may capitalize peak-cycle **$4.20** per share operating cash flow rather than mid-cycle owner cash near **$3.00** per share. **[Inference]** The February 2026 Antamina silver stream adds volume but also **$4.3 billion** upfront capital and term-loan leverage that screens may underweight.

---

## Executive summary
Wheaton is the leading precious-metals streamer with record FY2025 cash flow and filed 2026 production guidance of **860,000 to 940,000 GEOs**, driven by the Antamina silver stream from April 2026. At **~$125**, we expect **-2.21% per year** over seven years on mid-cycle **$3.00** per share owner cash (not peak **$4.20**). Dhando **partial**. Moat **stable**. Cycle **peak**. Stance **watch**.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Precious-metal price mean reversion after FY2025 records, compressing operating cash flow per share faster than new streams ramp.

- Antamina **$4.3 billion** upfront payment and term-loan leverage; net debt rises at April 2026 closing (EX-99.1). **[HUMAN REVIEW]** confirm close and early silver deliveries (no post-close filing in folder).
- Counterparty or jurisdictional failure on a major PMPA (40-F; EX-99.1 forward-looking statements).
- CEO succession and continued discipline on large deals under new leadership.
- Competition in streaming economics could lower returns on future PMPAs.
- Constancia production decline after Pampacancha pit depletion (EX-99.1 2026 outlook).

**Inversion:** If gold and silver stay elevated and Antamina delivers on schedule, reported cash flow could exceed normalized **$3.00** per share for several years, but that is a commodity-and-volume scenario, not our mid-cycle base gate.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** If we are wrong that this is a compounder, it is because we treated peak-cycle cash flow and optimistic GEO guidance as fully reflected while paying a multiple that embeds both.
