## What this business is
Wheaton Precious Metals is the largest precious-metals streaming company. **[Fact]** It provides upfront capital to miners under precious metal purchase agreements (PMPAs) and receives a long-lived share of gold, silver, and other metal production at low cash costs, without operating mines. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$2.3 billion**, net earnings **$1.5 billion**, and operating cash flow **$1.9 billion** (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/EX99_1_2025_results_202602.htm`). **[Fact]** Operating cash flow per share (basic) was **$4.197** in 2025 versus **$2.266** in 2024 (same source). **[Fact]** The portfolio spans **23 operating mines** and **25 development and other projects** (EX-99.1). **[Fact]** Wheaton is Canadian-domiciled, listed on NYSE, TSX, and LSE.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock may trade as fully priced precious-metals beta after a record 2025, while the contract book is still compounding through Antamina, new mine ramps, and a filed path to **~1.2 million** gold-equivalent ounces (GEOs) by 2030. **[Inference]** Index investors often bucket WPM with spot gold; the economics are closer to a diversified toll on global production with limited operating cost on Wheaton's books. **[Inference]** At roughly **$125** per share, the market may capitalize peak-cycle **$4.20** per share operating cash flow rather than mid-cycle owner cash near **$3.00** per share. **[Inference]** The February 2026 Antamina silver stream (largest precious-metals streaming transaction ever, per management) adds volume but also **$4.3 billion** upfront capital and term-loan leverage that screens may underweight.

---

## Executive summary
Wheaton is the leading precious-metals streamer: upfront capital for long-lived metal streams, minimal mine operating risk, and cash returns tied to gold and silver prices plus portfolio growth. FY2025 was a record year for revenue, earnings, and operating cash flow. Management filed 2026 production guidance of **860,000–940,000 GEOs**, up sharply from 2025, driven primarily by the Antamina silver stream effective April 1, 2026.

At today's price (mechanical refresh), we estimate about **-2.8% per year** over seven years on mid-cycle owner cash (**$3.00** per share, not FY2025 peak **$4.20**). Dhando is **partial**. Moat is **stable**. Cycle is **peak**. Stance: **watch**.

### Mental models in plain English

**Permutations & combinations (Stahl):** Cash flows come from many mines and metals; **85%** of attributable production is from assets in the lowest half of their respective cost curves (EX-99.1).

**Low risk, high uncertainty (Munger):** Wheaton does not operate pits, but bears counterparty, jurisdiction, commodity price, and now balance-sheet leverage from large stream acquisitions.

**Inversion (Munger):** What breaks the thesis? Sustained lower gold and silver prices, Antamina closing or integration failure, or competition forcing worse streaming terms on future deals.

**Run-rate vs one-off:** FY2025 and Q4 benefited from higher realized gold and silver prices and strong production at Salobo, Antamina, and Peñasquito (EX-99.1). That cyclical uplift is not assumed permanent in our normalized owner-cash start.

**Growth mechanism:** EX-99.1 guides **860,000–940,000 GEOs** in 2026 versus production that exceeded **670,000 GEOs** guidance in 2025. Antamina's additional silver stream is expected to add **~70,000 GEOs** from April 1, 2026, with Wheaton receiving **67.5%** of Antamina silver (up from **33.75%**). Long-term outlook targets **~1.2 million GEOs** by 2030 and maintenance at that level through 2035, from Antamina, Blackwater, Hemlo, Goose, Platreef, and development projects (Koné, Fenix, Kurmuk, Mineral Park, El Domo, Spring Valley, Copper World, Santo Domingo).

**Structural pillars:**

1. **Portfolio growth:** 2026 GEO guidance and 2030 outlook filed in EX-99.1; Spring Valley (Nov 2025) and Hemlo (Nov 2025) PMPAs added in 2025.
2. **Margin leverage:** Streaming model keeps purchase costs low; gross margin rises with spot metals.
3. **Capital allocation:** Antamina BHP PMPA requires **$4.3 billion** upfront on or around April 1, 2026, funded with cash, a **$1.5 billion** term loan, and an equity offering (EX-99.1); net debt at closing expected to rise materially.

**Leadership:** Haytham Hodaly succeeds Randy Smallwood as CEO effective March 31, 2026 (EX-99.1). **[HUMAN REVIEW]** on capital allocation under new CEO.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Precious-metal price mean reversion after FY2025 records, compressing operating cash flow per share faster than new streams ramp.

- Antamina **$4.3 billion** upfront payment and term-loan leverage; net debt rises at April 2026 closing (EX-99.1).
- Counterparty or jurisdictional failure on a major PMPA (40-F; EX-99.1 forward-looking statements).
- CEO succession and continued discipline on large deals under new leadership.
- Competition in streaming economics could lower returns on future PMPAs.
- Constancia production decline after Pampacancha pit depletion (EX-99.1 2026 outlook).

**Inversion:** If gold and silver stay elevated and Antamina delivers on schedule, reported cash flow could exceed normalized **$3.00** per share for several years, but that is a commodity-and-volume scenario, not our mid-cycle base gate.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** If we are wrong that this is a compounder, it is because we treated peak-cycle cash flow and optimistic GEO guidance as fully reflected while paying a multiple that embeds both.
