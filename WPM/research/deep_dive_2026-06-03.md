## What this business is
Wheaton Precious Metals is a precious-metals streaming company. **[Fact]** It provides upfront capital to mining companies under precious metal purchase agreements (PMPAs) and receives a long-lived share of gold, silver, and other metal production at low cash costs. **[Fact]** The company is Canadian-domiciled, listed on the NYSE, TSX, and LSE, with **454,033,830** common shares outstanding at December 31, 2025 (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/40-F_20260331_rpt20251231_acc0001193125_26_134908.htm`). **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$2.3 billion**, net earnings **$1.5 billion**, and operating cash flow **$1.9 billion** (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/EX99_1_2025_results_202602.htm`). **[Fact]** The model is asset-light: no mine operating costs on Wheaton's books; returns depend on metal prices, counterparties delivering production, and the portfolio of streams acquired over time.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock may look like a fully priced precious-metals proxy after a strong 2025 share-price run while hiding a durable, diversified toll on global production. **[Inference]** Index and macro investors often bucket WPM with spot gold beta; the business is closer to a royalty-like compounder with a growing contract book (Antamina and other PMPAs) than a levered miner. **[Inference]** At roughly **$125** per share, the market may be capitalizing peak-cycle operating cash flow (**$4.20** per share in 2025) rather than mid-cycle owner cash near **$3.00** per share. **[Inference]** OTC-style screening misses the quality of the counterparty portfolio and the partial downside protection versus operators (fixed purchase prices on streams, no pit-level cost blowouts).

---

### Mental models in plain English

**Permutations & combinations (Stahl):** Cash flows come from many mines and metals (gold, silver, cobalt, palladium); diversification reduces single-asset failure risk (`40-F` risk factors; EX-99.1 production mix).

**Low risk, high uncertainty (Munger):** Wheaton does not operate pits, but bears counterparty, jurisdiction, and commodity price risk. The bear case is metal price collapse or major PMPA default, not a single mine cost overrun.

**Inversion (Munger):** What breaks the thesis? Sustained sub-$2,000 gold with no portfolio growth, or a large counterparties' failure to deliver under PMPAs (40-F lists acceleration and production-estimate risks).

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Precious-metal price mean reversion after FY2025 records, compressing operating cash flow per share faster than new streams ramp.

- Counterparty or jurisdictional failure on a major PMPA (40-F risk factors).
- Antamina integration: **$4.3 billion** capital outlay must earn returns via delivered silver production.
- Competition in streaming economics could lower returns on future deals.
- Stock already re-rated: prior-year share price appreciation cited in annual report narrative may limit margin of safety.

**Inversion:** If gold and silver stay elevated and Antamina delivers on schedule, reported cash flow could exceed normalized **$3.00** per share for several years, but that is a commodity scenario, not the base stance gate.
