## What this business is

Giełda Papierów Wartościowych w Warszawie (Warsaw Stock Exchange, GPW) is the dominant exchange group in Central and Eastern Europe. **[Fact]** The parent company and 16 subsidiaries operate listed equity markets (Main Market, NewConnect), fixed-income platforms (Catalyst, Treasury BondSpot Poland), commodity and energy markets through Towarowa Giełda Energii (TGE), clearing (IRGiT), reference rates and indices (GPW Benchmark), and minority stakes including Armenia Securities Exchange. **[Fact]** Consolidated sales revenue was PLN 464.8 million in FY2024 (+4.5% year over year) and PLN 132.3 million in Q1 2025 (+11.9%). **[Fact]** The group employs roughly 569 people and listed on its own Main Market since November 2010.

## Why the market might be wrong

No dated structural discount or equity yield curve setup at today's price. **[Inference]** Polish capital-market recovery and record Main Market turnover in Q1 2025 may justify a quality premium, but at PLN 83.6 the stock prices much of that recovery already. **[Inference]** Foreign client revenue (37.1% of Q1 sales) and commodity gas spot growth are under-modeled in simple domestic-equity narratives, yet they do not clear our 15% annual return hurdle at spot.

## Executive summary

GPW is a Stahl croupier: fees on financial and commodity market activity plus recurring information-services and reference-rate revenue, with limited principal risk on core operations. FY2024 consolidated revenue reached a record PLN 464.8 million; Q1 2025 accelerated to PLN 132.3 million (+11.9%) with Main Market electronic order book turnover up 34.6% and cost-to-income at 65.8%, the lowest in twelve quarters. Financial market trading revenue rose 19.5% in Q1; commodity trading revenue rose 11.3%. Equity-method income from KDPW and other associates added PLN 10.3 million in Q1. State Treasury holds 51.8% of votes via Series A shares, which supports stability but caps private-sector governance optionality.

At PLN 83.6 per share, the Lawrence base case implies **6.6% per year** over seven years on normalized owner cash of PLN 3.35 per share, below the 15% hold band. Bull case reaches roughly 10.9% if turnover and commodity fees compound faster. Dhando is partial: high margins and dividend yield (~PLN 3.15 recommended for 2024) cushion downside, but volume cyclicality and state control are real tails. We propose **watch**; illustrative entry near PLN 48–52 for ~15% base annual return.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Equity turnover mean-reversion after 2024–2025 recovery; Polish political or regulatory shifts affecting market structure or PFSA fees.

**Inversion checklist:**
- If Main Market daily turnover falls back below PLN 1.2 billion for two consecutive quarters, growth assumptions fail.
- If State Treasury pursues policy that caps fee yields or diverts cash to non-shareholder goals, moat and alignment weaken.
- If intangible IT capex stays above PLN 40 million per year without revenue follow-through, owner cash conversion stays below earnings.

**Lens failure mode:** Operating lens assumes normalized fee volume persists; a prolonged domestic equity bear market breaks the base case without a dated recovery path.
