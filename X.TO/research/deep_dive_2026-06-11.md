## What this business is

TMX Group Limited (X.TO) operates Canada's primary market infrastructure: Toronto Stock Exchange and TSX Venture Exchange for equity capital formation, Montréal Exchange and BOX for derivatives, CDS for clearing and settlement, Alpha for alternative trading, and global data and analytics businesses including TMX Trayport (energy trading connectivity) and TMX VettaFi (ETF indexing). **[Fact]** The group reported **$1,717.2 million** of revenue in FY2025 and **$488.2 million** in Q1/26 (`X.TO/investor-documents/ir-tmx/annual_report_2025.pdf`, `X.TO/investor-documents/ir-tmx/q1_2026_mda.pdf`). Revenue is overwhelmingly fee-based on listings, trading volumes, clearing, and recurring data subscriptions rather than balance-sheet lending.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The market may treat TMX as a slow Canadian listings utility while underpricing the shift toward recurring global data and analytics. **[Inference]** Global Insights (GSIA) reached **$701.8 million** in FY2025 revenue (+16% year-over-year), now **41%** of consolidated revenue, with TMX Trayport and VettaFi driving double-digit growth. **[Inference]** At **~$50** per share (CAD), the stock trades near **~22 times** mid-cycle owner cash, which prices in quality but leaves limited margin of safety versus our **~15%** annual return target. There is **no dated predictive attribute** in our base case; the inefficiency is descriptive (quality compounder at full price), not a timed catalyst.

## Executive summary

TMX Group is a **croupier** on Canadian and select global capital and commodity market flows, with a durable **multi-sided** revenue stack across listings, trading and clearing, and fast-growing data/analytics. FY2025 owner cash (cash from operations minus capital spending) was **~$2.32** per share on **~278 million** shares; adjusted diluted earnings were **$2.13** per share after normalizing acquisition and litigation noise. At **$50.29** per share (CAD, 2026-06-11), the seven-year synthesis expected annual return is **9.38%** per year (Lawrence base **9.58%**). Bear **3.76%** and bull **12.64%** bracket scenarios. Moat is **stable**; dhando is **partial** (bounded bear but limited upside at spot). We propose **watch**: understand and like the franchise, but base return sits below our **~15%** hurdle without a lower entry price or faster-than-modeled GSIA growth. ## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** **Paying a full multiple for a croupier** while GAAP earnings lag adjusted cash flow due to acquisition amortization and integration spend.

- **Acquisition integration:** VettaFi, Newsfile, iNDEX, Verity, Cboe Canada/Australia add complexity and amortization (**$48M+** incremental D&A in 2024 per MD&A).
- **Volume cyclicality:** Trading and listing fees fall in prolonged equity bear markets.
- **Competition:** US ATS (AlphaX US), private markets, and global data vendors compress pricing power.
- **FX:** Trayport and BOX revenue in USD; CAD reporting adds earnings volatility (Q2/25 FX losses cited in MD&A).
- **Regulatory:** Canadian market structure changes could affect fee models.

**Inversion:** If we are wrong, TMX is a **compounding toll** where GSIA recurring mix and TM2X ($2B revenue target) deliver **12%+** returns even from ~$50, and the market is correctly paying up for durability.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** Mis-classifying acquisition-driven GAAP noise as cyclical impairment when adjusted owner cash keeps growing.
