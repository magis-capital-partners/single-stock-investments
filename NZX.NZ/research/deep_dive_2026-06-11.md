## What this business is

NZX Limited operates New Zealand's national market infrastructure: the Main Board equity market, debt markets, funds market, dairy and energy derivatives, clearing, settlement, and market data. The group also runs Smart (KiwiSaver, ETFs, and QuayStreet funds) and NZX Wealth Technologies, a custodial platform for advisers. Revenue is mostly recurring fees tied to listings, trading and clearing volumes, terminal data subscriptions, and funds under management. The company is small versus global exchange peers but is the dominant domestic toll collector on New Zealand capital-market activity.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The listed share price (~NZD 1.40) prices NZX mainly as a slow-growth exchange toll booth, yet more than half of operating revenue now comes from funds management and wealth technology, which grew 18% and 26% respectively in 2025 while traditional markets revenue was flat. Investors may underweight the structural KiwiSaver and platform migration tailwind and the April 2026 equity-derivatives launch. The predictive attribute is **multi-engine croupier**: quality toll economics plus wealth-platform compounding, with cyclical issuance risk on the exchange sleeve.

---

## Executive summary

NZX is a New Zealand market infrastructure and wealth-technology group: exchange tolls, funds management, and adviser platforms. FY2025 operating revenue was NZD 129.0 million (+7.3%), normalized operating earnings NZD 53.5 million (+11.6%), net profit NZD 21.5 million (+20.2% on a like-for-like basis), and free cash flow NZD 26.5 million (+25%). Funds under management reached NZD 15.8 billion (+17.6%); wealth-platform funds under administration reached NZD 19.9 billion (+23.1%). Q1 2026 operating revenue rose 5.0% year over year despite softer secondary-market fees, supported by funds and wealth technology. The stock trades near NZD 1.40 with a 4.5% dividend yield on 6.3 cents per share of fully imputed distributions. Moat is **stable** on domestic network effects; dhando is **partial** because earnings remain volume-sensitive and net tangible assets per share are negative. Base annual return at today's price is **11.8%** per year, below our ~15% hurdle. Stance: **watch**.

### Moat (Munger)

NZX benefits from regulatory licensing, integrated clearing and settlement, and multi-sided network effects across issuers, brokers, and data consumers. Smart and Wealth Technologies add switching costs for advisers and fund investors. The moat is **stable**, not widening: the addressable market is bounded by New Zealand GDP and savings flows, and technology investment must keep pace with global vendors.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** A prolonged drought in New Zealand capital raisings and trading activity stalls exchange-sleeve revenue faster than wealth-platform growth can offset, leaving the group valued as a ex-growth local bourse.

Additional risks:

- Leadership transition (acting CEO and CFO) during derivatives product launch.
- Wealth Technologies amortisation and migration spend: free cash flow depends on platform capex normalising as migrations complete.
- Fonterra contract loss and other issuer concentration changes in a small market.
- Negative net tangible assets per share: equity is not asset-backed in a liquidation sense.
- Regulatory or political pressure on market fees or KiwiSaver economics.
- Competitive pressure on adviser platforms from larger Australian financial institutions.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** If Smart and Wealth Technologies growth decelerate while exchange volumes mean-revert lower, NZX becomes a low-growth utility priced for past platform investment with stranded software amortisation.
