## What this business is
Berkshire Hathaway is a diversified holding company built around insurance float, wholly owned operating subsidiaries, and a large listed-equity portfolio managed from Omaha. **[Fact]** Reportable activities include property/casualty insurance (GEICO, Berkshire Hathaway Reinsurance, Berkshire Hathaway Primary), BNSF railroad, Berkshire Hathaway Energy, and a broad manufacturing/service/retailing group (Precision Castparts, Marmon, Lubrizol, Fruit of the Loom, Berkshire Hathaway Automotive, Pilot, and others) per `investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260302_rpt20251231_acc0001193125_26_083899.htm` Item 1. **[Fact]** Class B shares trade on the NYSE as **BRK.B**; each B share carries 1/1,500 the economic rights of Class A. **[Fact]** FY2025 after-tax **operating earnings** were **$44.5 billion**; GAAP **net earnings** were **$67.0 billion**, reflecting investment gains separate from operating results (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/8-K_20260302_rpt20260228_acc0001193125_26_085801.htm` Exhibit 99.1). **[Fact]** Insurance float was about **$176 billion** at December 31, 2025.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The market may anchor on headline GAAP net earnings volatility from mark-to-market equities and miss the steadier **operating earnings** stream Buffett highlights. **[Inference]** At roughly **$475** per B share the stock trades a few percent **below** December 2025 book (~**$520** per B), which can look cheap on book while still offering only mid-single-digit returns on normalized operating power. **[Inference]** Succession, cash pile deployment, and Apple concentration are well debated; the inefficiency here is mild (wide coverage, liquid mega-cap) unless operating earnings re-accelerate or buybacks tighten the share count faster than modeled.

## Executive summary
Berkshire is a fortress conglomerate: float-funded insurance, productive industrials/utilities/rail, and a marked equity portfolio. FY2025 operating earnings per B share were about **$20.62** (down from ~$22 in 2024) while book value per B approached **$520**. Cash generation remained strong (~**$46 billion** operating cash flow per the 2025 annual report letter).

**Headline 7-year return (total synthesis): about 6.0% per year** at **$475.37** per share on normalized operating earnings, mid-single-digit growth, and an 18-times year-10 exit multiple. Dhando is **partial** (book floor, float). Stance: **watch** until price offers a higher return on operating power or a clearer catalyst to deploy excess cash.
