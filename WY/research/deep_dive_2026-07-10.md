## What this business is
Weyerhaeuser Company is one of the world's largest private owners of timberlands, structured as a timber real estate investment trust. **[Fact]** The company owns or controls more than **10 million acres** of timberlands in the United States and manages long-term licenses covering more than **14 million acres** in Canada. **[Fact]** Three reportable segments drive economics: **Timberlands** (log harvest and stumpage), **Wood Products** (lumber, oriented strand board, and engineered wood products mills), and **Real Estate, Energy & Natural Resources** (higher-and-better-use land, energy leases, and related dispositions). **[Fact]** FY2025 net sales were **$6.905 billion** (down 3% from $7.124 billion); consolidated Adjusted EBITDA was **$1.021 billion**; Adjusted Funds Available for Distribution (Adjusted FAD) was **$397 million** (**~$0.55** per share on **723.2 million** basic shares). **[Fact]** At December 31, 2025 the balance sheet showed **$16.6 billion** total assets, **$11.5 billion** timber and timberlands (net, at depletion cost), **$5.57 billion** long-term debt, **$464 million** cash, and stockholders equity of **$9.43 billion** (**~$13.04** per share).

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The market may treat WY as a homogeneous lumber-cycle stock and miss the land floor and capital-return framework underneath cyclical Wood Products earnings. **[Fact]** Wood Products Adjusted EBITDA fell to **$250 million** in FY2025 from **$661 million** in FY2024 while Timberlands Adjusted EBITDA rose to **$581 million** from **$539 million**, showing the integrated model's offsetting segments. **[Inference]** At **~$23.51**, the stock trades roughly **80% above GAAP book equity** (~$13.04 per share) yet below a reasonable timberland fair-value mark (~$1,800 per acre on 10 million acres implies a much higher asset base than depletion-cost book). **[Inference]** Predictive attribute: **timberland acreage REIT with integrated mills** plus **lumber trough cycle**; mispricing may come from screening on depressed GAAP earnings ($0.45 EPS FY2025) rather than normalized owner cash and optionality from HBU sales, ENR leases, and the **$500 million** Monticello engineered wood products project (operations target **2027**).

---

## Executive summary
Weyerhaeuser is the largest private U.S. timberland owner: **10 million+ acres**, integrated mills, and a REIT cash-return framework targeting **75-80%** of Adjusted FAD through a **$0.72** base dividend plus buybacks and supplemental dividends. FY2025 Adjusted FAD was **$397 million** (**~$0.55** per share) at a lumber trough; we normalize owner cash to **~$0.65** per share for the base case. Leverage rose to **$5.57 billion** long-term debt in 2025 after term-loan refinancing. At **~$23.51**, our base case annual return is **-4.84%** per year on normalized Adjusted FAD (7-year total synthesis). Book equity and acreage provide a partial floor, but price sits well above GAAP book and spot owner cash does not clear our **~15%** hurdle. We propose **watch** pending lumber cycle recovery and proof that Monticello capacity lifts normalized Adjusted FAD without relying on lumpy land-sale gains.

**Primary risk:** Prolonged lumber and housing weakness keeps Wood Products Adjusted EBITDA depressed, forcing dividend coverage from balance sheet or supplemental payout cuts.

- **Leverage:** Long-term debt **$5.57B** (weighted average rate **5.11%**, maturity **6.5 years**); 2025 term-loan issuance increased flexibility but raised gross debt.

**Inversion:** What would make us wrong to watch? Two consecutive quarters of Wood Products Adjusted EBITDA above **$400M** with Adjusted FAD above **$0.75/sh** and net debt flat or down would re-open **hold** at a similar price.
