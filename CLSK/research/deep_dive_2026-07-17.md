## What this business is
CleanSpark is a U.S.-listed bitcoin miner that owns power-backed data center sites, operates roughly **1,027 megawatts** of infrastructure, and runs **336,544** ASIC miners (**241,934** in service at September 30, 2025). **[Fact]** FY2025 digital currency mining revenue was **$766.3 million** (+102% versus **$379.0 million** in FY2024). **[Fact]** Average operational hash rate was **45.6 exahash per second** (**4.3%** of global network hash). **[Fact]** FY2025 operating cash flow was **$461.0 million** and capital spending on productive assets was **$144.7 million**. **[Fact]** The company sells the majority of mined bitcoin for working capital but began retaining treasury balances and running a Digital Asset Management (Spot+) covered-call program in April 2025. **[Fact]** In October 2025 management added an AI data center leader and signed a non-binding framework with Submer for modular AI cooling while acquiring **271 acres** in Texas with **285 megawatts** of contracted power for future high-performance computing.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
**Predictive attribute:** complexity discount (GAAP earnings dominated by bitcoin fair-value marks, convertibles, and an unproven AI hosting pivot).

**[Inference]** The market may treat CleanSpark as a levered bitcoin proxy (rewarding FY2025 hash scale-up) while underpricing hashprice compression already visible in H1 FY2026 filings. **[Fact]** H1 FY2026 mining revenue was **$317.6 million**, down **7.7%** year over year despite a larger fleet. **[Fact]** Q2 FY2026 GAAP operating loss was **$345.7 million** in the quarter, driven by **$224.1 million** net fair-value loss on bitcoin transactions and measurement. **[Inference]** Conversely, bears may ignore the **$316 million** of FY2025 owner cash (operating cash flow minus capital spending) and the strategic value of **1 gigawatt-class** power footprint if AI hosting converts.

---

## Executive summary
At **$13.03** (2026-07-17 close), the base case yields **-8.2% per year** over ten years on normalized FY2025 owner cash of **$0.99 per share**. CleanSpark is a scaled U.S. bitcoin miner (**45.6 EH/s**, **4.3%** network share) pivoting toward AI data centers while carrying **$1.79 billion** of long-term debt and a growing convert stack. FY2025 owner economics were strong, but H1 FY2026 mining revenue fell year over year and GAAP losses returned when bitcoin marks moved against the treasury book. Marvin proposes **watch** until either price offers a mid-teens annual return on normalized owner cash or filings show durable hash-margin improvement plus a contracted AI hosting path.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Hashprice falls faster than CleanSpark can cut power cost or upgrade machines, compressing owner cash while debt and converts remain.

Additional risks:
- **$1.79 billion** long-term debt (March 2026) plus **$636 million** zero-coupon converts and bitcoin-collateral credit lines (10-K liquidity section).
- GAAP earnings swing with ASC 350-60 bitcoin fair-value marks (**$471 million** YTD loss H1 FY2026).
- AI data center build requires heavy upfront capex with revenue lag (10-K liquidity discussion).
- Customer/supplier concentration on Bitmain ASICs and mining pools.

**Lens failure mode (operating + optionality):** Using Adjusted EBITDA (**$823 million**) as owner cash while ignoring that it embeds bitcoin mark dynamics; treating FY2025 hash scale as permanent without H1 FY2026 revenue decline.

**Inversion:** What would make this work? Sustained BTC above **$100k**, hash share stable above **4%**, AI hosting contracts on existing **1 GW** footprint, and convert stack refinanced without massive dilution.
