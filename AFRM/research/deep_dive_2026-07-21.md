## Executive summary
Affirm is a scaled BNPL and consumer lending platform earning merchant network fees and interest on installment loans, funded largely through securitization trusts. FY2025 revenue reached **$3.22 billion** (+39% year over year), with GAAP profitability and **$793.9 million** of operating cash flow. Credit losses remain material: provision for credit losses was **$616.7 million** in FY2025 (~19% of revenue). The balance sheet pairs **$1.72 billion** of parent cash with **$8.87 billion** of long-term debt, most tied to receivables funding rather than corporate leverage.

At about **$75.32** per share, we expect **5.34%** per year over seven years on the base case. Proof-first component economic value sums to **$18.67 per share**, well below price, so Lawrence growth scenarios must carry most of the return. Dhando is **partial** because filing-locked owner cash and parent liquidity limit total loss in a normalized downturn, but securitization and credit cycles can still compress multiples. Moat is **narrow**: merchant integrations and underwriting data help, but card networks and peers can replicate installment at checkout. Stance: **watch**.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Credit losses rise in a consumer recession while funding costs increase, compressing net income and the owner-cash multiple per `10-K_20250828` risk factors.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** Treating peak-cycle provision rates as permanent understates the **`credit_funding_stress_reserve`** component.

Additional risks:

- Securitization market disruption raises funding cost or limits receivables growth (10-K liquidity and funding note).
- Merchant concentration (large e-commerce partners) shifts economics if take rates compress (10-K Item 1).
- Regulatory changes to late fees or disclosure reduce merchant adoption (10-K legal proceedings and regulation section).
- Share-based compensation and potential equity issuance dilute per-share owner cash (10-K stock compensation note).

**Inversion:** What would make this work? Sustained GMV growth with stable net charge-offs, Affirm Card driving repeat transactions at lower acquisition cost, and funding spreads staying below pre-2022 stress levels while OCF per share compounds.
