## What this business is
Heritage Global Inc. is a small-cap asset-services croupier: it earns fees brokering and liquidating industrial and financial assets, reselling lab equipment, and trading charged-off consumer loans through National Loan Exchange (NLEX). **[Fact]** The company also holds specialty-lending notes, equity-method joint ventures, cash and auction inventory, and since January 2026 operates the DebtX commercial-loan brokerage platform. **[Fact]** As of March 31, 2026, **34,734,754** common shares were outstanding and stockholders' equity was about **$67.8 million** (**$1.95** per share book value) (`HGBL/investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-Q_20260507_rpt20260331_acc0001193125_26_211813.htm`). **[Fact]** FY2025 net income was **$3.587 million** on **$5.711 million** operating income; Q1 2026 net income was **$0.717 million**, softer than the prior-year quarter (same 10-Q).

---

## Why the market might be wrong
Micro-cap neglect and complexity may leave the stock trading below book while fee franchises still earn mid-single-digit millions after corporate overhead. **[Inference]** The market may overweight specialty-lending borrower default (76% concentration on one borrower per FY2025 10-K) and Q1 2026 softness, underweighting NLEX's recurring brokerage fees and the optional DebtX earn-in. **[Inference]** Component proof math at base case sums to about **$1.91** per share of economic value versus a price near **$1.26**, suggesting modest upside if fee power holds and notes/JVs realize near carrying.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Specialty-lending borrower default and concentration (76% of gross notes from one borrower, FY2025 10-K) impair notes receivable and segment income.

- **DebtX integration:** Q1 2026 Commercial Loans operating loss and **$5.3M** goodwill require earn-in proof (10-Q).
- **Fee cyclicality:** Auction and NLEX volumes fall in benign credit cycles (10-K MD&A).
- **Tax and corporate drag:** FY2025 effective tax above 25% proxy; corporate overhead **~$5M** absorbs segment profits.
- **Micro-cap liquidity:** Small float limits institutional ownership and can widen bid-ask spreads.

**Lens failure mode (earnings):** If the market prices HGBL as a distressed lender rather than a fee croupier, multiple compression persists despite NLEX cash generation.
