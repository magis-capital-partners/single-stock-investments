## What this business is

Archer-Daniels-Midland (ADM) is one of the world's largest agricultural processors, turning crops into food, feed, fuel, and industrial ingredients. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$80.3 billion**, down from **$85.5 billion** in FY2024 and **$93.9 billion** at the FY2023 cycle peak. **[Fact]** The company operates through three reportable segments: **Ag Services & Oilseeds** (origination, crushing, refining), **Carbohydrate Solutions** (corn wet milling, starches, sweeteners), and **Nutrition** (human and animal ingredients). **[Fact]** FY2025 operating cash flow was **$5.45 billion** minus capital spending **$1.25 billion**, while reported net income fell to **$1.08 billion** (diluted EPS **$2.23**) on compressed crush margins. **[Inference]** ADM earns by arbitraging global crop flows, operating toll-like processing assets, and upselling specialty nutrition products on top of commodity throughput.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The stock may embed permanent earnings impairment after the accounting investigation and margin trough, while operating cash flow has already recovered. **[Fact]** FY2025 operating cash flow **$5.45 billion** rebounded from **$2.79 billion** in FY2024 even as net income stayed depressed. **[Fact]** On January 27, 2026, ADM settled with the SEC for a **$40 million** civil penalty and the DOJ closed its inquiry, resolving intersegment-sales investigations. **[Inference]** The market may overweight governance overhang and underweight mid-cycle crush-margin normalization and the Nutrition mix shift. **[Inference]** No approved third-party predictive attribute exists; the edge is **scale franchise at a cyclical trough** with **partial** downside protection from asset intensity, not a dated catalyst.

## Executive summary

ADM is a global agricultural processor with unmatched origination and crush scale, now exiting a commodity-margin trough and SEC/DOJ investigations. FY2025 free cash flow per share is about **$8.69** on **484 million** diluted shares; net debt is roughly **$7.4 billion** (~**$15** per share). At **~$86** (July 2026), the seven-year Lawrence owner-cash path implies about **12%** per year, but proof-first component math sums to **~$69** per share base economic value, below the quote. Moat is **stable** (network scale, not pricing power); **partial** dhando because leverage and commodity cyclicality cap the bear case. Marvin proposes **watch**: revisit if adjusted segment operating profit re-accelerates above **$3.5 billion** with net debt flat or falling.

ADM operates a **platform-scale agricultural network**: country elevators, river terminals, crushing plants, and nutrition formulation labs spanning six continents (10-K FY2025 Item 1).

### Mental models in plain English

**Moat durability (Munger):** Does scale protect the franchise? **Partially yes.** Fixed processing assets and origination relationships create switching costs for farmers and customers, but crush margins are commoditized and competed by Bunge, Cargill, and regional players.

**Moat trajectory (Munger):** Is the advantage widening? **Stable.** Nutrition mix improves revenue quality, but FY2024–FY2025 margin compression and controls remediation show execution risk.

**Deny compounding to others (Stahl):** Can peers replicate? **Partially.** Cargill (private) and Bunge match global scale; ADM's integrated Nutrition upsell is differentiated but not monopoly pricing.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Mid-cycle crush-margin recovery fails while **~$8.4 billion** of debt remains, forcing equity holders to fund trough years through reduced buybacks and a stagnant quote.

- **Inversion:** If global oilseed crush margins normalize to FY2023 levels and Nutrition exceeds **$500 million** adjusted operating profit sustainably, owner cash could re-rate toward **$90+** per share on the proof engine alone.
- Biofuel policy reversals or South American crop oversupply could keep margins depressed longer than base case assumes.
- Weather and trade disruption (Black Sea, South America logistics) swing quarterly earnings more than long-term moat.
- Although SEC/DOJ investigations closed, any recurrence of intersegment control failures would re-open governance discount.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** If FY2025 OCF recovery was largely working-capital release rather than sustainable crush economics, starting owner cash **$8.69/sh** overstates mid-cycle power and the stock is fairly priced at **~$86**.
