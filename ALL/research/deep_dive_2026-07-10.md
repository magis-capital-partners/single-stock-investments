## What this business is

The Allstate Corporation is one of the largest publicly held U.S. personal-lines property and casualty insurers. **[Fact]** It sells auto, homeowners, and other personal insurance through exclusive agents, independent agents, contact centers, and direct channels under the Allstate, National General, Direct Auto, and Answer Financial brands. **[Fact]** At year-end 2025 the company reported **211 million** policies in force, **$59.5 billion** of Property-Liability premiums written, and an **$83.24 billion** investment portfolio (`ALL/investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260220_rpt20251231_acc0000899051_26_000031.htm`, Item 1 Business). Protection Services (roadside, protection plans, dealer services, identity protection, and Arity telematics) and a Run-off Property-Liability segment round out the group. This is an underwriting and investment compounder, not a balance-sheet-free croupier.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

After the 2022-2024 auto and homeowners loss-inflation shock, Allstate repriced aggressively and cut expenses; the Property-Liability combined ratio fell to **85.2%** in 2025 from **104.5%** in 2023. **[Fact]** **[Inference]** The market may still overweight catastrophe headlines and the 2022-2023 earnings collapse while under-crediting durable pricing power, Transformative Growth distribution, and a **$4 billion** share-repurchase authorization announced for 2025-2026. **[Inference]** Conversely, FY2025 GAAP diluted earnings of **$38.06** per share and **42.3%** return on equity embed favorable prior-year reserve releases (**$1.81 billion**, 3.1 points on the combined ratio) and a peak-cycle underwriting margin; normalized owner cash is lower than spot GAAP earnings. No dated equity-yield-curve catalyst; return is franchise quality at a normalized earnings power.

## Executive summary

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Catastrophe losses and social inflation push the combined ratio back above 100%, wiping out underwriting income while the stock still prices mid-cycle earnings power.

- **Cycle reversal:** FY2025 **85.2%** combined ratio includes **3.1 points** of favorable reserve releases; normalized profitability is lower.
- **Investment portfolio:** **$83.24 billion** of assets; rising rates help net investment income but mark-to-market volatility flows through comprehensive income.
- **Regulatory / political:** State rate-approval friction could slow premium adequacy in key auto markets.

**Inversion:** What would make this fail? Two consecutive years with combined ratio above **100%**, forced equity issuance to support ratings, and buyback suspension. Survive via **$30.6 billion** of shareholders' equity and investment portfolio liquidity, but normalized return thesis breaks.

**Lens failure mode:** Operating lens fails if underwriting losses become sustained rather than cyclical, turning Allstate into a dilutive turnaround.
