## What this business is

Aflac Incorporated sells supplemental health and life insurance, mainly cancer and medical policies in Japan and accident, disability, and hospital indemnity products through U.S. worksite channels. **[Fact]** Customers are individuals who buy coverage at work or through agencies and banks; the company earns premiums, pays claims, and invests float in a large fixed-income portfolio. **[Fact]** FY2025 total revenues were **$18.93 billion**; net income was **$3.65 billion** (diluted earnings per share **$6.82**). **[Fact]** Aflac Japan generated roughly **$10.66 billion** of earned premium revenue and Aflac U.S. roughly **$6.90 billion** in FY2025 (`AFL/investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260225_rpt20251231_acc0001628280_26_011402.htm`). **[Fact]** Investments and cash totaled **$103.8 billion** against policy liabilities of **$69.6 billion** at year-end 2025. **[Inference]** This is a mature, high-persistency supplemental insurer with earnings levered to Japan demographics, U.S. voluntary benefits penetration, and investment spreads.

### Moat (Munger)

Aflac's moat is **distribution plus brand in supplemental niches**, not primary health insurance scale. Once policies are sold, persistency and renewal premiums create a long cash-flow tail; switching supplemental carriers is easier than switching primary medical, but the duck brand and payroll-deduction habit reduce churn. The investment portfolio is a supporting asset, not a separate hidden option, because spreads already flow through revenues.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** **Yen weakness and investment-mark volatility** reduce reported earnings and can compress the multiple even when supplemental persistency remains intact.

- Japan regulatory or tax changes to third-sector products.
- U.S. worksite enrollment stagnation if employers cut voluntary benefits.
- Credit losses in Japan government and corporate bond portfolio (low historically, non-zero).

**Inversion:** If we are wrong, Aflac is a permanently solid mid-single-digit cash compounder bought at a full price: you collect a **~2%** dividend yield and earn high-single-digit total returns only if buybacks accelerate or the multiple expands.

## Terms

- **Supplemental insurance:** Coverage that pays cash benefits for specific illnesses or accidents, sold in addition to primary medical insurance.
- **Persistency:** The rate at which policies remain in force and continue paying premiums.
- **Float:** Premiums collected before claims are paid, invested to earn spread income.
