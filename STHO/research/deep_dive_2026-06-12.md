## What this business is
Star Holdings is a Maryland statutory trust spun off from iStar Inc. on March 31, 2023, immediately before iStar merged with Safehold Inc. The company holds iStar's legacy non-ground-lease real estate: development projects, operating hotels, land, and loans, plus 13,522,651 shares of Safehold common stock received at the spin. Star does not originate new investments at scale; management's stated goal is to monetize the legacy portfolio and return capital through asset sales, debt paydown, and share repurchases. The company is externally managed by a Safehold subsidiary under a management agreement that charges a percentage of gross book value (excluding the Safehold stake).

---

## Why the market might be wrong
Spin-off liquidation vehicles often trade at deep discounts because they lack an earnings growth story, carry development risk, and sit outside index and screen universes. Star's GAAP earnings swing with Safehold mark-to-market moves and lumpy land sales, which obscures the underlying asset value. The Safehold stake alone was worth roughly $18 per Star share at June 2026 market prices, above the Star stock price near $9. That gap suggests the market prices the legacy real estate book at a large negative value despite active sales, loan repayments, and repurchases below book. The predictive attribute is **spinoff discount**: a dated wind-down with a visible collateral floor and shrinking share count.

---

## Executive summary
Star Holdings is a post-spin wind-down trust: legacy iStar real estate plus a large Safehold stake, managed to sell assets and shrink the share base. At about $9.22 per share, the stock trades near half of March 2026 book value per share and below the marked Safehold holding alone. Operating earnings are not a clean proxy for value because development spending and fair-value swings dominate the income statement. Our base case assumes the trust liquidates near book over about four years through continued sales, debt reduction, and buybacks, implying roughly **20.6%** at today's price. Dhando is partial: the Safehold floor limits downside, but development overruns, related-party management fees, and margin-loan constraints on selling Safehold shares cap the upside. We propose **accumulate** for investors who can hold through an illiquid, event-driven payoff. **[HUMAN REVIEW]** on related-party management alignment and exact Safehold sale timing.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Development spending on Magnolia Green and remaining Asbury sites exceeds sale proceeds, eroding equity while debt and management fees remain.

- Margin loan on Safehold shares may limit timing or size of SAFE sales, keeping value trapped while debt service continues (`10-K_20260217`).
- Q1 2026 loss shows earnings volatility from Safehold marks; a sustained Safehold price decline would reduce book faster than buybacks offset.
- Related-party management fee (1.5% stepping to 2.0% of gross book excluding Safe shares) consumes cash that could otherwise fund repurchases (`10-K_20260217`).
- Concentration in New Jersey waterfront and Virginia residential development exposes results to local housing and hotel demand.

**Lens failure mode (event):** If management reframes Star as a going-concern developer rather than a liquidator, the discount could persist indefinitely and the thesis fails.

**Inversion:** We are wrong if (i) legacy assets are worth far less than carrying values, (ii) Safehold falls enough that the floor disappears, or (iii) governance prevents buybacks despite excess cash.
