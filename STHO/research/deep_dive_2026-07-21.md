## What this business is

Star Holdings (STHO) is a Nasdaq-listed wind-down vehicle created in March 2023 when iStar Inc. merged with Safehold Inc. and spun off legacy non-ground-lease assets. **[Fact]** The company holds loans, operating properties, land (Asbury Park Waterfront and Magnolia Green), and approximately 13.5 million shares of Safehold Inc. (NYSE: SAFE). **[Fact]** It is externally managed by a wholly owned Safehold subsidiary and aims to maximize cash through asset sales, loan repayments, and share repurchases rather than dividends. **[Fact]** At March 31, 2026, total assets were $480.4 million, stockholders' equity was $240.2 million (~$19.88 per share on 12.08 million shares), cash and restricted cash were $62.1 million, and long-term debt was $207.0 million. **[Fact]** The Safe stake was marked at $183.0 million fair value ($13.53 per SAFE share).

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The market prices STHO like a broken small-cap REIT with volatile GAAP losses, not like a dated capital-return vehicle sitting on a proof-backed component value near **$18 per share** versus a quote near **$9**. **[Inference]** Q1 2026 reported a $10.3 million net loss driven largely by non-cash Safehold mark-to-market moves, which obscures loan repayments and balance-sheet simplification. **[Inference]** Complexity (VIE history, margin loan on the Safe stake, related-party Safehold management) keeps generalist investors away. The March 27, 2026 deconsolidation of the Asbury seller-financed venture (mezzanine repaid, guaranty released) reduced reported assets but improved net cash flexibility. Prior research also understated net debt per share (a false **-$1.19 per share** net cash/debt identity); corrected arithmetic shows about **-$12 per share** net of cash.

---

## Executive summary

Star Holdings is a legacy asset monetization vehicle with a marked Safehold stake, shrinking MD&A monetizing portfolio, Magnolia/Asbury residual real estate, and active repurchases. Book value per share is about $19.88 at March 31, 2026; the quote near $9 is roughly half of book with a margin loan secured by Safe shares but no operating moat. This is not a compounder. Our base case uses a five-year component proof schedule summing to about **$18.10 per share** economic value, implying about **14%** per year from today's price (contract annualized return). Downside is partially bounded by cash and asset marks, but timing and related-party management keep dhando at **partial**. We propose **watch** pending human review of Safehold alignment and monetization pace.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Legacy assets or the Safehold stake sell below carrying values, eroding equity faster than repurchases shrink the share count.

- **Inversion:** Prolonged low price with rising management fees and margin-loan constraints could trap value inside the vehicle.
- SAFE mark declines could force margin-loan prepayments or restrict share sales (10-K risk factors).
- External manager may prioritize Safehold interests in asset dispositions.
- Numeric LTV/collateral triggers on the margin loan are not disclosed (only that a March 2025 amendment eased them).

**Lens failure mode (event payoff):** If no dated monetization catalysts arrive, the equity remains an illiquid book discount without proof-sum convergence.
