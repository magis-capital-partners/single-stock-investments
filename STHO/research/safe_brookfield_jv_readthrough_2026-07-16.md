# SAFE x Brookfield ground lease joint venture: implied valuation read-through

**Date:** 2026-07-16
**Author:** Marvin
**Relevance:** Star Holdings (STHO) owns roughly 13.5 million Safehold (SAFE) shares. SAFE is not an onboarded ticker; this note lives under STHO because the stake is our exposure.

## Deal facts

Source: Safehold press release, June 11, 2026 (PRNewswire 302798431) and 8-K filed June 12, 2026 (SEC accession 0001104659-26-073173).

- Safehold contributed a portfolio of ground leases, diversified across the United States, generating approximately **$14 million of current annualized cash ground rent**, into a joint venture with a Brookfield affiliate. **[Fact]**
- Brookfield purchases a **non-controlling 49% interest at a gross valuation of approximately $348 million**. **[Fact]**
- Safehold keeps day-to-day control, consolidates the venture, and retains **call options beginning after year 7** to repurchase Brookfield's interest. Net proceeds go to debt repayment and general corporate purposes. **[Fact]**
- Advisors: Eastdil Secured and BofA Securities (Connect CRE, June 12, 2026). **[Fact, secondary source]**

## Deal pricing

- Implied cash cap rate on the contributed assets: **$14M / $348M = about 4.0%**. **[Fact, arithmetic]**
- Cash proceeds to Safehold: 49% of $348M, roughly **$170 million**. **[Inference; press release does not state the exact cash amount]**

## Read-through to all of SAFE

Portfolio inputs from the Q1 2026 supplemental (period ended March 31, 2026; earnings deck filed as exhibit 99.2 to the April 30, 2026 8-K):

| Input | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Core ground lease portfolio cost basis | $6.49 billion | Q1 2026 supplemental, portfolio reconciliation |
| Annualized cash yield on cost | 3.8% | Q1 2026 supplemental |
| Implied total annualized cash rent | about $247 million | 3.8% times $6.49B **[arithmetic]** |
| Total debt (includes pro-rata JV debt and revolver) | $5.02 billion | Q1 2026 supplemental |
| Shares outstanding | 71.8 million | Q1 2026 10-Q balance sheet |
| GAAP shareholders' equity | $2.43 billion, about $33.80 per share | Q1 2026 10-Q |

**Step 1. Value the whole portfolio at the deal cap rate.** $247M of cash rent divided by 4.02% gives a gross asset value of about **$6.1 billion**, which is roughly **94-95% of cost basis** (3.8% divided by 4.02%).

**Step 2. Bridge to equity.** Gross asset value $6.1B, plus loans receivable ($62M), the Star Holdings loan ($113M), cash and restricted cash ($28M), and some portion of equity investments ($0 to $286M; part of that line is already inside the core portfolio through two unconsolidated JV assets), minus total debt of $5.02B and net working capital items. That gives implied equity of roughly **$1.4 to $1.8 billion**, or about **$19 to $25 per share**, with a sensible base near **$20 per share**. **[Inference]**

**Step 3. Compare.**

| Benchmark | Per share |
|---|---|
| Deal read-through (this note) | ~$19-25, base ~$20 |
| Market close July 15, 2026 | $16.59 |
| GAAP book value | ~$33.80 |
| Market-implied cash cap rate at $16.59 | about 4.2% |

**Interpretation.** Brookfield paid a cash cap rate of about 4.0%, slightly richer than the roughly 4.2% the public market implies at the current share price. Because SAFE carries $5.0 billion of debt against a $6.5 billion cost basis, small cap rate moves swing the equity a lot: at 3.8% (cost basis parity) implied equity is near $23 per share; at 4.25% it is near $14. The deal validates asset values near cost basis and modestly above the market price, but gives **zero credit** to management's claimed $5.6 billion of unrealized capital appreciation and the $1.7 billion Caret valuation, and none of the straight-line rent receivable ($249M) survives a cash cap rate valuation. **[Opinion]**

## Caveats

- **Asset mix unknown.** We do not know which leases were contributed. If they were older acquired leases with above-average cash yields and weaker escalators, the 4.0% deal cap rate would be a premium price for those specific assets and the read-through to the rest of the portfolio would be too generous. If they were representative, the read-through holds. **[HUMAN REVIEW]**
- The 49% interest is non-controlling and comes with Safehold call options from year 7, which usually costs the buyer some price; the underlying asset value could be modestly higher than the headline. **[Inference]**
- Cash rent estimate of $247M is derived from the disclosed 3.8% yield and cost basis, not a disclosed dollar figure.

## STHO impact

- STHO's roughly 13.5M SAFE shares were marked at **$183M** at March 31, 2026 (about $13.55 per SAFE share). At the July 15 close of $16.59 the stake is worth about **$224 million**, a gain of roughly $41M, or about **$3.40 per STHO share** on 12.08M shares, before margin loan considerations. **[Inference]**
- The deal read-through (~$20 base) sits above the current SAFE price, mildly supportive of STHO's largest single asset. **[Opinion]**
