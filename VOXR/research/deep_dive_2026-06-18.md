## What this business is

Vox Royalty Corp. is a returns-focused mining royalty and streaming company listed on Nasdaq and the Toronto Stock Exchange. **[Fact]** The company holds more than **70** royalties, streams, and related interests with primary exposure to gold and select industrial metals across Australia, Canada, the United States, Brazil, Peru, and South Africa (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/40F2025_000165495426002978_voxr_ex992.htm` business description). **[Fact]** Vox earns cash from royalty and streaming contracts without operating mines or funding mine-level capital spending. **[Fact]** FY2025 royalty revenue was **$12.0 million** and net precious metal income was **$4.6 million**; net cash from operating activities was **$10.7 million** on **68.4 million** shares outstanding at December 31, 2025. **[Fact]** The company deployed **$73 million** of capital in 2025, including a **$60 million** Global Gold Portfolio acquisition in September 2025 (`investor-documents/ir-voxr/Vox_2025_Shareholder_Letter_10_February_2026.pdf`).

## Why the market might be wrong

The market may treat Vox as a small-cap gold-beta royalty stub, underweighting the step-change in producing assets and the embedded development book acquired in 2025. **[Inference]** FY2025 receipts of **$16.6 million** (+50% versus 2024) and operating cash flow of **$10.7 million** (+95% versus **$5.5 million** in 2024) partly reflect elevated gold prices and new streaming income that did not exist in 2024, so headline growth may not repeat at spot metal levels. **[Inference]** Management guides **$28–32 million** of receipts for 2026 (`investor-documents/ir-voxr/VOXR_-_Corporate_Presentation_-_June_Final.pdf`), driven by volume from acquired streams; if even half of that uplift converts to owner cash, the Lawrence model starting point looks conservative. **[Inference]** With **67** development and exploration assets versus **12** producing assets, commodity optionality compression (HK predictive attribute) may apply: the market prices current cash flow while the portfolio scales.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Gold and industrial metal prices mean-revert while G&A stays elevated, leaving owner cash per share near 2024 levels despite a larger share count.

- **Operator / project delay:** **67** development assets depend on third-party mine builds (Greenstone, Bowdens, South Railroad per presentation)
- **Dilution:** **$73M** capital deployed in 2025 funded partly by equity; future deals may repeat if receipts lag
- **Leverage:** **$6.7M** credit facility drawn at year-end; interest and covenants if metals weaken
- **Lens failure mode (asset lens):** If development book is overpriced in the **$60M** Global Gold Portfolio, book value overstates economic floor

**Inversion:** What would make this fail? Sustained **$1,800/oz** gold (or lower), flat producing volumes, and **$5M+** annual G&A would leave the stock a low-yield royalty stub with no index rerating. We would exit the thesis if FY2026 operating cash flow per share falls below **$0.10** without a clear recovery path.

## Terms (plain English)

- **Royalty:** A percentage of mine revenue or production paid to Vox without operating the mine.
- **Stream:** An agreement to purchase a portion of metal production at a set price and sell at market, keeping the spread.
- **Owner cash:** Cash from operations minus sustaining capital spending; for Vox, operating cash flow approximates owner cash.
