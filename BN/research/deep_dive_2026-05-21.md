## Executive summary

Brookfield Corporation is a **global alternative-asset manager and wealth platform** earning fees on **$614B fee-bearing capital** (Q1-26), plus stable cash flows from operating businesses (renewables, infra, real estate, private equity) and a scaling **insurance/wealth solutions** arm (**$180B** insurance assets). Q1 2026 **distributable earnings (DE) were $1.55B** ($0.66/share), with fee-related earnings (FRE) up **11%** YoY. Management repurchased **>$1B** of BN/BAM shares YTD 2026 and is advancing **BN + BNT (insurance) combination** to simplify corporate structure.

**One-line view:** High-quality capital allocator compounding fee streams on real-asset franchises — Stahl spinoff/simplification lens applies to ongoing corporate streamlining; Munger/Pabrai compounder lens for core thesis.

**Thesis status:** **strengthening**

## Munger lenses

### Circle of competence — pass (with complexity caveat)

Brookfield is an asset manager + holding company for real assets. Explainable at FRE/DE level; full consolidated GAAP requires specialist modeling.

### Moat

- **Scale in alternatives:** $614B fee-bearing capital, global origination network.
- **Sticky institutional relationships:** Flagship fund cycles, complementary strategies.
- **Insurance/wealth pivot:** Permanent capital characteristics when scaled.
- **Operating asset quality:** Essential infrastructure, contracted cash flows.

### Inversion — what would kill the thesis?

1. **Fundraising drought** → FRE compression; DE growth stalls.
2. **Realization window closes** (credit markets) → total DE falls even if core FRE stable.
3. **Insurance integration misstep** (BN+BNT) → capital trap or regulatory friction.
4. **Mark-to-market losses** on consolidated balance sheet (**$519.6B** assets Q1-26) spook equity holders.
5. **Key-person / succession risk** in partnership culture.

### Psychology / incentives

Shareholder letter explicitly downplays macro noise vs. long-term value — aligned with Pabrai patience. Buybacks during volatility suggest management views stock as undervalued (**inference** — verify vs intrinsic DE yield).
