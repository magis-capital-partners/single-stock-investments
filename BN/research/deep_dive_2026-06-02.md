## What this business is
Brookfield Corporation is a global alternative-asset manager and owner of real-asset platforms. **[Fact]** Institutional clients pay management and performance fees on capital deployed into infrastructure, real estate, credit, and private equity strategies. **[Fact]** Shareholder economics are measured as **distributable earnings (DE)** before and after realizations, not GAAP net income. **[Fact]** Q1 2026 fee-bearing capital was **$614B**; insurance and wealth platform assets **$180B**. **[Fact]** Q1 2026 DE was **$1.55B** (**$0.66/sh**); LTM DE per share **$2.54**. **[Inference]** Realizations are lumpy; base valuation uses DE growth excluding heroic realization years unless in the bull case.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
There is **no clear dated mispricing signal** (HK predictive attribute: **none**). **[Inference]** Listed holdco complexity and GAAP mark-to-market noise on **~$519.6B** consolidated assets may obscure steady **fee-related earnings (FRE)** growth (+11% YoY Q1). **[Inference]** **BN+BNT simplification** could narrow a persistent holdco discount over time, but timing is uncertain. Return is **DE compounding at price**, not a single liquidation date.

## Executive summary
Brookfield compounds fee-bearing capital (**$614B**, Q1 2026) with a scaling insurance/wealth pivot (**$180B** assets) and active capital return (buybacks >**$1B** BN/BAM YTD 2026). FRE grew **11%** YoY; the firm raised **$67B** and deployed **$53B** in the quarter per the shareholder letter. Marvin proposes **watch** on math; prior **hold** is a human override for the allocator sleeve. Primary risk: **fundraising drought** compressing FRE.
