## What this business is
Indian Energy Exchange Limited operates India's leading power market platform: spot and term electricity, renewable energy certificates (RECs), and gas, under Central Electricity Regulatory Commission oversight. **[Fact]** The company earns transaction and membership fees on matched volume; it does not take directional power risk. **[Fact]** FY2024 consolidated revenue was **₹350.8 crore** (+16% year on year per annual report cover charts), consolidated profit after tax about **₹302.5 crore**, and earnings per share about **₹3.38** (`official-reports/annual-reports/annual_report_fy_2023_24_bse.pdf`). **[Fact]** At **₹124.07** per share (Yahoo Finance, 2026-06-04), the stock trades near **39 times** our **₹3.2** per share owner-cash starting point.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
There is no dated structural discount. **[Inference]** Investors may capitalize India's short-term power-market growth and high returns on equity as if they persist at the current earnings multiple without regulatory or competitive pushback. **[Inference]** Our base return uses FY2024 owner cash per share with a small haircut, not a forward run rate from the Q4 FY25 investor deck until the FY2025 annual is on disk. Return is franchise earnings power at price, not a timed mispricing with a known catalyst date.

Indian Energy Exchange is a regulated croupier on Indian power and environmental markets: fees on electricity, REC, and gas volume with minimal balance-sheet risk. FY2024 filings show **₹350.8 crore** revenue and about **₹302.5 crore** profit after tax; the Q4 FY25 presentation points to continued volume growth but is not in our starting cash flow until the FY2025 annual is filed. Asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) stays **partial** because volume and fee regulation are cyclical and political. Gas and derivatives adoption are embedded in the growth path, not separate option terminals. We propose **watch** and would revisit nearer **₹95–100** where Lawrence base case approaches a mid-teens annual return.

- **Regulatory fee caps** reduce take rate per megawatt-hour.  
- **Competing exchanges** gain share in day-ahead or gas products.

**Inversion:** If we are wrong, IEX remains India's dominant power exchange with **2–9%** annual returns at spot, not a broken franchise.
