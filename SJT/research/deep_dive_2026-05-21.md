## Executive summary

San Juan Basin Royalty Trust is a **passive royalty vehicle** — not a Stahl croupier and not a Munger moat business. Unit holders receive ~75% net-overriding royalty on gas/oil from Hilcorp-operated San Juan Basin properties (New Mexico). The trust has **no employees**; Argent Trust Company is trustee.

**Critical fact (FY2025):** The Trust **received no royalty income from Hilcorp** in the year ended December 31, 2025 and **made no unit-holder distributions**. From May 2024 through March 2026, royalty income was zero while **Excess Production Costs** of **$6.2M gross ($4.6M net to Trust)** remain to be recovered from future gross proceeds before distributions resume. Cash reserves fell to **$14,380** at March 31, 2026; the trustee draws on a **line of credit** for administrative expenses.

**One-line view:** Simple depleting gas royalty stream with **commodity price leverage** — current **zero-distribution** status and production-cost recovery make this a **valuation of future gross proceeds**, not an operating compounder.

## Munger lenses

### Circle of competence — pass (simple) / moat — none

Explainable in five sentences: royalty pass-through on depleting gas reserves. **No moat** — reserves decline; economics set by Hilcorp operations and gas prices.

### Inversion — what kills the thesis?

1. **Prolonged zero royalty** — excess costs never recovered; line of credit exhausted.
2. **Natural gas price collapse** — even when royalties resume, distributable income falls.
3. **Reserve revisions down** — depletion accelerates.
4. **Operator disputes / cost allocations** — Hilcorp production cost definitions (ongoing trust litigation history).
5. **NYSE delisting** — 10-K flags listing compliance risk when distributions absent.
