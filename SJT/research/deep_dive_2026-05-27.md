## What this business is

San Juan Basin Royalty Trust holds a roughly 75% net-overriding royalty interest in Hilcorp-operated natural gas properties in northwestern New Mexico. **[Fact]** The Trust has no employees and no control over drilling or costs; Argent Trust Company administers distributions. **[Fact]** FY2025 royalty income from Hilcorp was **$0** and **no distributions** were made to unit holders (`SJT/investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260327_rpt20251231_acc0001193125_26_128710.htm`). **[Fact]** Excess production costs were **$6,186,819 gross ($4,640,114 net to the Trust)** as of March 31, 2026 after Q1 recovery of **$1,688,787 net** (`SJT/investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-Q_20260514_rpt20260331_acc0001193125_26_223189.htm`). **[Fact]** Cash reserves were **$14,380**; line of credit utilization **$750,514**; **46,608,796** units outstanding. **[Fact]** In each month from **May 2024 through March 2026**, the Trust received no royalty income (10-Q Note 4).

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The market may treat San Juan as a broken income trust heading for wind-down, missing the Horizon Kinetics equity yield curve lens on a transitory NPI deficit. **[Inference]** Net excess production costs fell from **$6,328,902** at December 31, 2025 to **$4,640,115** at March 31, 2026, roughly **$1.69 million** recovered in one quarter, but Hilcorp's 2025 capex overspend slowed the path versus HK's May/June 2025 reinstatement estimate. **[Inference]** Royalty trusts sit outside yield screens and index mandates, creating a market-structure discount. If distributions resume, cumulative payoff may exceed what a zero-yield price embeds; timing and going-concern risk are worse than HK assumed.
