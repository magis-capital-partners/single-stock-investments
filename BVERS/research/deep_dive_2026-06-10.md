## What this business is
Beaver Coal Company, Limited is a Pennsylvania limited partnership (OTC Pink: BVERS) that owns surface and mineral rights on roughly **40,000 acres** of coal-bearing land plus **~9,600 acres** without coal rights in Raleigh County, West Virginia. **[Fact]** The partnership earns revenue from coal royalties, minimum coal-lease rentals, timber and stone royalties, natural gas royalties, emissions-reduction fees, and surface leases including residential, commercial, and hospitality (Cabins at Pine Haven). **[Fact]** Beaver Management Corporation is the sole general partner; limited partnership units are restricted and require GP consent to transfer. **[Fact]** The current partnership term runs until **October 31, 2030** unless terminated earlier under the agreement. **[Fact]** FY2025 total revenue was **$7.80 million**; net income was **$4.94 million** (**$198.67** per unit); cash distributions were **$206.17** per unit.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The market may treat BVERS as an illiquid OTC royalty stub with no growth, missing a **shift from commodity coal royalties toward surface land-leasing income** that management is actively pursuing. **[Fact]** Surface rentals and other income rose **7.5%** to **$2.85 million** and reached **36.5%** of FY2025 revenue (from **31.1%** in 2024). **[Inference]** At an indicative **~$2,400** per unit versus GAAP partnership capital of **~$595** per unit, the unit trades near **4.0×** stated book because land and reserves are carried at historical cost (**$3.5 million** on **~50,552 timber acres**, or **~$69/acre**). **[Inference]** Predictive attribute: **market structure discount** (non-reporting LP, GP consent on transfers, average daily volume often in single digits) plus **commodity royalty cyclicality** on metallurgical coal. **[Inference]** Contracted minimum rental payments of **$13.06 million** over 2026–2030 (Note 6) partially bound the downside but do not eliminate met-coal price risk.

---

## Executive summary
Beaver Coal is a West Virginia land-and-royalty limited partnership: metallurgical coal royalties still supply most cash, but surface leasing, hospitality, and commercial ground rents are growing as a steadier mix. FY2025 distributions of **$206.17** per unit followed a weaker coal year; two lessees supplied **55%** of revenue. Debt is modest (**~$289 thousand** notes payable) against **$4.16 million** cash. At **~$2,400** per unit **[Assumption]**, our base case annual return is **11.81%** per year on filing-based owner cash of **$206.17** per unit (seven-year synthesis model, surface-mix growth). GAAP book is not an economic floor because coal lands and reserves are historically costed. Dhando is **partial**: contracted rentals and low leverage help, but customer concentration and partnership term (**2030**) cap conviction. We propose **watch** until price is refreshed on-exchange and post-2030 renewal terms are clearer.
