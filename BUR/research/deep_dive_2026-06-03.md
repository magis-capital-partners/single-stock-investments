## What this business is

Burford Capital is the largest listed litigation finance and legal asset management firm. **[Fact]** It funds commercial litigation and arbitration, earns returns when cases resolve, and manages capital for third parties. **[Fact]** The company is dual-listed on the NYSE and London Stock Exchange (BUR) and reports on a Burford-only segment basis for portfolio economics. **[Fact]** Revenue is driven by fair-value marks on a multi-billion-dollar Principal Finance portfolio, realized gains when cases pay, and a smaller asset management fee stream. **[Fact]** As of March 31, 2026, Burford held about $740 million in cash and marketable securities and had deployed about $1.75 billion in Principal Finance capital excluding the YPF-related matters that drove the first-quarter 2026 write-down.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The stock collapsed on a non-cash fair-value loss while the operating litigation engine still throws off cash. **[Inference]** First-quarter 2026 GAAP net loss of about $1.63 billion is almost entirely the YPF-related fair-value adjustment; management states there was no cash impact from that loss and that Burford already collected $236 million in cash proceeds plus over $100 million in profit on the matter historically. **[Inference]** Shares near $4.31 trade only modestly above tangible book ($3.17 per share) after the write-down, yet Burford-only cash receipts averaged $573 million over the last three years versus about $253 million of cash operating costs, and management cites sight of roughly $280 million in portfolio cash so far in 2026. **[Inference]** Complexity (fair-value accounting, leverage at 3.5 times net tangible equity, and a multi-year arbitration option on YPF) may keep generalist investors away even when normalized cash economics are not impaired.
