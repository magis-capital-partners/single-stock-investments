## What this business is
Zimmer Biomet Holdings is a global musculoskeletal medical technology company selling hip and knee implants, sports medicine and extremities products, craniomaxillofacial and thoracic devices, and enabling technologies including surgical robotics, navigation, and bone cement. **[Fact]** FY2025 net sales were **$8.23 billion** (+7.2% versus $7.68 billion in 2024). **[Fact]** The company reports three geographic operating segments (Americas, EMEA, Asia Pacific) and product categories including Knees (**$3.32 billion**), Hips (**$2.09 billion**), and S.E.T. (sports medicine, extremities, trauma, CMFT, thoracic). **[Fact]** Cash from operations was **$1.70 billion** and capital spending **$224 million**, yielding about **$7.41** of free cash flow per diluted share on **198.7 million** shares. **[Inference]** ZBH is a large-cap orthopedic compounder whose economics depend on procedure volumes, surgeon preference, and hospital contracting, with growing exposure to robotics and extremities after the Paragon 28 acquisition.

Return at today's price is **normalized owner cash flow**, not a catalyst with a single payoff date. ## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Global procedure volumes grow slower than guided while hospital pricing pressure and EU MDR costs persist, leaving owner cash flat as debt service and amortization absorb free cash flow.

- **Inversion (Munger):** What kills the thesis? Robotics fails to differentiate versus Stryker/Mako; U.S. sales-agent conversion disrupts knee share; Paragon integration destroys rather than adds extremities margin.
- **Leverage:** FY2025 debt issuance funded Paragon 28; refinancing risk if rates stay elevated.
- **Competition / site of care:** ASC-focused competitors may out-execute in faster-growing settings (10-K Item 1A).
- **Lens failure mode:** N/A (operating lens). If robotics were valued as a separate option, we would require milestone NAV or probability-weighted sizing per `option_treatment.md`; current scan embeds robotics in segment growth.
