## What this business is

PotlatchDeltic Corporation was a timberland real estate investment trust with an integrated wood products manufacturing arm. **[Fact]** At December 31, 2024 the company owned approximately **2.1 million acres** of timberland across seven U.S. states, plus **seven sawmills** and an industrial-grade plywood mill in Arkansas, Idaho, Michigan, and Minnesota. **[Fact]** Three reportable segments were **Timberlands**, **Wood Products**, and **Real Estate** (rural land sales and residential/commercial development). **[Fact]** FY2024 revenue was **$1,062 million**; net income **$21.9 million**; Total Adjusted EBITDDA **$232.1 million**; Cash Available for Distribution (CAD) **$67.5 million** (**~$0.85** per diluted share). **[Fact]** The company paid a **$1.80** per share annual dividend in FY2024, above CAD. **[Fact]** On **January 30, 2026** PotlatchDeltic merged into Rayonier Inc. (**RYN**); PCH common stock was delisted from Nasdaq. Holders received **1.8185 Rayonier shares plus $0.61 cash** per PCH share.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The market may have treated PCH as a pure high-yield timber REIT, missing the cyclical drag from **Wood Products** at a lumber trough and the lumpy **Real Estate** monetization program. **[Fact]** FY2024 Wood Products posted a **$(23.6) million** operating loss despite **$20.5 million** Adjusted EBITDDA, while Real Estate Adjusted EBITDDA reached **$147.0 million** on large rural parcel sales (including **57,389 acres** at **~$2,302/acre** average). **[Inference]** At the last standalone price of **$39.25** (December 31, 2024), the stock traded below timber book equity (**~$25.9** per share on **$2.04 billion** equity) yet above a normalized owner-cash yield because CAD covered only **~2.2%** of price while the dividend implied **~4.6%**. **[Inference]** Predictive attribute: **timberland acreage REIT plus merger event**; the Rayonier combination (closed January 2026) is the dominant payoff, not incremental standalone accumulation.

## Executive summary

PotlatchDeltic was a 2.1-million-acre timber REIT with mills and a rural land sale option. FY2024 CAD of **~$0.85** per share did not cover the **$1.80** dividend; **~$1.0 billion** long-term debt and a lumber-cycle trough weighed on per-share owner cash. At the last standalone price of **$39.25**, our base case annual return is **-9.95%** per year on normalized owner cash of **$0.85** per share (seven-year model, **12×** exit multiple). Timber book and acreage provide a partial floor, but sub-15% returns and the completed Rayonier merger make PCH an **archive** name. We propose **watch** (no new PCH exposure); track **RYN** for combined timber economics.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Standalone owner cash (**CAD ~$0.85/sh**) remained below the **$1.80** dividend through a lumber trough, forcing debt reliance or asset sales to maintain REIT payout optics.

- **Inversion:** If post-merger Rayonier harvest synergies and a normalized lumber cycle lift combined CAD per RYN share above the pro-forma dividend, the merged entity could compound despite weak standalone PCH math at **$39.25**.
- Prolonged weak housing starts would keep Wood Products losses and compress sawlog prices.
- Rural land sale gains are lumpy; absence of HBU transactions would drop Real Estate EBITDDA sharply (FY2024 included large Southern parcel sales).
- **~$1.0 billion** debt stack limits flexibility if CAD falls further (Q3 2025 YTD net income only **~$3.3M**).

**Lens failure mode (asset payoff):** If rural sale comps (**~$2,302/acre**) are not repeatable and timber book reflects overstated depletion basis in weak markets, the apparent acreage floor is illusory and the name becomes a low-yield value trap.
