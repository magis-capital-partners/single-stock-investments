## What this business is

Hellenic Exchanges–Athens Stock Exchange S.A. (ATHEX Group) is Greece's capital markets infrastructure operator. **[Fact]** The Group runs the Athens Stock Exchange, post-trading clearing and settlement (ATHEXCLEAR), and the central securities depository (ATHEXCSD), earning fees on trading, clearing, listings, market data, and technology services without taking directional market risk on customer positions. **[Fact]** FY2025 consolidated turnover was **€86.3 million** (+58.9% versus €54.3 million in FY2024), EBITDA **€42.2 million**, and net earnings after tax **€31.6 million** (`investor-documents/ir-athex/annual_report_2025.pdf`, Board report). **[Fact]** Weighted average shares were **57,850,000**; earnings per share **€0.622** (`annual_report_2025.pdf`, note 5.39). **[Fact]** In November 2025 Euronext completed a tender offer and holds **74.25%** of voting rights; a new board was constituted in January 2026.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

FY2025 was an exceptional volume year (+58.9% turnover) ahead of Greece's FTSE Russell upgrade from Advanced Emerging Market to **Developed Market** status effective **21 September 2026**. **[Inference]** Screens and passive models may still treat ATHEX as a small emerging-market venue, while filings show developed-market preparation and Euronext integration already priced into activity. **[Inference]** The market may also capitalize FY2025 peak cash flows; our base case normalizes owner cash below the FY2025 spot of **€0.87** per share from operating activities minus capital spending. **Predictive attribute:** FTSE developed-market reclassification (dated Sept 2026) plus Euronext liquidity cross-listing synergy; partially offset because Euronext already controls the float.

## Executive summary

HEE (EXAE.AT) is a national exchange croupier: trading, clearing, listing, data, and digital services on Greek capital markets. FY2025 filings confirm **€86.3 million** turnover, **€42.2 million** EBITDA, **€31.6 million** net income, and **€53.6 million** net cash from operating activities on **57.85 million** shares. At **€7.49** per share (Yahoo EXAE.AT, 2026-06-11), we expect about **10.0% per year** over 7 years on **€0.42** mid-cycle owner cash per share (below our 15% core hurdle). Dhando is **partial**: monopoly franchise and net cash balance sheet, but revenue is cyclical and Euronext ownership limits independent upside. We propose **watch** and would revisit near **€6.00–6.25** where base-case return approaches our core band.

### Mental models in plain English

**Croupier toll (Stahl):** Does the company earn fees on activity without principal risk? **Yes.** Turnover is fee-based; clearing members post collateral to ATHEXCLEAR funds, not Group capital at risk (`annual_report_2025.pdf`, Clearing Fund section).

**Volume vs share (Stahl):** Is handled volume growing? **Yes.** FY2025 turnover **€86.3M** versus **€54.3M** FY2024 (+58.9%); post-trading segment revenue **€45.5M** versus prior year (`annual_report_2025.pdf`, segment note 5.6).

**Fee model resilience (Stahl):** Does recurring revenue cushion transaction cyclicality? **Partially.** Listing and data services (~17–22% of turnover) are steadier; trading and post-trading fees (~72%) still drive upside in active markets.

**Cycle normalization (Hohn):** Are we underwriting peak activity as normal? **No.** Base owner cash **€0.42** per share sits between FY2024 trough (**€0.26**) and FY2025 peak (**€0.87**), explicitly below FY2025 operating cash minus capital spending.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** **Peak earnings trap** if FY2025 Greek trading volumes normalize after the FTSE developed-market event and the market reapplies a lower multiple on transaction revenue.

- **Euronext control (74.25%)** may lead to squeeze-out, delisting, or dividend policy set for group priorities, not minority IRR.  
- **Prolonged Greek macro weakness** reduces listing and trading fees despite structural reforms.  
- **Regulatory fee pressure** from Hellenic Capital Market Commission could compress take rates.

**Inversion:** If we are wrong, ATHEX remains a debt-free national croupier with **€106.6 million** cash and mid-single-digit to low-double-digit returns, not a broken franchise.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** Thesis fails if handled volume stagnates for several years while Euronext integration costs persist without revenue synergy.
