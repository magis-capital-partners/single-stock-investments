## What this business is
Adobe Inc. sells creative, document, and marketing software primarily through annual and monthly subscriptions. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$23.77 billion** (+10.5% versus **$21.51 billion** in FY2024), with **$22.90 billion** from subscription revenue. **[Fact]** Three reportable segments are Digital Media (Creative Cloud and Document Cloud), Digital Experience (enterprise marketing and analytics), and Publishing (legacy products). **[Fact]** The company reports a single operating segment for management purposes but discloses segment revenue and gross margin in the annual filing. **[Fact]** Customers are creative professionals, enterprises, and knowledge workers worldwide; revenue is diversified across Americas, EMEA, and Asia-Pacific.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The market may be treating Adobe as a mature, slow-growth desktop software vendor while subscription recurring revenue and generative-AI product cycles are still compounding. **[Fact]** Remaining performance obligation (RPO) was **$22.52 billion** at FY2025 year-end, with **65%** expected to convert to revenue within twelve months. **[Inference]** Generalists may overweight competitive fear from low-cost generative-AI image tools and underweight Adobe's installed base, workflow integration, and enterprise contracts. At **$222.65**, the stock trades near **9.6%** free cash flow yield on FY2025 owner cash, which may underprice a double-digit grower with **$22.52 billion** of contracted backlog.

---

## Executive summary
Adobe is a subscription software compounder with exceptional gross margins in Digital Media and a large enterprise marketing stack in Digital Experience. FY2025 free cash flow was **$9.85 billion** (**$23.07** per share on 427 million diluted shares), operating income **$8.71 billion**, and the company returned **$11.28 billion** via share repurchases. At **$222.65** per share (NASDAQ close 2026-07-09), we expect **32.0%** per year over seven years on filed FY2025 owner cash (Lawrence base). Proof-first component schedule sums to **$656** per share base case versus price **~$223**. Durable competitive advantage (Munger moat) is **stable**: Creative Cloud and Acrobat retain workflow lock-in, but generative-AI alternatives are a real competitive front. Asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) is **partial**: strong cash generation and buybacks limit distress risk, yet the equity can de-rate if AI seat growth stalls. Stance context **watch** pending human capital authority.

### Mental models in plain English

**Switching costs (Munger):** Creative professionals store assets, presets, and team workflows inside Adobe tools. **[Fact]** Digital Media gross margin was **95%** in FY2025. **[Inference]** Migration to rival tools is possible but costly in retraining and file compatibility.

**Critical mass (Lawrence):** Creative Cloud is the default collaboration standard in design agencies and marketing departments. **[Inference]** Enterprise Experience Cloud contracts add multi-year stickiness separate from individual seats.

**Moat trajectory (Munger):** Is competitive advantage widening? **Stable, not widening.** Generative AI lowers the barrier to entry for image creation; Adobe's response is embedded Firefly models and AI credits inside subscriptions. **[Inference]** Moat depends on continued product leadership, not on historical desktop dominance alone.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Generative-AI alternatives erode Creative Cloud net seat additions and force price concessions faster than Firefly monetization offsets.

- **Competitive substitution:** Low-cost tools may cap individual-tier pricing (10-K risk factors).
- **Enterprise IT budget pressure:** Digital Experience deals are large and cyclical with marketing spend.
- **Stock-based compensation:** $1.94B FY2025 dilutes economic ownership if buybacks slow.
- **Debt-funded returns:** **[Fact]** Long-term debt **$6.21B** FY2025; January 2025 note offering added **$2.0B** aggregate principal (`8-K_20250117_*.htm`).
- **Inversion (Munger):** What kills the thesis? Two consecutive quarters of RPO growth below revenue growth plus declining Digital Media net new ARR would force bear-case growth and moat downgrade to **eroding**.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** If we are wrong that subscription pricing power persists, owner cash growth in the model overstates bear risk and the stock may be fairly priced at a lower multiple.
