## Executive summary

Mesabi Trust is a passive royalty trust (**13,120,010** units outstanding per `MSB/investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-Q_20251212_rpt20251031_acc0001104659_25_120673.htm`) earning income from pellets and other iron ore products shipped by Northshore Mining from Silver Bay, Minnesota under long-dated leases, with **Cleveland-Cliffs Inc.** as operator and parent. Cash available to unitholders is almost entirely a function of **tons shipped, realized pricing, and whether bonus royalty thresholds are met**—not operating execution by the Trust.

Two legal tracks matter: (1) the industry context that **Horizon Kinetics** summarized—a prior arbitration broadly favorable to the Trust on disputed premium royalties (`_system/reference/investment-wisdom/horizon-kinetics/HK-Q3-2025-Commentary-extract.txt`), and (2) a **September 26, 2025** initiation of **new** arbitration described in Item 3 of the latest **10-K** (`MSB/investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260422_rpt20260131_acc0001104659_26_046864.htm`) concerning idling (**May 2022–April 2023**) and **underpaid royalties on intercompany shipments from 2023 through the present**.

Recent cash-flow optics are bifurcal: **physical volume recovered** versus **bonus royalty collapsing**. Exhibit 99.1 to the **May 4, 2026** 8-K states calendar **Q1 2026** shipments credited to the Trust were **938,572** tons vs **457,728** tons in calendar Q1 2025 (`MSB/investor-documents/sec-edgar/8-K_20260504_exhibit_msb-20260430xex99d1.htm_acc0001104659_26_054941.htm`), yet **bonus royalty was $0** because deemed prices were below the **2026 adjusted bonus threshold of $71.70/ton**, and Cliffs flagged use of pricing tied to **third‑party activity in September 2025**. That same exhibit notes this is effectively the **first zero-bonus outcome in many years** other than during the Covid-era idling. By contrast, the **January 30, 2026** royalty receipt for calendar **Q4 2025** included **bonus royalty of $1,041,580** on total payments of **$4,943,488** (`MSB/investor-documents/sec-edgar/8-K_20260203_rpt20260130_acc0001104659_26_009757.htm`).

Balance-sheet resilience after special distributions/arbitration cash movements shows **cash & equivalents $23,191,923** and **unallocated reserve $21,087,860** at **October 31, 2025** vs **$100,204,531** cash and **$23,327,717** reserve at **January 31, 2025** on the same 10-Q. **Nine months ended October 31, 2025** reported **royalty income $13,156,985** versus **$19,613,693** prior year (same filing); the prior-year period also carried a **$71,185,029** arbitration-award revenue line illustrating how **lumpy** reported income can be.

**Inversion / failure mode:** trust cash flows degrade if Cliff’s pellets realize **persistently weak prices**, bonus tiers stay **uncollectible**, royalty reporting remains **opaque** (Trust relies substantially on Operator data despite independent monitoring—see Trustees’ disclosure-controls discussion in the FY2026 10-K Item 9A), or **prolonged contested outcomes** defer cash.

## Facts (from primary sources)

2. **Operator & asset:** Quarterly royalty reports originate from Cleveland-Cliffs re Northshore shipments from Silver Bay, MN (`8-K_20260504_exhibit_msb-20260430xex99d1.htm_acc0001104659_26_054941.htm`).
3. **New arbitration (September 26, 2025):** Item 3 of `10-K_20260422_rpt20260131_acc0001104659_26_046864.htm` describes Mesabi Trust’s AAA arbitration against Northshore & Cliffs seeking damages/declarations for prior idling and **underpaid royalties on intercompany shipments from 2023–present**.
4. **Nine-month financials (unaudited) to October 31, 2025:** Royalty income **$13.16M**; total revenues **$13.93M**; net income **$11.14M**; net income **$0.8493**/unit YTD vs **$6.6469**/unit YoY inflated by arbitration award timing; distributions declared **$1.02**/unit nine months (`10-Q_20251212_rpt20251031_acc0001104659_25_120673.htm`). Net income quarter ended Oct 31, 2025 **$2.767M** ($0.2109/unit) vs **$78.326M** ($5.9699/unit) YoY largely due to arbitration line in prior-year quarter.
5. **Calendar Q4 2025 royalty receipts (paid Jan 30, 2026):** Total **$4,943,488** comprising base **$3,622,142**, bonus **$1,041,580**, adjustments **+$66,572**, Mesabi Land Trust share **$213,194**, tons credited **956,512** vs **1,110,800** YoY (`8-K_20260203_rpt20260130_acc0001104659_26_009757.htm`).
6. **Calendar Q1 2026 royalty receipts (paid Apr 30, 2026 narrative):** Base **$1,201,501**, bonus **$0**, positive prior-quarter adjustments **$243,986**, Mesabi Land Trust **$179,813**, aggregate **$1,625,300**; deemed prices below **2026 threshold $71.70**; shipments **938,572** tons vs **457,728** (`8-K_20260504_exhibit_msb-20260430xex99d1.htm_acc0001104659_26_054941.htm`).
7. **Distribution sizing signal:** Trustees declared **$0.26**/unit payable Feb 20, 2026 vs **$5.95**/unit same timing prior year reflecting arbitrated/special payouts then (`8-K_20260116_rpt20260116_acc0001104659_26_004561.htm` Items 2.02 narrative).
8. **Dependence on Operator data:** FY2026 10-K Item 9A acknowledges reliance on Cliff’s quarterly reports, notes **Northshore did not furnish written certifications** analogous to issuer SOX control attestations even as Trustees deem controls effective (`10-K_20260422_rpt20260131_acc0001104659_26_046864.htm`).

## Inferences

1. **Run-rate royalties are cyclical commodity calls with contractual kickers.** Bonus royalty mechanics tie Trust economics to pellet pricing corridors; normalization requires scenario work on thresholds, not headline EPS.
2. **Legal process is structural, not episodic.** A prior favorable outcome (outside commentary) plus a **fresh** overlapping arbitration materially affects tail risk/timing uncertainty for cash—even absent operating assets on the Trust’s balance sheet (nominal leased-property assignments).
3. **Equity-yield-curve analogue may apply selectively.** Approved HK memory discusses **temporary distribution disruption with bounded timelines** paired with dormant economic assets (`_system/memory/MEMORY.md`, Horizon **Transitory problems** bullets). Mesabi intermittently resembles that pattern via legal recovery and payout volatility, but capex/competing claims are modest compared to hydrocarbon trusts—**timeline risk is principally legal/arbitration and pricing.**

---

## Opinions (Marvin judgment)

1. Mesabi behaves more like **depleting contractual optionality on iron pellet economics** than a Munger-style operating moat—the investment question is bounded upside vs **operator behavior + realized pricing**.
2. Stahl “croupier/infrastructure exchange” archetypes (`_system/reference/investment-wisdom/INDEX.md`) are **misleading** lenses here; Horizon Kinetics’ **metal composite / royalty trust framing** aligns better intellectually—even though we still apply **generic inversion**.
3. **Dhando asymmetry:** Trust has **minimal operating liabilities** relative to industrials (**good tail “don’t lose much” on balance-sheet risk**); **tails are bad** mainly through **capital impairment from chronic low realized prices/legal loss**, not covenant stress.
