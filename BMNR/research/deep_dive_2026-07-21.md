## What this business is
Bitmine Immersion Technologies is a U.S.-listed digital asset company that pivoted in calendar 2025 from small-scale bitcoin immersion mining and equipment sales into an **Ethereum treasury strategy**. **[Fact]** Management reoriented the business in the third calendar quarter of 2025 to prioritize ETH accumulation, staking, and capital markets funding while winding down proprietary self-mining. **[Fact]** At May 31, 2026 the company held **5,416,945 ETH** at fair value **$10.86 billion** plus **203 BTC** at **$15 million** fair value. **[Fact]** Common shares outstanding were **579.7 million** versus **232.4 million** at August 31, 2025 after repeated ATM and private placements. **[Fact]** Nine-month revenue through May 31, 2026 was **$59.9 million**, dominated by **$56.9 million** of staking and validation revenue; legacy mining and equipment were immaterial. **[Fact]** Thomas J. Lee serves as executive chairman following a 2025 board leadership change tied to the treasury pivot.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Continued ATM and private placement issuance dilutes ETH per share faster than the token price appreciates, destroying look-through value even in a rising ETH market.

**Lens failure mode (levered):** If issuance pauses and ETH per share stabilizes, the current discount to May 2026 ETH fair value could mean revert; if issuance accelerates, the equity becomes a financing vehicle, not a treasury.

Additional risks:
- **Mark volatility:** Unrealized gains and losses flow through net income (**$9.04B** loss nine months FY2026), obscuring operating economics and potentially triggering financing covenants or investor fatigue.
- **Custody and staking counterparty risk:** Protocol, validator, and custodian failures could impair part of the stack (10-K FY2025 risk factors).
- **Regulatory and tax:** Deferred tax liability **$92.3M** at May 31, 2026; digital asset tax and securities rules evolving (10-Q Q3 FY2026).
- **Inversion:** What would make this fail? ETH sustained below purchase cost **and** ongoing dilution **and** inability to earn staking spread above corporate burn.
