## What this business is
Aptiv PLC is a global industrial technology supplier to the automotive and adjacent mobility markets. **[Fact]** The company designs and manufactures intelligent sensors, high-performance compute, connection systems, and electrical distribution solutions that enable safer, greener, and more connected vehicles. **[Fact]** After the April 1, 2026 spin-off of Electrical Distribution Systems as Versigent (NYSE: VGNT), remaining Aptiv operates two reportable segments: **Intelligent Systems** (formerly Advanced Safety and User Experience) and **Engineered Components** (connection systems and cable management). **[Fact]** FY2025 consolidated net sales were **$20.4 billion** on a pre-spin basis; post-spin remaining revenue is roughly **$11.6 billion** (Intelligent Systems **$5.8 billion** plus Engineered Components **$5.8 billion** external sales per FY2025 segment note). **[Fact]** Aptiv is incorporated in Jersey, headquartered in Schaffhausen, Switzerland, and trades on the NYSE under APTV (CIK 0001521332).

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock near **$59.86** may still trade as a messy, levered auto-parts conglomerate rather than a slimmer software-and-interconnect franchise after Versigent. **[Inference]** FY2025 GAAP net income collapsed to **$165 million** (from **$1.79 billion** in 2024) after a **$648 million** Wind River goodwill impairment and large deferred-tax movements, while operating cash flow stayed **$2.19 billion**. Screeners focused on earnings per share miss the cash-generation story. **[Inference]** The completed Versigent spin removes the lower-multiple wiring harness mix and **$178 million** of separation costs from the rear-view mirror, but index rebalancing and event-driven sellers may keep a complexity discount into mid-2026. **[Inference]** Intelligent Systems carries embedded edge-software optionality via Wind River after the impairment reset; the market may treat the write-down as proof of failure rather than a cleaned-up balance sheet for software-defined vehicle content growth.

## Executive summary
Aptiv is a post-spin automotive technology supplier focused on Intelligent Systems (ADAS, compute, Wind River software) and Engineered Components (high-performance interconnects). FY2025 pre-spin revenue was **$20.4 billion**; segment adjusted operating income was **$2.46 billion**; operating cash flow **$2.19 billion**; GAAP net income to Aptiv **$165 million** after Wind River impairment. Q1 2026 revenue was **$5.09 billion**, up about five percent year over year; net income **$191 million**.

At **$59.86** per share on pro forma post-Versigent owner cash of **$5.15** per share, we expect about **18.88% per year** over seven years. Dhando is **partial** because auto cyclicality, OEM pricing pressure, and **$7.5 billion** long-term debt cap asymmetry despite strong cash conversion. Moat is **stable** on engineered-in programs but **unproven** on Wind River software economics after impairment. Stance: **hold**, pending human review of pro forma owner-cash normalization after the Versigent spin.

#### Moat (Munger)

**Stable, not widening.** Engineered-in automotive programs create switching costs once platforms are awarded, and Aptiv's global scale supports R&D amortization across major OEMs. **[Fact]** No single customer exceeded 10% of net sales in FY2025 (10-K). **[Inference]** Moat is weaker in commoditized harness-like products (now mostly Versigent) than in compute and interconnects. Wind River was intended to widen software moat; the **$648M** impairment signals that edge-software economics are still unproven at scale. **[Opinion]** Post-spin Aptiv is a better moat candidate than pre-spin, but we need two to three quarters of pro forma margins before upgrading moat to widening.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Global auto production downturn combined with aggressive OEM price-downs erodes segment adjusted operating income faster than content growth offsets.

**Secondary risks:**
- China localization displaces Western tier-one content on EV platforms
- Wind River fails to earn returns above cost of capital after impairment
- Motional JV requires further capital with no monetization
- Refinancing **$7.5B+** long-term debt in higher-rate environment

**Inversion:** We are wrong if post-spin Intelligent Systems and Engineered Components grow adjusted operating income **8%+** annually for three years while Aptiv retires debt and repurchases shares at current prices, delivering returns above **25%** per year without heroic multiples.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** Treating FY2025 GAAP net income **$165M** as normalized owner earnings instead of **$2.19B** operating cash flow minus maintenance capital spending.
