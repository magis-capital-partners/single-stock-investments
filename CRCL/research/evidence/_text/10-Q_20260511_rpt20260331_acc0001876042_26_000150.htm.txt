or &#8220;Arc Blockchain&#8221; is our Layer-1 blockchain designed with unique characteristics to bring the world&#8217;s leading enterprises, financial institutions, and governments onto the internet financial system. &#8226; &#8220;blockchain&#8221; and &#8220;blockchain networks&#8221; are systems in which transactions are recorded across computers linked in peer-to-peer (&#8220;P2P&#8221;) networks. &#8226; &#8220;canonically bridged USDC&#8221; refers to USDC that has been issued by Circle on one blockchain and then officially bridged to another via Circle-supported infrastructure. &#8226; &#8220;Circle Applications&#8221; are our products and services that facilitate coordination on the internet via digital assets, delivering practical utility to institutions, developers, and end-users. &#8226; &#8220;Circle Digital Assets&#8221; are our digital assets, USDC, EURC, and USYC, which serve as fundamental units of value for onchain economic coordination. &#8226; &#8220;Circle Liquidity Services&#8221; are our services that provide institutional minting, reserving, redemption, and foreign exchange services for our stablecoins, supported by our integration with the existing financial system. &#8226; &#8220;cross-chain transfer protocol&#8221; or &#8220;CCTP&#8221; is a protocol that enables USDC to flow securely between blockchains. &#8226; &#8220;digital wallets&#8221; are electronic devices, online services, or software programs that allow one party to make electronic transactions with another party bartering digital currency units for other digital assets, goods, and services. &#8226; &#8220;gas fee&#8221; is the fee required to conduct a transaction or execute a contract on a blockchain. &#8226; &#8220;interoperability&#8221; is the ability of different blockchains to exchange information and work together frictionlessly. &#8226; &#8220;Layer-1 blockchain&#8221; is the foundational blockchain that provides essential services like recording transactions and ensuring security. &#8226; &#8220;meaningful wallets&#8221; or &#8220;MeWs&#8221; are onchain digital asset wallets holding $10 or more of USDC. &#8226; &#8220;neo-bank&#8221; is a financial technology firm that offers apps, software, or other technologies to streamline mobile and online banking, which may or may not possess a traditional banking license. &#8226; &#8220;onchain applications&#8221; are software applications that use digital assets and smart contracts and deliver services using blockchain networks. &#8226; &#8220;on-ramps&#8221; and &#8220;off-ramps&#8221; are channels between fiat currency and stablecoins. &#8226; &#8220;payment stablecoins&#8221; are stablecoins that (i) are designed to maintain a stable value relative to a reference fiat currency on a one-for-one basis, (ii) can be redeemed for such reference fiat currency on a one-for-one basis, and (iii) are backed by assets held in a reserve that are considered low-risk and readily liquid with a value in such reference fiat currency that meets or exceeds the redemption value of the stablecoins in circulation. &#8226; &#8220;private key&#8221; is a string of letters and numbers that allows a person to access and manage his/her digital assets in a digital wallet or other custodial solution, similar to a password. 3 &#8226; &#8220;secured overnight financing rate&#8221; or &#8220;SOFR&#8221; is a benchmark interest rate published by the Federal Reserve Bank of New York that reflects the cost of borrowing cash overnight collateralized with US Treasury securities. &#8226; &#8220;smart contracts&#8221; are programs built on blockchain networks that automatically execute certain actions when a predefined set of criteria are met. &#8226; &#8220;stablecoin&#8221; is a digital asset whose value is designed to track the price of an underlying asset or another unit of value. &#8226; &#8220;superapp&#8221; is an application that offers multiple services and features within a single platform. &#8226; &#8220;TMMF&#8221; or &#8220;Tokenized Fund&#8221; is an onchain, tokenized representation of a traditional money market fund. &#8226;
