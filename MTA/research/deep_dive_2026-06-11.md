## What this business is

Metalla Royalty & Streaming Ltd. (NYSE American and TSX Venture: MTA) is a Canadian precious-metals royalty and streaming company. **[Fact]** It holds **99** royalties and streams across North America, South America, and Australia, earning cash from gold, silver, and copper production on third-party mines without operating mines or mine-level capital spending (`investor-documents/ir-mta/corporate-presentation.pdf`, May 2026). **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue from royalty interests was **$11.7 million** (+100% versus **$5.9 million** in 2024); cash from operating activities was **$4.4 million** (versus cash **used** of **$2.6 million** in 2024); attributable gold-equivalent ounces (GEOs) were **3,436** (+38% versus **2,481** in 2024) (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/exhibit99-3.htm` MD&A). **[Fact]** The company reported a net loss of **$4.2 million** in 2025 (versus **$5.5 million** in 2024), with general and administrative expense of **$6.6 million** and share-based payments of **$2.9 million** (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/exhibit99-2.htm`). **[Fact]** Cash and cash equivalents were **$9.8 million** at December 31, 2025; long-term borrowings under the BMO/NBF revolving credit facility were **$12.2 million** (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/exhibit99-2.htm` balance sheet).

## Why the market might be wrong

The market may treat Metalla as a broken cash-flow story because reported earnings are negative while the share price embeds a large development portfolio. **[Inference]** FY2025 operating cash margin per attributable GEO was **$3,457** (versus **$2,401** in 2024), but corporate overhead still exceeds operating profit at this scale (`exhibit99-3.htm`). **[Inference]** Management and sell-side consensus NAV per share (**$7.75** on the March 2026 factsheet) implies the stock trades at a discount to asset value if development royalties (Côté/Gosselin, Taca Taca, Copper World) start on schedule. **[Inference]** The predictive attribute is **dormant_asset**: **79%** of internal net present value sits in development-stage assets not yet in run-rate cash (`corporate-presentation.pdf`). **[Inference]** If the market prices only today's **~$0.05** per share of operating cash flow, it may underweight the six-year **>35%** compound annual growth rate on GEOs that management projects from **3,436** GEOs (2025) toward **12,000–23,500** (2029–2031) (`corporate-presentation.pdf`).

## Executive summary

Metalla is an emerging mid-tier royalty company: record FY2025 revenue and operating cash flow, **99** assets, and a development-heavy portfolio anchored by Côté/Gosselin, Tocantinzinho, Taca Taca, and Copper World. At approximately **$6.50** per share (NYSE MTA, pending mechanical price sync), we expect about **-14.0%** per year on the base seven-year owner-cash model (Lawrence base **-16.3%**; bull **-7.5%**). Current cash flow is tiny relative to price and general and administrative expense; the investment case rests on NAV convergence and GEO volume growth, not today's dividend yield (no regular dividend since 2020). Moat is **unproven** at this scale; dhando is **partial** (NAV discount and low net debt, but negative Lawrence return at spot). We propose **watch** below the 15% accumulate bar, with optionality overlay sized separately from Lawrence cash flow.

#### Moat (Munger)

**Unproven.** Contractual royalties on producing mines can be durable, but Metalla is still sub-scale: general and administrative expense (**$6.6M**) dwarfs operating profit, and the portfolio is younger than peers Franco-Nevada or Royal Gold. Moat could ** widen** if development assets enter production on schedule and overhead is absorbed by cash flow; until then, competitive advantage is unproven.

- **Côté/Gosselin first royalty payment (~2028):** IAMGOLD updated mine plan incorporating Gosselin expected H2 2026 (`corporate-presentation.pdf`)
- **GEO volume toward 12k–23.5k band (2029–2031):** Management six-year outlook (`corporate-presentation.pdf`)

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** General and administrative expense and share-based compensation consume operating cash while development royalties remain pre-cash (net loss **$4.2M** on **$4.4M** operating cash flow).

- Operator delays or failures on Côté/Gosselin, Taca Taca, or Copper World (Metalla does not control mines).
- Commodity price mean reversion from levels embedded in operator guidance and consensus NAV.
- Endeavor mine restart timing after Q4 2025 safety suspension (MD&A quarterly note).
- Revolving credit facility (**$12.2M** drawn) refinanced or tightened if cash flow lags.
- **Lens failure (asset):** If consensus NAV is optimistic and development options are worth zero, the stock is a expensive call on **~$0.05** per share cash flow with negative Lawrence return.
