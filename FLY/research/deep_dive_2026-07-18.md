## What this business is

Firefly Aerospace Inc. is a Nasdaq-listed U.S. space and defense technology company selling end-to-end mission solutions: small and medium launch (Alpha and Eclipse rockets), spacecraft (Blue Ghost lunar lander and Elytra orbital vehicles), and defense software and sensing (SciTec, acquired October 2025). **[Fact]** Customers are primarily U.S. national security agencies, NASA, and prime contractors. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$159.9 million** (+163% versus **$60.8 million** in FY2024). **[Fact]** Q1 2026 revenue was **$80.9 million** (+45% year-over-year versus **$55.9 million**). **[Fact]** The company had **1,409 employees** at December 31, 2025, including **436** with active national security clearances (10-K Item 1).

## Why the market might be wrong

**Predictive attribute:** backlog versus burn (milestone cash collections versus operating losses).

**[Inference]** The market may treat Firefly as a scaled defense compounder because FY2025 operating cash flow was **$204.9 million** and remaining performance obligations (RPO) were **$652.6 million** at March 31, 2026. **[Fact]** Q1 2026 operating loss was **($95.7 million)** on **$80.9 million** revenue, with research and development expense alone at **$67.5 million** (10-Q Q1 2026). **[Fact]** Cash fell from **$793.0 million** at December 31, 2025 to **$326.2 million** at March 31, 2026, including **$277.4 million** cash paid for the SciTec acquisition (10-Q cash flow statement). **[Inference]** Investors may underweight Eclipse development risk, launch failure tail, SciTec integration drag, and the gap between deferred-revenue-backed cash and sustainable owner free cash per share.

## Executive summary

At **$19.27**, the base case is **2.2%** per year over seven years on normalized owner cash of **$0.35** per share. Firefly is a national-security-weighted launch and spacecraft company with **$652.6 million** of contracted backlog and milestone billing that typically collects roughly **90%** of contract value before launch. Revenue is growing (**$80.9 million** in Q1 2026), but the company still lost **$96.7 million** in the quarter as research and development and SciTec integration costs ran ahead of gross profit (**$17.5 million**, 21.6% margin). Cash is **$326 million** after the SciTec deal. Moat is unproven, dhando is partial at best, and Marvin proposes **watch** until filing-grounded evidence shows operating leverage on backlog conversion.
