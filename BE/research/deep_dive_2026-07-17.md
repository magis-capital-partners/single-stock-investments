## What this business is

Bloom Energy Corporation manufactures and installs solid oxide fuel cell systems marketed as **Energy Servers** for on-site electric power. **[Fact]** Customers buy product blocks, pay for installation, and often sign long-term service or electricity contracts on the installed base. **[Fact]** FY2025 total revenue was **$2,024 million** (+37% versus FY2024): product **$1,531 million**, installation **$204 million**, service **$228 million**, and electricity **$60 million**. **[Fact]** The company also develops **Bloom Electrolyzers** for hydrogen production on the same platform. **[Fact]** South Korea is the second-largest market with roughly **682 megawatts** deployed through SK ecoplant and SK eternix partnerships; the U.S. is the largest market by revenue. **[Fact]** Cash and equivalents were **$2,491 million** at March 31, 2026, against long-term recourse debt of roughly **$2,599 million**.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

**Predictive attribute:** narrative premium (AI datacenter power scarcity priced ahead of durable per-share owner cash).

**[Inference]** The stock may be discounting a multi-year AI datacenter power bottleneck where Bloom ships high-margin blocks faster than history suggests. **[Fact]** Q1 2026 product revenue was **$653 million** (+208% year over year) and total revenue **$751 million** (+130%), far above FY2025 run-rate. **[Inference]** The market could be underweighting convert dilution (diluted shares **319.7 million** in Q1 2026 versus **227.4 million** FY2025 average), **$2.6 billion** recourse debt, and the cyclicality of natural-gas-linked economics. **[Fact]** FY2025 owner cash (operating cash flow minus capital spending) was about **$57 million**, or **$0.25 per share** on FY2025 diluted shares, despite operating income turning positive at **$73 million**.

---

## Executive summary

At **$221.89** (2026-07-17 close), the base case yields a deeply negative annual return on normalized FY2025 owner cash of **$0.25 per share** (mechanical refresh will pin the exact percentage). Bloom Energy is a solid oxide fuel cell manufacturer riding an AI datacenter power narrative: Q1 2026 revenue more than doubled year over year and operating profit was **$72 million** in a single quarter. Filing-based owner economics remain thin relative to price, with heavy convertible debt, share count up roughly **40%** in one year, and South Korea still anchoring the installed base. Marvin proposes **watch** until price offers a mid-teens annual return on normalized owner cash or filings prove the Q1 product surge is durable through backlog conversion and margins.

#### Thesis pillars

1. **AI datacenter behind-the-meter power (structural tailwind, early proof).** **[Fact]** Management cites AI data center demand in risk factors. **[Fact]** Q1 2026 product revenue rose **208%** year over year to **$653 million**. **[Inference]** If interconnection delays persist, Bloom can win fast-deploy projects; if grid power cheapens or gas spikes, economics compress.

2. **South Korea installed base and partnerships (structural).** **[Fact]** Roughly **682 MW** deployed in Korea with SK ecoplant and SK eternix. **[Fact]** Service revenue was **$228 million** FY2025 (+6.9%). **[Inference]** Korea provides recurring cash and reference deployments, but geographic concentration is a risk.

3. **Operating leverage after scale (cyclical / inflecting).** **[Fact]** Operating profit improved from **$23 million** to **$73 million** FY2024 to FY2025 while revenue rose **37%**. **[Inference]** Q1 2026 operating margin near **9.6%** on **$751 million** revenue; sustaining double-digit margins at scale is the bull case.

4. **Balance sheet funded growth but dilutes owners (event).** **[Fact]** Net financing cash inflow **$1,508 million** FY2025; cash **$2.45 billion** year-end. **[Fact]** Diluted share count rose to **319.7 million** in Q1 2026. **[Inference]** Converts traded balance sheet flexibility for per-share economics.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Datacenter order spike proves transient and convert dilution leaves owners with low per-share cash on a leveraged balance sheet.

**Secondary risks:**

- Natural gas price spike erodes fuel cell economics versus grid power
- Korean partnership or regulatory change in key market
- Competing on-site power (gas turbines, batteries, grid) wins datacenter sockets

**Inversion (Munger):** What would make this fail? AI load shifts back to grid after interconnection clears; Bloom ships product but service attach and margins disappoint; converts convert into a dilution overhang above **350 million** shares.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** Treating Q1 2026 revenue as steady-state run-rate without backlog evidence would overstate IRR.
