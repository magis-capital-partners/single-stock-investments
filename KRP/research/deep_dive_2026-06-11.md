## What this business is
Kimbell Royalty Partners, LP is a Fort Worth-based publicly traded partnership that owns mineral and overriding royalty interests across U.S. oil and gas basins and pays monthly cash distributions to common unitholders. **[Fact]** The partnership elects to be taxed as a corporation for U.S. federal income tax purposes (`10-K_20260226`, Overview). **[Fact]** As an owner of non-cost-bearing interests, KRP receives a share of production revenues net of post-production costs and taxes but does not fund drilling, lease operating expense, or plugging and abandonment (`same 10-K`). **[Fact]** As of February 20, 2026, **93,396,488** common units were outstanding (`same 10-K`). **[Fact]** FY2025 total revenues were **$333.8 million**, operating income **$132.8 million**, and net income per common unit **$0.62** (`same 10-K`, consolidated statements). **[Fact]** FY2025 cash distributions paid to common unitholders totaled **$149.4 million**, or about **$1.60** per unit (`same 10-K`, cash flow statement).

---

## Why the market might be wrong
Yield-oriented investors may treat KRP as a simple bond proxy and miss the embedded acreage option on **~12.3 million gross mineral acres** and **~4.7 million gross ORRI acres**, with more than half of aggregate acreage in the Permian and Mid-Continent. **[Inference]** Screeners that rank on trailing GAAP earnings rather than cash distributions understate owner yield because depreciation and acquisition accounting suppress net income per unit relative to cash paid. **[Inference]** The partnership's **$441.5 million** revolver balance and Apollo-affiliated preferred equity may be priced as permanent leverage overhang rather than as financing for accretive acquisitions that expand the royalty footprint. **[Inference]** Q1 2026 revenue and earnings softness may be extrapolated as structural when FY2025 production still rose to **25,760 Boe/d** (`10-K_20260226`).

---

## Executive summary
Kimbell Royalty Partners is a passive U.S. mineral and royalty owner that pays monthly cash distributions while growing through bolt-on acquisitions funded with equity and revolver debt. FY2025 revenues were **$333.8 million**, distributions about **$1.60** per unit, and proved reserves **72,944 MBoe** with production **25,760 Boe/d**. The partnership had **$441.5 million** drawn on a **$625 million** secured revolver at year-end 2025, which limits dhando versus debt-free royalty peers. At **$15.52** per unit and **$1.60** starting owner cash, Marvin's base case is **12.6% per year** over seven years, below our **~15%** target, so stance is **watch**. Q1 2026 revenue and earnings per unit fell year over year; we use FY2025 distribution run-rate in the base case. No approved third-party sources are in the base IRR. Undeveloped net acres are an overlay option only.

### Mental models in plain English

**Permutations & combinations (Stahl):** Owner cash can rise from commodity price, production on existing royalties, or new mineral/ORRI acquisitions. **[Fact]** FY2025 production was **9.40 MMBoe** (**25,760 Boe/d**), up from **9.10 MMBoe** in 2024 (`10-K_20260226`).

**Low risk, high uncertainty (Munger):** KRP does not operate wells; downside is commodity and acquisition discipline, not operating blow-ups. **[Fact]** The partnership is not obligated to fund capex on third-party operations (`10-K`, Overview).

**Market structure discount (Horizon Kinetics):** Mineral royalty LPs are often excluded from simple dividend screens despite high cash payout ratios. **[Inference]** Taxable-as-corporation structure avoids K-1 complexity but also removes some MLP yield-buyer bid.

**Inversion (Munger):** Thesis fails if distributions fall sustainably below **$1.20** per unit while unit count rises from dilutive equity deals and revolver balance stays elevated.

**Dhando (Pabrai):** Heads are commodity up-cycles plus accretive deals raising per-unit cash; tails are distribution cuts plus dilution, partially cushioned by non-operating structure but hurt by **$441.5M** revolver service. **[Inference]**

**Circle of competence (Munger):** The model is simple (royalty cash in, distributions out), but commodity timing and acquisition pricing require honest **[Assumption]** labels on growth rows.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Oil, natural gas, and NGL price declines reduce revenues and monthly distributions (`10-K` risk factors).

- **Leverage:** **$441.5 million** outstanding on a **$625 million** borrowing-base revolver at Dec 31, 2025; repayments reduce cash available for distribution (`10-K`).
- **Preferred equity:** Series cumulative convertible preferred units (Apollo-affiliated) rank senior in distributions (`10-K`, organization chart).
- **Dilution:** Equity offerings and unit issuance for acquisitions can grow unit count faster than per-unit cash (`10-K`, 424B5 shelf filings).
- **Q1 2026 softness:** Revenues **$65.5M** vs **$84.2M** prior-year quarter; net income **$0.04** vs **$0.20** per unit (`10-Q_20260507`).
- **Concentration:** Permian and Mid-Continent represent ~54% of aggregate acreage (`10-K`).

**Lens failure mode (asset):** If the market is right that distributions are peak-cycle and undeveloped acres stay undeveloped, total return equals yield minus price erosion with limited option payoff.
