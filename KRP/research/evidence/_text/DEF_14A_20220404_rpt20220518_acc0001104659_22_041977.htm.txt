Our current partnership agreement requires us to make distributions to common unitholders within 45&#160;days following the end of each fiscal quarter. As a result of this requirement we must declare our quarterly distribution prior to the date that we announce earnings for the particular quarter, and we must declare and pay our quarterly distributions earlier than our mineral and royalty company peers. We believe this difference in timing has led to confusion among some of our investors, and we believe that KRP would benefit by announcing its quarterly distributions on substantially the same schedule as our peers. The A&amp;R Partnership Agreement will amend this requirement to permit us to announce our quarterly distribution concurrently with our earnings announcement for a particular quarter and to pay our quarterly distributions generally on the same timetable as our peers, while still retaining the requirement that we make our quarterly distributions within a designated period of time after the end of each quarter. Specifically, under the&#160;A&amp;R Partnership Agreement, we will be required to pay our quarterly distribution no later than 60&#160;days after the end of the first, second and third quarters of each year, and no later than 90&#160;days after the end of the fourth quarter of each year. If any amount received by an employee is characterized as an excess parachute payment, the employee is subject to a 20% excise tax on the amount of the excess pursuant to Section&#160;4999 of the Code, and the Company is denied a tax deduction with respect to such excess. Overview of Our Executive Compensation Program Our General Partner has sole responsibility for conducting our business and managing our operations, and its executive officers and its Board make decisions on our behalf. As is typical of publicly traded limited partnerships, we do not directly employ any of the persons responsible for managing our business. Our General Partner&#8217;s executive officers manage and operate our business as part of the services provided by Kimbell Operating Company, LLC (&#8220; Kimbell Operating &#8221;) to our General Partner under a management services agreement. All of our General Partner&#8217;s executive officers and other employees necessary to operate our business are employed and compensated by Kimbell Operating or an entity with which Kimbell Operating arranges for the provision of services. The compensation for all our executive officers is indirectly paid by us pursuant to the management services agreement with Kimbell Operating. Neither Kimbell Operating nor any affiliated entity has entered into any employment agreement with any of its executive officers. Our General Partner&#8217;s Conflicts and Compensation Committee has adopted an annual review process for our executive compensation program. This annual review typically occurs in December of each year, and the most recent review was conducted in December&#160;2021. This process allows us to adjust our compensation practices and targets based on prevailing market and industry conditions at the time, which aligns our compensation philosophy with our business objectives and, thereby, the interests of our executive officers with those of our unitholders. Our Compensation Philosophy Our compensation program is designed to reward performance and to align the interests of our executive officers with those of our unitholders. As discussed further below, we seek to tie our compensation metrics to the achievement of performance goals and the creation of unitholder value. Our long-term incentives, in the form of restricted unit awards, represent a significant portion of the total compensation paid to our executive officers. In addition, these equity awards incentivize the creation of unitholder value and encourage retention of executives and key employees through the use of multi-year vesting schedules.
