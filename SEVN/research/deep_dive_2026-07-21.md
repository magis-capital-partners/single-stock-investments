## What this business is
Seven Hills Realty Trust is a commercial mortgage real estate investment trust that originates and holds first mortgage loans on middle-market transitional commercial properties across the United States. **[Fact]** As of March 31, 2026 it held 26 floating-rate loans with total commitments of $776.0 million, an average loan size of $30.0 million, financed with secured repurchase facilities from four banks. **[Fact]** The trust is externally managed by Tremont Realty Capital, a wholly owned subsidiary of The RMR Group, which also owns roughly 20% of SEVN's shares. **[Fact]** SEVN pays Tremont a base management fee of 1.5% of equity per year plus an incentive fee equal to 20% of core earnings above a 7% annual hurdle. **[Fact]**

---

## Why the market might be wrong
SEVN trades at $8.40 per share, or about 0.58 times its $14.47 book value per share, a discount typical of commercial mortgage REITs during a period of broad worry about office and other transitional commercial real estate credit. **[Fact]** SEVN's own loan book does not show that stress: no realized losses through the first quarter of 2026, a weighted average loan-to-value ratio of 66%, a weighted average risk rating of 2.8 out of 5, and no loans secured by office property in central business districts. **[Fact]** In December 2025 the trust raised $65.2 million of gross proceeds in a rights offering, and as of March 31, 2026 it still held $56.6 million of cash and $44.0 million of unfunded loan commitments waiting to be invested at a current weighted average yield near 7.8%. **[Fact]** Distributable earnings per share fell to $0.24 in the first quarter of 2026, from $0.28 in the fourth quarter of 2025, purely because that new capital has not finished earning a full quarter of income yet. **[Inference]** The market appears to be pricing the temporary earnings dip and sector-wide office fear together, rather than SEVN's own underwriting.

---

## Executive summary
Seven Hills Realty Trust lends against middle-market commercial property at a weighted average loan-to-value of 66% and has recorded no realized credit losses. A December 2025 capital raise temporarily diluted distributable earnings to $0.24 per share this quarter, but $100 million of cash and unfunded commitments still need to be invested at roughly 7.8% yields, which should lift that figure back toward the $0.28 run rate over the next year or two. At $8.40 per share, a 42% discount to book value, we expect about **14.6%** per year over seven years on a base case that assumes only a partial narrowing of that discount and a return to the pre-raise earnings run rate. The dividend yield alone is 13.3%. The manager is an external affiliate of The RMR Group, which is a genuine conflict-of-interest risk, and the discount to book value could persist longer than modeled if commercial real estate credit worsens. Moat is unproven and dhando is partial. We propose **watch**.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** A commercial real estate credit shock, particularly in office, causes realized losses, forces a dividend cut, and keeps the price-to-book discount wide or wider for years rather than months.

- Office is 23% of the loan portfolio by property type per `SEVN-Q1-2026-Earnings-Presentation.pdf`; even without central-business-district exposure, suburban office credit can deteriorate quickly in a downturn (two office loans already carry a risk rating of 4)
- External management fees continue to grow with equity raised even if per-share returns do not, per the fee schedule in `10-K_20260218_rpt20251231_acc0001452477_26_000010.htm`, which weakens the case that management is fully aligned with shareholders
- The distributable earnings recovery depends on redeploying $100 million of cash and unfunded commitments at good risk-adjusted spreads; if new loan opportunities are scarce or lower-yielding, the recovery to the $0.28 run rate slips

**Inversion:** If we are wrong, SEVN is not a mispriced, well-underwritten book. It is a small, externally managed mortgage REIT correctly priced for a coming credit cycle that the loan-level metrics have not yet revealed, and the discount to book value is a warning, not an opportunity.

**Lens failure mode:** See the levered lens failure mode note under Payoff & return above.
