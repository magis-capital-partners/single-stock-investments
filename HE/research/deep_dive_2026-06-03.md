## What this business is
Hawaiian Electric Industries is the parent of Hawaii's regulated electric utility and related businesses serving most of the state. **[Fact]** Consolidated revenues were about **$3.09 billion** in fiscal 2025 (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260227_rpt20251231_acc0000354707_26_000008.htm`). **[Fact]** The company reports an **Electric Utility** segment plus **All Other** activities. **[Fact]** Earnings and cash flow are set largely by the Hawaii Public Utilities Commission through allowed returns on rate-base capital, not by open-market pricing alone. **[Fact]** The common dividend has been suspended since the August 2023 Maui wildfires while the company funds legal and restoration obligations. **[Fact]** HE completed the sale of its **90.1%** stake in American Savings Bank at the end of calendar 2024 and is now essentially a pure-play utility (**[Inference]** from Horizon Kinetics Q1 2025 commentary, context tier).

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock still trades like a distressed credit event at roughly **$13** per share (Stooq, 2026-06-03), down sharply from pre-fire levels. **[Inference]** Index and income funds that sort on dividend yield never see the name, which creates a **predictive attribute** (Horizon Kinetics equity yield curve): the payoff is known in direction (regulated recovery, dividend reinstatement, credit repair) but mis-timed for twelve-month holders. **[Inference]** The market may be anchoring on trough GAAP earnings (**$0.71** diluted EPS in FY2025 per 10-K XBRL) instead of mid-cycle owner cash once wildfire payments normalize. **[Opinion]** Patience is the edge; precision on timing and residual liability is not.

## Executive summary
Hawaiian Electric is a regulated Hawaii utility recovering from the 2023 Maui wildfire overhang, with the bank subsidiary sold and the dividend still suspended. At **$13.37** per share, Marvin's base case blends trough FY2025 owner cash with a normalized recovery path and implies about **12.05%** annual return over seven years, below our **~15%** bar. The regulated monopoly and capped downside support a **partial** asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando), but residual legal risk and capex keep the moat only **narrow**. **Stance: watch** until dividend policy, credit trajectory, or a cheaper entry clarifies the equity-yield-curve thesis. Horizon Kinetics commentary is noted as **context only**, not in base IRR.

## Business & moat
### What (Stahl + Lawrence bucket)

**[Fact]** HE operates as a **regulated utility** (Lawrence bucket: regulated_utility): revenues near **$3.1 billion**, with earnings sharing mechanisms that cap both upside and downside versus allowed return on equity. **[Fact]** FY2025 **net income** was about **$126 million** (XBRL), far below FY2024's **$1.4 billion**, which included large settlement-related items. **[Fact]** **Operating cash flow** was about **$391 million** in FY2025 versus **$487 million** in FY2024. **[Fact]** **Stockholders' equity** at the parent was about **$1.61 billion** at December 31, 2025. **[Fact]** **Shares outstanding** about **172.6 million** (Q1 2026 10-Q).

### Mental models

- **Regulated monopoly (Munger moat):** Service territory and PUC oversight create pricing power within a band; political and catastrophe risk can break the usual utility calm.
- **Inversion:** What kills the thesis? A new wildfire liability wave, inability to recover costs in rates, or credit stress that blocks capex for grid hardening.
- **Equity yield curve (Horizon Kinetics, context):** Known recovery path, wrong holder horizon; dividend suspension hides the name from yield screens.

### Business mechanics (Hohn)

**Operating snapshot.** Customers pay regulated electric bills; the utility invests in generation, transmission, and distribution across isolated island grids. Growth comes from rate-base additions (renewables, resilience) approved by regulators, not from volume explosions.

**Thesis pillars.**

1. **Regulated earnings floor:** Allowed ROE frameworks limit downside absent a new catastrophe (**[Fact]** HK Q1 2025 describes earnings-sharing mechanics; **[Fact]** 10-K segment structure).
2. **Liability chapter maturing:** Wildfire settlements and legal reserves reduce tail uncertainty versus 2023-2024 (**[Inference]**; monitor 10-K contingency note each quarter).
3. **Simpler structure:** Bank sale removes dual-regulator complexity and frees capital for the utility (**[Fact]** HK Q1 2025 on Dec 2024 bank sale).
4. **Dividend optionality:** Reinstatement restores the historical income buyer base (**[Inference]**; timing **[Assumption]**).

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Residual wildfire or climate liability exceeds reserved amounts and forces dilution or delayed dividend.

**Inversion checklist:**

- If the PUC denies cost recovery, earnings stay trapped at trough levels.
- If credit metrics weaken, capex deferral hurts reliability and growth.
- If management mis-allocates bank-sale proceeds, equity holders bear the mistake.

**Lens failure mode (event lens):** Thesis fails if the "recovery event" stalls: no dividend by year five, no credit upgrade, and legal headlines return.
