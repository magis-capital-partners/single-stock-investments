## What this business is
Amazon.com is a global retailer and technology platform. **[Fact]** Customers pay for physical and digital goods (North America and International stores), cloud infrastructure (AWS), advertising, and third-party seller services. **[Fact]** Cash to shareholders comes from operating cash flow after capital spending on logistics and data centers, not from a single product line. **[Fact]** FY2025 net sales were **$716.9B** (+12%); Q1 2026 net sales **$181.5B** (+17%). **[Fact]** AWS is the profit engine: Q1 2026 AWS sales **$37.6B** (+28%) with segment operating income margin **37.7%**. **[Inference]** Reported free cash flow is temporarily depressed by AI-related capex while operating cash flow remains large.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
There is **no clear dated mispricing signal** (HK predictive attribute: **none**). **[Inference]** The market prices Amazon as a growth compounder with embedded AI optionality, not as a distressed asset. **[Inference]** The debate is whether **normalized owner cash flow** after capex (not TTM FCF of **$1.2B**) supports the multiple at **~$263**. Return is **franchise earnings power at price**, contingent on capex moderating toward **~$90B** sustainable spend vs TTM purchases **$147.3B**.

## Executive summary
Amazon runs four reinforcing engines: retail logistics density, AWS, high-margin advertising (>$70B TTM), and custom silicon (Trainium/Graviton/Nitro). Q1 2026 consolidated operating income **$23.9B** (+30%) and AWS re-acceleration (+28%) confirm operating momentum. **[Fact]** TTM operating cash flow is **$148.5B**, but TTM FCF is only **$1.2B** because capex consumed nearly all OCF. **[Inference]** Q1 net income **$30.3B** includes **~$16.8B** pre-tax gain on Anthropic and must be normalized for earnings-power analysis.

At **~$263** (Stooq, 2026-05-25), Lawrence base case uses **$5.35/sh** normalized FCF (OCF less mid-cycle capex, not spot TTM FCF). That yields **3.6%** 10-year IRR (bear **-2.1%**, bull **7.6%**). The business has a **stable** durable competitive advantage (Munger moat) and **partial** asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando): strong OCF floor, but equity is sensitive to capex duration and multiple compression. Marvin proposes **watch** until capex normalizes or entry price improves; prior **hold** is a human override for a strategic compounder sleeve.

### Mental models in plain English

**Moat durability (Munger):** Can customers and developers stay locked in despite competition? **Yes.** AWS workload migration cost, Prime habit, and fulfillment speed create stickiness (`AMZN/investor-documents/ir-amzn/AMZN-Q1-2026-Earnings-Release.pdf`).

**Moat trajectory (Munger):** Is the competitive advantage widening or eroding? **Widening in cloud/AI**, stable in retail logistics; erosion risk is regulatory breakup, not near-term share loss (`AMZN/investor-documents/ir-amzn/2025-Shareholder-Letter-Final.pdf`).

**Deny compounding to others (Stahl):** Does scale block rivals from matching economics? **Partially yes.** Trainium/Graviton and **$147B** TTM capex raise the capital bar; Microsoft and Google remain credible at hyperscaler scale (same Q1 release).
