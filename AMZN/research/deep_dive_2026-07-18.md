## What this business is

Amazon.com is a global retailer and technology platform. **[Fact]** Customers pay for physical and digital goods (North America and International stores), cloud infrastructure (AWS), advertising, and third-party seller services. **[Fact]** FY2025 net sales were **$716.9 billion** (+12% YoY); consolidated operating income **$80.0 billion** (`10-K` FY2025). **[Fact]** Q1 2026 net sales **$181.5 billion** (+17% YoY); operating income **$23.9 billion** (+30% YoY per earnings release). **[Fact]** TTM operating cash flow through Q1 2026 was **$148.5 billion**, while TTM payments to acquire productive assets were **$151.0 billion** (`10-Q` Mar 2026 extract), leaving reported free cash flow near **$1.2 billion** on the company's supplemental definition. **[Inference]** Owner cash is temporarily depressed by AI-related capex while operating cash flow remains among the largest in the world.

## Executive summary

Amazon runs four reinforcing engines: retail logistics density, AWS, high-margin advertising (>$70 billion TTM), and custom silicon (Trainium/Graviton/Nitro). Q1 2026 AWS sales **$37.6 billion** (+28%) and consolidated operating income **$23.9 billion** (+30%) confirm operating momentum. **[Fact]** Q1 2026 net income **$30.3 billion** includes **~$15.6 billion** of other non-operating income (Anthropic-related) and must be normalized for earnings-power analysis.

At **~$255** (vendor close 2026-07-15), Lawrence total synthesis is **3.2%** per year on **$5.35** normalized owner cash per share (OCF less mid-cycle capex, not spot TTM FCF). The business has a **stable** moat and **partial** dhando: strong OCF floor, but equity is sensitive to capex duration and multiple compression. Marvin proposes **watch**; portfolio registry shows **hold** as a human override for the strategic compounder sleeve.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Prolonged AI capex near **$151 billion** TTM without AWS, ads, and silicon returns showing up in normalized free cash flow per share.

- AWS growth deceleration and margin compression from hyperscaler competition
- Regulatory/antitrust on retail or cloud (FTC settlement charges in FY2025)
- GAAP noise from Anthropic and similar investment marks
- Insider selling (Bezos) — context only, not a thesis breaker alone

**Inversion:** Amazon is a permanently over-earning franchise priced for perfection: low-single-digit IRRs while OCF stays strong but never converts to shareholder FCF at the modeled pace.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** Using TTM FCF **~$1.2B** or Q1 GAAP net income **$30.3B** (with Anthropic mark) as starting owner cash without normalization.
