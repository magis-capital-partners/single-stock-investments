## What this business is
Amazon.com is a global retailer and technology platform. **[Fact]** Customers pay for physical and digital goods (North America and International stores), cloud infrastructure (AWS), advertising, and third-party seller services. **[Fact]** Cash to shareholders comes from operating cash flow after capital spending on logistics and data centers, not from a single product line. **[Fact]** FY2025 net sales were **$716.9 billion** (+12%); Q1 2026 net sales **$181.5 billion** (+17%). **[Fact]** AWS is the profit engine: Q1 2026 AWS sales **$37.6 billion** (+28%) with segment operating income margin **37.7%**. **[Inference]** Reported free cash flow is temporarily depressed by AI-related capital spending while operating cash flow remains large.

Return is **franchise earnings power at price**, contingent on capital spending moderating toward a **~$90 billion** sustainable run rate versus trailing purchases of **$147.3 billion**.

## Executive summary
Amazon runs four reinforcing engines: retail logistics density, AWS, high-margin advertising (more than **$70 billion** trailing twelve months), and custom silicon (Trainium, Graviton, Nitro). Q1 2026 consolidated operating income **$23.9 billion** (+30%) and AWS re-acceleration (+28%) confirm operating momentum. **[Fact]** Trailing twelve-month operating cash flow is **$148.5 billion**, but trailing free cash flow is only **$1.2 billion** because capital spending consumed nearly all operating cash flow. **[Fact]** Q1 2026 net income **$30.3 billion** includes roughly **$16.8 billion** pre-tax gain on Anthropic and must be normalized for earnings-power analysis.

At about **$255** (vendor close 2026-07-15; confirm live), Lawrence total synthesis is **3.2% per year** on **$5.35 per share** normalized owner cash (operating cash flow less mid-cycle capital spending, not spot trailing free cash flow). The business has a **stable** durable competitive advantage (Munger moat) and **partial** asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando): strong operating cash flow floor, but equity is sensitive to capital spending duration and multiple compression. Marvin proposes **watch** on return math; registry stance **hold** remains a human override for the strategic compounder sleeve.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** **Prolonged AI capital spending overshoot** (trailing purchases **$147.3 billion** vs operating cash flow **$148.5 billion**) without AWS, ads, and silicon returns showing up in normalized free cash flow per share.

- **AWS growth deceleration** and margin compression from hyperscaler price competition.
- **Regulatory/antitrust** on retail or cloud.
- **GAAP noise** from Anthropic and similar investment marks distorting quarterly net income trends.

**Inversion:** If we are wrong, Amazon is a permanently over-earning franchise priced for perfection: you earn low-single-digit returns while operating cash flow stays strong but never converts to shareholder free cash flow at the modeled pace.
