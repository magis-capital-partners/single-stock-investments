#### History (summary)

- 1997–1999: Origins in Hong Kong hedge-fund operations; Simplex Asset Management (Japan) established 1999.
- 2006: Holding company structure; Simplex Financial Holdings as parent.
- 2009: First ETF listed (WTI crude-linked, Osaka/TSE).
- 2015: Listed on **TOKYO PRO Market**.
- 2019: Storm Harbor Securities (associate).
- 2021–2024: Wind-down of legacy HK entity; Simplex Capital Investment; Simplex Heritage (multi-family office).

#### Business description

The group is a **holding company** centered on **Simplex Asset Management Co., Ltd.**, with Hong Kong AM, capital investment, and heritage/MFO units. Core activity is **investment management and advisory**.

**GAAP reporting:** Single segment — **investment management and advisory**. Commercial lines (not separate GAAP segments):

1. **Funds** — Institutional mandates (family offices, pensions, governments, banks); product-first, often smaller capacity strategies; Hong Kong license from 2014.
2. **ETFs** — Listed products beyond plain Nikkei/TOPIX (leveraged, inverse, thematic, active ETFs including PBR-focused listings 2023).
3. **Open innovation** — Intermediation between investors and banks on total-return swap / solution structuring.

The group positions itself as one of few **independent** Japanese asset managers (not tied to a major bank securities group).

### Management policy and challenges

**Mission:** “Independent asset manager that puts investors first.”

**Revenue model:** Base fees on AUM plus **performance (success) fees** tied to fund returns; success fees are volatile with markets. Management prioritizes covering fixed costs with **base fees** for stability.

**Strategic priorities**

1. Diversify products beyond Japan equity-heavy revenue.
2. Broaden investor base while maintaining performance track record.
3. Recruit and retain global talent (compensation and platform).
4. Strengthen governance and internal controls post-PRO Market listing (2015).

## Part III — Business risks (investor should read full Japanese PDF)

Material risks disclosed include:

1. **Market environment** — Success fees and AUM can fall sharply in weak markets; redemptions may accelerate.
2. **Third-party reliance** — Custody banks, brokers, and other vendors; service failures could disrupt operations.
3. **Systems** — Network/server failure despite contingency planning.
4. **Small organization** — ~50 employees group-wide; key-person dependency.
5. **Dependence on CEO** — Hiromasa Mizushima is central to management and fund oversight and is a major shareholder; incapacity could hurt results.
6. **Regulation** — Japan FSA (Type II broker-dealer registration, investment management, advisory); also Hong Kong and BVI rules; breach could mean suspension or client withdrawals.
7. **Natural disaster / pandemic** — Business continuity risk.
8. **J-Adviser contract** — PRO Market listing requires a J-Adviser (Japan M&A Center); termination could affect listing status (see filing for conditions).
