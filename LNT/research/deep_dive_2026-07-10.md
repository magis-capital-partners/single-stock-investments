## What this business is
Alliant Energy is a regulated electric and natural gas utility holding company serving Iowa and Wisconsin through Interstate Power and Light (IPL) and Wisconsin Power and Light (WPL). **[Fact]** Consolidated revenues were **$4.36 billion** in fiscal 2025, up from **$3.98 billion** in 2024 (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260220_rpt20251231_acc0000352541_26_000007.htm`, XBRL). **[Fact]** The company earns returns authorized by state regulators on rate-base capital invested in generation, transmission, distribution, and gas infrastructure. **[Fact]** FY2025 net income was **$810 million** and diluted earnings per share were **$3.15** on roughly **257 million** shares. **[Fact]** Management targets a **$2.14** annual dividend per share in 2026, a **5%** increase from the **$2.03** paid in 2025. **[Fact]** A **$12.4 billion** construction and acquisition plan for 2026 through 2029 is focused on renewables, energy storage, dispatchable gas, and grid hardening to serve growing load, including data centers under executed electric service agreements.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock trades like a bond-proxy utility: investors buy the **2.8%** dividend yield at **$75.70** and credit quality, not a growth franchise. **[Inference]** That framing may underprice the rate-base growth embedded in **$3.1 billion+** annual capital spending and data-center interconnection agreements disclosed in the 10-K. **[Inference]** Conversely, the market may be right that the **IPL retail electric base-rate moratorium** through September 2029 caps near-term Iowa earnings acceleration even as capital spending rises. **[Opinion]** There is no dated mispricing catalyst; the edge, if any, is patience on a regulated compounding path at a full (not cheap) price.

---

## Executive summary
Alliant Energy is a Midwest regulated utility compounding rate base through a large renewables, storage, and gas build-out while paying a growing dividend. FY2025 earnings per share reached **$3.15** on **$4.36 billion** of revenue; Q1 2026 earnings per share of **$0.87** rose **5%** year over year. At **$75.70** per share (Yahoo close, July 9, 2026), Marvin's base case implies about **5.47%** annual return over seven years on starting owner cash of **$3.15** per share, well below our **~15%** bar. The regulated franchise supports a **narrow** moat and **partial** asymmetric payoff (regulated floor, but interest-rate and execution risk on **$10.95 billion** of long-term debt). **Stance: watch** until either a cheaper entry or clearer proof that data-center load converts to authorized returns faster than the Iowa moratorium allows. No approved third-party sources inform base IRR.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Capital spending overruns or regulatory disallowance delay rate-base recovery while **$10.95 billion** of debt magnifies interest expense.

**Inversion checklist:**

- If data-center load connects faster than planned, Wisconsin and Iowa earnings could beat moratorium constraints.
- If regulators approve accelerated cost recovery, the equity yield could re-rate toward growth-utility peers.
- If interest rates fall, refinancing tailwinds could lift total return without operational change.

**Lens failure mode (operating lens):** Thesis fails if allowed returns compress and earnings per share growth stalls near dividend growth only, leaving total return trapped at **8%–10%** with no price margin of safety.
