## What this business is

Applied Digital builds and operates high-power data centers for AI and HPC workloads. **[Fact]** Tenants pay long-term take-or-pay lease rent for energized megawatts; Applied Digital does not own the GPUs. **[Fact]** FQ3 2026 (Feb 28, 2026) revenue was $126.6 million (+139% YoY) with $44.1 million adjusted EBITDA excluding the held-for-sale Cloud Services segment. **[Fact]** On May 20, 2026 the company announced Polaris Forge 3: a 15-year lease with the same U.S. investment-grade hyperscaler as Delta Forge 1 for 300 MW critical IT load (~$7.5 billion base revenue, ops from August 2027). **[Fact]** Total contracted critical IT load is now **1,200 MW** (net) across **four** campuses with **~$31 billion** base contracted lease revenue (~65% from investment-grade hyperscalers). **[Fact]** GAAP net income to common was $100.9M in FQ3 (includes $59.7M discontinued-operation gain on Cloud Services held-for-sale per `10-Q_20260408_rpt20260228_acc0001144879_26_000030.htm`); equity remains subordinate to secured debt and preferred.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The market prices Applied Digital as a speculative AI infrastructure story with binary execution risk, not as a contracted lease book approaching cash-flow breakeven. **[Inference]** GAAP losses, stock-based compensation ($48.9 million in FQ3), and CoreWeave concentration push generalists away before adjusted economics scale with energization. **[Inference]** If 600+ MW energizes on disclosed timelines, forward owner cash flow per share could exceed what a loss-making GAAP multiple implies. There is no clean equity yield curve with one dated payoff; return depends on a campus energization stack over several years.

## Executive summary

Applied Digital is transitioning from crypto-era hosting to a **1.2 GW** contracted lease book across four AI Factory campuses. FQ3 2026 adjusted EBITDA was $44.1 million; GAAP net income was $100.9M but includes a $59.7M discontinued-operation gain on Cloud Services held-for-sale, so continuing economics still require adjusted metrics. The May 20, 2026 **Polaris Forge 3** deal adds 300 MW and ~$7.5 billion base revenue with the same hyperscaler as Delta Forge 1, lifting disclosed contracted revenue to **~$31 billion**. At $46.85 (Stooq, May 25, 2026), Marvin floor IRR is **11.5%** on **$1.25/sh** forward owner cash; **blended best ~12.6%** on **$1.35/sh** (partial PF3 ramp credit, not full bull adoption). Bull **19.3%** if PF1–PF3 and Delta Forge hit timelines. Moat is unproven and dhando fails because equity sits below secured creditors. We propose **watch**: lease momentum strengthens the bull case, but blended IRR **does not clear** the **15%** bar and capital structure gates keep accumulate closed.
