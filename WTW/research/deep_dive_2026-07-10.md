## What this business is

Willis Towers Watson (WTW) is a global advisory, broking, and solutions company focused on people, risk, and capital. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$9.7 billion** (-2% reported, **+5% organic**), split between **Health, Wealth & Career (HWC)** at **$5.3 billion** (~55% of segment revenue) and **Risk & Broking (R&B)** at **$4.3 billion** (~45%). **[Fact]** The company earns commissions on insurance placements (tied to carrier premium levels), fee-based consulting on employee benefits and executive compensation, and outsourced benefits administration. **[Fact]** FY2025 operating cash flow was **$1.8 billion**; capital expenditures and capitalized software were **$229 million**; share repurchases **$1.65 billion**; dividends **$358 million**. **[Fact]** Q1 2026 revenue was **$2.4 billion** (+8.5% versus $2.2 billion prior year) with diluted EPS **$3.10** (+33%).

## Why the market might be wrong

There is **no dated mispricing catalyst** (HK predictive attribute: **none**). **[Inference]** The market may anchor on the **2024 goodwill impairment** ($1.0 billion, TRANZACT-related) and reported net income of **$98 million**, underweighting the normalized **$1.6 billion** FY2025 net income and **+5% organic** revenue trajectory. **[Fact]** Management simplified the portfolio: sold TRANZACT (Dec 2024), received **$750 million** Willis Re earnout (2025), and acquired tech-broker **Newfront** (Dec 2025). **[Inference]** Generalists treating WTW as a pure P&C cycle play may miss the fee-durable HWC consulting mix and international health growth (double-digit organic in FY2025). The edge is **quality franchise at ~18 times owner cash**, not a timed yield-curve setup.

## Executive summary

WTW is a scaled global insurance and benefits advisory platform with two profitable segments, strong organic growth (**+5%** FY2025, **+8.5%** Q1 2026 revenue), and aggressive capital return (**$1.65 billion** repurchases FY2025). FY2025 owner cash is about **$15.62** per share on **~99 million** diluted shares. At about **$290** per share (July 2026), Lawrence synthesis implies about **10.1%** per year over seven years, below our **15%** accumulate hurdle. Moat is **stable**: global client relationships, regulatory licenses, and data scale, but P&C premium cycles and goodwill write-down history cap widening. Dhando is **partial**: asset-light model and dividend (**$3.84** annualized) bound distress, but **$5.8 billion** long-term debt and premium-cycle exposure limit asymmetry at ~18 times free cash flow. Marvin proposes **watch**.

### Mental models in plain English

**Moat durability (Munger):** Does scale protect the franchise? **Partially yes.** Long-dated client relationships, regulatory licensing across jurisdictions, and integrated data/analytics create switching costs. But broking commissions depend on carrier premium levels the company does not control.

**Moat trajectory (Munger):** Is the advantage widening? **Stable.** R&B operating income rose to **$1.1 billion** from **$958 million** on organic growth and operating leverage. HWC held **$1.7 billion** segment operating income despite TRANZACT divestiture. Goodwill impairment in 2024 signals past overpayment, not current erosion.

**Deny compounding to others (Stahl):** Can peers replicate? **Partially.** Marsh McLennan, Aon, and Gallagher compete globally; WTW's scale supports specialty broking and international health, but not monopoly economics.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** P&C premium cycle turns soft, compressing Risk & Broking commission revenue faster than consulting fees can offset. **[Fact]** WTW discloses that commission levels follow premium trends set by carriers, not by WTW (10-K FY2025 Item 1).

**Secondary risks:**

- **Goodwill / M&A:** **$10+ billion** goodwill on balance sheet; 2024 **$1.0 billion** impairment after TRANZACT sale shows write-down risk if acquired units underperform (10-K FY2025).
- **Integration:** Newfront acquisition (Dec 2025) requires technology and culture integration; failure would waste senior-note-funded purchase price (10-K Note 3; Q1 2026 10-Q).
- **Regulatory:** U.S. and EU scrutiny of broker compensation and inducements could cap fee growth (10-K risk factors).

**Inversion (Munger):** What would make this fail? Organic revenue growth turns negative for two years, R&B margins compress below **20%**, and management funds buybacks with debt while FCF stagnates. Monitor organic revenue, segment operating income, and net debt each quarter.

**Lens failure mode (operating lens):** Thesis rests on normalized owner cash compounding; a prolonged soft P&C market without consulting offset would break the growth path without balance-sheet distress.
