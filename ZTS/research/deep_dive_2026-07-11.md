## What this business is
Zoetis Inc. (NYSE: **ZTS**) is the world's largest pure-play animal health company, selling medicines, vaccines, diagnostics, and services for companion animals (dogs and cats) and livestock (cattle, swine, poultry, fish). **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$9.47 billion** (up **2%** versus **$9.26 billion** in FY2024). **[Fact]** The company reports two species segments: **companion animal** revenue of roughly **$6.59 billion** (~70% of sales) and **livestock** revenue of roughly **$2.76 billion** (~29%), plus a small other line. **[Fact]** Cash from operations was **$2.90 billion** and capital spending on property and equipment was **$621 million**, yielding about **$5.14** of owner cash per diluted share on **443.8 million** weighted-average diluted shares. **[Inference]** Zoetis is a global pricing-power compounder in pet medicines and livestock biologics, with innovation concentrated in parasiticides (Simparica), dermatology (Apoquel, Cytopoint), and pain (Librela), offset by periodic U.S. companion softness.

## Why the market might be wrong
There is **no classic dated mispricing signal** (HK predictive attribute: **none**). **[Inference]** Generalists may anchor on the **2013 Pfizer spin** narrative or treat animal health as a slow-growth pharma adjacency, missing that parasiticide and biologic franchises can re-price annually through the veterinarian channel. **[Fact]** The Simparica franchise grew **12%** operationally in FY2025; Simparica Trio U.S. sales topped **$1 billion** and remains the #1 U.S. canine parasiticide. **[Fact]** Q1 2026 U.S. companion animal revenue fell **11%** operationally while international companion rose **15%** and international livestock rose **19%**, showing geographic mix matters. **[Inference]** At today's price the market may be discounting debt-funded buybacks and U.S. companion volatility, or the quote may reflect a dislocation versus historical multiples; return here is **normalized owner cash at price**, not a separate pipeline sum-of-the-parts.

## Executive summary
Zoetis is the global leader in animal health, with durable companion franchises and a normalized livestock portfolio after the 2024 medicated feed additive divestiture. FY2025 cash from operations was **$2.90 billion**; after **$621 million** of capital spending, owner cash is about **$5.14 per share**. Management guides **2026** operational revenue growth of **3% to 5%** and adjusted net income growth of **3% to 6%**.

At about **$77** (Yahoo Finance, 2026-07-10), the Lawrence synthesis implies about **17.2% per year** on normalized owner cash, above our **15%** accumulate hurdle. The business has a **stable** durable advantage (Munger moat) on veterinarian-trusted brands and R&D scale, with **partial** asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando): strong cash generation and a **$2.03** annual dividend, but **$9.0 billion** of long-term debt and **$3.2 billion** of FY2025 share repurchases limit balance-sheet flexibility if companion growth stalls. Marvin proposes **hold** on math; **[HUMAN REVIEW]** on leverage-funded buybacks and price quote sanity. No approved external sources are in the base case.

4. **Capital return:** **$3.2 billion** of repurchases and **$2.03** dividend per share in FY2025, partially debt-funded (**$9.0 billion** long-term debt).

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** U.S. companion animal revenue stays weak while debt maturities and interest expense absorb cash that would otherwise fund innovation and buybacks.

**Inversion checklist:**

- If livestock normalizes lower after MFA divestiture, consolidated growth falls below guidance.
