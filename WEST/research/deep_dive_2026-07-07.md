## What this business is

Westrock Coffee Company is a vertically integrated beverage solutions provider that roasts coffee, blends tea and flavors, and packages products for large foodservice, restaurant, convenience, and private-label retail customers. **[Fact]** The company operates as a "brand-behind-the-brand" partner: it develops custom beverage programs rather than selling a single consumer-facing label. **[Fact]** Two reportable segments exist as of December 31, 2025: **Beverage Solutions** (value-added coffee, tea, extracts, ready-to-drink formats) and **Sustainable Sourcing & Traceability (SS&T)** (traceable green-coffee procurement and commodity forward contracts). **[Fact]** FY2025 consolidated net sales were **$1.19 billion**, up roughly **40%** versus FY2024. **[Fact]** The company went public in August 2022 via a SPAC merger and trades on Nasdaq under ticker WEST.

## Why the market might be wrong

The stock near **$7.04** may still reflect a post-SPAC, loss-making, capex-heavy manufacturer rather than a scaling beverage platform approaching operating break-even. **[Inference]** Q1 2026 delivered the first quarterly **operating profit** (**$3.2 million**) in recent filings while revenue grew **44%** year over year, yet the share price sits well below the March 2024 follow-on offering price of **$9.88**. **[Inference]** GAAP net loss (**$8.5 million** in Q1 2026) and negative stockholders equity screen out simple earnings-based buyers, while Consolidated Adjusted EBITDA of **$69.7 million** in FY2025 (up from **$47.2 million** in FY2024) signals improving underlying economics masked by interest expense and working-capital drag. **[Inference]** Complexity discount applies: leveraged balance sheet, Conway facility ramp, commodity hedging, and micro-to-mid-cap illiquidity keep generalist investors away despite diversified customers (no single customer above 10% of revenue in 2024–2025).

## Executive summary

Westrock Coffee is a turnaround beverage contract manufacturer scaling revenue through liquid extracts, ready-to-drink formats, and its Conway, Arkansas production hub. FY2025 revenue reached **$1.19 billion** (+40%); Consolidated Adjusted EBITDA was **$69.7 million**; net loss was **$90.4 million**; and the company ended FY2025 with **$49.9 million** cash against **$360.7 million** long-term debt and thin equity. Q1 2026 showed an inflection: revenue **$308.8 million** (+44% year over year) and **operating income $3.2 million**, though net loss persisted at **$8.5 million** and stockholders equity was negative **$20.2 million**.

At **$7.04** per share (July 7, 2026 close), our base scenario implies about **5% per year** over five years on a normalized path as scale-up costs roll off and Conway capacity utilization improves. Dhando is **partial**: covenant headroom and revenue momentum limit near-term blow-up risk, but interest burden and negative owner cash today cap upside asymmetry. Moat is **unproven** in a competitive contract-roasting market. Stance: **watch**. Bear case assumes covenant or commodity stress toward **$4** per share; bull case assumes deleveraging and margin recovery toward **$14** **[Assumption]**.

Moat **unproven** until post-ramp returns on invested capital exceed cost of debt.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Interest expense and working-capital needs absorb operating gains before they reach common equity, forcing dilutive refinancing if Conway ramp slips.

- Green-coffee commodity volatility and tariff uncertainty on imported equipment (FY2025 10-K MD&A)
- Secured net leverage covenant step-downs through 2026; breach could accelerate debt or restrict liquidity
- Customer program delays at Conway facility reduce capacity utilization
- Negative stockholders equity limits strategic flexibility and may raise cost of capital
- Integration and acquisition risk cited in risk factors; history includes S&D Coffee transaction complexity
- Seasonality and foodservice demand cyclicality

**Inversion:** What would make this fail? Revenue growth stalls, commodity costs spike without pass-through, and covenant breach forces dilutive equity at prices below **$5**.

**Lens failure mode:** Treating WEST as a normalized **compounder** on FY2025 GAAP losses misprices the business; it is a leveraged operating turnaround where owner cash trails operating income.
