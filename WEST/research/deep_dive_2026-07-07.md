## What this business is
Westrock Coffee Company is a vertically integrated beverage solutions provider that roasts coffee, blends tea and flavors, and packages products for large foodservice, restaurant, convenience, and private-label retail customers. **[Fact]** The company operates as a "brand-behind-the-brand" partner: it develops custom beverage programs rather than selling a single consumer-facing label. **[Fact]** Two reportable segments exist as of December 31, 2025: **Beverage Solutions** (value-added coffee, tea, extracts, ready-to-drink formats) and **Sustainable Sourcing & Traceability (SS&T)** (traceable green-coffee procurement and commodity forward contracts). **[Fact]** FY2025 consolidated net sales were **$1.19 billion**, up roughly **40%** versus FY2024. **[Fact]** The company went public in August 2022 via a SPAC merger and trades on Nasdaq under ticker WEST.

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock near **$7.04** may still reflect a post-SPAC, loss-making, capex-heavy manufacturer rather than a scaling beverage platform approaching operating break-even. **[Inference]** Q1 2026 delivered the first quarterly **operating profit** (**$3.2 million**) in recent filings while revenue grew **44%** year over year, yet the share price sits well below the March 2024 follow-on offering price of **$9.88**. **[Inference]** GAAP net loss (**$8.5 million** in Q1 2026) and negative stockholders equity screen out simple earnings-based buyers, while Consolidated Adjusted EBITDA of **$69.7 million** in FY2025 (up from **$47.2 million** in FY2024) signals improving underlying economics masked by interest expense and working-capital drag. **[Inference]** Complexity discount applies: leveraged balance sheet, Conway facility ramp, commodity hedging, and micro-to-mid-cap illiquidity keep generalist investors away despite diversified customers (no single customer above 10% of revenue in 2024 and 2025).

## Executive summary
Westrock Coffee is a turnaround beverage contract manufacturer scaling through liquid extracts, ready-to-drink formats, and its Conway, Arkansas production hub. FY2025 revenue was **$1.19 billion** (+40%); Consolidated Adjusted EBITDA **$69.7 million**; net loss **$90.4 million**; long-term debt **$360.7 million**. Q1 2026 inflected with revenue **$308.8 million** (+44% year over year) and **operating income $3.2 million**, though net loss remained **$8.5 million** and stockholders equity was negative **$20.2 million**.

At **$7.04** per share, we expect about **-6.37%** per year over seven years on normalized owner cash of **$0.15** per share after Conway ramp costs normalize. Dhando is **partial** because covenant headroom and revenue momentum limit near-term blow-up risk, but interest expense near **$55.7 million** caps upside asymmetry. Moat is **unproven** in contract roasting. Stance: **watch**.
