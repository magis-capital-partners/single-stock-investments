## What this business is
Zebra Technologies is an enterprise asset intelligence company that sells rugged mobile computers, barcode and RFID data capture, label printers, machine vision, self-service touchscreens, and attached software and services to frontline workers in retail, manufacturing, transportation, and healthcare. **[Fact]** FY2025 consolidated net sales were **$5.40 billion** (+**8.3%** versus 2024) with operating income of **$700 million** (~**13.0%** margin) and net income of **$419 million** (**$8.18** diluted earnings per share). **[Fact]** In 2025 the company reorganized into two reportable segments: **Connected Frontline (CF)** ($2.96 billion sales, **$585 million** operating income) and **Asset Visibility and Automation (AVA)** ($2.44 billion sales, ~**$115 million** operating income). **[Fact]** Free cash flow was about **$831 million** in FY2025 (cash from operations **$917 million** less capital spending **$86 million**), or roughly **$16.23** per diluted share on **51.2 million** shares.

There is **no clear dated mispricing signal** (predictive attribute: **none**). **[Inference]** The stock may already reflect a durable installed-base franchise and recent automation adjacency deals (Matrox, Photoneo, Elo). **[Fact]** Q1 2026 net sales rose **14.3%** year over year to **$1.50 billion** with operating income **$215 million** (+**10%**), which may indicate recovery momentum not fully priced if the market is anchored to FY2025 margin compression from the Elo integration. ## Executive summary
Zebra is an **enterprise asset intelligence compounder** with a razor-and-blade model across mobile computing, printing, RFID, and growing services and software attach. FY2025 free cash flow per share is about **$16.23** on **$917 million** of operating cash flow and **$86 million** of capital spending. The company repurchased **$587 million** of stock while closing the **$1.30 billion** Elo acquisition and the **$62 million** Photoneo deal, leaving cash at **$125 million** and long-term debt at **$2.36 billion**. Q1 2026 showed **14%** revenue growth and diluted earnings per share of **$2.72**. At about **$275** per share (NYSE close 2026-07-10), our base synthesis implies **13.15% per year** over seven years, below the **15%** hold band. Moat is **stable** on workflow validation and installed base; **partial** dhando because leverage and integration risk cap asymmetry at a premium multiple. Marvin proposes **watch**. Primary risk: **Elo integration and elevated leverage** limiting buybacks and compressing margins if automation demand softens.

### Mental models in plain English

**Moat durability (Munger):** Do validated enterprise workflows lock in customers? **Yes.** Retail, warehouse, and manufacturing customers standardize on Zebra mobile computers, printers, and data capture because downtime is costly and software integrations are embedded (`10-K FY2025` Business section).

**Moat trajectory (Munger):** Is the advantage widening? **Stable.** Services and software rose to **$978 million** (**18%** of revenue) and RPO reached **~$1.17 billion**, but FY2025 gross margin in CF fell **200 bps** to **47.2%** after Elo (`10-K FY2025` CF segment table).

**Deny compounding to others (Stahl):** Can Honeywell, Datalogic, or Cognex replicate scale? **Partially.** Peers compete in niches; Zebra's breadth across CF and AVA is hard to clone, though machine vision remains fragmented (`10-K FY2025` competition disclosure).
