## What this business is
Bullish Global Limited operates an institutional-focused digital asset exchange (Bullish Exchange) and owns CoinDesk, a crypto media, indices, data, and events platform. **[Fact]** The Bullish Exchange runs a global central limit order book with automated market making, spot and derivatives products, and portfolio margin. **[Fact]** CoinDesk spans Indices (CoinDesk 20 and benchmarks), Data (270k+ trading pairs), Insights (Coindesk.com, Consensus events), and related subscription and licensing revenue. **[Fact]** FY2025 digital asset sales (gross customer turnover passed through the exchange) were **$244.8 billion**; gross spread after cost of digital assets derecognized was about **$78 million**. **[Fact]** Other revenues (CoinDesk, fees, subscriptions, liquidity services) were **$158.9 million** in FY2025. **[Fact]** The company reported a FY2025 net loss of **$785.5 million** and cash from operations of **$28.6 million** on **127.7 million** basic shares. **[Fact]** Cash was **$87.9 million** at year-end 2025; borrowings due to a related-party SPV were **$514.8 million**. **[Fact]** Customer digital assets safeguarded were **$117.6 million** at December 31, 2024 per filing XBRL tags.

## Why the market might be wrong
**Predictive attribute:** complexity discount (pass-through turnover masks thin spread economics; treasury and related-party capital structure obscure operating cash).

**[Inference]** The market may treat Bullish as a scaled crypto infrastructure compounder because of CoinDesk brand, block.one lineage, and large reported digital asset sales. **[Fact]** Gross exchange spread was only about **$78 million** on **$244.8 billion** of digital asset sales in FY2025, and FY2025 net loss included **$675 million** of fair value loss on digital assets held plus **$52 million** of finance expense on related-party debt. **[Inference]** Investors may underweight customer concentration among market makers, related-party governance (block.one / Brendan Blumer ecosystem), and the difference between safeguarded customer assets and equity owner cash.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Related-party debt stack and treasury fair value volatility convert a thin-spread croupier into a levered crypto balance sheet at the wrong cycle phase.

Secondary risks:
- Loss of top market makers or index licensees (customer concentration per 20-F)
- Regulatory action on exchange, stablecoin collateral, or CoinDesk editorial conflicts
- Reputational contagion from block.one history (SEC 2019 settlement, 2025 class action settlement cited in 20-F)
- DeFi and DEX share gain reducing centralized exchange tolls

**Inversion:** What would make this work? Sustained growth in other revenues above **$200M** with stable OCF above **$50M**, related-party debt refinanced to arm's-length terms, and exchange gross spread expanding without proportional admin growth. Filings would need two consecutive years proving that pattern.
