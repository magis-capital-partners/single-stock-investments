## What this business is
Cadiz Inc. is a California water and land company developing the Mojave Groundwater Bank in San Bernardino County. **[Fact]** The company owns roughly **46,000 acres** in the Mojave Desert with permitted rights to conserve **2.5 million acre-feet** of groundwater over 50 years (average **50,000 acre-feet per year**) and to bank up to **one million acre-feet** of imported water in the underlying aquifer (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260331_rpt20251231_acc0001437749_26_010517.htm`, Item 1). **[Fact]** Cadiz also owns a **220-mile** idle natural-gas pipeline acquired in 2021 that management plans to convert for water conveyance, plus **43 miles** of pipeline right-of-way entitlements. **[Fact]** Operating revenue today comes mainly from **ATEC** water-filtration equipment sales (**$14.5 million** in FY2025), alfalfa farming on **760 acres**, and agricultural leases. **[Fact]** The company formed **Mojave Water Infrastructure Company LLC (MWI)** in December 2024 to finance an estimated **$1.25 to $1.5 billion** buildout with public, tribal, and private equity partners. **[Fact]** As of May 11, 2026, **84,086,286** common shares were outstanding (NASDAQ: CDZI).

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock may trade as a perennial "water project" story with dilution overhang, while filings show concrete permits, contracted offtake, and a capital structure taking shape around MWI. **[Fact]** In 2024 Cadiz signed agreements for **21,275 acre-feet per year** of supply to be delivered via the Northern Pipeline, with filing guidance that initial throughput may be **20,000 to 23,000 AFY** after value engineering (`10-K FY2025`, Item 1). **[Fact]** Supply pricing is estimated to net Cadiz approximately **$850 per acre-foot** (2024 dollars, inflation-adjusted) at the wellhead after shared capital and operating costs. **[Inference]** GAAP book value (**~$0.28** per share at December 31, 2025) carries land and pipeline at historical cost and does not capitalize the permitted water bank, so price-to-book screens understate the asset option. **[Inference]** Predictive attribute: **dormant asset** plus **equity yield curve**. Institutional holders may avoid the name until MWI financing closes and water deliveries begin, creating a timing gap between permit value and run-rate cash.

---

## Executive summary
Cadiz owns permitted Mojave groundwater storage and a 220-mile pipeline path, plus ATEC filter sales (**$16.3 million** FY2025 revenue). The company still loses money (**$31.1 million** net FY2025) and carries **$78.3 million** of debt while raising **~$451 million** through project vehicle MWI (Lytton **$51 million** facility signed October 2025). **We expect 6.2% per year** over seven years to a **$6.00** per share payoff if partial financing and pipeline conversion proceed. Dhando is **partial**; moat **unproven**. Stance **watch** until binding MWI equity and an in-service date.

**Unproven.** If constructed and operated, a permitted groundwater bank with dedicated pipeline interconnects to California aqueducts would resemble a localized water toll. Today there is no scaled delivery, heavy reliance on capital partners, and a long history of shareholder dilution. Moat assessment upgrades only after durable contracted cash flow at the wellhead.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** MWI fails to secure **~$451 million** of equity and complementary debt/grants, forcing more dilution or asset sales while debt matures.

**Inversion checklist:**

- If Lytton draw and PE commitments do not bind, can Cadiz fund operations for 24 months without punitive equity issuance?
- If Northern Pipeline throughput caps at **20,000 AFY**, does contracted **21,275 AFY** require Southern Pipeline capital earlier than modeled?
- If ATEC growth slows, is there any path to profitability without Mojave Bank?
- If secured debt maturities slip past June 2027, does Heerema relationship force dilutive restructuring?

**Lens failure mode (asset):** Treating GAAP book (**~$0.28** per share) as a floor ignores that fair value lives in permits and pipeline routes not fully marked on the balance sheet, while **debt exceeds equity**. The correct floor is probability-weighted project success, not book.
