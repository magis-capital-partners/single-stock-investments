## What this business is
Aehr Test Systems (AEHR) designs and sells burn-in and wafer-level test systems used to stress-test semiconductors before shipment, with growing focus on silicon carbide power devices for electric vehicles and industrial markets. **[Fact]** Shares trade on Nasdaq (AEHR). **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$65.0 million** (down **2%** year over year), operating income **$13.4 million** (up from **$10.1 million**), and net income **$14.6 million** (diluted earnings **$0.50** per share) per `AEHR/investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20250728_rpt20250530_acc0001654954_25_008553.htm`. **[Fact]** FY2025 cash was **$49.2 million** with no material long-term debt filed; inventory was **$42.0 million** against deferred revenue of only **$1.3 million**.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock may trade as a secular SiC wafer-level winner while near-term orders and cash conversion tell a cyclical equipment story. **[Inference]** Nine-month FY2026 revenue of **$44.9 million** trails the prior-year pace, and cash fell from **$49.2 million** to **$24.5 million** by February 2026 per the Q3 10-Q. **[Inference]** Inventory at **$42 million** is large relative to **$65 million** annual revenue, suggesting the market may overestimate how quickly SiC adoption converts to distributable owner cash. **[Inference]** Proof-first component math at base case sums to about **$2.89** per share of filing-locked economic value versus a price near **$77**, so the market embeds SiC TAM and semicap recovery well beyond what the conservative component schedule captures today.

Aehr Test Systems sells burn-in and wafer-level test equipment with a silicon carbide growth narrative. FY2025 margins expanded on slightly lower revenue, but Q3 FY2026 shows a revenue trough and cash burn from **$49M** to **$25M**.
