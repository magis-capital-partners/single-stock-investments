## What this business is

B3 S.A. – Brasil, Bolsa, Balcão operates Brazil's primary exchange and over-the-counter market infrastructure: listed equities and derivatives, fixed-income registration and trading (including Tesouro Direto), clearing, custody, market data, and technology services. **[Fact]** FY2024 total revenues were **R$10,572.7 million** (+6.6% versus 2023) with net revenue **R$9,513.5 million** (+6.5%). **[Fact]** Recurring net income was **R$4,783.9 million** (+4.0%); net income attributable to shareholders **R$4,576.6 million** (+10.8%). **[Fact]** The company has no controlling shareholder; gross debt was **R$13.4 billion** (2.0 times recurring EBITDA) at December 31, 2024. Revenue scales with handled volume and fee schedules, not balance-sheet lending on the core exchange franchise.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

There is no dated structural discount or royalty-trust exclusion here. **[Inference]** Brazil's prolonged high Selic cycle suppressed cash-equities average daily traded volume (**−3.8%** in 2024), while OTC fixed income, derivatives, and technology/data grew through the same period. **[Inference]** The market may overweight cyclical equity weakness and underweight the **~30%** of revenue from newer initiatives cited in the 2024 annual summary, plus recurring technology and data fees that inflate margins when listed cash equities are soft. Return at today's price is franchise earnings power, not a timed mispricing with a known catalyst date.

---

## Executive summary

B3 is Brazil's dominant market infrastructure croupier: exchange, OTC, clearing, and data tolls with minimal principal risk on core operations. FY2024 filings confirm record **R$10.6 billion** revenue, **R$4.8 billion** recurring net income, and **R$5.3 billion** returned to shareholders (116% payout via dividends, interest on equity, and buybacks). Cash equities volumes fell on high rates, but listed derivatives ADV rose **15.1%**, OTC fixed income grew **15.0%**, and technology/data revenue rose **10.5%**. At **R$15.05** per share, we expect about **13.11% per year** over 7 years on **R$0.87** recurring owner cash per share (below our about-fifteen-percent-per-year target). Asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) is **partial**: diversified fee stack and capital return help, but transaction revenue remains cyclical and **R$13.4 billion** gross debt adds leverage risk. We propose **watch**; illustrative entry near **R$12.50–13.50** would approach ~15% on the base case. **[HUMAN REVIEW]:** Confirm BRL price and FX translation for non-BRL portfolios.

### Mental models in plain English

**Croupier toll (Stahl):** Does the company earn fees on activity without taking principal risk on core operations? **Yes.** Trading, clearing, listing, custody, and data fees (`official-reports/Annual_Report_2024_Management_Report.pdf`, Management Report 2024).

**Volume vs share (Stahl):** Is handled volume growing in offsetting products when equities slow? **Yes.** Cash equities ADTV **−3.8%** but derivatives ADV **+15.1%** and OTC issuances **+19.1%** (same source).

**Fee model resilience (Stahl):** Does recurring revenue cushion transaction cyclicality? **Partially.** Technology, data, and access **R$2,156.7 million** (20.4% of revenue) grew **10.5%** while cash-equities trading revenue fell **1.9%**.

**Cycle normalization (Hohn):** Are we underwriting peak activity as normal? **No.** Base owner cash uses recurring net income (**R$0.87** per share), not gross operating cash that includes collateral and financial-investment swings.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** **Peak earnings trap** if high-rate Brazil keeps cash equities volumes depressed while the market applies a lower multiple to cyclical transaction revenue.

- **Regulatory intervention** on fees or market structure compresses take rates.  
- **Debt stack (R$13.4B gross)** amplifies refinancing risk if local rates stay elevated.  
- **Competitive leakage** to off-exchange crypto or international venues erodes listed derivatives RPC.

**Inversion:** If we are wrong, B3 remains Brazil's entrenched market infrastructure croupier trading at a full multiple with low-teens returns, not a broken franchise.
