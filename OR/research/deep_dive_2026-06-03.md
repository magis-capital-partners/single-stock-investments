## What this business is

OR Royalties Inc. (formerly Osisko Gold Royalties Ltd) is a Montreal-based precious metals royalty and streaming company listed on the NYSE and TSX. **[Fact]** The company holds a diversified portfolio of gold, silver, and copper royalties and streams on mines operated by third parties across the Americas, Australia, and other regions. **[Fact]** It earns revenue when operators produce and sell metal; OR Royalties has no mine operating staff and reports in U.S. dollars under IFRS. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue from royalties and streams was a record $277.4 million on 80,775 gold equivalent ounces (GEOs) earned, with operating cash flow of $245.6 million. **[Fact]** The company returned capital through dividends ($0.211 per share declared in 2025), share repurchases (1.1 million shares), and accretive royalty acquisitions including Namdini and a Gold Fields portfolio announced in early 2026.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The stock may look fully valued on a trailing multiple after a strong gold price year while underpricing a visible GEO growth path. **[Inference]** Management guides 80,000 to 90,000 GEOs in 2026 at roughly 97% cash margin, but the five-year outlook targets 120,000 to 135,000 GEOs by 2030 as Windfall, Cariboo, South Railroad, and other development assets start paying. **[Inference]** FY2025 reported net earnings of $1.10 per share overstate recurring power because of a $54.4 million gain on deemed disposal of the Osisko Development associate; adjusted earnings were $0.88 per share. **[Inference]** Royalty interests are carried at cost on the balance sheet, so book value does not mark portfolio metal exposure to spot prices or reserve upgrades at producing and development assets.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Precious metal price decline combined with operator delays on development assets that underpin the 2030 GEO outlook.

**Commodity leverage.** Revenue and GEO economics move with gold, silver, and copper prices; FY2025 benefited from strong metal markets.

**Operator dependence.** OR Royalties does not operate mines; Eagle Gold stoppage (2024) and impairment show asset-level failure modes.

**Acquisition risk.** Large 2026 commitments (Namdini up to $103.5M, Gold Fields portfolio $115M) must earn returns above cost of capital.

**Lens failure mode (optionality):** Treating FY2025 net earnings ($1.10/sh) as recurring owner cash overstates return; one-time associate disposal gain and cyclical metal prices can inflate apparent cheapness.
