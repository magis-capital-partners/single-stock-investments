## What this business is

Bursa Malaysia Berhad operates Malaysia's national stock exchange and related marketplace infrastructure. **[Fact]** Customers pay trading and clearing fees on cash equities and derivatives, listing and depository charges, Islamic market (Bursa Suq Al-Sila') fees, and market-data subscriptions. **[Fact]** FY2024 operating revenue was **RM757.7 million** (+27.8% versus last year) and profit attributable to owners was **RM310.1 million** (`official-reports/2024-12-31_Condensed_Report_Q4.pdf`). **[Fact]** The group had **809.3 million** ordinary shares outstanding and net assets per share of **RM1.08** at 31 December 2024 (same filing). **[Fact]** Q1 2025 operating revenue was **RM177.7 million** (-1.7% year-over-year) with owners' profit **RM68.4 million** (-8.8%) (`official-reports/2025-03-31_Condensed_Report_Q1.pdf`). The exchange also runs Bursa Carbon Exchange and digital investor channels under the refreshed multi-asset exchange brand.

## Why the market might be wrong

There is no dated structural discount or catalyst stack here. **[Inference]** FY2024 was a strong volume year (operating revenue +27.8%, IPO count 20 versus 7 in 2023) and the stock trades near **27×** trailing earnings on KLSE screens; the market may treat peak trading activity as a new normal. **[Inference]** Our base return uses mid-cycle owner cash below FY2024 spot free cash flow and below the **40 sen** total dividend declared for FY2024. **[Inference]** Predictive attribute: **cycle normalization** on Malaysia average daily value (ADV), not an equity yield curve or K-1 exclusion story.

## Executive summary

Bursa Malaysia is a domestic exchange toll collector: high margins on trading, clearing, listing, and data with minimal balance-sheet lending risk. FY2024 filings show **RM757.7 million** operating revenue, **RM310.1 million** owners' profit, and **RM436.6 million** cash from operations; Q1 2025 already shows softer securities fees (-12.2% trading revenue in the quarter). At about **RM8.55** per share **[Assumption]**, our base case annual return is **pending mechanical refresh** per year on **RM0.40** mid-cycle free cash flow per share (below our about-fifteen-percent-per-year target). Asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) is **partial**: regulated monopoly-like position and net-cash balance sheet help, but revenue is tied to local market turnover. Moat is **stable** but not widening while regional competition (Singapore) persists. We propose **watch** until price offers mid-cycle cash yield plus growth near our hurdle, or until Q2–Q4 2025 volumes confirm a higher sustainable run rate.

### Dhando (Pabrai)

**Partial.** Downside: Malaysia market turnover mean-reversion could cut owners' profit toward FY2023 **RM252m** (~**31 sen** EPS) while the balance sheet carries surplus cash (**RM367m** cash equivalents at 31 Dec 2024, excluding clearing floats). Upside: sustained IPO pipeline and derivatives/data mix lift mid-cycle cash toward **RM0.45–0.50** per share. At **RM8.55**, upside to **RM10** is ~17% versus ~25–30% Pabrai screen.

### Stance proposal

**Watch.** Moat stable and dhando partial, but base annual return at today's price sits below our ~15% target on mid-cycle owner cash. Revisit on price near **RM6.50–7.00** (roughly **6%** cash yield on normalized **RM0.40** plus growth) or if H2 2025 volumes prove FY2024 run rate sustainable.

Bear case: securities revenue reverts; annual return low single digits. Bull case: multi-asset mix sustains **4–5%** cash growth; return approaches low double digits.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Malaysia cash-market turnover mean-reverts after FY2024's retail and IPO surge, compressing trading fees faster than the data/listing layer grows.

- **Regulatory fee caps** on trading or clearing could compress margin (no current filing signal).
- **Regional competition** for listings (Singapore) could slow listing revenue growth.
- **Technology spend:** IT maintenance +19.8% in Q1 2025 YoY; sustained elevation would drag owner cash.

**Inversion (Munger):** What would make this fail? A prolonged bear market in Malaysian equities with ADV down **25%+** and no offset from derivatives/data. Clearing-house cash balances are segregated and not owner cash; do not treat **RM2.85 billion** client margin cash as distributable.

**Lens failure mode:** Misclassifying peak FY2024 EPS (**38.3 sen**) as sustainable owner cash would overstate return at today's price.
