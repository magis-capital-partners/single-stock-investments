## What this business is

Nasdaq Inc. operates global market infrastructure: the Nasdaq Stock Market and other exchanges, clearing and transaction services, index licensing, market data, and financial-technology software for anti-financial crime, regulatory reporting, and capital-markets workflows. **[Fact]** Customers pay listing, transaction, subscription, and SaaS fees; Nasdaq does not take directional market risk on customer positions. **[Fact]** FY2025 net revenues less transaction-based expenses were **$5,249 million**, operating income **$2,331 million**, and net income **$1,788 million** (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260212_rpt20251231_acc0001628280_26_007703.htm`). **[Fact]** Q1 2026 net revenues less transaction-based expenses were **$1,407 million** (+14% versus **$1,237 million** in Q1 2025); operating income **$657 million** (+20%); net income **$519 million** (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-Q_20260424_rpt20260331_acc0001628280_26_027105.htm`). Adena Friedman is Chair and CEO.

## Why the market might be wrong

There is no dated payoff or structural discount here. **[Inference]** Q1 2026 operating income reflects record U.S. cash-equity and equity-derivative volumes plus a **$89 million** net gain on divestitures; the market may treat that pace as permanent. **[Inference]** Our base return uses FY2025 owner cash per share (**$3.44**), not the Q1 2026 earnings run rate. Return is franchise earnings power at price, not a mispricing signal with a known catalyst date.

## Executive summary

Nasdaq Inc. is a toll collector on global capital-markets activity: exchange trading, clearing, index and data subscriptions, and financial-technology SaaS with minimal principal risk. FY2025 filings confirm **$5,249 million** net revenue less transaction expenses, **$2,331 million** operating income, and **$12,227 million** stockholders' equity; Q1 2026 accelerated on strong Market Services volumes and **$517 million** Financial Technology revenue (+20% year over year). At **$86.66** per share (Yahoo Finance, 2026-06-11), we expect about **9.26% per year** over 7 years on **$3.44** mid-cycle free cash flow per share (below our portfolio return target for a core add). Asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) stays **partial** because transaction revenue is cyclical and **$8.6 billion** long-term debt adds acquisition-integration risk. We propose **watch** and would revisit near **$58–65** where the Lawrence base case approaches our core-return hurdle.

### Mental models in plain English

**Croupier toll (Stahl):** Does the company earn fees on activity without taking principal risk? **Yes.** Market Services and Capital Access revenues carry high operating leverage (`10-K_20260212_rpt20251231_acc0001628280_26_007703.htm`).

**Volume vs share (Stahl):** Is handled volume growing? **Yes.** FY2025 total net revenues less transaction expenses rose **12.9%** versus FY2024; Q1 2026 operating income **$657 million** versus **$547 million** prior-year quarter (`10-Q_20260424_rpt20260331_acc0001628280_26_027105.htm`).

**Fee model resilience (Stahl):** Does recurring revenue cushion transaction cyclicality? **Partially.** Capital Access ARR **$1,340 million** and FinTech subscriptions are steadier; Market Services transaction fees still drive upside in volatile markets.

**Cycle normalization (Hohn):** Are we underwriting peak activity as normal? **No.** Base free cash flow **$3.44** per share from FY2025 cash from operations minus capital spending, explicitly below the Q1 2026 earnings pace (`NDAQ/research/valuation.json`).

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** **Peak earnings trap** if Q1 2026 U.S. equity volumes normalize and the market reapplies a lower multiple on transaction revenue.

- **Regulatory pressure** on equity market structure or FinTech pricing compresses take rates.  
- **Prolonged low volatility** reduces cash-equity and derivatives average daily volume.  
- **Debt service** (**$367M** interest FY2025 on **$8.6B** long-term debt) limits capital return if volumes soften.

**Inversion:** If we are wrong, Nasdaq remains a high-quality croupier trading at a full multiple with low-single-digit to high-single-digit returns, not a broken franchise.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** Thesis fails if handled volume stagnates for several years while FinTech ARR growth decelerates below mid-single digits.
