 
 
 
TEXAS PACIFIC LAND CORPORATION 
RULES GOVERNING CONDUCT 
2025 ANNUAL MEETING OF STOCKHOLDERS 
We welcome you to the 2025 Annual Meeting of Stockholders (the “Annual Meeting”) of 
Texas Pacific Land Corporation (the “Company ,” “we,” “our” or “us” ). In the interest of an 
orderly Annual Meeting, all stockholders are required to comply with  the following rules  
governing conduct: 
1. The Company’s bylaws describe requirements for meetings of our stockholders, 
and we will conduct the Annual Meeting consistent with those requirements.  The Chairman of 
the board of directors of the Company will preside as the Chair of the Annual Meeting (the 
“Chair”) in accordance with the Company’s bylaws. He will conduct the Annual Meeting in his 
discretion and in accordance with the organizational documents of the Company, these rules and 
applicable law. The only business matters to be conducted at the Annual Meeting are the matters 
set forth in the Notice of Annual Meeting of Stockholders, whic h was included with  the 
Company’s proxy statement for the Annual Meeting. 
2. Because this is a meeting of our stockholders, only our stockholders are permitted 
to ask questions during the Annual Meeting. You need to have held stock as of the close of 
business on the record date of September 11, 2025 to vote or submit questions during the Annual 
Meeting. If you have voted your shares prior to the start of the Annual Meeting, your vote has 
been received by the Company’s inspector of elections and there is no need to vote your shares 
during the Annual Meeting unless you wish to revoke your proxy or change your vote. 
3. If a stockholder has a question about one of the matters being considered at the 
Annual Meeting, such question may be asked during the Annual Meeting. We will answer 
questions on any of the specific proposals to be voted on by the stockholders at the Annual 
Meeting either during or after the Annual Meeting, as determined by the Company. 
4. The views, constructive comments, and criticisms of our stockholders are 
welcome, but the purposes of the Annual Me eting will be observed, and we may not address any 
questions that are: 
(a) irrelevant to the business of the Annual Meeting; 
(b) related to the conduct of the Company’s ordinary business operations; 
(c) in substance repetitious of statements made by other persons;  
(d) derogatory references to persons or comments that are otherwise in bad 
taste;

(e) personal claims or grievances, or proposals to further a personal interest; 
(f)  over three minutes; 
(g)  related to pending or threatened litigation; 
(h)  related to material non -public information of the Company or other 
information that the Company is required or well -advised to keep 
confidential; or 
(i) otherwise deemed out of order or inappropriate by the Chair in his sole 
discretion. 
 In making any determinations as to whether to address a question at the Annual Meeting , 
the Chair may, in his sole discretion, consult with counsel. 
5. In the event of any disruption or technical difficulty that interrupts the Annual 
Meeting, the Chair may adjourn the Annual Meeting, or take such other action that he determines 
is appropriate in light of the circumstances. 
6. The use of cameras, sound recording equipment, communication devices or any 
other similar equipment to record the Annual Meeting is prohibited without the express written 
permission of the Company. The redistribution of meeting materials is also prohibited without 
the express written permission of the Company. 
7. Conduct that is not in compliance with these rules or that is not appropriate will 
not be permitted and may be a basis for expulsion or immediate termination of a stockholder’s 
access to the Annual Meeting by the Chair.
