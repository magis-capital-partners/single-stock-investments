## What this business is
Texas Pacific Land Corporation owns roughly **880,000** surface acres and royalty interests across the Permian Basin in West Texas. **[Fact]** The company collects cash from oil and gas royalties, easements and other surface income, land sales, and a fast-growing **Water Services and Operations** segment (Texas Pacific Water Resources). **[Fact]** FY2025 consolidated revenue was **$798.2M** (+81% versus 2024); operating income **$592.2M**; net income **$481.4M** (**$6.97** diluted EPS). **[Fact]** FY2025 cash from operations was **$545.9M**; the balance sheet had **$144.8M** cash and **$1.46B** stockholders' equity at year-end. **[Fact]** Q1 2026 revenue was **$236.8M** (+21% YoY); operating income **$182.3M**; Q1 cash from operations **$162.0M**. **[Inference]** TPL behaves like a scarce-asset toll collector on Permian drilling and water infrastructure rather than a traditional operating oil company.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
There is **no clear dated mispricing signal** (HK predictive attribute: **none**). **[Fact]** GAAP stockholders' equity (**~$21/sh**) **does not reflect** the fair value of TPL's core assets: the 10-K states that **Assigned** land and royalty interests from the **1888** Declaration of Trust carry **no value on the balance sheet** because fair market value was never determined at formation. **[Inference]** The market at **~$393** prices Permian scarcity and cash generation; comparing price to GAAP book (**~19×**) is **misleading** for dhando or floor analysis. Lawrence 10-year returns on operating cash flow remain the right metric for **current yield at price**; a separate **NAV / acreage optionality** overlay is required for hidden asset value.

---

#### GAAP book vs fair value (hidden asset)

**[Fact]** FY2025 10-K (`10-K_20260218`, accounting policies):

- **Assigned** land and royalty interests from the **February 1, 1888** Declaration of Trust: **no value assigned** on the consolidated balance sheets.
- Balance sheet **Land** line: **$0** (zero). **Property, plant and equipment, net:** **$164.5M** (mostly water infrastructure). **Intangibles, net:** **$32.8M**.
- **Stockholders' equity ~$1.46B (~$21/sh)** is largely **accumulated earnings** from royalties, easements, and water, not a mark-to-market of acreage.
- Only **acquired** oil and gas royalties are capitalized (successful efforts); marginal 2025 example: **177 NRA** for **$3.5M** (~**$19,800/NRA**).

**[Inference]** Land has appreciated massively in the Permian since 1888, but that appreciation **does not flow through GAAP book**. The market cap (**~$27B** at **~$393**) embeds fair-value expectations for **~882k** surface acres and **~224k** NRA that financial statements do not show.

**[Inference]** Lawrence IRR on **operating cash flow** answers "what return do I earn on current cash generation at this price?" Hidden land value affects **NAV / terminal optionality** (future royalties, easements, land sales, water on undeveloped acreage), not necessarily near-term IRR unless modeled explicitly.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** **Permian activity and completion intensity decline**, slowing royalty and water volumes while the stock embeds premium growth.

- **Commodity price collapse** reduces operator economics on TPL acreage.
- **Produced-water regulation or litigation** raises operator costs or restricts TPWR volumes.
- **Governance / capital allocation** after Horizon Kinetics' reduced role (LCI 2026 note: **[HUMAN REVIEW]**).
- **Multiple compression** from **~50×** cash-flow starting point if growth normalizes.

**Inversion:** If we are wrong, Permian super-cycle persistence and water infrastructure scarcity drive double-digit cash-flow growth for years, supporting a **3%+** bull path and eventual re-rating. That requires heroic growth from an already elevated base; we do not underwrite it in the base case.
