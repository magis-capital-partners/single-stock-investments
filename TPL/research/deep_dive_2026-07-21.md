## What this business is
Texas Pacific Land Corporation owns roughly **882,000** surface acres and royalty interests across the Permian Basin in West Texas. **[Fact]** The company collects cash from oil and gas royalties, easements and other surface income, land sales, and a fast-growing **Water Services and Operations** segment (Texas Pacific Water Resources). **[Fact]** FY2025 consolidated revenue was **$798.2M**; operating income **$592.2M**; net income **$481.4M** (**$6.97** diluted EPS). **[Fact]** FY2025 cash from operations was **$545.9M**; Q1 2026 revenue was **$236.8M** (+21% YoY) with **$162.0M** quarterly operating cash flow. **[Inference]** TPL behaves like a scarce-asset toll collector on Permian drilling and water infrastructure rather than a traditional operating oil company.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
There is **no clear dated mispricing signal** (HK predictive attribute: **none**). **[Fact]** GAAP stockholders' equity (**~$21/sh**) **does not reflect** the fair value of TPL's core assets: the 10-K states that **Assigned** land and royalty interests from the **1888** Declaration of Trust carry **no value on the balance sheet**. **[Inference]** The market at **~$414** prices Permian scarcity and cash generation; comparing price to GAAP book is **misleading** for dhando or floor analysis. Lawrence seven-year returns on operating owner cash remain the right metric for **current yield at price**; a separate proof-first component schedule values risked royalty inventory, surface acreage, and milestone options.

---

## Executive summary
At **~$414** (market quote 2026-07-15), the Lawrence owner-cash model produces **−8.2%** per year on **$7.02/sh** starting owner cash after fixed-asset spending and share-based compensation (bear **−12.6%**, bull **−2.9%**). Proof-first additive components sum to **~$246 per share** at base case versus **~$414** price. TPL is a dual-segment Permian infrastructure owner: **Land and Resource Management** (**$490.7M** FY2025 revenue) plus **Water Services and Operations** (**$307.5M**). Q1 2026 accelerated (**+21%** revenue YoY).

The business quality is strong (scarce acreage, pricing on surface and water access) but **dhando is partial**: GAAP book is **not** a downside floor because **~882k** surface acres and **~224k** net royalty acres from the 1888 trust are **off balance sheet at zero**. Dividend yield on **$2.13/sh** regular dividends in 2025 is **~0.5%** at spot. Marvin proposes **watch**.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** **Permian activity and completion intensity decline**, slowing royalty and water volumes while the stock embeds premium growth.

- **Commodity price collapse** reduces operator economics on TPL acreage.
- **Produced-water regulation or litigation** raises operator costs or restricts TPWR volumes.
- **Governance / capital allocation** after Horizon Kinetics' reduced role (**[HUMAN REVIEW]**).
- **Multiple compression** from an elevated cash-flow starting multiple if growth normalizes.

**Inversion:** If we are wrong, Permian super-cycle persistence and water infrastructure scarcity drive double-digit cash-flow growth for years, supporting the bull path and eventual re-rating. That requires heroic growth from an already elevated base; we do not underwrite it in the base case.
