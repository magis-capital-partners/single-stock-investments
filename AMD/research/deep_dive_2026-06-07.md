## What this business is
Advanced Micro Devices designs high-performance CPUs, GPUs, and adaptive computing chips for data centers, client PCs, gaming consoles, and embedded markets. **[Fact]** Customers include hyperscale cloud providers, PC OEMs, console makers, and industrial OEMs. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$34.6 billion** (+34% versus last year); operating profit **$3.7 billion**; net income **$4.3 billion**. **[Fact]** Reportable segments are Data Center (**$16.6 billion**), Client and Gaming (**$14.6 billion** combined), and Embedded (**$3.5 billion**). **[Fact]** Cash to shareholders comes from continuing operating cash flow after capital spending and foundry prepayments.

## Why the market might be wrong
There is **no clear dated mispricing signal** (HK predictive attribute: **none**). **[Inference]** The market prices AMD as the credible number-two AI accelerator and server CPU challenger, embedding share-gain optionality against NVIDIA. **[Inference]** The debate is whether **MI300 ramp margins** and **Data Center growth durability** justify **~$120** when Lawrence base cash-flow math delivers only mid-single-digit returns unless bull-case share gains materialize.

## Executive summary
AMD is gaining share in AI accelerators (Instinct MI300) and server CPUs (EPYC). FY2025 free cash flow per share was **$3.37** on **$6.5 billion** continuing operating cash flow and **$1.0 billion** capital spending. Data Center revenue grew **32%** to **$16.6 billion** with segment operating profit **$3.6 billion**. The Xilinx FPGA franchise adds embedded optionality, but consolidated returns at the placeholder price are below our **15%** hurdle.

The business has a **stable** moat and **partial** asymmetric payoff: AI upside is open, but the bear case includes custom silicon pressure and PC cyclicality. Marvin proposes **watch**. ## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** **AI accelerator share stalls** as hyperscalers prioritize internal custom silicon and NVIDIA's CUDA ecosystem, leaving Data Center growth below the **16%** segment assumption.

- TSMC advanced-node allocation constraints limit MI350 shipments.
- PC and gaming downturn pressures Client and Gaming segment cash flow.
- Integration risk: Xilinx synergy under-delivers versus embedded growth assumptions.
- Competitive pricing from NVIDIA compresses Instinct GPU margins.

**Inversion:** If we are wrong, AMD is the durable second source for AI compute with Data Center operating profit already **$3.6 billion**, and **4.5%** understates a multi-year share-gain runway.
