## What this business is
Advanced Micro Devices designs high-performance CPUs, GPUs, and adaptive computing chips for data centers, client PCs, gaming consoles, and embedded markets. **[Fact]** Customers include hyperscale cloud providers, PC OEMs, console makers, and industrial OEMs. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$34.6 billion** (+34% versus last year); operating profit **$3.7 billion**; net income **$4.3 billion**. **[Fact]** Reportable segments are Data Center (**$16.6 billion**), Client and Gaming (**$14.6 billion** combined), and Embedded (**$3.5 billion**). **[Fact]** Cash to shareholders comes from continuing operating cash flow after capital spending and foundry prepayments.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
There is **no clear dated mispricing signal** (HK predictive attribute: **none**). **[Inference]** The market prices AMD as the credible number-two AI accelerator and server CPU challenger, embedding share-gain optionality against NVIDIA. **[Inference]** Proof-first component math sums to **~$96 per share** versus **~$550** market, so the debate is whether MI300 ramp margins and Data Center growth durability justify the premium when Lawrence base cash-flow math is deeply negative at spot.
