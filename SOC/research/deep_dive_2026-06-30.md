## What this business is
Sable Offshore Corp. owns the Santa Ynez Unit, a cluster of federal offshore oil leases and three production platforms (Hondo, Harmony, Heritage) off the California coast, plus the onshore Santa Ynez Pipeline System that moves crude to market. **[Fact]** The company bought the field from Exxon Mobil in February 2024 and spent 2024–2025 restarting pipelines and platforms after years of shutdown. **[Fact]** First oil sales began March 29, 2026. **[Fact]** As of June 18, 2026, 52 of 77 completed wells were online at Harmony and Heritage, averaging about **43,000 gross barrels of oil per day** (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/424B5_20260630_rpt_acc0001628280_26_046144.htm`). **[Fact]** The field carries a **100% working interest with an 83.6% net revenue interest** (10-K), and its 2014 full-year peak was about **29,000 barrels of oil per day** gross. **[Fact]** The stock closed at **$3.08** on June 30, 2026, down about **56%** from $6.97 the prior day after the dilutive financing was announced.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The market may be treating SOC either as a failed SPAC or as a normal E&P name, when it is actually a **dated capital-markets binary**: a cross-conditioned refinancing must close before the Exxon senior secured term loan matures July 24, 2026. **[Inference]** The 56% one-day drop to $3.08 shows the market repricing toward the diluted, leveraged reality. **[Inference]** Production is real (43k bbl/d) but that is likely elevated post-shut-in flush versus the 2014 peak of 29k bbl/d, so bears may overstate near-term default risk while bulls may overstate the sustainable production rate.

---

## Executive summary
Sable Offshore restarted California's Santa Ynez field and is now raising **$100 million** of equity, **$300 million** of convertible notes, and **$675 million** of new secured term debt to repay roughly **$992 million** owed to Exxon by **July 24, 2026**. Production is about **43,000 gross barrels of oil per day** from 52 online wells (83.6% net revenue interest), with Platform Hondo expected in the third quarter of 2026. The balance sheet is levered: post-transaction debt would be **$975 million** at **15%** on the term loan, with mandatory hedging and excess-cash-flow sweeps. The stock fell **56% to $3.08** on June 30 when the dilutive package was announced, so we value the equity from that price. **Bear case (35% weight):** refinancing fails and the field is sold; after the $980M secured Exxon claim, residual equity is about **$0.35** (**−66% per year** over two years). **Base case:** Transactions close; 42,000 bbl/d net of royalty; about **$305 million** EBITDA at 5.0 times; equity **$3.73** in five years (**3.9% per year**). **Bull case:** 52,000 bbl/d, firmer oil, converts convert; equity about **$10.22** (**27.1% per year**). Moat **none**. Dhando **partial**. Stance **watch**.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** The cross-conditioned June 2026 Transactions fail to close before the **July 24, 2026** Exxon maturity, forcing acceleration or distressed restructuring while California regulatory risk remains live.

1. **Refinancing failure (Jul 24, 2026):** Exxon can accelerate; $30M amendment fee already paid; P&A waiver expires on default.
2. **Syndication risk:** Term Loan B must be placed with third-party lenders; press reports weak demand **[PENDING APPROVAL]**.
3. **Cross-conditionality:** Equity offering, notes, and Term Loan B must all close together; one leg fails, all fail.
4. **California regulatory / litigation:** Pipeline shutdown would stop revenue while fixed costs and debt remain.
5. **Dilution:** $100M equity at depressed price plus $300M converts (conversion price TBD in preliminary filing) plus warrant overhang.
6. **Interest and covenants:** 15% Term Loan B, mandatory 100% production hedge, excess-cash-flow sweep, 1.25x MOIC at maturity on TL B.
7. **Production risk:** Compressor limits cap wells online; Hondo timing; gas-oil ratio uncertainty.
8. **Going concern:** Auditors emphasized going concern in 424B5; accumulated deficit **$1.3 billion**.

**Pre-mortem:** If we are wrong and the stock doubles, the market is pricing successful refi plus faster ramp than our base. If we are right to watch, either the July window closes without funding or debt service consumes the field's cash flow.

#### IRR arithmetic (show your work)

1. Gross barrels = 42,000 × 365 = **15,330,000**
2. Net barrels = 15,330,000 × 83.6% = **12,815,880**
3. Revenue = 12,815,880 × $58 = **$743M**
4. Lease operating expense = 15,330,000 × $24 = **$368M**
5. EBITDA = $743 − $368 − $70 = **$305M**
6. Net debt = ($675 + $300) − $135 = **$840M**
7. Enterprise value = $305 × 5.0 = **$1,527M**
8. Equity value = $1,527 − $840 = **$687M**
9. Value per share = $687 ÷ 184 = **$3.73**
10. Annual return = ($3.73 ÷ $3.08)^(1/5) − 1 = **3.9% per year**
