## What this business is
Sable Offshore Corp. owns the Santa Ynez Unit, a cluster of federal offshore oil leases and three production platforms (Hondo, Harmony, Heritage) off the California coast, plus the onshore Santa Ynez Pipeline System that moves crude to market. **[Fact]** The company bought the field from Exxon Mobil in February 2024 and spent 2024–2025 restarting pipelines and platforms after years of shutdown. **[Fact]** First oil sales began March 29, 2026. **[Fact]** As of June 18, 2026, 52 of 77 completed wells were online at Harmony and Heritage, averaging about **43,000 gross barrels of oil per day** (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/424B5_20260630_rpt_acc0001628280_26_046144.htm`). **[Fact]** The stock trades on NYSE at **$6.97** (June 29, 2026 close, same filing).

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The market may be treating SOC either as a failed SPAC or as a normal E&P name, when it is actually a **dated capital-markets binary**: a cross-conditioned refinancing must close before the Exxon senior secured term loan matures July 24, 2026. **[Inference]** Weak reported demand for the syndicated Term Loan B (press, **[PENDING APPROVAL]**) could mean the equity is mispriced for a near-term default, or that successful close would unlock a producing asset the market still discounts. **[Inference]** Production is real (43k bbl/d) but compressors limit well count until gas volumes recover, so bears may overstate near-term cash flow while bulls may overstate ramp speed.

---

## Executive summary
Sable Offshore restarted California's Santa Ynez field and is now raising **$100 million** of equity, **$300 million** of convertible notes, and **$675 million** of new secured term debt to repay roughly **$992 million** owed to Exxon by **July 24, 2026**. Production is about **43,000 barrels of oil per day** from 52 online wells, with Platform Hondo expected in the third quarter of 2026. The balance sheet is levered: post-transaction debt would be **$975 million** at **15%** on the term loan, with mandatory hedging and excess-cash-flow sweeps. We model this as a **levered equity stub**, not a steady free-cash-flow compounder. **Bear case (30% weight):** refinancing fails or operations are shut; equity approaches zero (**-73% per year** over two years). **Base case:** transactions close, production stabilizes near **48,000 barrels per day**, equity worth about **$13** in five years (**13.3% per year**). **Bull case:** full ramp plus strong oil; equity about **$28** (**32.1% per year**). Moat **none**. Dhando **partial** (producing asset floor exists only if refi closes). Stance **watch**.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** The cross-conditioned June 2026 Transactions fail to close before the **July 24, 2026** Exxon maturity, forcing acceleration or distressed restructuring while California regulatory risk remains live.

1. **Refinancing failure (Jul 24, 2026):** Exxon can accelerate; $30M amendment fee already paid; P&A waiver expires on default.
2. **Syndication risk:** Term Loan B must be placed with third-party lenders; press reports weak demand **[PENDING APPROVAL]**.
3. **Cross-conditionality:** Equity offering, notes, and Term Loan B must all close together; one leg fails, all fail.
4. **California regulatory / litigation:** Pipeline shutdown would stop revenue while fixed costs and debt remain.
5. **Dilution:** $100M equity at depressed price plus $300M converts (conversion price TBD in preliminary filing) plus warrant overhang.
6. **Interest and covenants:** 15% Term Loan B, mandatory 100% production hedge, excess-cash-flow sweep, 1.25x MOIC at maturity on TL B.
7. **Production risk:** Compressor limits cap wells online; Hondo timing; gas-oil ratio uncertainty.
8. **Going concern:** Auditors emphasized going concern in 424B5; accumulated deficit **$1.3 billion**.

**Pre-mortem:** If we are wrong and the stock doubles, the market is pricing successful refi plus faster ramp than our base. If we are right to watch, either the July window closes without funding or debt service consumes the field's cash flow.
