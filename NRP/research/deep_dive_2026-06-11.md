## What this business is

Natural Resource Partners L.P. is a Houston-based master limited partnership that owns mineral interests and other subsurface rights across the United States and collects royalties, overriding royalties, and lease fees from third-party operators. **[Fact]** The partnership does not mine, drill, or produce minerals; it leases acreage and receives cash from lessees (`NRP/investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260227_rpt20251231_acc0001437749_26_006147.htm`, Business). **[Fact]** As of May 6, 2026, **13,250,412** common units were outstanding (`NRP/investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-Q_20260506_rpt20260331_acc0001437749_26_015150.htm`). **[Fact]** The partnership reports two operating segments: **Mineral Rights** (~**99%** of FY2025 revenues) and **Soda Ash** (a **49%** non-controlling equity interest in Sisecam Wyoming) (`same 10-K`). **[Fact]** FY2025 total revenues and other income were **$207.3 million**, down from **$268.0 million** in 2024 and **$370.0 million** in 2023 (`same 10-K`).

---

## Why the market might be wrong

Yield-focused investors may treat NRP as a simple coal royalty bond and miss the breadth of the **~13 million acre** mineral footprint, including oil and gas, aggregates, and emerging carbon-sequestration and renewable lease opportunities. **[Inference]** K-1 partnership tax reporting and MLP structure can exclude the name from dividend screeners despite meaningful asset backing. **[Inference]** The market may be pricing Sisecam Wyoming's soda ash weakness as permanent, while the partnership's **$276 million** equity-method carrying value and Q1 2026 capital contribution suggest management still sees long-term option value (`10-Q_20260506`). **[Inference]** Post-2023 debt refinancing and distribution cuts to **$0.75** per quarter may look like peak-cycle normalization rather than a trough with bounded downside given **$30 million** cash and a **~0.4x** leverage ratio at March 31, 2026 (`10-Q_20260506` debt footnote).

---

## Executive summary

Natural Resource Partners is a passive mineral royalty MLP: coal-heavy Mineral Rights generate almost all cash, while a **49%** stake in Sisecam Wyoming adds cyclical soda ash exposure. FY2025 revenues fell to **$207 million** and recurring distributions settled at **$0.75** per unit per quarter (**$3.00** per year excluding the March 2025 **$1.21** special tax distribution). Net debt is manageable after refinancing (**~$60 million** revolver at March 31, 2026). At today's price and **$3.00** starting owner cash, Marvin's Lawrence base case annual return is below our **~15%** bar, so stance is **watch**. Dhando is **partial**: asset-rich balance sheet and low leverage, but coal cycle and paused Sisecam distributions cap upside. No approved third-party sources are in the base return.

### Mental models in plain English

**Permutations & combinations (Stahl):** Owner cash can rise from met/thermal coal prices, lease volumes on existing properties, new carbon or renewable leases on the **13 million acre** footprint, or resumed Sisecam Wyoming distributions. **[Fact]** FY2025 Mineral Rights revenue was **$204.2 million** vs Soda Ash **$3.1 million** (`10-K_20260227`).

**Low risk, high uncertainty (Munger):** Leverage covenant **3.25x** must be met to pay more than **$0.45**/unit (`10-K` partnership agreement summary). At March 31, 2026 leverage was **~0.4x** with interest coverage **21.4x** (`10-Q`). Uncertainty is coal demand and soda ash pricing, not balance-sheet blow-up.

**Inversion (Munger):** Thesis fails if recurring distributions fall below **$2.00**/unit for multiple years while the unit count rises and Sisecam distributions stay at zero.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Prolonged weakness in coal prices and production volumes reduces royalty revenues and quarterly distributions (`10-K` risk factors).

- **Customer concentration:** Alpha Metallurgical Resources, Foresight, and Alabama Kanu Holdings are significant Mineral Rights lessees (`10-K` footnotes).
- **Soda ash:** Lower sales prices cut Sisecam distributions; Q1 2026 had **no** distribution and a **$39.2 million** capital contribution (`10-Q`).
- **Debt covenants:** Leverage above **3.25x** restricts distributions above **$0.45**/unit (`10-K` partnership agreement).
- **Regulatory / ESG:** Coal exposure and energy transition may compress terminal multiples for royalty MLPs. **[Inference]**

**Lens failure mode (asset):** If the market is right that coal royalties are in structural decline with no offset from acreage options, total return equals yield only with price erosion.
