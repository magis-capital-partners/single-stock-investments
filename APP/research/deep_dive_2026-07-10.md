## What this business is
AppLovin Corporation (APP) operates an AI-powered advertising platform that connects advertisers with mobile app publishers and, increasingly, connected-TV inventory. **[Fact]** The company reports two segments: **Advertising** (AppDiscovery user acquisition, MAX in-app bidding, Adjust analytics, Wurl CTV) and **Apps** (a portfolio of 200+ free-to-play mobile games). **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$5.48 billion** (+70% year over year), net income **$3.43 billion** (63% net margin), and free cash flow **$3.97 billion**. **[Fact]** Q1 2026 revenue was **$1.84 billion** (+59% YoY) with **$1.3 billion** of free cash flow. The core economic engine is AXON, an AI recommendation system that matches advertiser demand with publisher supply in real-time auctions at microsecond speed.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The market may be treating AppLovin as a cyclical mobile-gaming ad vendor when the business has pivoted toward a durable, high-margin software platform. **[Fact]** Advertising revenue was **68%** of FY2024 total and grew **91%** in segment Adjusted EBITDA that year, while Apps was **32%** and management is conducting a **strategic review** of the Apps portfolio. **[Inference]** Generalists anchoring on 2022 losses or gaming cyclicality may underweight the AXON data flywheel: more advertiser spend improves models, which improves return on spend, which attracts more spend. There is no dated catalyst; the inefficiency is **descriptive** (complex ad-tech story, dual-class control, privacy-regulation overhang) rather than a timed equity yield curve setup.

---

## Executive summary
AppLovin is a scaled performance-marketing platform with exceptional cash economics (82% Adjusted EBITDA margin in FY2025, 85% in Q1 2026). At **$520.43** (NASDAQ, 2026-07-09), base expected annual return at today's price is **8.37%** per year on filed FY2025 owner cash, well below our **15%** bar. Competitive advantage (Munger moat) is **stable**: AXON-driven auction scale and advertiser ROI data create a reinforcing loop, though privacy regulation and platform policy changes remain structural risks. Asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) is **partial**: net cash **$2.5 billion** and massive free cash flow limit fundamental downside, but the stock trades at roughly **44×** FY2025 free cash flow per share, leaving little margin of safety if growth decelerates. We propose **watch**.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Privacy and platform-policy changes (Apple IDFA, Google Privacy Sandbox, app-store rules) degrade AXON targeting efficiency, compressing advertiser return on spend and auction volumes. Source: `AppLovin-Corporation-10K-FY24_As-Filed.pdf` Item 1A (risk factors on IDFA and platform dependence).

**Lens failure mode (operating):** If Adjusted EBITDA margin falls below **75%** while revenue growth drops under **20%**, the software-platform narrative fails and the name re-rates as a cyclical ad vendor. Monitor quarterly in `Financial-Update-Q1-2026.pdf` margin charts.

**Inversion (Munger):** What would make this fail?

- Regulators restrict cross-app tracking data sharing, breaking the AXON data flywheel (`10-K` Item 1A).
- Google or Meta replicates performance marketing at lower take rates, compressing auction spreads.
- Apps portfolio strategic review stalls, keeping management attention split across gaming and platform (`10-K` Item 1).
- Buybacks at ~$520 destroy per-share economics if growth stalls (Q1 2026 $1.0B repurchases per press release).

**Secondary risks:**

- Customer concentration: no individual customer was 10%+ of revenue in FY2022-2024 (`10-K` Note 14).
- International tax rate shifts and stock-based compensation (R&D was 14% of revenue in FY2024).
- AI model arms race raises compute and talent costs; R&D spend was $639M in FY2024.
- Dual-class structure concentrates voting control with founders (proxy/governance review pending).
