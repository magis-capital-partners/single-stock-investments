## What this business is
IonQ, Inc. is a U.S. public quantum computing company building trapped-ion quantum systems and selling access through quantum-computing-as-a-service (QCaaS), specialized hardware contracts, and consulting on quantum algorithms. **[Fact]** The company has expanded through acquisitions into quantum networking (id Quantique), satellite synthetic-aperture radar imagery (Capella Space), ion-trap chip technology (Oxford Ionics), atomic clocks and sensors (Vector Atomic), and quantum networking infrastructure (Skyloom Global, closed January 2026). **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$130.0 million** (+202% versus **$43.1 million** in FY2024). **[Fact]** FY2025 net loss was **$512.1 million** and net cash used in operating activities was **$283.2 million** (10-K FY2025 MD&A). **[Fact]** At March 31, 2026, cash was **$493.5 million** (down from **$1,030.9 million** at December 31, 2025) on **358.8 million** basic weighted-average shares (10-Q Q1 2026).

## Why the market might be wrong
**Predictive attribute:** quantum platform narrative versus normalized owner cash burn (revenue acceleration versus operating losses and dilution).

**[Inference]** The market may price IonQ as the winning quantum platform because revenue more than doubled in FY2025 and Q1 2026 revenue was **$64.7 million** versus **$7.6 million** in Q1 2025. **[Fact]** Q1 2026 operating loss was **($271.5 million)** on that revenue, with research and development expense **$125.7 million** in the quarter alone (10-Q Q1 2026). **[Fact]** Q1 2026 GAAP net income of **$804.6 million** includes a **$1,057.6 million** gain on change in fair value of warrant liabilities, not recurring owner cash (10-Q Q1 2026 income statement). **[Inference]** Investors may underweight acquisition integration risk (2025 deals represented roughly **39%** of FY2025 revenue per 10-K Item 9A), share count near **359 million**, and the gap between gross-profit improvement and still-negative operating cash flow.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Revenue growth fails to translate into normalized owner cash before cash is consumed or equity is diluted further.

- **Technology risk:** Trapped-ion scaling and error correction may lag competing modalities (10-K risk factors).
- **Integration risk:** 2025–2026 acquisitions added **~39%** of FY2025 revenue but increase amortization and goodwill (**$166.3M** Skyloom goodwill, 10-Q Note 3).
- **Capital structure risk:** Warrant and equity financing history; Q1 warrant fair-value swings distort GAAP earnings.
- **Lens failure mode:** Treating Q1 GAAP net income or NOL balance as owner cash overstates dhando and implied return.

**Inversion:** If operating loss narrows for four consecutive quarters while unearned revenue and QCaaS usage grow without >10% dilution, reassess moat and stance.
