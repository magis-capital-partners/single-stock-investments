## What this business is
CME Group is the world's largest listed derivatives marketplace: futures and options on interest rates, equity indexes, foreign exchange, energy, metals, and agricultural products, plus clearing, market data, and collateral services. **[Fact]** Customers pay transaction, clearing, and market-data fees; CME does not take directional market risk on customer positions. **[Fact]** FY2025 total revenues were **$6,520.6 million**, operating income **$4,229.5 million**, and net income **$4,072.2 million** (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260226_rpt20251231_acc0001156375_26_000009.htm`). **[Fact]** Q1 2026 revenues were **$1,880.1 million** with operating income **$1,309.7 million** and net income **$1,154.3 million** (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-Q_20260424_rpt20260331_acc0001156375_26_000020.htm`). Terry Duffy is Chairman and CEO.

## Why the market might be wrong
There is no dated payoff or structural discount here. **[Inference]** Q1 2026 operating margin is far above a typical mid-cycle year because rates and volatility lifted interest-rate and equity-index volumes; the market may treat that run rate as permanent. **[Inference]** Our base return uses FY2025 owner cash per share (**$11.40**), not the Q1 2026 earnings pace. Return is franchise earnings power at price, not a mispricing signal with a known catalyst date.

## Executive summary
CME Group is a toll collector on global derivatives activity: listed futures and options, clearing, and data with minimal principal risk. FY2025 filings confirm **$6,520.6 million** revenue, **$4,229.5 million** operating income, and **$28.7 billion** stockholders' equity; Q1 2026 accelerated to **$1,880.1 million** revenue on strong volumes. At **$252.65** per share (Stooq, 2026-06-03), we expect about **12.01% per year** over 7 years on **$11.40** mid-cycle free cash flow per share (below our portfolio return target for a core add). Asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) stays **partial** because transaction revenue is cyclical. Clearing-member default risk is remote but real; the April 2026 **$7 billion** clearing credit facility amendment is standard infrastructure, not a growth option. We propose **watch** and would revisit near **$210–220** where Lawrence base case approaches our core-return hurdle.

**Volume vs share (Stahl):** Is handled volume growing? **Yes.** FY2025 total revenues rose versus FY2024; Q1 2026 revenues **$1,880.1 million** versus **$1,642.3 million** in the prior-year quarter (`10-Q_20260424_rpt20260331_acc0001156375_26_000020.htm`).

**Fee model resilience (Stahl):** Does recurring revenue cushion transaction cyclicality? **Partially.** Market data and connectivity fees are steadier; interest-rate and equity-index transaction fees still drive upside in volatile markets.

**Cycle normalization (Hohn):** Are we underwriting peak activity as normal? **No.** Base free cash flow **$11.40** per share from FY2025 cash from operations minus capital spending, explicitly below the Q1 2026 earnings pace (`CME/research/valuation.json`).

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** **Peak earnings trap** if Q1 2026 rates and equity-index volumes normalize and the market reapplies a lower multiple on transaction revenue.

- **Regulatory pressure** on fees or event-contract products compresses take rates.  
- **Prolonged low volatility** reduces interest-rate and equity-index ADV.  
- **Clearing stress** (member default or collateral disruption) is low probability but would hit reputation and capital return.

**Inversion:** If we are wrong, CME remains a high-quality croupier trading at a full multiple with 5–11% returns, not a broken franchise.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** Thesis fails if handled volume stagnates for several years while competitors gain share in key products.
