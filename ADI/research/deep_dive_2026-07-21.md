## What this business is

Analog Devices, Inc. designs, manufactures, and markets high-performance analog, mixed-signal, and digital signal processing integrated circuits, software, and subsystems. **[Fact]** Shares trade on Nasdaq (ADI). **[Fact]** FY2025 consolidated revenue was **$11.0 billion**, operating income **$2.93 billion**, and net income **$2.27 billion** (diluted earnings **$4.56** per share) per `ADI/investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20251125_rpt20251101_acc0000006281_25_000153.htm`. **[Fact]** FY2025 cash from operations was **$4.81 billion**, capital spending **$0.53 billion**, and free cash flow about **$4.28 billion** on roughly **496.7 million** diluted shares (**$8.61** per share).

## Why the market might be wrong

The stock may trade as a post-Maxim analog oligopolist while the market underweights cyclical recovery and overweights peak-cycle memories from FY2023. **[Inference]** FY2025 revenue rose **17%** from the FY2024 trough (**$9.43 billion** to **$11.02 billion**), yet generalists may still anchor on inventory-correction headlines rather than **$8.61** per share of filing-locked free cash flow. **[Inference]** Proof-first component math at base case sums to about **$210** per share of economic value versus a price near **$372**, suggesting the market prices analog moat durability, AI/data-center power content, and capital returns beyond what the conservative component schedule captures today. **[Inference]** Net debt of roughly **$6.1 billion** after the Maxim acquisition is material but serviceable against **$4.28 billion** of annual free cash flow.

## Executive summary

Analog Devices is a scaled high-performance analog semiconductor franchise spanning industrial automation, automotive electrification, communications infrastructure, and emerging data-center power delivery. FY2025 results show strong cash conversion: **$8.61** of free cash flow per diluted share on **$11.0 billion** of revenue, with Industrial at **45%** and Automotive at **30%** of end-market mix. The business is understandable as a pricing-power compounder, but leverage from the Maxim deal and cyclical end markets keep the bear case only partially bounded at today's multiple.

At about **$372.46** per share, we expect the mechanical synthesis base return to be computed in Phase 3 (Lawrence owner-cash path pending refresh). Proof-first component economic value base case is **$209.96** per share, so mechanical return math and filing-locked component sum may diverge if synthesis weights growth scenarios heavily. Dhando is **partial**. Stance: **watch**.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** A simultaneous industrial inventory correction and communications end-market softness could compress free cash flow per share faster than buybacks offset per-share economics, while **$8.6 billion** of debt limits balance-sheet flexibility.

- **Cyclical end markets:** FY2023-to-FY2024 revenue fell **23%** from peak; FY2025 recovery may stall if industrial customers destock again (`10-K_20251125` end-market tables).
- **Leverage / integration:** Maxim acquisition left **~$6.1B** net debt; refinancing risk if rates stay elevated **[Assumption]**.
- **Customer concentration:** Large OEM and distributor relationships create revenue lumpiness in auto and industrial **[Assumption]**.
- **Competition:** Texas Instruments and other analog peers compete on price and capacity in downturns.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** If we are wrong, it is because we treated ADI as a durable compounder while cyclical compression erodes the **$8.61** per share free cash flow anchor faster than the market's premium multiple implies.
