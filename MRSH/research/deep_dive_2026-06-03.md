## What this business is

Marsh & McLennan Companies is a global professional services firm organized in two segments: **Risk and Insurance Services** (Marsh brokerage, Guy Carpenter reinsurance) and **Consulting** (Mercer health and wealth, Marsh Management Consulting). **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$27.0 billion** and operating income **$6.2 billion** (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260209_rpt20251231_acc0000062709_26_000022.htm`). **[Fact]** The company earns fees and commissions placing insurance and advising on risk, plus consulting mandates; it also earns **fiduciary interest income** on client premiums held in trust (**$403 million** in FY2025), which is separate from brokerage fees. **[Fact]** Common stock trades on the NYSE under ticker **MRSH** (symbol change from MMC effective February 2026 per `8-K_20260219_rpt20260219_acc0001193125_26_059544.htm`).

## Why the market might be wrong

The stock may look like a mature, fully priced broker at roughly **19 times** trailing earnings while understating durable mid-single-digit organic growth and high cash conversion. **[Inference]** After the **MRSH** ticker change, index and data-feed friction may keep some screens on legacy **MMC** symbols, leaving a short-lived attention discount. **[Inference]** Investors may treat **2024** cash flow as normal when **$8.5 billion** went to the **Thrive** acquisition (`PaymentsToAcquireBusinessesNetOfCashAcquired` in the 10-K cash flow statement), making **FY2025** free cash look like a one-time spike rather than a repeatable **~$5 billion** annual level.

## Executive summary

Marsh & McLennan is a high-quality insurance and consulting compounder with **23%** operating margins and consistent earnings growth (**diluted EPS $8.43** in FY2025 vs **$8.18** in FY2024). The balance sheet carries meaningful debt (**~$19.6 billion** long-term and current debt per 10-K) and acquisition integration risk, so dhando is **partial**, not full. At **$157.36** per share (Stooq, 2026-06-03), filing-based owner cash supports a **7-year base annual return of about 17%** on the Lawrence model (stance gate); Marvin synthesis is similar after scenario weights.

**Headline 7-year return: 17.1% per year** (Lawrence base case; see Valuation & IRR). Stance proposal: **hold** (IRR band 15–20%; moat and dhando gates pass). No approved third-party sources in base IRR.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Commercial insurance pricing cycles from hard market toward competition, slowing Risk and Insurance organic growth faster than consulting offsets.

- **Leverage:** **~$19.6 billion** debt against **$6.2 billion** operating income; refinancing and rates matter.
- **Integration:** Thrive and other deals must earn returns above repurchase yield.
- **Fiduciary / regulatory:** Client money handling and E&O litigation (reserve **$91 million** current).
- **Lens failure mode (operating):** If owner cash is overstated because fiduciary working capital inflates GAAP operating cash flow, apparent IRR would fall; **[HUMAN REVIEW]** compare to company-adjusted free cash flow in next earnings release.
