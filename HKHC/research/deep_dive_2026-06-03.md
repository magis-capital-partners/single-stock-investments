## What this business is

Horizon Kinetics Holding Corporation (HKHC) is the public parent of Horizon Kinetics Asset Management LLC, a fundamental, contrarian investment adviser. **[Fact]** Revenue comes mainly from investment advisory and sub-advisory fees on mutual funds, ETFs, closed-end funds, separately managed accounts, and private partnerships. **[Fact]** The stock trades on OTCQX under ticker HKHC. **[Fact]** FY2025 consolidated revenue was $72.8 million and operating income was $11.1 million; net income was $5.1 million after consolidation and non-controlling interests. **[Fact]** Stockholders' equity at December 31, 2025 was $339 million, or about $18.85 per share on 17.98 million shares outstanding. **[Fact]** The balance sheet is dominated by consolidated investment products (total assets near $2 billion), but economics to HKHC shareholders are the advisory platform and equity, not client AUM on the balance sheet.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The market may be paying a **franchise premium** for Murray Stahl's Horizon brand, permanent-capital alignment with FRMO, and exposure to the same research ecosystem that owns large Texas Pacific Land (TPL) weights in client portfolios. **[Inference]** Generalists may treat HKHC like a high-growth asset manager without normalizing **TPL concentration risk** (65% of AUM in TPL at year-end 2025, up from 41% in 2024). **[Inference]** Conversely, skeptics may dismiss HKHC as only a consolidated fund wrapper and miss that **operating income turned positive** in FY2025 after a prior-year operating loss, with advisory revenue up roughly 31% year over year. **[Inference]** At about $27.50 per share (Stooq, June 2, 2026), the stock trades **above book** while our Lawrence path on normalized operating owner cash implies a **below-zero** annual return at today's price unless growth and terminal multiples prove much higher than base case.

---

## Executive summary

HKHC is the listed advisory parent of Horizon Kinetics, tied to the FRMO permanent-capital complex. FY2025 showed stronger fee revenue and restored operating profit, but client assets are heavily concentrated in TPL, creating fee beta and reputation risk. Book value is about $18.85 per share; the OTC quote near $27.50 is a premium to GAAP equity. Our Lawrence base case on operating owner cash of about $0.59 per share implies about **-5.8% per year** over the model horizon at today's price, below our ~15% target. Downside is partially bounded by equity and cash, but not a classic Pabrai "heads I win, tails I don't lose much" at spot. We propose **watch**: understand the Horizon sleeve via FRMO; do not add HKHC on operating cash IRR alone without a lower entry or a catalyst path we can underwrite.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** **TPL concentration** in client AUM (65% at Dec 31, 2025). A severe TPL drawdown hits fees, performance marketing, and reputation together.

**Inversion (what would make us wrong):**
- Operating owner cash per share is materially higher than $0.59 once proprietary fund economics are attributed to parent shareholders.
- Market is correctly pricing a **catalyst stack** (fund listings, MIAX/HK marks, buybacks) not visible in GAAP operating income.
- Book value compounds quickly enough that price-to-book mean reversion delivers returns without high Lawrence IRR on cash flow.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** Treating consolidated fund **revenue** or **net income** as HKHC owner cash overstates or understates the true per-share economics.
