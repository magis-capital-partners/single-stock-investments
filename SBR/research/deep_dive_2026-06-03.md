## What this business is

Sabine Royalty Trust is a Texas express trust that holds non-operating royalty and mineral interests across producing and proved undeveloped oil and gas properties in Florida, Louisiana, Mississippi, New Mexico, Oklahoma, and Texas. **[Fact]** The Trust has no employees; Argent Trust Company serves as trustee and distributes essentially all distributable income monthly. **[Fact]** As of February 27, 2026, there were **14,579,345** units outstanding (`SBR/investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260227_rpt20251231_acc0001193125_26_081251.htm`). **[Fact]** Calendar Q1 2026 royalty income was **$14.19 million** and distributable income was **$13.04 million** ($0.89 per unit for the quarter) with cash and short-term investments of **$8.26 million** at March 31, 2026 (`SBR/investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-Q_20260508_rpt20260331_acc0001193125_26_213810.htm`). **[Fact]** The May 2026 monthly distribution was **$0.497900** per unit (`SBR/investor-documents/sec-edgar/8-K_20260505_exhibit_sbr-ex99_1.htm_acc0001193125_26_205699.htm`).

## Why the market might be wrong

Yield-oriented screens often skip royalty trusts, K-1 complexity, and depletion narrative, so a long record of cash distributions can trade below a simple sum of expected payouts. **[Inference]** Horizon Kinetics frames Sabine as a localized inflation beneficiary: monthly pass-through of oil and gas prices with minimal overhead (~0.7% of revenues in their commentary). **[Inference]** Engineering has added proved reserves over decades despite production, which the market may under-weight when pricing the trust on near-term distribution noise alone.

## Executive summary

Sabine Royalty Trust is a passive oil and gas royalty vehicle with **14.58 million** units, monthly distributions, and no operating capex at the trust level. FY2025 cash distributions totaled about **$5.16** per unit (sum of monthly 8-K exhibits); trailing twelve months are about **$4.74** per unit. Q1 2026 distributable income was **$0.89** per unit as gas prices helped offset lower oil volumes. At **$77.23** (Stooq, June 3, 2026), the trailing distribution yield is about **6.1%**. Our base case uses a five-year equity yield curve with cumulative distributions and a modest terminal unit value for a **7.2%** annual return at today's price. Dhando is **partial** (commodity and depletion risk, but no operating leverage). We propose **watch**: attractive cash yield and HK-style inflation hedge, but below our **~15%** accumulate bar on Lawrence math alone.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Oil and natural gas price and volume decline reduces monthly distributions faster than the market implies.

- **Depletion:** Proved reserves decline over time unless offset by drilling and technology (10-K reserve section).
- **Regulatory:** Methane fees, environmental rules on operators affect net royalties (10-K regulation section).
- **Concentration:** Royalty properties across six states but commodity exposure is undiversified.
- **Lens failure:** If commodity prices fall for multiple years, the equity yield curve base case fails; trust does not have MSB-style legal recovery option.

### How we calculated the annual return (show your work)

1. **Price today** = **$77.23** per unit.
2. **Base payoff** at year 5 = **$110.00** (cumulative cash return path plus terminal unit value in scenario notes).
3. **Total return multiple** = 110.00 / 77.23 = **1.424**.
4. **Annualized return** = 1.424^(1/5) − 1 = **7.2%** per year.
