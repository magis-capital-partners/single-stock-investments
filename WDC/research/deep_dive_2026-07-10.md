## What this business is

Western Digital is now a pure-play hard disk drive (HDD) company after spinning off its flash memory business as Sandisk in early calendar 2025. **[Fact]** Customers are cloud hyperscalers, enterprises, and consumer/OEM channels buying capacity enterprise (nearline), mission-critical, and client drives. **[Fact]** Continuing operations revenue was **$9.52 billion** in fiscal 2025 ended June 27, 2025 (+50.6% versus **$6.32 billion** in fiscal 2024 continuing revenue). **[Fact]** Operating profit on continuing operations was **$2.33 billion** (24.5% margin) versus **$403 million** prior year. **[Fact]** Owner cash in fiscal 2025 was approximately **$3.56 per share** after **$1.69 billion** operating cash flow and **$412 million** capital spending, on **359 million** diluted shares. **[Fact]** Western Digital retained a **28.8%** equity stake in Sandisk after the separation.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

There is **no dated predictive attribute** (HK: **none**). **[Inference]** The market may be extrapolating AI-driven nearline HDD demand as a multi-year supercycle, embedding peak-cycle owner cash and a full valuation of the retained Sandisk stake into WDC's price. **[Inference]** The alternative view is that HDD economics remain cyclical despite the duopoly: cloud digestion pauses, exabyte growth normalizes, and today's price leaves little margin of safety even if near-term earnings stay elevated.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** **Nearline HDD demand rolls over** while the stock prices in a long supercycle on elevated owner cash, producing poor returns even if absolute cash flow stays positive for a time.

- Cloud customer concentration and procurement leverage in duopoly pricing.
- Competitive pressure from solid-state alternatives in performance tiers (HDD wins on $/TB nearline, not all workloads).
- Sandisk stake volatility: **28.8%** retained exposure to NAND cycle separate from HDD operations.
- Debt extinguishment and non-operating gains can inflate earnings without adding recurring cash flow.
- Transition services and tax agreements with Sandisk add execution and litigation risk post-spin.

**Inversion:** If we are wrong, AI data-center build-outs extend nearline HDD growth for years, fiscal 2026 run-rate owner cash (~**$8+** per share annualized) persists, buybacks shrink the share base, and the retained Sandisk stake appreciates — making today's price a fair toll on storage infrastructure.
