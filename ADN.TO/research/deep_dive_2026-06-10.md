## What this business is
Acadian Timber Corp. is a Canadian pure-play timberland owner and forest manager listed on the Toronto Stock Exchange (TSX: ADN). **[Fact]** The company manages approximately **2.4 million** acres across Eastern Canada and the Northeastern United States: **775,000** freehold acres in New Brunswick, **300,000** freehold acres in Maine, and timber services on roughly **1.3 million** acres of New Brunswick Crown licensed land. **[Fact]** Core operations are sustainable forest management and the sale of softwood and hardwood sawlogs, pulpwood, and biomass to about **85** regional customers; FY2025 timber sales and services revenue was **CAD 87.0 million** on **996,200** cubic metres harvested. **[Fact]** The company also earns fees from Crown land management, recreation access passes, waterfront leases, real estate reviews, and a voluntary carbon credit project on Maine conservation easement land (Anew – Katahdin Forestry Project). **[Fact]** Macer Forest Holdings Inc. owns approximately **52%** of outstanding common shares and reinvests **100%** of its dividends through the dividend reinvestment plan (DRIP).

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The market may treat ADN as a simple high-yield timber stock whose **CAD 1.16** annual dividend is fully supported by recurring harvest cash flow, missing the trough-year owner-cash profile after lumpy **2024** carbon credit sales and the Maine operating transition. **[Fact]** FY2025 Free Cash Flow was **CAD 6.6 million** (**CAD 0.37** per share) while dividends declared were **CAD 20.9 million** (**CAD 1.16** per share); **2024** Free Cash Flow was **CAD 29.7 million** (**CAD 1.69** per share) aided by **752,100** voluntary carbon credits sold for **CAD 24.6 million**. **[Inference]** At **CAD 17.35** (June 10, 2026), the stock trades roughly **12%** below IFRS book value of **CAD 19.67** per share, a modest discount for a **~1.1 million** acre owned land base in a jurisdiction with long product life. **[Inference]** Predictive attribute: **timberland acreage REIT** plus **market structure discount** (small-cap Canadian income vehicle, related-party control, DRIP-dependent payout mechanics).

---

## Executive summary
Acadian Timber is a lean, acreage-heavy forest operator: New Brunswick freehold and Crown management provide the cash engine; Maine freehold is transitioning to internal harvesting after contractor capacity issues; carbon credits and real estate remain optional upside layers. FY2025 owner cash troughed at **CAD 0.37** per share with no carbon sales, while the quarterly **CAD 0.29** dividend continued, supported by Macer’s full DRIP participation and a solid balance sheet (**CAD 17.4 million** net liquidity, **CAD 110 million** long-term debt). At **CAD 17.35**, our base case annual return is **-3.7%** per year on filing-based owner cash with partial credit for the book-value discount on owned timberland (seven-year model, **16×** exit multiple on year-7 cash flow). The Lawrence owner-cash path alone is near **-9%** per year at this price. Book equity provides a partial floor, but dividend coverage risk and Maine margin recovery timing prevent a full dhando classification. We propose **watch** pending proof that Maine internal operations restore Free Cash Flow toward the **CAD 0.29** quarterly dividend without relying on another carbon sale.
