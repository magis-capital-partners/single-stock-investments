## What this business is

PrairieSky Royalty Ltd. is Canada's largest passive oil and natural gas royalty company. **[Fact]** It collects cash from third-party operators on **18.6 million acres** of royalty lands (**9.9 million** fee-simple mineral title acres plus **8.7 million** gross overriding royalty acres) without drilling wells, paying operating costs, or bearing abandonment liabilities (`PrairieSky-Royalty-Presentation-April-2026-POST-AGM.pdf`). **[Fact]** FY2025 funds from operations were **$353.0 million** (**$1.50** per share) on royalty production averaging **25,855** barrels of oil equivalent per day; dividends declared were **$1.04** per share (`PSK-2025-YE-MDA.pdf`). **[Fact]** Q1 2026 funds from operations were **$94.9 million** (**$0.41** per share) on **26,293** BOE per day; net debt was **$257.7 million** with **232.4 million** shares outstanding (`2026-Q1-MDA-FINAL.pdf`). **[Fact]** The company employs roughly **70** Calgary-based staff and returns essentially all excess cash via dividends and opportunistic share repurchases.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The market may price PrairieSky as a mature Western Canadian yield stock and underweight the perpetual undeveloped acreage option embedded in fee-simple mineral title. **[Inference]** Third-party spuds on PrairieSky lands totaled **201** wells in Q1 2026 (Clearwater, Mannville Stack, Duvernay, Basal Quartz), with oil royalty production up **6%** year over year while the company carries **zero** operating capital spending. **[Inference]** US and PFIC tax friction (15% Canadian withholding, Form 8621) may keep some yield-focused US accounts on the sidelines despite a **~3.1%** cash dividend at today's price and a conservative **65%** payout ratio in Q1 2026.

---

## Executive summary

PrairieSky is a high-margin royalty toll on Western Canadian drilling: no field operations, perpetual fee lands, and growing oil-weighted production from emerging plays (Clearwater, Mannville Stack, Duvernay). At **C$34.46** (2026-06-03), we expect **5.8%** per year on the base seven-year owner-cash case (bear **-0.9%**, bull **10.1%**). FY2025 funds from operations of **$1.50** per share and a **$1.06** sustainable dividend anchor cash yield; Q1 2026 run-rate is higher at **$0.41** per quarter. Moat is **stable** on land scarcity and operator relationships; dhando is **partial** because commodity prices and net debt (**0.6x** trailing EBITDA) cap downside but do not eliminate cyclical FFO swings. We propose **watch** below our **15%** accumulate bar, with the undeveloped acreage stack as bull-case optionality rather than base-case cash flow.

### Moat (Munger)

PrairieSky's competitive advantage (Munger moat) is **stable**: fee-simple mineral title is finite, perpetual, and cannot be replicated by the Crown. Operators choose PrairieSky lands for faster lease certainty, seismic data access, and non-competing royalty-only posture. The moat does not eliminate commodity cyclicality or dependence on **~335** lessees' capital budgets.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Sustained weakness in Western Canadian realized oil and natural gas prices compresses funds from operations even if drilling activity remains strong.

- **Inversion:** If WTI and AECO recover while spud counts hold, FFO per share could re-rate toward Q1 2026 run-rate (**$1.64** annualized) without new acquisitions.
- Net debt (**$257.7M**) and bank facility draws limit flexibility in a prolonged downturn.
- Royalty revenue accrual and prior-period adjustment complexity (auditor key matter FY2025) create timing noise in quarterly FFO.
- US PFIC reporting and withholding may cap demand from US taxable accounts.

**Lens failure mode (operating + optionality):** Treating undeveloped acreage as fully valued in base-case owner cash would overstate return; Lawrence base excludes undeveloped terminal value by design.
