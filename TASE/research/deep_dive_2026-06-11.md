## What this business is
The Tel Aviv Stock Exchange Ltd. (TASE) operates Israel's central market infrastructure: listing, trading, clearing, settlement, and market-data services for equities, bonds, derivatives, and mutual funds. **[Fact]** The company earns transaction fees, listing levies, clearing-house charges, and data or connectivity subscriptions; it does not take directional trading risk. **[Fact]** FY2024 revenue was **NIS 437.9 million** (+12% versus NIS 389.9 million in 2023); net profit **NIS 101.4 million** (+22%); adjusted EBITDA **NIS 186.3 million** (42.6% of revenue) per the company earnings release dated 2025-03-04 ([PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/tase-reports-the-results-of-the-financial-statements-for-the-fourth-quarter-and-full-year-2024-302391920.html)). **[Fact]** At **NIS 145.30** per share (Yahoo Finance TASE.TA, 2026-06-11), the stock trades near **140 times** our **NIS 1.04** per share normalized owner-cash starting point from filed FY2024 earnings.

## Why the market might be wrong
There is no dated structural discount. **[Inference]** Investors may capitalize Israel's 2024 equity rally (TA-125 index +29% per the same release) and the fast-growing data and connectivity line (+28% revenue in 2024) as if mid-teens volume and fee growth persist without cyclical give-back. **[Inference]** Our base return uses FY2024 owner cash per share with a small haircut, not the trailing twelve-month earnings per share figure shown on data terminals (~NIS 2.36), until the FY2025 annual report is on disk. Return is franchise quality at a full price, not a timed mispricing with a known catalyst date.

## Executive summary
Tel Aviv Stock Exchange is a regulated croupier on Israeli capital markets: fees on trading, listing, clearing, and data with minimal balance-sheet risk. FY2024 results show **NIS 437.9 million** revenue and **NIS 101.4 million** net profit; data distribution and connectivity grew strongly while trading and clearing commissions rose **7%**. At **NIS 145.30** per share (Yahoo, 2026-06-11), we expect about **-12.94%** per year over 7 years on **NIS 1.04** normalized owner cash per share (well below our portfolio hurdle). Asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) stays **partial** because local market activity is cyclical and geopolitical shocks can interrupt volume. Dual-listing and MAOF clearing growth are embedded in the growth path, not separate option terminals. We propose **watch**; the stock embeds faster growth and a higher terminal multiple than our filing-grounded base case supports at this price.
