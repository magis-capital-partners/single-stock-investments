## What this business is

The Tel Aviv Stock Exchange Ltd. (TASE) operates Israel's central market infrastructure: listing, trading, clearing, settlement, and market-data services for equities, bonds, derivatives, and mutual funds. **[Fact]** The company earns transaction fees, listing levies, clearing-house charges, and data or connectivity subscriptions; it does not take directional trading risk. **[Fact]** FY2024 revenue was **NIS 437.9 million** (+12% versus NIS 389.9 million in 2023); net profit **NIS 101.4 million** (+22%); adjusted EBITDA **NIS 186.3 million** (42.6% of revenue) per the company earnings release dated 2025-03-04 ([PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/tase-reports-the-results-of-the-financial-statements-for-the-fourth-quarter-and-full-year-2024-302391920.html)). **[Fact]** At **NIS 145.30** per share (Yahoo Finance TASE.TA, 2026-06-11), the stock trades near **140 times** our **NIS 1.04** per share normalized owner-cash starting point from filed FY2024 earnings.

Return is franchise quality at a full price, not a timed mispricing with a known catalyst date.

## Executive summary

Tel Aviv Stock Exchange is a regulated croupier on Israeli capital markets: fees on trading, listing, clearing, and data with minimal balance-sheet risk. FY2024 results show **NIS 437.9 million** revenue and **NIS 101.4 million** net profit; data distribution and connectivity grew **28%** while trading and clearing commissions rose **7%**. At **NIS 145.30** per share (Yahoo, 2026-06-11), we expect about **-12.94%** per year over 7 years on **NIS 1.04** normalized owner cash per share (well below our portfolio hurdle). Asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) stays **partial** because local market activity is cyclical and geopolitical shocks can interrupt volume. Dual-listing and MAOF clearing growth are embedded in the growth path, not separate option terminals. We propose **watch**; the stock embeds faster growth and a higher terminal multiple than our filing-grounded base case supports at this price.

**Croupier toll (Stahl):** Does the company earn fees on activity without taking principal risk? **Yes.** FY2024 net margin ~23% on revenue (NIS 101.4M / NIS 437.9M) with EBITDA margin ~42.6%.

**Volume vs share (Stahl):** Is handled activity growing? **Yes.** Revenue +12% FY2024; trading and clearing commissions **NIS 166.6 million** (+7%); data and connectivity **NIS 90.8 million** (+28%) per earnings release commentary.

**Fee model resilience (Stahl):** Can regulation compress economics? **Partially.** TASE operates under Israeli securities law as the central exchange; incumbency helps but fee schedules and index-usage pricing can change.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** **Multiple compression** if 2024-level activity and data-fee growth normalize while the market retains a premium valuation on recent earnings momentum.

- **Geopolitical or macro shocks** reduce local trading and listing activity.  
- **Regulatory fee changes** on index usage or clearing services.  
- **Competitive pressure** from alternative trading or offshore listing venues.

**Inversion:** If we are wrong, TASE remains Israel's central exchange with durable margins, but at **NIS 145** the base case still implies negative annual returns on FY2024-normalized owner cash unless growth far exceeds our base assumptions.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** Thesis fails if handled volume stagnates for several years while the earnings multiple stays elevated.
