## What this business is
Dorchester Minerals, L.P. is a Dallas-based master limited partnership that owns net royalty and mineral interests across U.S. oil and gas basins and distributes essentially all available cash to common unitholders each quarter. **[Fact]** The partnership has no operating employees; revenue is royalty and net profits interest cash from properties operated by third parties. **[Fact]** As of February 24, 2026, **48,255,450** common units were outstanding (`DMLP/investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260224_rpt20251231_acc0001437749_26_005376.htm`). **[Fact]** FY2025 operating revenues were **$152.8 million**, net income **$57.4 million** ($1.16 per unit), and declared distributions **$2.775346** per unit (`same 10-K`). **[Fact]** The partnership agreement requires quarterly distributions equal to **100%** of available cash after reserves (`same 10-K`, Business section).

---

## Why the market might be wrong
Yield-oriented investors may treat Dorchester as a static bond proxy and miss accretive growth from unit-funded mineral acquisitions in 2024-2025. **[Inference]** GAAP net income understates cash distributions because of timing between production and cash receipts. **[Inference]** Screeners focused on earnings yield rather than distribution yield may mis-rank the name versus simpler royalty trusts. **[Inference]** Concentration risk with Exxon Mobil and Chevron-related operators (~25% of FY2025 operating revenues) may be priced as permanent rather than diversified across thousands of small properties.

---

## Executive summary
Dorchester Minerals is a passive royalty MLP that pays out quarterly cash tied to oil and gas prices and acquired acreage. FY2025 distributions were **$2.78** per unit on **$152.8 million** of revenue; Q1 2026 activity improved versus prior-year quarter per the March 2026 10-Q. The partnership funds growth primarily by issuing common units for mineral exchanges (Colorado and Permian deals in 2024-2025). At **$28** per unit and **$2.78** starting distributions, Marvin's Lawrence base case is **13.8%** per year over seven years, a **watch** stance just below our **~15%** bar. Q1 2026 revenue improved year over year; commodity and dilution risks remain. Dhando is **partial**: no corporate debt, but commodity and operator risk are material. No approved third-party sources are in the base IRR.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Oil and natural gas price decline reduces revenues and quarterly distributions (`10-K` risk factors).

- **Operator control:** Dorchester does not operate wells; drilling and cost decisions are third-party (`10-K`).
- **Depletion:** Without replacement acquisitions, underlying reserves decline (`10-K`).
- **Dilution:** Unit issuance for acquisitions can grow unit count faster than per-unit cash (`10-K` 40% issuance limits apply to certain deals).
- **Customer concentration:** ~25% revenues from Exxon Mobil and Chevron-related operators FY2025 (`10-K`).

**Lens failure mode (operating):** If the market is right that distributions are peak-cycle, yield buyers face total return = yield only with price erosion.
