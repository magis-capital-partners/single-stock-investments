Global military conflicts and political uncertainty,&#160;fluctuating&#160;interest rates, changes in tariff rates, global supply chain disruptions, concerns about a potential economic downturn or recession, recent measures to combat persistent inflation, and actions taken by OPEC and its non-OPEC allies, collectively OPEC+, continued to contribute to economic and pricing volatility during 2025. These hostilities have disrupted and may further disrupt the flow of oil, and have contributed to, and may continue to contribute to, price volatility.
