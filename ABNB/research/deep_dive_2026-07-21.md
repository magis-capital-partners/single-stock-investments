## What this business is
Airbnb operates a global two-sided marketplace connecting hosts who list homes and experiences with guests who book short-term stays and activities. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$12.2 billion** (up **10%** versus last year) on **533 million** Nights and Seats Booked and **$91.3 billion** of Gross Booking Value. **[Fact]** The company is asset-light: it does not own the properties on its platform and earns primarily from service fees on guest and host transactions. **[Fact]** FY2025 free cash flow was **$4.6 billion**; cash and cash equivalents were **$6.9 billion** at year-end. **[Inference]** Owner economics are driven by booking volume, average daily rate, and take rate, with seasonality tied to global travel patterns.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
There is **no classic dated mispricing signal** (HK predictive attribute: **none**). **[Inference]** The market often values Airbnb as a post-pandemic travel recovery story, while the business has shifted toward a durable, capital-light toll on global nights with rising marketing and stock-based compensation masking GAAP earnings. **[Fact]** Q1 2026 revenue grew **18%** year over year even as free cash flow was flat at **$1.7 billion**, reflecting timing of unearned fees versus GBV. **[Inference]** Generalists may focus on net income (**$2.5 billion** in FY2025, down **5%**) and miss that cash generation remains strong. **[Inference]** Bears may underweight the **$3.8 billion** of 2025 share repurchases and **$5.6 billion** remaining authorization as evidence of aligned capital return at scale.

---

## Executive summary
Airbnb is the leading global short-term rental platform: **533 million** nights booked in FY2025, **$91.3 billion** GBV, and **$4.6 billion** of free cash flow. Q1 2026 accelerated with revenue **+18%** and nights **+9%**. Adjusted EBITDA was **$4.3 billion** in FY2025 (**36%** margin) and **$519 million** in Q1 2026 (**19%** margin, seasonally low).

At about **$147** per share (Yahoo Finance, 2026-07-21), the Lawrence base case implies about **14.1%** per year on normalized owner cash of **$7.38** per share, below our **15%** accumulate hurdle. The business has a **stable** durable advantage (Munger moat) on brand and network scale, with **partial** asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando): strong balance sheet and buybacks, but regulatory and tax friction cap downside protection at the equity level. Marvin proposes **watch** until price offers a better return or third-party work is indexed. No approved external sources are in the base case.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** **Lodging-tax and short-term rental regulation** escalates in major cities, raising compliance cost, capping supply, or forcing take-rate concessions.

- **Marketing and SBC inflation** outpace GBV growth, compressing GAAP earnings and sentiment even when FCF holds (FY2025 net income **-5%** on revenue **+10%**).
- **Travel recession** reduces nights and ADR simultaneously; seasonality amplifies Q4/Q1 weakness.
- **Competitive pressure** from Booking, Vrbo, and hotel direct channels on urban and resort markets.

**Inversion:** If we are wrong, Airbnb is a global oligopoly platform with **$4.6 billion** annual FCF and aggressive buybacks: you earn low-teens returns while the market re-rates on nights durability.

**Lens failure mode:** Operating lens fails if regulatory liabilities become a balance-sheet event (levered lens) or if undeveloped experiences optionality dominates value (optionality lens). Current filings support operating lens only.
