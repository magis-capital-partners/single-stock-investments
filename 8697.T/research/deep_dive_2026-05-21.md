## Executive summary

Japan Exchange Group is a **canonical Stahl croupier**: a toll collector on Japan's primary cash-equity, derivatives, and clearing flows with minimal principal risk on core operations. FY2025 (year ended March 2026) was exceptional — operating revenue **¥198.7B** (+22.5% YoY), operating margin **~58.5%**, ROE **23.1%** — driven mainly by higher cash-equity turnover and clearing-related collateral income, not by balance-sheet risk-taking.

**One-line view:** Dominant Japanese market infrastructure with extraordinary margins and volume leverage, but earnings are **cyclical with market activity**; the investment case is quality + toll-booth economics, not hidden value.

**Thesis status:** **strengthening** (near-term fundamentals) with **cyclicality risk** (medium-term).

## Munger lenses

### Circle of competence — pass

JPX is understandable: regulated exchange + clearing + data fees tied to Japanese capital markets activity. Five-sentence summary is feasible.

### Moat

- **Regulatory / network:** Primary listing and trading venue for Japan; clearing and settlement integration (JSCC).
- **Switching costs:** Issuers, brokers, and clearing members face high friction leaving the national infrastructure.
- **Scale:** ETF AUM on TSE-listed products exceeded ¥100T (Medium-Term Plan update) — ecosystem depth.

### Inversion — what would kill the thesis?

1. **Prolonged slump in Japanese equity turnover** (retail/inbound flow reverses) → trading fees compress sharply.
2. **Policy/regulatory fee caps or forced competition** undermining pricing power.
3. **Clearing collateral income normalizes** as rates fall or pass-through rules change — headline revenue growth slows even if core fees stable.
4. **Operational/system failure** damaging trust (low probability but existential for a croupier).
5. **Governance/capital misallocation** — large M&A or non-core bets outside infrastructure (monitor Medium-Term Plan execution).

### Psychology / incentives

Management incentives tied to market development and ROE targets (Medium-Term Plan cites **23.1% ROE** after year one). Watch for promotional disclosure around market-reform successes vs sustainable volume baselines.
