## What this business is
Akamai Technologies operates a global edge platform that delivers web content, blocks cyberattacks, and runs cloud compute workloads for enterprises. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$4.21 billion** (+5.4% versus last year) across three solution lines: Compute (**$2.24 billion**), Security (**$1.26 billion**), and Delivery (**$708 million**). **[Fact]** Customers pay subscription and usage fees for CDN traffic, security bundles (WAF, bot management, zero trust), and cloud infrastructure (including Linode and GPU services). The business is capital-intensive at the network layer but generates substantial cash: **$1.52 billion** cash from operations in FY2025.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock trades like a mature CDN utility (**~19 times** FY2025 free cash flow per share) while more than half of revenue now comes from faster-growing Compute. **[Inference]** Q1 2026 operating profit fell **26%** year-over-year even as revenue rose **5.8%**, so the market may be correctly punishing near-term margin compression from cloud build-out. **[Inference]** If compute mix sustains high-single-digit growth and margins stabilize after lease and capex digestion, today's price could understate a platform transitioning from legacy delivery to edge cloud. No dated catalyst; mispricing read is **quality transition at a compressed multiple**, not an equity yield curve.

### Mental models in plain English

**Moat durability (Munger):** Does scale protect the franchise? **Partially yes.** Decades of edge PoP build-out and embedded enterprise workflows create switching costs. Cloud compute faces fiercer price competition than legacy CDN.

**Moat trajectory (Munger):** Widening or eroding? **Stable with mix shift.** Compute revenue grew **~10%** FY2025 while Security revenue fell **~5%**. Delivery grew **~12%** on a smaller base. Net moat depends on whether security stabilizes.

**Deny compounding to others (Stahl):** Can rivals replicate? **Not trivially at global edge scale**, but Cloudflare and hyperscalers compete aggressively in security and compute. Akamai must defend with bundled edge value, not price alone.

**Capital allocation (Munger/Pabrai):** Management returned **$800 million** via buybacks in FY2025 while funding network leases and capex. **[Fact]** Tom Leighton (co-founder) sold shares in August 2025 per Form 4 filings; insider conviction score is weak (ICS **2.95**). Buybacks at **~19 times** cash flow are rational if Compute margins recover; dilution risk rises with **+8 million** new incentive shares approved May 2026.
