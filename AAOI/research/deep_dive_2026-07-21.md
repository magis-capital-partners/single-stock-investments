## What this business is
Applied Optoelectronics designs and manufactures fiber-optic networking products for internet data centers, cable television, telecom, and fiber-to-the-home markets. **[Fact]** The company is vertically integrated: it fabricates laser chips in Sugar Land, Texas, and assembles transceivers and equipment in Taiwan and China. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$455.7 million** (+83% versus FY2024); gross profit **$136.9 million** (30.0% margin). **[Fact]** End markets in FY2025 were data center **43%**, CATV **54%**, telecom **3%**, and FTTH **<1%** of revenue. **[Fact]** The company employed **4,691** people at year-end 2025, mostly in China and Taiwan manufacturing.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
**Predictive attribute:** complexity discount (small-cap optical supplier with 95% revenue from five customers, heavy China/Taiwan manufacturing, and episodic equity raises).

**[Inference]** The market may be treating AAOI either as a pure AI-datacenter winner (ignoring customer concentration and capex) or as a legacy CATV cyclical (ignoring the datacenter mix shift to **54%** of Q1 2026 revenue). **[Inference]** Filings show Microsoft and distributor Digicomm together accounted for roughly **82%** of FY2025 revenue; any socket loss would move earnings faster than headline growth rates imply. **[Fact]** FY2024 net income of **$186.7 million** included a large tax benefit; FY2025 net income was **$38.2 million** on higher revenue but lower operating margin and warrant-related contra-revenue.

---

## Executive summary
At **$99.77** (2026-07-17 close), the base case yields **-13.44% per year** over seven years on normalized owner cash of **$0.74 per share**. Applied Optoelectronics is a vertically integrated optical transceiver supplier riding datacenter bandwidth upgrades and a CATV equipment cycle, with FY2025 revenue up **83%** and Q1 2026 revenue up **51%** year over year. Owner economics are distorted by a **$179 million** capital spending year that exceeded operating cash flow, plus share count rising from **60 million** to **76 million** diluted shares after equity offerings. Customer concentration (top five customers **95%** of revenue) and geographic manufacturing exposure limit moat durability and asymmetric payoff. Marvin proposes **watch** until price offers a mid-teens annual return on filing-based owner cash.

#### Thesis pillars

1. **Datacenter bandwidth upgrade (structural).** **[Fact]** Management describes the internet data center market as the fastest-growing segment, driven by 800 Gbps and higher optical interconnects. **[Fact]** Data center revenue grew from **$148.5 million** (60% of revenue) in FY2024 to **$195.7 million** (43% of revenue) in FY2025 while total revenue surged; Q1 2026 datacenter was **$81.4 million** (**54%** of quarter revenue).

2. **CATV Quantum Bandwidth cycle (cyclical).** **[Fact]** CATV revenue jumped from **$87.7 million** to **$245.1 million** (+179%) in FY2025 as MSO network upgrades accelerated and direct MSO sales expanded. **[Inference]** This pillar is cyclical, not durable compounder economics.

3. **Vertical laser manufacturing (structural cost).** **[Fact]** Laser chips are fabricated only in Sugar Land; most transceivers use in-house lasers. **[Fact]** **199** U.S. patents and **140** China/Taiwan patents at December 31, 2025. **[Inference]** Cost advantage matters only if yield and volume scale without customer dual-sourcing.

4. **Balance sheet rebuilt via equity (event).** **[Fact]** Cash rose to **$439.7 million** at March 31, 2026 after equity offerings (multiple 424B5 filings in 2025-2026). **[Inference]** Dilution traded for capacity funding; per-share economics matter more than enterprise growth.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Loss of Digicomm or Microsoft as a major customer would remove most revenue base before diversification proves out.

**Secondary risks:**
- Capital spending remains elevated with negative reported free cash flow after growth projects complete.
- Further equity dilution despite recent raises.
- Tariffs, export controls, or disruption affecting China/Taiwan manufacturing (10-K risk factors, February 2026 tariff discussion).
- Manufacturing yield issues at MBE/MOCVD laser fab (single-site Sugar Land).

**Inversion:** What would make this fail? A hyperscaler qualifies a second-source 800G module at lower price, or CATV upgrade pause, while AOI still carries lease and capex obligations from Taiwan expansion.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** Treating FY2024 net income or peak AI sentiment as sustainable owner earnings without normalizing tax, warrants, capex, and share count.
