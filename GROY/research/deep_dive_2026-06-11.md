## What this business is
Gold Royalty Corp. (GROY) is a Vancouver-based precious metals royalty and streaming company listed on NYSE American and the TSX. **[Fact]** The company holds a portfolio of gold-focused royalties, streams, and land-agreement interests on mines operated by third parties across the Americas and other regions; it has no mine operating staff and reports in U.S. dollars under IFRS. **[Fact]** GROY was spun out of GoldMining Inc. in 2021 and follows a dual strategy: acquiring cash-flowing royalties and a lower-cost "royalty generator" land model that creates pre-production royalties from surface agreements. **[Fact]** FY2025 royalty revenue was a record $15.6 million (+55% year-over-year), total revenue including land proceeds and interest was $17.8 million, and operating cash flow was $6.2 million on 188.0 million weighted-average shares. **[Fact]** At December 31, 2025 the company had no outstanding debt, $12.4 million cash, and $75.0 million available on its secured revolving credit facility; 224.5 million common shares were outstanding.

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock may look like a low-yield micro-cap royalty stub while underpricing a growing producing base plus a large book of non-producing royalties. **[Inference]** FY2025 revenue was concentrated in four mines (Borborema, Côté Gold, Cozamin, Vares = 79.7% of revenue), but Borborema reached commercial production only in 2025 and Canadian Malartic's Odyssey underground project (38.6% of balance-sheet asset weight) is advancing on schedule. **[Inference]** Reported net loss of $4.1 million overstates economic weakness: adjusted EBITDA was a record $9.8 million and adjusted net loss was $1.7 million after stripping fair-value noise on the repaid gold-linked loan and other non-recurring items. **[Inference]** Royalty interests are carried at cost on the balance sheet, so book value (~$3.11 per share at year-end) does not mark portfolio metal exposure or development optionality at spot prices.

## Executive summary
Gold Royalty Corp. is an early-stage precious metals royalty aggregator spun from GoldMining in 2021. FY2025 royalty revenue hit a record $15.6 million (+55%) and operating cash flow turned positive at $6.2 million, but owner cash per share is still only about $0.033 against a $2.77 share price. The company repaid all debt in 2025 and holds $75 million of undrawn revolver capacity, which supports continued royalty acquisitions. Most of the portfolio remains on non-producing assets, including Canadian Malartic's Odyssey project (38.6% of balance-sheet weight). Dhando is partial: no recourse debt helps the floor, but commodity prices and dilution are real tails.

**Base case annual return: -7.41% per year** over seven years on FY2025 operating cash flow per share. Stance: **watch**.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Precious metal price decline combined with delayed development at Canadian Malartic or other large development assets that underpin balance-sheet value.

**Revenue concentration.** 79.7% of FY2025 revenue came from Borborema (23.3%), Côté Gold (27.1%), Cozamin (8.6%), and Vares (20.7%). An operator stoppage, grade issue, or jurisdiction dispute at any one property would materially affect near-term cash. Canadian Malartic alone represents 38.6% of total assets.

**Early-stage cash yield.** Operating cash flow of $6.2 million on ~188 million weighted shares implies ~$0.033 per share, far below the ~$2.77 share price. Even record revenue growth (+55%) does not close the gap without years of compounding or a much lower price.

**Dilution and acquisition risk.** FY2025 equity offering issued 25.9 million shares for $107.2 million gross proceeds. Share count rose to 224.5 million at year-end and 230.8 million by March 18, 2026. Future royalty deals may require more equity if producing cash cannot fund acquisitions.

**Accounting and tax noise.** Reported net loss of $4.1 million includes fair-value gains on the repaid gold-linked loan and other mark-to-market items. Deferred tax liabilities on mineral interests ($134 million) reflect economic royalty value not visible in near-term cash.

**Foreign and market-structure frictions.** 87.4% of revenue from properties outside the U.S.; PFIC status expected for 2025; limited SK1300 reserve disclosure as a royalty company.

**Lens failure mode (optionality):** Using book value (~$3.11/sh equity) or adjusted EBITDA ($9.8M) alone overstates near-term return at today's price. Development royalties may never reach production, and GAAP carrying values do not mark metal-linked economic value.
