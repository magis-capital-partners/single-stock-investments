## What this business is
Albemarle Corporation is a global specialty chemicals company and the largest Western lithium producer, operating lithium conversion (Energy Storage), bromine and related specialties, and refining catalysts (Ketjen). **[Fact]** Shares trade on the NYSE (ALB). **[Fact]** FY2025 consolidated net sales were **$5.14 billion**, operating loss **$367 million**, and net loss **$511 million** (diluted loss **$5.76** per share) per `investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260211_rpt20251231_acc0000915913_26_000018.htm`. **[Fact]** FY2025 cash from operations was **$1.28 billion** while capital spending was **$590 million**, and Q1 2026 cash was **$1.09 billion** against total debt of **$1.88 billion** per `investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-Q_20260506_rpt20260331_acc0000915913_26_000072.htm`.

## Executive summary
Albemarle is a capital-intensive specialty chemicals and lithium conversion company with integrated resource positions in Chile, Australia, and the United States. FY2025 results show trough lithium pricing: net sales fell to **$5.14 billion** from **$9.62 billion** in FY2023 while operating cash flow remained **$1.28 billion**. The business is understandable as a capital-cycle chemical producer with a narrow scale moat in bromine and conversion, but the bear case is only partially bounded given **$1.88 billion** of debt against positive equity.

At about **$118.20** per share, we expect **12.9%** per year over seven years on the synthesis base case (Lawrence owner-cash path **11.2%** per year). Proof-first component economic value base case is **$18.75** per share, so mechanical return math and filing-locked component sum diverge if the market embeds cycle recovery beyond conservative proofs. Dhando is **partial**. Stance proposal: **watch**.
