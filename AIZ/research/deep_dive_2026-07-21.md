## What this business is

Assurant is a specialty insurance and extended-protection company that distributes through partners rather than selling directly to consumers. **[Fact]** The company operates two reportable segments: **Global Lifestyle** (Connected Living mobile device solutions, extended service contracts, Global Automotive vehicle protection) and **Global Housing** (lender-placed homeowners, manufactured housing, flood, and renters insurance). **[Fact]** FY2025 consolidated revenues were **$12.8 billion** and net income **$872.7 million** on **51.1 million** diluted shares. **[Fact]** The business model is B2B2C: Assurant embeds protection products inside carrier, lender, retailer, and OEM channels across North America, Latin America, Europe, and Asia Pacific. **[Inference]** Economics come from recurring premiums and fees on large installed bases, plus investment income on marked fixed-income portfolios backing unearned premium reserves.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The stock trades near **16 times** trailing earnings while the market may underweight Assurant's **Connected Living mobile attach** and **lender-placed housing franchise** durability. **[Fact]** Global Housing Adjusted EBITDA rose **28%** in FY2025 to **$858.7 million**, helped by lower reportable catastrophes and lender-placed policy growth; Global Lifestyle Adjusted EBITDA grew **4%** to **$801.3 million** on Connected Living mobile programs. **[Inference]** Conversely, the market may overweight near-term catastrophe-light housing results while underpricing mobile partner concentration and a pending subsidiary divestiture loss noted in the 10-K. **[Fact]** Debt to total capital is **27.3%** with net debt only **~$373 million** after **$1.8 billion** cash, limiting financial distress risk. **[Inference]** No dated HK predictive attribute exists; the edge is **specialty franchise quality at a modest premium**, not a catalyst with a known payoff date.

Assurant sits between partners (carriers, lenders, retailers) and end consumers, earning fees and underwriting spread on protection products that partners white-label. The model creates earnings diversification across mobile, automotive, and housing lines (10-K Item 1).

### Mental models in plain English

**Moat durability (Munger):** Does embedded distribution protect the franchise? **Partially yes.** Mobile device protection requires carrier integration, claims infrastructure, and device logistics. Lender-placed insurance ties into mortgage servicing systems. Partners rarely switch mid-contract.

**Moat trajectory (Munger):** Is the advantage widening? **Stable.** Connected Living mobile programs grow globally, but partner concentration (major carriers) creates renewal risk.

**Deny compounding to others (Stahl):** Can peers replicate? **Partially.** Large P&C insurers and warranty administrators compete, but Assurant's global B2B2C scale and partner integrations are hard to clone quickly.

**Bounded bear (Pabrai):** Filing-locked book value **~$115 per share** and modest net debt **~$7 per share** limit downside versus speculative growth stories, but housing catastrophes are not a hard floor.

**Time arbitrage (Horizon Kinetics):** No dated predictive attribute; specialty insurers re-rate on catastrophe seasons and partner renewals. Patience matters only if owner cash grows through cycles.

### Thesis pillars

1. **Connected Living mobile toll:** Net earned premiums **$5.38 billion** in Connected Living FY2025 (+12% YoY); **53%** of Lifestyle revenue from mobile device solutions (10-K segment footnote).
2. **Global Housing lender-placed engine:** Net earned premiums **$2.77 billion** FY2025 (+13%); Adjusted EBITDA **$858.7 million** (+28%) on policy growth and favorable non-catastrophe loss experience (10-K MD&A).
3. **Investment float spread:** Net investment income **$527.3 million** FY2025 on **$8.6 billion** marked fixed-income portfolio; debt to total capital **27.3%** (10-K).
4. **Capital return discipline:** Share count declined from **53.2 million** (FY2023) to **51.1 million** (FY2025); August 2025 refinanced **$175 million** 6.10% notes with **$300 million** 5.55% notes due 2036.

**Primary risk:** Global Housing earnings are catastrophe-sensitive; a return to above-average reportable catastrophes would compress Housing Adjusted EBITDA and net income faster than Lifestyle growth offsets.

**Inversion:** What would make this fail? Loss of a major mobile carrier partner; regulatory restriction on lender-placed insurance; sustained investment portfolio losses if rates spike and fixed-income marks deteriorate.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** If partner concentration proves fragile (not just renewal timing), the B2B2C moat erodes and terminal multiples compress below 10 times owner cash.
