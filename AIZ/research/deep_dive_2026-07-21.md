## What this business is
Assurant is a specialty insurance and extended-protection company that distributes through partners rather than selling directly to consumers. **[Fact]** The company operates two reportable segments: **Global Lifestyle** (Connected Living mobile device solutions, extended service contracts, Global Automotive vehicle protection) and **Global Housing** (lender-placed homeowners, manufactured housing, flood, and renters insurance). **[Fact]** FY2025 consolidated revenues were **$12.8 billion** and net income **$872.7 million** on **51.1 million** diluted shares. **[Fact]** The business model is B2B2C: Assurant embeds protection products inside carrier, lender, retailer, and OEM channels across North America, Latin America, Europe, and Asia Pacific. **[Inference]** Economics come from recurring premiums and fees on large installed bases, plus investment income on marked fixed-income portfolios backing unearned premium reserves.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock trades near **16 times** trailing earnings while the market may underweight Assurant's **Connected Living mobile attach** and **lender-placed housing franchise** durability. **[Fact]** Global Housing Adjusted EBITDA rose **28%** in FY2025 to **$858.7 million**, helped by lower reportable catastrophes and lender-placed policy growth; Global Lifestyle Adjusted EBITDA grew **4%** to **$801.3 million** on Connected Living mobile programs. **[Inference]** Conversely, the market may overweight near-term catastrophe-light housing results while underpricing mobile partner concentration and a pending subsidiary divestiture loss noted in the 10-K. **[Fact]** Debt to total capital is **27.3%** with net debt only **~$373 million** after **$1.8 billion** cash, limiting financial distress risk. **[Inference]** No dated HK predictive attribute exists; the edge is **specialty franchise quality at a modest premium**, not a catalyst with a known payoff date.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Global Housing earnings are catastrophe-sensitive; a return to above-average reportable catastrophes would compress Housing Adjusted EBITDA and net income faster than Lifestyle growth offsets (`10-K_20260219_rpt20251231_acc0001267238_26_000010.htm` MD&A).

- **Partner concentration:** Connected Living depends on major carrier renewals; loss of a top mobile partner would remove a double-digit revenue slice (`10-K_20260219_rpt20251231_acc0001267238_26_000010.htm` Item 1A).
- **Lender-placed regulatory risk:** State or federal restrictions on lender-placed homeowners insurance could impair Housing premium growth (`10-K_20260219_rpt20251231_acc0001267238_26_000010.htm` risk factors).
- **Investment mark risk:** Rising rates could produce fixed-income unrealized losses in AOCI even when cash flows remain intact (`10-K_20260219_rpt20251231_acc0001267238_26_000010.htm` Note 21).
- **Pending divestiture:** 10-K references loss on pending subsidiary sale and restructuring costs that may recur in 2026.

**Inversion:** What would make this fail? Loss of a major mobile carrier partner; regulatory restriction on lender-placed insurance; sustained investment portfolio losses if rates spike and fixed-income marks deteriorate.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** If partner concentration proves fragile (not just renewal timing), the B2B2C moat erodes and terminal multiples compress below 10 times owner cash.
