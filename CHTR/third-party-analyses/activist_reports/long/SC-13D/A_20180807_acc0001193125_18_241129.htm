Item 5. Interest in Securities of the Issuer

Item 5(a) and (b) of the Schedule 13D are amended and supplemented to read as follows:

(a) The Reporting Persons are the beneficial owner of 33,507,797 shares of Class A Common Stock (including Class B Common Units and Convertible Preferred Units on an as-converted, as-exchanged basis). The 33,507,797 shares of Class A Common Stock constitute approximately 12.8% of the outstanding shares of Class A Common Stock, based on approximately 231.9 million shares of Class A Common Stock outstanding as of June 30, 2018. In addition, Michael A. Newhouse is the beneficial owner of 1,691 shares of restricted Class A Common Stock received by him in connection with his services as a director of the Issuer and Samuel I. Newhouse, III, is the beneficial owner of 538 shares of Class A Common Stock.

(b) The Reporting Persons have the sole power to (i) vote or direct the voting of 33,507,797 shares of Class A Common Stock beneficially owned by them as described in the Schedule 13D (including Class B Common Units and Convertible Preferred Units on an as-converted, as-exchanged basis) and (ii) dispose or direct the disposition of such shares, in each case, subject to the terms of the previously disclosed Operating Agreement, Exchange Agreement, Proxy and Right of First Refusal Agreement and Second Amended and Restated Stockholders Agreement, as described in the Schedule 13D. Michael Newhouse has sole voting and dispositive power over 1,691 shares of restricted Class A Common Stock beneficially owned by him. Samuel I. Newhouse, III, has sole voting and dispositive power over the 538 shares of Class A Common Stock beneficially owned by him.

Item 5(c) of the Schedule 13D is amended by adding the following paragraphs at the end thereof:

On August 6, 2018, A/N established a credit facility backed by an equity collar transaction and an aggregate of 6,000,000 Class B Common Units of Charter Communications Holdings, LLC (“Units”) to provide financial flexibility to support its ongoing estate planning and its investment program and for other general corporate purposes. The transactions cover less than 20% of A/N’s holdings in Charter and, absent physical settlement or default by A/N, have no effect on A/N’s voting and Board of Directors rights. A/N remains committed to being a long-term shareholder of Charter and has no intent to sell any shares other than through its continued pro-rata participation in Charter’s buyback program.

The equity collar transactions consist of a purchased put option and a sold call option (each, a “Collar Transaction”), pursuant to Share Collar Transactions Confirmations substantially in the form of Exhibit 7(q) (each, a “Collar Confirmation”) with Credit Suisse Capital LLC, Citibank, N.A. and Bank of America, N.A. (the “Banks”). The Collar Transactions cover an aggregate of 6,000,000 shares of Class A Common Stock and expire over two sets of expiration dates, one running from August 16, 2021 to August 31, 2021, inclusive, and the other from August 15, 2023 to August 30, 2023, inclusive. The Collar Transactions have a put strike price of $247.4860 and a call strike price of $371.5202 and $408.2063, respectively. The Collar Transactions will be settled in cash unless A/N elects physical settlement, in which case it expects to exchange Units for Class A Common Stock sufficient to satisfy its obligations under the Collar Transactions.

Simultaneously with entering into the Collar Transactions, A/N entered into revolving loan facilities (each, a “Loan Transaction”) with each of the Banks, pursuant to Revolving Loan Transactions Confirmations substantially in the form of Exhibit 7(r) (each, a “Loan Confirmation”). Under the Loan Transactions, A/N may, subject to satisfaction of certain conditions, draw an amount up to the present value of the put strike price multiplied by the number of underlying shares of Class A Common Stock under the Collar Transactions.
