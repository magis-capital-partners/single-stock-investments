### Investor-wisdom rules applied

- Horizon Kinetics: prioritize the 80% free-cash-flow-margin royalty and toll structure over GAAP land carrying value.
- Groundbreaker: contiguous powered land and local water are scarce physical bottlenecks, but first-order power enthusiasm may already be priced.
- Klarman: transaction-backed land value and uncontracted catalysts are shown separately.
- Marks: sponsor control, related-party revenue, and leverage remain claims against realization value.

### Limitations

- LandBridge does not disclose Alpha Digital rent, customer commitments, or full powered-land economics.
- Per-acre purchases include different mixes of fee title, leasehold, water, pore space, and existing contracts and cannot be applied uniformly.
- The 20% dormant-acreage split is provisional pending parcel-level revenue attribution.

## Decision explanation

LandBridge has scarce Delaware Basin acreage and a capital-light fee model. Actual purchases validate roughly $0.9k-$7.1k per acre and approximately 7.6x EBITDA for cash-flowing packages, while its current fee engine already receives a premium 15x base multiple. After improving the dormant-acreage and powered-land option marks, $78.12 remains well above base value but is close to the high economic case. The result is a watch, with the market already paying for successful digital-infrastructure conversion and continued acquisition accretion.

**Strongest counter-explanation:** LandBridge may be evolving from an oilfield surface landlord into a uniquely advantaged power, water, pore-space, and digital-infrastructure platform whose recurring economics deserve a multiple above the base case.

**Falsifiers:**

- Alpha Digital discloses contracted rent or royalty economics that independently add more than $8 per economic unit.
- Arm's-length revenue excluding WaterBridge compounds above 12% while net leverage declines.
- Acquisitions continue below 8x forward EBITDA without equity dilution or related-party leakage.

## Economic claim

Each listed Class A share represents one OpCo unit. Valuation therefore uses 27.839 million Class A plus 49.178 million exchangeable Class B/OpCo units, rather than dividing consolidated cash flow by Class A shares alone. Sponsor control, tax distributions, and related-party WaterBridge transactions remain governance risks.
