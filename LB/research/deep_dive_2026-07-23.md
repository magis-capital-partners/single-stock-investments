## What this business is

LandBridge Company LLC owns and actively manages more than **315,000** surface acres in the Delaware Basin. **[Fact]** The company collects fee-based revenue from surface use royalties, easements, resource sales, resource royalties, and oil and gas royalties. **[Fact]** FY2025 consolidated revenue was **$199.1 million** (+81% versus 2024); consolidated net income **$72.4 million**; net income attributable to Class A shareholders **$30.1 million**; company-reported free cash flow **$120 million** (`8-K_20260225_exhibit_lb-ex99_1.htm`). **[Fact]** Q1 2026 revenue **$51.0 million** (+16% YoY); free cash flow **$40.9 million**; 2026 adjusted EBITDA outlook **$210–230 million** (`8-K_20260506_exhibit_lb-ex99_1.htm`). **[Fact]** LandBridge IPO'd in June 2024 at **$17** per Class A share. Five Point Infrastructure / LandBridge Holdings retains control through Class B units and related-party agreements with **WaterBridge (WBI)**. This is Permian land infrastructure, not an E&P operator.

## Why the market might be wrong

The market may treat LandBridge as a high-growth land compounder after the post-IPO re-rating, or dismiss it as a sponsor-controlled fee stream with no asset floor. **[Inference]** At **~$78**, the stock embeds a large premium to our proof-backed component base sum (**~$41** per economic unit) while Lawrence base owner cash still yields roughly **1.5%** per year on normalized Class A free cash flow. **[Fact]** GAAP book value for Class A holders (**~$12** per share) reflects acquired intangibles, not a mark-to-market of Delaware acreage. **[Inference]** Predictive attribute: **IPO / Up-C complexity** and **digital-infrastructure narrative** ahead of filed contract economics (Alpha Digital base option remains zero in SEC primary sources).

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Sponsor-controlled Up-C structure may allocate disproportionate benefit to non–Class A unitholders while Class A bears listing, governance, and leverage visibility.

- **Permian cycle:** Surface royalties fall if drilling slows (`10-K` risk factors).
- **Related-party dependence:** WaterBridge ecosystem revenue may not reprice on arm's-length terms.
- **Leverage:** **~$535M** long-term debt at Q1 2026.
- **Lens failure mode (asset):** If digital-infrastructure optionality fails to convert to contracted cash, the stock re-rates toward normalized fee yield only; insufficient at current price.

**Inversion:** Sustained Permian collapse, loss of key easements, sponsor extracting value via related-party terms, or debt covenant stress.
