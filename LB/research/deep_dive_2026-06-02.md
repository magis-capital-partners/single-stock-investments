## What this business is
LandBridge Company LLC owns and actively manages more than **315,000** surface acres in the Delaware Basin of West Texas and southeast New Mexico. **[Fact]** The company collects fee-based revenue from surface use royalties, easements, resource sales, resource royalties, and oil and gas royalties as operators and infrastructure companies develop land. **[Fact]** FY2025 consolidated revenue was **$199.1 million** (+81% versus 2024); consolidated net income **$72.4 million**; net income attributable to Class A shareholders **$30.1 million**; cash from operations **$126.3 million**; company-reported free cash flow **$120 million** (`8-K_20260225_exhibit_lb-ex99_1.htm`). **[Fact]** LandBridge IPO'd in June 2024 at **$17** per Class A share and traded at **~$73** on June 2, 2026 (Stooq). **[Fact]** Five Point Infrastructure / LandBridge Holdings retains control through Class B units and related-party agreements with **WaterBridge** (produced-water infrastructure; portfolio holding **WBI**). This is Permian land infrastructure, not an E&P operator.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The market may treat LandBridge as a high-growth land compounder after the post-IPO re-rating, or dismiss it as a sponsor-controlled fee stream with no asset floor. **[Inference]** At **~$73**, the stock embeds **~4.3×** the IPO price while Lawrence base owner cash yields roughly **1.8%** per year on normalized Class A free cash flow. **[Fact]** GAAP book value for Class A holders (**~$12** per share) reflects acquired intangibles and retained earnings, not a mark-to-market of **315k** Delaware acres; it is a weak dhando floor but not the TPL-style zero-book pattern. **[Inference]** Predictive attribute: **IPO / Up-C complexity**: public Class A shares own a minority economic slice of OpCo cash flows while sponsor units retain control and related-party revenue is material.

---

## Executive summary
LandBridge is a fee-based Delaware Basin land manager with strong operating proof: FY2025 revenue **$199M** (+81%), adjusted EBITDA **$177M**, and **$120M** consolidated free cash flow. Q1 2026 revenue **$51M** (+16% YoY) with free cash flow **$41M** and raised 2026 adjusted EBITDA outlook **$210–230M**. The asset base (**315k+** acres) and WaterBridge ecosystem tie are strategically valuable, but Class A investors receive only **~42%** of consolidated net income and face **~$560M** long-term debt plus sponsor governance. At **~$73** (NYSE, June 2, 2026), we expect **1.83%** per year on **$1.82** starting free cash flow per Class A share (8%/4% growth, 18× exit). Dividend yield is **~0.7%** ($0.48 annualized). Moat is unproven; dhando is partial on acreage scarcity but not on price. We propose **watch**.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Sponsor-controlled Up-C structure allocates disproportionate economic benefit to non–Class A unitholders while Class A bears listing, governance, and leverage visibility.

- **Permian cycle:** Surface royalties and easements fall if drilling and completions slow (`10-K` risk factors).
- **Related-party dependence:** WaterBridge and Five Point ecosystem revenue may not reprice on arm's-length terms.
- **Leverage:** **~$560M** long-term debt amid rising-rate/refi risk (`10-Q_20260507`).
- **Lens failure mode (asset):** If digital-infrastructure optionality fails to convert to contracted cash, the stock re-rates toward normalized fee yield only; insufficient at current price.

**Inversion:** What would make this fail? Sustained Permian activity collapse, loss of key easements, sponsor extracting value via related-party terms, or debt covenant stress.
