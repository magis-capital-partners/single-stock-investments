## What this business is
Archer Aviation is a pre-revenue developer of electric vertical take-off and landing aircraft, led by the Midnight air-taxi vehicle designed for urban and regional passenger service. **[Fact]** Shares trade on NYSE (ACHR). **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$0.3 million**, research and development expense was **$493.9 million**, and the company reported a net loss of **$618.2 million** per `investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260302_rpt20251231_acc0001824502_26_000019.htm`. **[Fact]** At March 31, 2026, cash was **$951.1 million**, short-term investments **$824.8 million**, and weighted-average diluted shares were **766.9 million** per `investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-Q_20260511_rpt20260331_acc0001824502_26_000038.htm`.

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock may trade as a binary certification lottery while filing-locked liquid assets already cover a meaningful share of the price. **[Inference]** Net liquid assets of roughly **$2.21 per share** (cash, short-term investments, and restricted cash less debt and warrant liabilities) are not zero, so complete equity wipeout requires either faster burn than disclosed or a large dilution event not yet in the share count. **[Inference]** Conditional partner agreements, including United Airlines' milestone-based purchase framework up to **$1.0 billion** of aircraft plus a **$500 million** option, may be ignored in screens that see only **$1.6 million** of Q1 2026 revenue. **[Inference]** Proof-first component math at base case sums to about **$4.21 per share** versus a price near **$5.31**, so the market embeds modest certification optimism beyond the conservative schedule rather than extreme heroics.

## Executive summary
Archer is a pre-commercial electric air-taxi developer burning roughly **$600 million** per year while pursuing FAA type certification for Midnight, scaling manufacturing in California and Georgia, and building defense partnerships. FY2025 revenue was **$0.3 million**; Q1 2026 revenue was **$1.6 million**, mostly non-recurring. The balance sheet dominates today: about **$1.78 billion** of liquid assets against debt near **$80 million**, but diluted share count rose from **624 million** to **767 million** in one year.

At about **$5.31** per share, we expect **-3.3%** per year over seven years on the scenario base case. Proof-first component economic value sums to **$4.21 per share**, slightly below price. Dhando is **partial** because filing-locked net liquid assets near **$2.21 per share** limit total loss in a liquidation-style bear, but burn and ATM issuance can still impair per-share value. Moat is **unproven** until certification and repeat customer economics exist. Stance: **watch**.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** FAA certification delay or failure leaves Midnight without commercial revenue while burn continues and share count rises per `10-K_20260302_rpt20251231_acc0001824502_26_000019.htm` risk factors.

**Lens failure mode (asset):** Treating conditional partner orders as booked revenue before milestone completion overstates option value (10-K Note 12).

Additional risks:

- Dilution from S-3 and 424B5 offerings erodes per-share liquid asset floor (`424B5_*.htm` in `document_inventory.json`).
- eVTOL competitor certification or airline partner shift reduces Archer's share of conditional United demand (10-K Item 1 competition).
- Interest income falls if rates decline while operating burn stays fixed in dollars (10-Q interest income line).

**Inversion:** What would make this work? Type certification on public timeline, United converting conditional orders to firm deposits, and unit manufacturing cost curve proving sub-scale economics before major dilution.
