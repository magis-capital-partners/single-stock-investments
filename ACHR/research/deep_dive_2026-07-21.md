## What this business is

Archer Aviation is a pre-revenue developer of electric vertical take-off and landing aircraft, led by the Midnight air-taxi vehicle designed for urban and regional passenger service. **[Fact]** Shares trade on NYSE (ACHR). **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$0.3 million**, research and development expense was **$493.9 million**, and the company reported a net loss of **$618.2 million** per `investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260302_rpt20251231_acc0001824502_26_000019.htm`. **[Fact]** At March 31, 2026, cash was **$951.1 million**, short-term investments **$824.8 million**, and weighted-average diluted shares were **766.9 million** per `investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-Q_20260511_rpt20260331_acc0001824502_26_000038.htm`.

## Executive summary

Archer is a pre-commercial eVTOL developer burning roughly **$600 million** per year while building Midnight certification, manufacturing lines in California and Georgia, and defense partnerships. FY2025 revenue was negligible; Q1 2026 revenue was **$1.6 million**, mostly non-recurring. The balance sheet is the story today: about **$1.78 billion** of liquid assets against modest debt near **$80 million**, but share count rose from **624 million** to **767 million** diluted shares in one year.

At about **$5.31** per share, we expect a negative annual return on the Lawrence scenario base case pending mechanical refresh. Proof-first component economic value base case is **$4.21 per share**, slightly below price. Dhando is **partial** because of the liquid asset floor. Moat is **unproven** until certification and repeat customer economics exist. Stance: **watch**.

### Business mechanics (Hohn)

Archer designs and builds Midnight, a piloted eVTOL aircraft for air-taxi use cases. Manufacturing spans a "golden line" in Silicon Valley and a high-volume facility in Georgia. Revenue today is immaterial; economic progress shows up in certification milestones, test flight progress, and partner contracts. FY2025 R&D was **$493.9 million** and general and administrative expense was **$128.2 million**. Q1 2026 R&D was **$171.7 million** on **$1.6 million** of revenue. The company funds operations from equity offerings and interest on its cash and short-term investment portfolio (**$52.8 million** net interest income FY2025).

Thesis pillars:

1. **Certification path:** FAA type certification for Midnight unlocks Part 135 commercial operations.
2. **Partner validation:** United conditional purchase framework and Stellantis manufacturing support provide external demand signals if milestones hit.
3. **Balance-sheet runway:** Roughly **$1.78 billion** liquid Q1 2026 versus **~$600 million** annual net loss extends runway several years at current burn if no acceleration.
4. **Defense optionality:** U.S. Air Force contracts add non-passenger revenue paths and test credibility.

### Moat (Munger)

Moat is **unproven**. There is no commercial service, no repeat customer economics, and no filed proof of pricing power. Midnight's design and manufacturing know-how could become a moat only after certification and scaled operations. Today the moat assessment is deferred.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** FAA certification delay or failure leaves Midnight without commercial revenue while burn continues and share count rises.

**Lens failure mode (asset):** Treating conditional partner orders as booked revenue before milestone completion overstates option value.

Additional risks:

- Dilution from S-3 and 424B5 offerings erodes per-share liquid asset floor.
- eVTOL competitor certification or airline partner shift reduces Archer's share of conditional demand.
- Interest income falls if rates decline while operating burn stays fixed in dollars.

**Inversion:** What would make this work? Type certification on public timeline, United converting conditional orders to firm deposits, and unit manufacturing cost curve proving sub-scale economics before major dilution.
