## What this business is

Reddit, Inc. (RDDT) operates a global community platform where users gather in topic-based forums ("subreddits") to share content, ask questions, and discuss interests. **[Fact]** The company monetizes primarily through advertising on its mobile apps and website, with additional revenue from data licensing and premium subscriptions. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$2.20 billion** (+69% versus FY2024 **$1.30 billion**), with net income **$529.7 million** after a **$(484.3) million** loss in FY2024. **[Fact]** Average daily active uniques ("DAUq") reached **126.8 million** in Q1 2026 (+17% year over year). Reddit went public in March 2024 and reports a single operating segment.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The market may be pricing Reddit as a mature social ad platform when monetization is still early. **[Fact]** Q1 2026 average revenue per unique ("ARPU") was **$5.23**, up **44%** year over year, while DAUq grew **17%**, showing yield expansion ahead of user growth. **[Inference]** Generalists may anchor on pre-2024 loss history or compare Reddit to Meta's scale, missing that Reddit's ad stack automation, vertical diversification, and data-licensing deals are still ramping. There is no dated catalyst; the inefficiency is **descriptive** (recent IPO, unproven moat at scale) rather than a timed equity yield curve setup.

---

## Executive summary

Reddit is a fast-growing community platform converting engagement into advertising and licensing revenue. FY2025 free cash flow was **$684.2 million** (**$3.67** per share on weighted-average shares), and Q1 2026 annualized free cash flow run-rate is materially higher. At **$193.30** (NYSE, 2026-07-08), base expected annual return at today's price is **4.55%** per year on filed FY2025 owner cash, well below our **15%** bar. Durable competitive advantage (Munger moat) is **unproven**: network effects exist within communities but platform-wide switching costs are weaker than mega-cap peers. Asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) is **partial**: cash-rich balance sheet (**$2.8 billion** cash and marketable securities Q1 2026) limits downside, but the stock trades at a premium multiple that requires sustained ARPU growth. We propose **watch**.

Reddit is a **multi-sided network** (Lawrence bucket): users and moderators create content; advertisers buy access to intent-rich conversations; AI partners license data. It is not a balance-sheet croupier.

### Mental models in plain English

**Critical mass (Lawrence):** Subreddit communities need both contributors and readers. **[Fact]** Over **80 million** people searched on Reddit weekly in Q4 2025, supporting a search-destination option embedded in engagement (`10-K FY2025`).

**Moat trajectory (Munger):** Is competitive advantage widening? **Unproven at platform scale.** Community-level loyalty is strong, but users can multi-home to X, Discord, or Facebook groups. **[Inference]** Moderator labor and community norms are a soft moat, not a hard switching cost.

**Croupier toll (Stahl):** Reddit earns fees on attention (ads, licensing), not principal risk. **Partial toll** on digital attention, with take rate still rising via ARPU.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Multiple compression if ARPU growth decelerates while the market still prices Reddit as a high-growth platform.

- **User engagement volatility:** DAUq can fall around social unrest, moderation controversies, or bot cleanup (10-K risk factors).
- **Advertiser concentration / cyclicality:** Ad revenue is seasonal (Q4 strongest) and macro-sensitive.
- **AI licensing commoditization:** Data deals may re-price as competitors offer similar corpora.
- **Inversion (Munger):** What kills the thesis? Sustained DAUq decline plus flat ARPU for four quarters would force bear-case growth and erode moat to **eroding**.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** If we are wrong that ARPU can keep rising, owner cash growth understates bear risk and the stock remains expensive on filed free cash flow.
