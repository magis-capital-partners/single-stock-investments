## What this business is
CoreWeave, Inc. rents specialized graphics processing unit (GPU) cloud infrastructure to artificial intelligence labs and enterprises. **[Fact]** The company operates AI-native data centers built around NVIDIA GPU clusters (GB200/GB300 systems, InfiniBand networking) and sells compute, storage, and orchestration services under long-term contracts. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$5.1 billion** (up from **$1.9 billion** in 2024 and **$229 million** in 2023). **[Fact]** At December 31, 2025, remaining performance obligations (contract backlog) totaled **$60.7 billion** versus **$15.1 billion** a year earlier, with a weighted-average contract duration of about **five years**. **[Fact]** The company remains loss-making at the net income line: **$1.2 billion** net loss in FY2025 and **$740 million** in Q1 2026, while reporting **$3.1 billion** of net cash from operating activities in FY2025. **[Fact]** Total debt was **$25.1 billion** at March 31, 2026.

## Why the market might be wrong
**Predictive attribute:** growth narrative versus leverage (headline revenue and backlog versus interest expense, customer concentration, and refinancing risk).

**[Inference]** The market may price CoreWeave as a hyperscaler-compounding cloud franchise because revenue tripled year over year, the stock joined the Nasdaq-100, and NVIDIA invested **$2.0 billion**. **[Fact]** Microsoft alone accounted for roughly **67%** of FY2025 revenue; the top two customers were **77%** in 2024. **[Fact]** Interest expense, net was **$1.2 billion** in FY2025 on **$21.6 billion** of debt at year-end (rising to **$25.1 billion** by Q1 2026). **[Inference]** Investors may underweight the gap between positive operating cash flow (boosted by contract timing and capitalized interest) and equity-level owner cash after debt service, GPU depreciation (**$2.5 billion** FY2025), and continued build-out capex. **[Inference]** The **$60.7 billion** backlog is not per-share net asset value; conversion timing, margin after depreciation, and customer non-performance risk must be modeled explicitly.

## Executive summary
At **$73.21**, the base case is **6.2% per year** over seven years on normalized starting owner cash of **$1.25 per share** (not raw operating cash flow). CoreWeave is the largest independent GPU cloud renter we cover, with FY2025 revenue **$5.1 billion**, Q1 2026 revenue **$2.1 billion**, and **$60.7 billion** of contracted backlog. Microsoft is **~67%** of revenue; OpenAI and Meta have multi-billion-dollar master agreements. The business generates operating cash flow (**$3.1 billion** FY2025) but loses **$1.2 billion** at net income because interest expense (**$1.2 billion**) and depreciation overwhelm thin operating margins (operating loss **$46 million** FY2025). Total debt is **$25.1 billion** against **$4.8 billion** of equity. Moat is unproven (customer and supplier concentration, hyperscaler competition), dhando is none at this price versus unbounded refinancing and concentration tails, and Marvin proposes **watch** until operating income sustainably covers interest and customer concentration diversifies.
