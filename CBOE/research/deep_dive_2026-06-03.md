## What this business is
Cboe Global Markets operates exchange, clearing, and market-data infrastructure across options, equities, futures, and foreign exchange. **[Fact]** Customers pay transaction, clearing, listing, and subscription fees; the company does not take principal market risk. **[Fact]** FY2025 total revenues were **$4,714.2 million** (+15% versus **$4,094.5 million** in FY2024); operating income **$1,467.1 million**; net income **$1,100.0 million** (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260220_rpt20251231_acc0001628280_26_010013.htm`). **[Fact]** Q1 2026 net revenues were **$1,272.8 million** (+6% versus **$1,195.0 million** in Q1 2025); net income **$385.7 million** (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-Q_20260501_rpt20260331_acc0001628280_26_029112.htm`). The franchise is anchored in proprietary index options (SPX, VIX) and a multi-venue U.S. equities stack (BZX, BYX, EDGA, EDGX) plus European and Asia-Pacific derivatives.

## Why the market might be wrong
There is no dated payoff or structural discount. **[Inference]** FY2025 and Q1 2026 benefited from elevated volatility and index trading; the stock at **~$285** embeds a premium multiple on strong recent cash flow. **[Inference]** Our base return uses FY2025 free cash flow per share (**$16.00**), not a peak run rate from Q1 2026 alone. Return is franchise earnings power at price, not a mispricing with a known catalyst date.

## Executive summary
Cboe Global Markets is a toll collector on global derivatives, cash equities, and market-data subscriptions with minimal balance-sheet risk. FY2025 filings confirm **$4,714.2 million** revenues, **$1,467.1 million** operating income, and **$1,752.6 million** operating cash flow; Q1 2026 held momentum at **$1,272.8 million** net revenues. At **$285.23** per share, we expect about **13.42%** per year over 7 years on **$16.00** mid-cycle free cash flow per share (below our about-fifteen-percent-per-year accumulate bar). The SPX/VIX franchise and recurring Data Vantage fees support a durable moat, but transaction revenue remains cyclical. Asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) is **partial** because volume can mean-revert faster than the multiple compresses. We propose **watch** and would revisit near **$250–255** where the model reaches roughly fifteen percent per year on the base case.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** **Peak earnings trap** if volatility and index trading normalize while the market keeps a premium multiple on transaction revenue.

- **Competitive share loss** in U.S. equities to newer exchanges compresses take rates.
- **Regulatory intervention** on options market structure or data fees.
- **Foreign exchange translation** (comprehensive income includes **$78.3M** FX adjustment FY2025) affects reported equity, not core U.S. cash flow.

**Inversion:** What would make this fail? A multi-year vol drought plus fee cuts on SPX/VIX products would push operating income toward FY2023 levels (~**$1,058M**) and compress the multiple simultaneously.
