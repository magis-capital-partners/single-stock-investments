## What this business is

Ameriprise Financial is a U.S. wealth and asset management company built around a branded network of more than **10,000 financial advisors**. **[Fact]** Through Advice & Wealth Management, Ameriprise offers financial planning, fee-based wrap accounts, brokerage, banking, and insurance solutions to retail and mass-affluent clients. **[Fact]** Columbia Threadneedle Investments provides global asset management to retail, institutional, and advisor channels. **[Fact]** FY2025 consolidated revenue was **$18.9 billion** (up **5.5%** versus last year) and GAAP net income was **$3.56 billion** (**$36.28** diluted earnings per share). **[Fact]** Total Advice & Wealth Management client assets reached **$1.2 trillion** at year-end 2025 (+**13%**), with wrap assets of **$670 billion** (+**17%**). **[Inference]** Owner economics are driven by fees on client assets, transactional activity, and spread income from Ameriprise Bank and certificate products, not by balance-sheet lending like a bank.

## Why the market might be wrong

There is **no classic dated mispricing signal** (HK predictive attribute: **none**). **[Inference]** Generalists often bucket Ameriprise with capital-markets or life-insurance conglomerates and anchor on GAAP book equity (**$6.5 billion**) against **$191 billion** of total assets, missing that most balance-sheet assets are client or policyholder accounts rather than shareholder capital at risk. **[Fact]** FY2025 adjusted operating earnings per share rose **14%** to **$39.29**, and the company returned **$3.4 billion** to shareholders (**88%** of adjusted operating earnings). **[Inference]** The market may underweight the durability of wrap-net inflows (**$12.1 billion** in Q4 2025, **7.4%** annualized flow rate on wrap assets) and advisor productivity gains (**$1.1 million** adjusted operating net revenue per advisor, +**8%**). Return here is **normalized owner earnings at price**, not a sum-of-the-parts on insurance float.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** **Equity market decline** reduces fee-based revenue on wrap and advisory assets faster than expense cuts, compressing earnings and the valuation multiple simultaneously.

- **Advisor attrition** to competing platforms could slow net flows and productivity gains.
- **Insurance closed-block claims** (long-term care) or spread compression if rates fall further.
- **Regulatory change** on advice standards or annuity sales practices could raise compliance costs.

**Inversion:** If we are wrong, Ameriprise is a best-in-class wealth compounder returning most earnings to owners: you earn adequate total return from buybacks and dividends even if the Lawrence model's conservative exit multiple understates a **53%** return on equity franchise.

**Lens failure mode:** Operating lens fails if insurance liabilities are mispriced (levered lens) or if client assets prove ephemeral in a prolonged bear market. Current filings support operating lens as primary.
