## What this business is

Workday, Inc. sells cloud software for human capital management (HCM) and financial management to large and mid-sized enterprises worldwide. **[Fact]** FY2026 revenue (year ended January 31, 2026) was **$9.55 billion** (+13.1% versus **$8.45 billion** in FY2025), with subscription services **$8.83 billion** and professional services **$719 million**. **[Fact]** The company reports a **single operating segment** spanning Financial Management, HCM, Planning, Spend Management, and the Workday Cloud Platform. **[Fact]** Customers are chief financial officers, chief human resources officers, and IT leaders replacing legacy on-premise ERP systems. **[Fact]** Americas revenue was **$7.18 billion** (75% of total) in FY2026; EMEA **$2.38 billion**.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The market may be pricing Workday as a mature, low-margin SaaS vendor while subscription backlog and operating leverage are still compounding. **[Fact]** Remaining performance obligation (RPO) was **$28.1 billion** at FY2026 year-end, with **$8.8 billion** expected to convert within twelve months. **[Inference]** Generalists may overweight competitive fear from Oracle and SAP bundles and underweight Workday's unified data model, multi-year contracts, and AI upsell on renewals. At a premium multiple, the stock may still embed **double-digit subscription growth** that filings support but the headline price-to-cash-flow ratio obscures.

---

## Executive summary

Workday is an enterprise cloud compounder with a large subscription backlog and improving operating margins. FY2026 free cash flow was approximately **$2.78 billion** (**$10.36** per share on **268.1 million** diluted shares), operating income **$721 million**, and the company repurchased **$2.90 billion** of stock. At approximately **$230** per share (pending live quote refresh), we expect a **low single-digit to low-teens** per year return over seven years on filed FY2026 owner cash (mechanical refresh will set the exact base percent). Durable competitive advantage (Munger moat) is **stable**: switching costs and workflow integration defend the installed base, but Oracle and SAP limit moat widening. Asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) is **partial**: recurring revenue and buybacks bound distress risk, yet the equity trades at a full multiple on owner cash. We propose **watch**.

### Mental models in plain English

**Switching costs (Munger):** HR and payroll data, org charts, and general-ledger configurations live inside Workday. **[Fact]** Subscription revenue was **92%** of FY2026 total revenue. **[Inference]** Migration to a rival ERP is possible but costly in re-implementation, retraining, and business disruption.

**Critical mass (Lawrence):** Workday is a standard platform for Fortune 500 HCM and an expanding finance-cloud footprint. **[Inference]** Cross-sell from HCM into Financial Management raises lifetime value per customer.

**Moat trajectory (Munger):** Is competitive advantage widening? **Stable, not widening.** Oracle and SAP bundle cloud modules with existing databases; AI point solutions attack recruiting and spend workflows. **[Inference]** Moat depends on product breadth and renewal rates, not on being the only cloud option.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Oracle and SAP bundle cloud ERP with existing database relationships, slowing Workday net-new finance logos and forcing price concessions on renewals.

- **Competitive substitution:** Incumbent ERP vendors can undercut on total cost of ownership for dual HCM/finance deals (10-K risk factors).
- **Stock-based compensation:** $1.63B FY2026 dilutes economic ownership if buybacks slow.
- **Acquisition integration:** $2.1B acquisition spend in FY2026 (HiredScore, Evisort) must earn returns or goodwill impairs future owner cash.
- **Enterprise IT budget cycles:** Large deals slip when CFOs freeze discretionary software spend.

**Inversion (Munger):** What would make this fail? Sustained RPO growth below subscription revenue growth for four quarters, combined with operating margin contraction, would signal moat erosion and force a bear-case de-rating.
