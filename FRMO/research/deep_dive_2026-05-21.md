## Executive summary

FRMO is **not** an operating croupier — it is a **holding company / intellectual-capital vehicle** affiliated with Horizon Kinetics, holding concentrated stakes in market infrastructure (Miami International Holdings), Horizon Kinetics Holding Corp. (HKHC), managed funds, and smaller digital-asset exposures. Fair-value accounting makes reported earnings **highly volatile**; book value is the more stable anchor.

**One-line view:** Leveraged bet on Murray Stahl's Horizon ecosystem + exchange stakes (MIH IPO mark-up), with ~82% of equity in a single investment position — attractive only if one trusts the underlying assets and accepts mark-to-market noise.

**Thesis status:** **strengthening** (underlying marks and NAV) with **volatility risk**

## Business overview

FRMO Corp. (Delaware, est. 1993) describes itself as an **intellectual capital firm** identifying investment strategies and business opportunities. Key relationships:

- **Horizon Kinetics / HKHC:** 4.42% of HKHC; earns substantially all advisory fees via product-specific royalty interests ($10.2M royalty participation on balance sheet)
- **Horizon Kinetics Hard Assets LLC (HKHA):** ~21.8% equity interest; common control with officers/directors
- **MIH:** Fair value investment post-IPO; lockup agreements for pre-IPO holders including FRMO
- **Managed funds:** Horizon Multi-Strategy Fund LP, Winland Holdings, others via limited partnerships
- **Digital assets:** ~$10.9M fair value + digital mining assets (immaterial vs total assets)
- **Synteq Digital** (Feb 2026): new cost investment from contribution

Total assets **$919.5M** (Feb 28, 2026) vs **$713.4M** (May 31, 2025) — growth driven by investment marks.

## Munger lenses

### Circle of competence — borderline

Requires understanding Horizon funds, fair-value hierarchy, VIE/noncontrolling structures, and MIH lockup. Five-sentence summary is possible but **not** simple.

### Moat

- **No operating moat** at FRMO level — moat lives in **underlying stakes** (exchanges, Horizon research franchise)
- **Related-party concentration:** Leases office from Horizon Kinetics LLC; HKHA common control — **incentive-caused bias** flag (Munger)

### Inversion — what would kill the thesis?

1. **MIH or major fund position marks down** — 82% equity concentration
2. **Horizon/HKHC franchise impairment** — royalty stream declines
3. **Digital asset / mining losses** (smaller but volatile)
4. **OTC disclosure / governance** concerns — only 50 shareholders of record; limited SEC filing history
5. **Lockup expiry** on MIH — supply/overhang on exchange stake liquidity
