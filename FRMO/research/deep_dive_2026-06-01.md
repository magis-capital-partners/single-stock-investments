## What this business is
FRMO Corporation is a Delaware holding company affiliated with Horizon Kinetics. It does not run an operating business. **[Fact]** It owns concentrated stakes in investment partnerships, exchange infrastructure (Miami International Holdings), Horizon Kinetics Holding Corp., and smaller fund and digital-asset positions. Revenue to shareholders comes from fair-value marks on those investments, advisory royalty interests, and fund economics, not from product sales. **[Fact]** As of February 28, 2026, total assets were $919.5 million and FRMO-attributable stockholders' equity was $376.7 million, about $8.55 per share. **[Fact]** Roughly 82% of FRMO's equity sits in a single consolidated investment position ("Investment A"). The stock trades OTC with thin liquidity.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The market prices FRMO like a volatile mark-to-market fund, not like a sum of parts. **[Inference]** OTC trading, fair-value earnings swings (Q3 FY2026 diluted EPS $1.89 vs a $0.54 loss a year earlier), and concentration in one line item push generalist investors away. **[Inference]** Private stakes (MIH post-IPO, HKHC, Winland, catalyst names) are likely carried below economic value in GAAP marks. Horizon Kinetics calls this a **dormant asset** case: value sits in subsidiaries and listing events, not in trailing GAAP earnings. There is no clean equity yield curve with one dated payoff; return depends on a catalyst stack over several years.

---

## Executive summary
FRMO is a permanent-capital vehicle tied to Murray Stahl's Horizon ecosystem. Book value per share is about $8.55; the OTC quote near $6.70 (Stooq, May 22, 2026) is roughly 22% below book with negligible debt and high insider ownership. Reported earnings are noisy because unrealized investment gains drove Q3 FY2026 net income to FRMO of $83.4 million; that is not operating cash flow. A ten-year free-cash-flow model is the wrong primary tool here. Our base case uses a five-year sum-of-parts catalyst path: payoff $18 per share from $6.70 implies about 21.9% annualized return, with a bear case near 7.2% if marks stall. Downside is bounded by book and asset floor, not bankruptcy. Complexity and related-party ties keep asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) at partial, but the optionality gate passes. We propose **accumulate** for the Horizon ecosystem sleeve.

#### IRR arithmetic (show your work)

**Base case** (SOTP catalyst; `irr_method`: yield_curve / optionality). Payoff **$18** and horizon **5 years** are **our assumptions** in `valuation.json` — not a number management promised.

**Step 1 — Price today (what you pay now)**  
- OTC close **$6.70** per share (Stooq 2026-05-22). This is the market price anyone can pay today.

**Step 2 — Anchor from filings (what book says today)**  
- FRMO-attributable book **$8.55** per share (Feb 28, 2026 quarterly, `2026-02-28_Quarterly_Report.pdf`).  
- Discount to book: ($8.55 − $6.70) ÷ $8.55 ≈ **22%** (you pay less than accounting net worth per share).

**Step 3 — Build payoff $18 by adding the parts (sum-of-parts)**  

We do **not** start from “2.1× book.” We **add lines** that must sum to **$18.00**. The multiples at the end are just division checks.

**3a. Shares (denominator for every per-share line)**  
- FRMO common shares **44,022,781** (`2026-02-28_Quarterly_Report.pdf`).  
- Every “per share” = **dollars ÷ 44,022,781**.
