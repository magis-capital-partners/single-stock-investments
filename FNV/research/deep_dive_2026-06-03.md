## What this business is

Franco-Nevada is a Toronto- and New York-listed gold-focused royalty and streaming company. **[Fact]** It earns cash from gross-metal-equivalent ounces (GEOs) on third-party mines without operating production assets or mine-level capital spending. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$1,822.8 million** (+64%); operating cash flow **$1,493.7 million** (+80%); **519,106** GEOs sold including **11,208** from Cobre Panamá (`investor-documents/ir-fnv/NR-Franco-Nevada-Reports-Record-2025-Results-vF-2026-03-10.pdf`). **[Fact]** The company reports **no debt** and **$3.1 billion** available capital at year-end 2025, rising to **$3.4 billion** at March 31, 2026 (`investor-documents/ir-fnv/NR-Franco-Nevada-Reports-Record-Q1-2026-Results-vFinal-2026-05-12.pdf`). **[Fact]** Quarterly dividend is **$0.44** per share (16% increase for 2026; **$1.76** annualized).

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The market may treat Franco-Nevada as a passive gold beta at a peak multiple and underweight the portfolio option stack. **[Inference]** Record FY2025-Q1 2026 cash flows embed elevated gold, silver, and (in Q2 outlook) oil prices; a quality premium may compress if commodities normalize. **[Inference]** Cobre Panamá restart, recent acquisitions (Cascabel buy-back, Antapaccay with Glencore), and operator exploration spend on the royalty book are not fully in 2026 GEO guidance. **[Inference]** Unlike operating miners, Franco-Nevada benefits from higher oil prices without proportionate cost inflation (CEO Q1 2026 remarks).

## Executive summary

Franco-Nevada is the largest listed gold royalty company: record cash generation, zero debt, and a deep acquisition pipeline. At **$229.28** (NYSE, 2026-06-03), we expect **4.5%** per year on the base seven-year owner-cash model (bull **9.3%**; total synthesis **6.12%**). FY2025 delivered **$1.49 billion** operating cash flow and **519k** GEOs; Q1 2026 revenue **$650.7 million** (+77%) with **$520.4 million** operating cash flow (includes **$49.5 million** CRA refund). 2026 GEO guidance is **510,000–570,000** total GEOs (~90% precious metals). Moat is **stable** on contractual royalties and balance sheet; dhando is **partial** (commodity price risk, low yield at price). We propose **watch** below the 15% accumulate bar.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Precious metal price mean reversion from levels embedded in 2026 guidance ($4,500/oz gold assumption per NR).

- Cobre Panamá restart delays or limited stockpile processing only.
- Operator disruptions at key assets (Antamina sequencing, underground transitions).
- Acquisition discipline: paying full price for scale vs optionality (management counters in CEO letter).
- **Lens failure (asset):** If royalty portfolio is fairly valued as a bond-like gold proxy, optionality adds no edge at **>30×** owner cash.
