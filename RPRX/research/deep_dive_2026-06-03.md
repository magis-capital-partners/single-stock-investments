## What this business is

Royalty Pharma plc is the largest buyer of pharmaceutical royalties and a partner to biotech innovators. **[Fact]** The company holds royalties on more than 35 commercial therapies and receives payments tied to partners' product sales. **[Fact]** FY2025 portfolio revenue was $2.38 billion; operating income was $1.56 billion. **[Fact]** Class A shareholders received $0.88 per share in dividends in 2025 (~$378 million). **[Fact]** The balance sheet carries about $9.6 billion of debt against $19.6 billion of assets (Dec 31, 2025). The stock trades on NASDAQ as RPRX.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The market may be pricing **portfolio growth and new royalty deployment** more than near-term cash returned to Class A holders. **[Inference]** At ~$55 per share, the implied multiple on reported earnings is high versus the dividend yield (~1.6%). **[Inference]** If royalty collections grow with partner sales and management continues accretive acquisitions without over-levering, today's price could be fair on a look-through asset basis even when a conservative ten-year cash-flow model clears poorly. **[Inference]** Complexity from noncontrolling interests (RPI LLC / Class B) may keep generalist investors away, creating a specialist inefficiency rather than a simple earnings miss.

---

## Executive summary

Royalty Pharma is a diversified pharma royalty aggregator with durable top-line-linked cash collections, a growing marketed portfolio, and an active deal pipeline (including Q1 2026 Biohaven-related financing). FY2025 revenue rose to $2.38 billion while net income was $771 million. At $54.97, the Lawrence base case implies **about -0.9% per year** over seven years on $1.65 starting owner cash per share with mid-single-digit growth, **below** our ~15% accumulate bar. Bull case ~3.2% if new royalties and marks compound faster. Moat is **stable** (diversified therapy exposure); dhando is **partial** because of leverage and reinvestment-heavy capital allocation. We propose **watch**: quality franchise, but math at this price does not justify a new core position without a lower entry or higher distributable cash proof.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Loss-of-exclusivity or weak launch curves on high-weight royalty products reduce collections while fixed debt remains.

- Leverage (~$9.6B debt) raises refinancing and rate risk if collections stall.
- Reinvestment risk: new royalties must earn returns above implied market expectations.
- Complex structure (NCI, Class B, RPI LLC) obscures Class A look-through cash.
- **Lens failure mode (asset):** If market re-rates portfolio as bond-like yield only, multiple compresses unless dividend growth accelerates materially.

**Inversion:** What would make us wrong to pass? Sustained double-digit growth in distributable cash per Class A share with deleveraging, proving $55 is mid-cycle not peak.
