## Executive summary

Copart operates a **global online salvage-vehicle auction network** — a toll-booth on total-loss and remarketed vehicles with extraordinary returns on tangible capital, minimal financial leverage, and a balance sheet that benefits from **float** (buyer/seller funds held before settlement). FY2025 (ended July 31, 2025) delivered **~$4.6B revenue** and **~$1.7B operating income** per management narrative; GAAP contract revenue was **$3.97B** with **$1.55B net income** (diluted EPS **$1.59**).

**One-line view:** Dominant salvage-auction infrastructure with pricing power and network scale; near-term U.S. service-revenue growth has **paused** (Q2 FY2026 U.S. service fees down YoY) while international and interest income help — a quality compounder that requires monitoring of assignment volumes and insurance-seller concentration, not just the multiple.

**Thesis status:** **intact** — business quality and balance sheet remain exceptional; **watch** U.S. volume/fee trends in H1 FY2026.

## Business overview

Copart provides online auctions and related services for **salvage and clean-title vehicles**, primarily from insurance companies, banks/finance, fleets, charities, and dealers. Revenue mix:

- **Service revenues** — buyer fees, seller/listing fees, storage, transport-related charges (core toll).
- **Vehicle sales** — principal transactions where Copart sells vehicles on its own account (pass-through gross-up).

Geographic segments: **U.S.** and **International** (Canada, U.K., Europe, Brazil, Middle East, etc.). Management emphasizes global yard coverage and VB3 online bidding.

**Fact** — FY2025 MD&A: revenues **$4.6 billion**, operating income **$1.7 billion**.  
**Fact** — XBRL FY2025: contract revenue **$3,968,662K**; operating income **$1,696,714K**; net income **$1,552,449K**.  
**Source:** `10-K_20250926_rpt20250731_acc0001628280_25_042946.htm`

## Competitive position & capital allocation

- **Market structure:** Duopoly dynamics with Insurance Auto Auctions (IAA); Copart historically gained share via online model and yard density.
- **Pricing:** Fee increases and mix (higher ASP salvage) have been historical drivers; insurer pushback appears in U.S. service-revenue softness.
- **Capital allocation:** Growth capEx into land/yards; no large M&A dependency; shareholder returns via buybacks (monitor DEF 14A / 8-K cadence). Retained earnings **$8.1B** (FY2025).
- **Float economics:** Non-operating interest income is material — **incentive** to grow volume and settlement balances.

**Opinion** — Copart is a **capital-light toll** once yards exist; incremental returns depend on land entitlements and insurer relationships more than R&D.
