## Executive summary

CoStar is the **dominant U.S. commercial real estate data and marketplace platform** (CoStar Suite, LoopNet, Apartments.com, STR, etc.), expanding into **residential** (Homes.com) and **3D/digital twin** (Matterport, closed Feb 2025). FY2025 revenue was **$3.25B** (+~8% implied from prior year filings) with **79% gross margin**, but **GAAP operating income collapsed to $72M** and **net income to $7M** after heavy acquisition amortization, integration costs, and Homes.com marketing investment. Adjusted EBITDA was **$480M** (~15% of revenue).

**One-line view:** Wide-moat information monopoly in CRE with expensive residential land-grab — **compounder economics on subscription revenue**, but **not** a clean Pabrai earnings compounder until GAAP noise from Matterport/Homes.com normalizes.

**Thesis status:** **unclear** — moat likely intact; **earnings quality and capital deployment** need two more quarters of proof.

## Moat deep-dive (primary task for CSGP)

### What is defensible?

1. **CoStar Suite data collection** — hundreds of researchers, decades of comps; brokers need it for pricing mandates.
2. **LoopNet two-sided network** — commercial listings liquidity.
3. **Switching costs** — enterprise contracts, training, workflow integration.

### What is not yet proven?

1. **Homes.com** — share gains vs. entrenched portals require sustained marketing; GAAP losses reflect this.
2. **Matterport** — strategic adjacency; separate competitive set (3D / prop tech).
3. **AI risk** — automated valuation/listing tools may compress marginal value of some analytics tiers (speculative; watch customer churn).

**Opinion** — CRE moat remains **wide**; residential is **optionality**, not core dhando downside protection today.
