## What this business is
CoStar Group, Inc. (CSGP) is the dominant U.S. commercial real estate (CRE) data and marketplace platform. **[Fact]** Brokers and owners pay subscriptions for CoStar Suite research and analytics; LoopNet and related marketplaces monetize listing liquidity and advertising. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$3.25B** at roughly **79%** gross margin; **~89%** of revenue came from contracts of one year or longer. **[Fact]** The company is funding an expensive residential push (Homes.com) and absorbed Matterport (Feb 2025) and Domain (Aug 2025), which depress GAAP earnings while adj. EBITDA remains positive (**$480M** FY2025, **$161M** Q1 2026).

---

## Why the market might be wrong
A **transitory problem** may dominate: GAAP net income trough (**$7M** FY2025) and Homes.com marketing spend scare generalists away from a sticky CRE subscription core. **[Inference]** The stock is down roughly **54%** over twelve months to **~$33**, while CRE switching costs and data depth remain intact. There is no dated catalyst; residential share gain is optional upside, not required for base CRE economics.

---

## Executive summary
CoStar combines wide-moat CRE subscriptions with a contested residential land-grab. Normalized free cash flow per share is thin after Homes.com and acquisition amortization, but adj. EBITDA run-rate (~**$644M** annualized from Q1 2026) shows operating earnings power. At **~$33.21** (NASDAQ, ~May 26, 2026), base expected annual return at today's price (Lawrence) is **9.81%** on **$0.95** normalized FCF/share, below the **15%** bar. Durable competitive advantage (Munger moat) is stable in CRE; asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) is partial with residential burn tail risk. We propose **watch** until Homes.com spend moderates or price offers a mid-teens IRR on normalized cash flow.
