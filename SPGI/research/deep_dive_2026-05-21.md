## Executive summary

S&P Global is a **canonical Stahl croupier / index-infrastructure** compounder: ratings, indices (AUM-linked fees), Market Intelligence subscriptions, Platts energy benchmarks, and Mobility data — fees on financial and commodity market activity with minimal principal risk.

**FY2025:** Reported revenue **$15.336B** (+8% YoY); GAAP diluted EPS **$14.66** (+19%); adjusted diluted EPS **$17.83** (+14%); adjusted operating margin **+140 bps**. Returned **$6.2B** to shareholders (113% of adjusted FCF).

**Q1 2026:** Revenue **$4.171B** (+10% YoY, +9% organic); adjusted diluted EPS **$4.97** (+14%); adjusted operating margin **51.8%** (+100 bps). Mobility spin on track (Investor Day May 2026); upstream software divestiture announced April 2026.

**One-line view:** Essential financial intelligence toll-booth with ratings/indices leverage to issuance and AUM — cyclical at the margin but extraordinary margins through cycles.

**Thesis status:** **intact** (strong fundamentals) / **strengthening** near-term — corporate actions (Mobility spin, divestitures) add complexity but core croupier economics remain durable.

## Munger lenses

### Circle of competence — pass

Ratings + indices + data subscriptions are explainable; post-IHS Markit integration adds complexity but segments are mappable.

### Moat

- **Regulatory / benchmark entrenchment:** Ratings required for many bond covenants; S&P 500 is central benchmark.
- **Switching costs:** Index tracking, data terminal integration, benchmark references in contracts.
- **Scale:** AI integration cited as accelerant in Q1 2026 — moat depends on keeping product essential vs commoditized AI substitutes.

### Inversion — what would kill the thesis?

1. **Regulatory breakup or ratings oligopoly erosion** (DOJ, EU).
2. **Passive/active shift reducing index fee pools** (partially offset by AUM growth).
3. **Issuance drought** compressing Ratings transaction revenue for multiple years.
4. **AI-native competitors** undercutting MI data at lower price.
5. **Mobility spin / divestiture execution** destroying value or distracting management.

### Psychology / incentives

Heavy buybacks ($5.0B in 2025); management incentives tied to adjusted EPS and margin expansion — watch for non-GAAP adjustments masking core decay.
