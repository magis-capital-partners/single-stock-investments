## What this business is
S&P Global sells financial intelligence: credit ratings on debt issuance, index licensing tied to benchmark AUM, Market Intelligence data subscriptions, Platts commodity benchmarks, and Mobility analytics (spin planned mid-2026). **[Fact]** Customers pay transaction fees when markets issue debt, recurring subscriptions for data, and AUM-linked index fees. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$15,336M** with operating income **$6,478M** and net income **$4,471M** (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260211_rpt20251231_acc0000064040_26_000013.htm`). **[Fact]** Q1 2026 revenue was **$4,171M** (+10% vs **$3,777M**) with GAAP diluted EPS **$4.69** vs **$3.54** and operating income **$2,002M** vs **$1,578M** (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-Q_20260428_rpt20260331_acc0000064040_26_000024.htm`). The business earns toll-like fees with minimal principal risk on core operations.

## Executive summary
S&P Global is index and ratings infrastructure: fees on debt issuance, benchmark tracking, and data subscriptions with extraordinary margins. FY2025 filings confirm **$15,336M** revenue, **$6,478M** operating income, **$4,471M** net income, and **$31,127M** stockholders' equity; Q1 2026 revenue **$4,171M** (+10% YoY) with operating income **$2,002M** (+27% YoY) and diluted EPS **$4.69** (+33% YoY). Mobility spin mid-2026 should simplify the portfolio toward core toll booths. At **~$411**, Lawrence base case implies **~10.1%** annualized return over ten years on **~$17.5** mid-cycle FCF per share, below the 15% hold band. Bear case **~5.8%** if issuance stalls; bull **~13.1%** with sustained AUM and issuance tailwinds. Asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) is **partial**: subscription floor helps, but the transaction layer is cyclical. We propose **watch**; incumbent quality sleeves may keep **hold** despite sub-15% IRR.
