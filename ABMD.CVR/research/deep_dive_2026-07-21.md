## What this business is
ABMD.CVR is not an operating company. It is the **contingent value right** issued to former Abiomed shareholders when Johnson & Johnson completed its acquisition on **December 22, 2022**. **[Fact]** Each CVR is a non-tradeable contractual claim to receive up to **$35.00** in cash if three defined commercial and clinical milestones are achieved under the CVR Agreement. **[Fact]** Holders received one CVR for each Abiomed share tendered at **$380.00** upfront cash. **[Fact]** Approximately **25.76 million** shares were tendered (~57% of then-outstanding Abiomed stock). **[Fact]** Abiomed now operates as a standalone business within JNJ MedTech; the CVR is a tail cash option on Impella net sales and regulatory/clinical success, not equity in JNJ.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Abiomed/JNJ net sales fail to reach **$3.7 billion** in the measurement windows, and FDA or Class I guideline trials miss fixed deadlines, leaving holders with **$0** on some or all legs after years of illiquid waiting.

- **Inversion:** If Impella penetration accelerates under JNJ MedTech and STEMI/HRPCI trials succeed, holders could receive near the full **$35** stack, implying large convex upside from a low reference entry.
- JNJ net-sales definitions and Permitted Deductions can reduce counted sales versus investor expectations (`d428734dex101.htm` Net Sales definition).
- Tax withholding on milestone payments reduces net cash to holders (CVR Agreement section 2.4(c)).
- Non-tradeability limits price discovery; OTC **0.000** quote is not a reliable exit.
- Measurement-period timing shifts can convert a **$17.50** on-time net-sales payment into an **$8.75** delayed payment only (`d428734dex101.htm`).

**Lens failure mode (event lens):** If JNJ sells or winds down the Impella portfolio in a way that triggers a Company Sale clause without milestone achievement, payment mechanics may differ from base-case assumptions; re-read CVR Agreement change-of-control sections before relying on base probabilities.
