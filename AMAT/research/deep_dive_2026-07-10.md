## What this business is

Applied Materials makes and services wafer fabrication equipment used to manufacture semiconductors. **[Fact]** Customers are foundry, logic, memory, and packaging fabs worldwide. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$28.4 billion** (+4.4% versus FY2024); operating profit **$8.3 billion** (29.2% margin); net income **$7.0 billion**. **[Fact]** The company reports two segments: **Semiconductor Systems** (**$20.8 billion**, 73% of revenue) and **Applied Global Services** (**$6.4 billion**, 22%). **[Fact]** Owner cash in FY2025 was **$7.05 per share** after **$8.0 billion** operating cash flow and **$2.3 billion** capital spending, on **808 million** diluted shares.

## Executive summary

Applied Materials is the largest wafer fab equipment supplier, with a durable installed base and a growing services franchise. FY2025 free cash flow per share was **$7.05** on **$8.0 billion** operating cash flow minus **$2.3 billion** capital spending. Semiconductor Systems margins expanded to **35.5%** on mix and pricing; Q2 FY2026 revenue grew **11%** year over year, suggesting the cycle is still firm.

At **$588.66** (2026-07-09 close), the base case yields **-9.0% per year** over seven years (bear **-16.2%**, bull **-5.7%**). The franchise has a **stable** moat and **partial** asymmetric payoff: enormous cash generation and buybacks, but the equity embeds peak-cycle optimism and customer concentration (19% and 15% of revenue from two customers). Marvin proposes **watch** until price offers a mid-teens annual return on filing-based owner cash. Full IRR assumptions are in **Valuation & IRR** below.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** **WFE spending rolls over** while the stock still trades near a peak-cycle multiple on record owner cash, producing negative returns even if absolute cash flow remains high.

- Customer concentration: two customers were **19%** and **15%** of FY2025 revenue; a capex pause at either fab hurts disproportionately.
- China export and geopolitical restrictions on advanced tool shipments.
- Memory spending cuts after HBM build-out (DRAM was **26%** of SS revenue).
- Competition from Lam, TEL, and KLA in adjacent process steps.
- Goodwill and acquisition integration (FY2025 **$41M** impairment as reminder of M&A risk).

**Inversion:** If we are wrong, AI-driven WFE spending sustains **double-digit** revenue growth for several years, FCF per share rises toward **$10+**, and the current price proves reasonable for a toll on the world's chip capacity expansion.
