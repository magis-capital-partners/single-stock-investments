## What this business is

Alphabet Inc. owns Google (Search, YouTube, Android, Chrome) and Google Cloud. **[Fact]** Advertisers and enterprises pay for ad auctions, cloud compute, and subscriptions; consumers use free products that drive query volume and data. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$402.8B** (+15%); Q1 2026 revenue **$109.9B** (+22%). **[Fact]** Google Services produced **$89.6B** (+16%) in Q1; Google Cloud **$20.0B** (+63%). **[Fact]** Cash generation is strong (FY2025 OCF **$125.3B**, capex **$52.5B**, FCF **~$72.8B**), but management guides **$180–190B** capex in 2026 for AI infrastructure. **[Inference]** Owner economics depend on whether Cloud ROI outruns the capex step-up.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

There is **no clear dated mispricing signal** (HK predictive attribute: **none**). **[Inference]** The stock embeds Cloud AI optionality and Search resilience; it is not a classic dormant-asset or holdco-discount story. **[Inference]** Lawrence 10-year math on **FY2025 FCF/sh ($5.85)** understates medium-term earnings power if **2026 capex** is transitory and Cloud backlog (>**$460B** cited by management) converts. Return is **franchise cash flow at price**, with the bear case driven by Search disruption plus capex without ROIC.

## Executive summary

Alphabet is a dual-engine compounder: Google Services funds AI and Cloud investment while Cloud re-accelerates (+63% Q1 2026). Operating margin expanded to **36.1%** in Q1 2026 (from 33.9% prior year). **[Fact]** GAAP diluted EPS **$5.11** in Q1 includes large unrealized equity gains (~**$37.7B**); normalize before peer comparison.
