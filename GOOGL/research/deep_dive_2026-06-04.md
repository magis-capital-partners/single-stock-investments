## What this business is
Alphabet Inc. owns Google (Search, YouTube, Android, Chrome) and Google Cloud. **[Fact]** Advertisers and enterprises pay for ad auctions, cloud compute, and subscriptions; consumers use free products that drive query volume and data. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$402.8B** (+15%); Q1 2026 revenue **$109.9B** (+22%). **[Fact]** Reportable segments are **Google Services**, **Google Cloud**, and **Other Bets** (Waymo, etc.), plus **Alphabet-level activities** (centralized AI R&D). **[Fact]** FY2025 consolidated OCF **$125.3B**, capex **$52.5B**, owner FCF **~$72.8B**; management guides **$180–190B** capex in 2026. **[Inference]** Owner economics depend on Cloud ROI versus the capex step-up and whether Search monetization holds as interfaces shift to AI.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
There is **no clear dated mispricing signal** (HK predictive attribute: **none**). **[Inference]** At **~$386**, the stock prices Cloud AI optionality and Search resilience together; it is not a holdco-discount or dormant-asset story. **[Inference]** A single consolidated FCF growth rate hides **divergent segment economics**: Services throws off most cash today while Cloud and Alphabet-level spend absorb the 2026 capex guide. **[Inference]** Lawrence 10-year math on **FY2025 FCF/sh ($5.85)** may understate medium-term earnings if capex normalizes; the segment build makes that tension explicit.

## Executive summary
Alphabet is a dual-engine compounder: Google Services funds AI and Cloud investment while Cloud re-accelerates (+63% Q1 2026 revenue).
