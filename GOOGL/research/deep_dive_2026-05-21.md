## Executive summary

Alphabet is a **dual-engine compounder**: Google Services (Search, YouTube, ads, subscriptions) funds massive AI/cloud capex while Google Cloud accelerates into enterprise AI infrastructure. FY2025 consolidated revenues reached **$402.8B** (+15% YoY); Q1 2026 revenues **$109.9B** (+22% YoY) with Cloud **+63%** to $20.0B.

**One-line view:** Dominant search/ads cash machine with Cloud as the growth and AI optionality leg — moat quality high, but **AI disruption cuts both ways** (Search interface risk vs Cloud/infrastructure benefit) and **$180–190B 2026 capex** raises capital allocation scrutiny.

**Thesis status:** **strengthening** (fundamentals) with **AI/capex watch items**

## Munger lenses

### Circle of competence — pass (with AI caveat)

Search/ads/Cloud model is explainable; AI trajectory adds uncertainty but business lines are identifiable.

### Moat

- **Search & data:** Query volume at "all time high" (CEO Q1 2026); proprietary user/data flywheel
- **YouTube:** Two-sided content platform; 350M paid subscriptions (company disclosure)
- **Cloud:** Enterprise AI backlog **> $460B** (CEO Q1 2026) — switching costs rising
- **Network effects:** Gemini integrated across products

### Inversion — what would kill the thesis?

1. **AI disintermediates Search** — users get answers without ads (zero-click / agent browsing)
2. **Cloud capex without returns** — $180–190B 2026 capex guide; margin compression
3. **Antitrust / regulatory** — remedies affecting default search or bundling
4. **Other Bets burn** — Waymo, etc. ($2.1B operating loss Q1 2026 Other Bets segment)
5. **Competitive cloud pricing** — hyperscaler race to zero on AI inference

### Psychology / incentives

Dual-class control (Founders); massive SBC and capex. Map who benefits from AI capex cycle (management narrative vs ROIC).
