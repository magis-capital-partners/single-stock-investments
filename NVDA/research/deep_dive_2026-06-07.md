## What this business is
NVIDIA designs and sells accelerated computing platforms for artificial intelligence, data centers, gaming, professional visualization, and automotive. **[Fact]** Customers are hyperscale cloud providers, enterprises, PC gamers, creative professionals, and automakers. **[Fact]** FY2026 revenue was **$215.9 billion** (+65% versus last year); operating profit **$130.4 billion**; net income **$120.1 billion**. **[Fact]** The company reports four market platforms: Data Center (**$193.7 billion**, 90% of revenue), Gaming (**$16.0 billion**), Professional Visualization (**$3.2 billion**), and Automotive (**$2.3 billion**). **[Fact]** Cash to shareholders comes from operating cash flow after modest capital spending on labs and tooling, not from a lending or asset-management model.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
There is **no clear dated mispricing signal** (HK predictive attribute: **none**). **[Inference]** The market prices NVIDIA as the default AI compute supplier with a widening software moat (CUDA), not as a cyclical semiconductor at trough earnings. **[Inference]** The debate is whether **growth deceleration from FY2026's peak** is already in the multiple at **~$140**, or whether networking attach, sovereign AI demand, and robotics platforms extend the growth runway beyond what a single blended growth rate captures.

---

## Executive summary
NVIDIA is the dominant AI accelerator franchise. FY2026 free cash flow per share was **$3.94** on **$102.7 billion** operating cash flow and **$6.0 billion** capital spending. Data Center revenue grew **68%** to **$193.7 billion**, driving consolidated operating margin above **60%**. The CUDA software stack and Blackwell product cycle reinforce a durable competitive advantage, but the equity already embeds exceptional growth.

At a **~$140** placeholder price, the base case yields **11.9% per year** over seven years (bear **1.3%**, bull **16.6%**). The business has a **widening** moat and **partial** asymmetric payoff: enormous cash generation, but the bear case is an AI capex pause plus custom silicon share shift. Marvin proposes **watch** until either price improves or FY2027 filings confirm sustained Data Center growth at base-case assumptions. Full IRR assumptions are in **Valuation & IRR** below.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** **AI infrastructure capex pauses** while the market still prices NVIDIA as a sustained **60%+** grower, compressing the terminal multiple before owner cash per share catches up.

- Custom ASIC programs at hyperscalers reduce GPU share faster than segment growth assumes.
- China export-control rules cap Data Center revenue in key geographies.
- Gaming cyclical downturn coincides with Data Center deceleration, removing a profit buffer.
- Customer concentration: a small number of hyperscalers drive most Data Center revenue.

**Inversion:** If we are wrong, NVIDIA remains the essential AI compute toll with **$100 billion+** annual operating cash flow, and **8.6%** understates a franchise that re-rates on any growth surprise.
