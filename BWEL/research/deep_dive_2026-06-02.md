## What this business is
J.G. Boswell Company is a family-controlled California agribusiness founded in 1921 and headquartered in Corcoran. **[Fact]** The company produces, processes, and markets cotton, cottonseed products, tomatoes, small grains, and orchard crops on a large scale in the San Joaquin Valley. **[Fact]** It also owns subsurface oil, gas, and mineral interests on its land and on third-party land in California and Oregon (`investor-documents/ir-bwel/2025-06-30_Annual_Report.pdf`, note 1). **[Fact]** Shares trade OTC Pink (BWEL) with very thin volume; the company is not an SEC registrant and publishes audited annual reports to shareholders via OTC Markets. **[Fact]** Fiscal year ends June 30; FY2025 had 964,210 shares outstanding and stockholders' equity of about $755 million (~$783 per share).

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock may look expensive on trailing earnings after a loss year while hiding a durable low-cost farm franchise and balance-sheet optionality. **[Inference]** FY2025 net loss reflects inventory net-realizable-value charges, flood-related costs, and crop volatility, not a permanent collapse of earning power (FY2024 net income was $46.7 million). **[Inference]** At roughly $549 per share the market prices below GAAP book (~$783), which itself likely understates land, water, and mineral rights carried at historical cost ($106 million land line). **[Inference]** OTC illiquidity and Boswell family control keep the name off institutional radars, so the discount may persist without a catalyst.

---

## Executive summary
Boswell is a cyclical, family-owned California farm and ginning operator with mineral-rights optionality and a long dividend history. FY2025 was a trough year (net loss $28.9 million) after a strong FY2024; management still paid $17.50 per share in dividends and repurchased stock. At $548.50 the stock trades near 0.7 times June 2025 book value with thin OTC liquidity. Lawrence base case annual return is **1.1%** on mid-cycle owner cash; yield-curve sensitivity to economic NAV near **850** per share over ten years is roughly **5%**. Dhando is partial (price below book, but earnings volatility and leverage). We propose **watch**: quality asset, wrong entry IRR for the 15% bar unless a deeper trough or better crop normalization appears.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Cyclical crop losses and inventory write-downs repeat while leverage on $280M credit lines limits dividend flexibility.

**Cyclical earnings.** FY2025 net loss after FY2024 peak demonstrates commodity and weather sensitivity. NRV charges of $42.8 million hit cost of sales.

**Leverage.** Current notes payable rose to $150.3 million from $35.7 million as the company drew on $200M and $80M credit lines (note 4). Net interest expense was $7.8 million in FY2025.

**Concentration.** Two customers exceeded 10% of receivables and revenue; one supplier exceeded 10% of payables.

**Water and regulation.** San Joaquin Valley farming depends on water allocation; flood and drought both appeared in recent filings.

**Liquidity.** OTC Pink trading (~61 shares on 2026-06-01 Stooq print) makes size and exit costly.

**Governance opacity.** No SEC proxy; family control limits minority influence.

**Inversion checklist:** Permanent loss of cost advantage, dividend cut below sustainable owner cash, or forced equity issuance would invalidate the watch-for-entry thesis.
