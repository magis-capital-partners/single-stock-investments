## What this business is

Citigroup Inc. (NYSE: C) is a global systemically important bank with five operating businesses: Services, Markets, Banking, US Personal Banking, and Wealth. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenues were **$85.2 billion**, up **6%** reported, with net income **$14.3 billion** (**$6.99** per share). **[Fact]** Tangible common equity was **$169.6 billion** at December 31, 2025 (**$97.06** tangible book value per share on **1,747.5 million** common shares). **[Fact]** Return on tangible common equity was **7.7%** ( **8.8%** excluding a Russia-related notable item). **[Inference]** Owner economics are a regulated balance-sheet claim: tangible capital, normalized franchise returns, bounded excess-capital release, and explicit stress reserves, not industrial free cash flow.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The market may be paying **~1.4 times** tangible book for a bank still spending **~$3.3 billion** annually on transformation while RoTCE sits below a **~10%** cost-of-equity hurdle. **[Inference]** Simplification, exit of non-core consumer franchises, and record Services revenue could lift normalized returns, but CET1 headroom above **11.6%** regulatory minimum is not freely distributable until stress buffers, RWA growth, and transformation costs fade. **[Inference]** Partial dhando exists at tangible book; upside requires proof that franchise normalization and capital release exceed credit-cycle and regulatory reserves.

---
