## Executive summary

QuidelOrtho is a diagnostics company formed by the **2022 merger of Quidel (point-of-care) and Ortho Clinical Diagnostics (lab/transfusion)**. The combined entity spans immunoassay, molecular, lab instruments, and transfusion — a **complex, integration-heavy** business, not a Stahl croupier.

**FY2025:** Reported revenue **$2,730.2M** (~−2% vs $2,782.9M prior year); **adjusted EBITDA $597.0M** (margin **21.9%**, up from 19.5%). Ongoing restructuring, portfolio simplification, and respiratory revenue normalization post-COVID.

**Q1 2026:** Revenue **$692.8M** (−10.5% reported vs prior year quarter per earnings deck); FY2026 guide **$2.70–2.75B** revenue and **$615–630M** adjusted EBITDA.

**One-line view:** Post-merger diagnostics platform with improving margins but **shrinking/top-line pressure** — thesis depends on integration completion and non-respiratory growth, not COVID-era baselines.

**Thesis status:** **weakening** — margin improvement is positive but revenue decline, merger complexity, and macro/reimbursement risks outweigh near-term confidence.

## Munger lenses

### Circle of competence — caution

Explainable at high level, but **segment-level economics and merger accounting** require deep primary-source work — borderline circle edge.

### Moat

- **Installed instruments + recurring consumables** in labs and POC.
- **Switching costs** in hospital lab workflows.
- **Threats:** China reimbursement cuts (cited in Q1 2026 deck), respiratory seasonality, Abbott/Roche/Siemens scale.

### Inversion — what would kill the thesis?

1. **Integration failure** — synergy targets missed; goodwill impairment (already impaired goodwill in prior periods per filings).
2. **Sustained revenue decline** beyond respiratory normalization.
3. **Leverage/refinancing stress** if FCF disappoints (review 10-K debt schedule).
4. **Product pipeline delays** (molecular strategy, LEX Diagnostics acquisition risk cited in presentations).
5. **Reimbursement/regulatory shocks** in U.S. or China.

### Psychology / incentives

Restructuring and "self-help" narrative can mask demand weakness — verify against **organic volume**, not just adjusted EBITDA.
