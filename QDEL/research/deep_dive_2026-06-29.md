## What this business is
QuidelOrtho is a post-merger diagnostics company (Quidel plus Ortho Clinical Diagnostics, 2022). **[Fact]** It sells lab instruments, transfusion-medicine tests, point-of-care rapid assays, and early-stage molecular diagnostics to hospitals, labs, and blood centers. **[Fact]** Revenue is largely recurring consumables on installed bases. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$2,730.2M** (-1.9% YoY per `10-K_20260219...htm`); adjusted EBITDA was **$597.0M** (21.9% margin). Q1 FY2026 revenue was **$619.8M** (-10.5% YoY per `10-Q_20260506...htm`). FY2026 guidance: revenue **$2.70 to $2.75B**; adjusted EBITDA **$615 to $630M** (company deck).

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock near **~$13.79** prices consolidated distress at roughly **5.4 times** enterprise value to EBITDA, while **Labs plus Immunohematology** (75% of revenue) grew mid-single digits ex-COVID in FY2025. **[Inference]** Margin expansion (+240 bps FY2025) is real, but revenue still falls and leverage (~4 times net debt to EBITDA) caps the multiple. **[Fact]** On **June 27, 2026**, the Financial Times reported QuidelOrtho is exploring a **~$1.5 billion** sale of its point-of-care unit (Advent International among interested buyers) to cut merger debt. **[Inference]** If completed, that is a dated deleveraging catalyst not in our base Lawrence IRR until SEC confirmation.

---
