## What this business is

Nucor Corporation is the largest steel producer in the United States by tonnage, built almost entirely on electric-arc-furnace (EAF) mini-mills fed by scrap and direct-reduced iron. **[Fact]** The company reports three operating segments: steel mills (sheet, plate, bar, and structural products), steel products (joist, deck, rebar fabrication, towers, and other downstream businesses), and raw materials (scrap yards, DRI, and brokerage). **[Fact]** FY2025 pretax segment earnings were **$2.383 billion** for mills, **$1.229 billion** for products, and **$153 million** for raw materials, with corporate eliminations of **$1.197 billion**. **[Fact]** Steel-mill shipments were **25.271 million tons** at **83%** capacity utilization. **[Fact]** Nucor is also in the late stages of a multi-year capital program, including a West Virginia sheet mill targeted at roughly **3 million tons** of annual capacity for about **$3.65 billion** net of state incentives. **[Fact]**

---

## Why the market might be wrong

We do not see a durable mispricing signal at today's price. **[Inference]** Nucor's cost position, balance-sheet strength, and project pipeline may justify a premium to our through-cycle component base of about **$179 per share** versus a price near **$237**. **[Inference]** The market may be capitalizing mid-cycle spreads and successful project completion, while our base case normalizes 2025 peak mill utilization and keeps an explicit supply-response reserve. **[Inference]** If projects earn above their cost of capital and industry supply discipline holds, the bull component schedule (**~$334 per share**) could prove conservative; if imports and new domestic capacity depress spreads, the low case (**~$68 per share**) is the relevant stress path.

---

## Executive summary

Nucor is a best-in-class cyclical steel compounder with a vertically integrated EAF model and a heavy but largely funded growth capex wave. **[Fact]** FY2025 was strong: **$7.52** diluted earnings per share, **$3.23 billion** operating cash flow, and moderate leverage (**24.4%** funded debt to capital). **[Fact]** Our proof-complete component schedule values one share at about **$179** on a through-cycle base versus **$237** today, implying about **−3.9%** per year over seven years. **[Inference]** Downside looks partially bounded by net cash flexibility and an undrawn revolver, but steel spreads can compress quickly. We propose **watch**: quality franchise, insufficient return at this price on normalized economics.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Global and domestic steel supply additions plus import pressure compress spreads while startup and project costs remain elevated. **[Fact/inference]**

**Inversion (what would make this fail):**
- West Virginia or other major projects exceed budget and fail to reach peer utilization.
- Industry utilization falls below **70%** for multiple quarters while spreads shrink.
- Scrap/DRI input inflation outruns selling prices.

**Lens failure mode:** Treating 2025 peak mill earnings as normalized owner cash; our proofs explicitly capitalize through-cycle economics instead.
