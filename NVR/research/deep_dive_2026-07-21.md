## What this business is

NVR, Inc. is a U.S. homebuilder operating Ryan Homes, NVHomes, and Heartland Homes in the Mid-Atlantic and Southeast. **[Fact]** Unlike land-heavy peers, NVR controls finished lots through lot-purchase agreements (LPAs) with forfeitable cash deposits rather than owning most land on balance sheet. **[Fact]** FY2025 net income was **$1.340 billion**; homebuilding pretax income was **$1.610 billion** and mortgage-banking pretax income was **$152 million**; aggregate cash was about **$1.916 billion** against **$909 million** of senior notes (`NVR/research/evidence_reconciliation_2026-07-15.json`, NVR 2025 Form 10-K). **[Fact]** At year-end 2025 NVR controlled roughly **207,000** lots and carried **$851 million** of net contract-land deposits after a **$111 million** allowance.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

NVR's capital-light lot option can look like peak-cycle earnings at a fair price when deposit impairments are rising. **[Inference]** FY2025 deposit impairment charges of **$75.9 million** (versus **$7.2 million** in 2024) and a **17%** cancellation rate show affordability stress, yet the stock near **$6,498** trades close to Marvin's **~$6,550** component base case. **[Inference]** The market may correctly price limited upside from here while under-appreciating how quickly NVR can abandon lots and preserve balance-sheet flexibility relative to land-heavy builders, or it may be right that normalized owner earnings are below peak 2025 GAAP.

---

## Executive summary

NVR is a high-return homebuilder with a distinctive forfeitable-deposit land strategy, captive mortgage banking, and aggressive share repurchases. At about **$6,498** per share, Marvin's proof-first component base case implies roughly **0.1%** per year over seven years to **~$6,550** per share economic value. That is a **watch** stance: quality franchise, partial dhando from lot-option downside asymmetry, but no meaningful margin of safety at today's price. ## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Prolonged affordability-driven cancellation and margin compression with sustained deposit impairments above 2025 levels.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** Treating peak 2025 GAAP as normalized owner earnings overstates value; the **$5,600/sh** homebuilding base already embeds a **12.8×** multiple on after-tax filing anchor.

**Inversion checklist:**

1. If cancellations stay **>17%** and impairments remain **>$75M**, normalized earnings fall below proof low case.
2. If NVR must fund **$733.9M** of additional deposits without commensurate community IRR, surplus cash is overstated.
3. If buybacks continue above **~$6,550/sh** intrinsic, per-share value creation turns negative on marginal repurchases.
