## What this business is
Xcel Energy is a regulated electric and natural gas utility holding company serving roughly **3.9 million electric customers** across eight states through four utility subsidiaries: Northern States Power (Minnesota and Wisconsin), Public Service Company of Colorado, and Southwestern Public Service (Texas and New Mexico). **[Fact]** Consolidated operating revenues were **$14.7 billion** in fiscal 2025 (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260225_rpt20251231_acc0000072903_26_000009.htm`, XBRL). **[Fact]** The company earns authorized returns on invested rate-base capital set by state regulators, not through open-market pricing. **[Fact]** Electric revenues were **$12.2 billion** and natural gas revenues **$2.5 billion** in 2025. **[Fact]** Management is executing a **$60 billion** base capital plan from 2026 through 2030 focused on transmission, renewables, distribution, and generation to serve rising load including data centers and industrial electrification.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
Utility investors often treat Xcel as a bond proxy: dividend yield near **3%**, visible rate-base growth, and a clean-energy brand. **[Inference]** That framing may underprice the **rate-base compounding** embedded in the five-year **$60 billion** capex plan and 2026 ongoing earnings guidance of **$4.04 to $4.16** per share (versus **$3.80** ongoing in 2025). **[Inference]** Conversely, the market may be **under-discounting wildfire tail risk and dilution**: the **$287 million** Marshall wildfire settlement hit Colorado earnings in 2025, and management plans roughly **$7 billion** of equity issuance through 2030 alongside **$22.8 billion** of new debt. **[Opinion]** There is no obvious mispricing edge at today's price; the stock looks fairly valued for a quality regulated compounder with above-average growth capex, which is why Marvin lands below our **~15%** return hurdle.

## Executive summary
Xcel Energy is a multi-state regulated utility investing heavily in grid modernization, renewables, and transmission across Minnesota, Colorado, Texas, New Mexico, and Wisconsin. FY2025 ongoing diluted earnings were **$3.80** per share (GAAP **$3.42** after the Marshall wildfire charge); Q1 2026 diluted earnings were **$0.89**, up from **$0.84** a year earlier.
