### Investor-wisdom rules applied

- Horizon Kinetics: dormant land, mineral, and water assets can have substantial value despite little current income or GAAP recognition.
- Groundbreaker: segment land by infrastructure and use rather than applying one portfolio average.
- Groundbreaker/TPL playbook: capitalize contracted third-party-funded tolls, then retain the non-depleting land reversion without counting the same cash flow twice.
- Klarman: distinguish gross economic NAV from the value a minority shareholder can reasonably realize after time, control, and liquidity discounts.

### Limitations

- Groundbreaker's lease-rate and project build uses company updates and named projects, but full contracts are not public.
- Water remains unadjudicated and mineral overlap with commercial deposits is not fully mapped.
- The buyer's economic claim to stapled LLC interests requires further legal reconciliation; no unproven look-through value is added.

## Decision explanation

AZLCZ is not fairly described by tax-basis book value or the prior $1,108 heavily risked schedule. Groundbreaker's segmented comparable NAV is approximately $1,878/$3,445/$6,332 per share. After explicit timing, completion, minority-control, and liquidity discounts, our risked range is $925/$1,935/$4,280. At $2,355 the stock is above risked base value but below complete comparable economic NAV, making it a patient watch rather than a rejection of the land and royalty thesis.

**Strongest counter-explanation:** Northeast Arizona renewable, transmission, water, mineral, and railway scarcity may be worth far more per acre than the conservative residual marks, while aggressive repurchases can compound per-share value rapidly in the tiny float.
