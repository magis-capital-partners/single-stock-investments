## What this business is
Aztec Land and Cattle Company, Limited is a 140-year-old Arizona land lessor incorporated in 1885 and re-domesticated to Delaware in 2023. **[Fact]** The corporation and related entities own roughly 239,000 acres of surface land and 318,000 acres of mineral rights in Navajo and Coconino Counties (`investor-documents/ir-azlcz/2025-12-31_Annual_Report.pdf`, Note 1). **[Fact]** Revenue comes mainly from renewable energy ground leases (solar and wind), grazing leases, access agreements, and related farm operations; the company also holds a 77.5% economic interest in the Apache Railway short line through The Apache Railroad Company, LLC. **[Fact]** Shares trade OTC Pink (AZLCZ) with thin volume; the company is not an SEC registrant and publishes audited tax-basis annual reports via OTC Markets. **[Fact]** At December 31, 2025 the company had 141,666 shares outstanding and tax-basis stockholders' equity of $7.76 million (~$55 per share).

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock may look like a tiny book-value oddity while embedding a large, hard-to-replicate land and infrastructure bundle in northeast Arizona. **[Inference]** Tax-basis financials carry land at roughly $13 per acre and assign no balance-sheet value to mineral rights transferred to Aztec Minerals, LLC, so accounting equity understates economic assets (TPL-style GAAP misstatement pattern). **[Inference]** Renewable lease income has become the majority of cash collections, and one solar project entered a 30-year operations term in 2025, yet the market may still price AZLCZ as a legacy ranch rather than a phased renewable landlord. **[Inference]** OTC Pink listing, stapled LLC units, related-party financing, and 40-year Little Colorado water adjudication keep institutional screens away, sustaining a market-structure discount even as the share price has risen sharply.

---

## Executive summary
Aztec is one of Arizona's largest private landowners, monetizing northeast Arizona acreage through wind and solar ground leases while retaining water, mineral, and railway optionality (~239k surface / ~318k mineral acres; `2025-12-31_Annual_Report.pdf` Note 1). Marvin normalizes on FY2024 run-rate owner cash of about $15 per share, excluding FY2025 land sales and cattle charges. At $2,287 the stock trades near 42 times tax-basis book and about 1.2 times Marvin's blended economic net asset value (~$1,875 per share). Renewable rent is the majority of cash collections; management repurchased 8,066 shares in 2025 at roughly $928 per share on thin OTC volume.

At today's price we expect **-6.28%** per year over seven years on normalized owner cash with Groundbreaker royalty-ramp growth (contracted leases toward ~$9.9M steady-state revenue by 2029). Dhando is partial given real assets and related-party risk. Stance: **watch**.
