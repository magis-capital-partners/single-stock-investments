## What this business is

Aztec Land and Cattle Company, Limited is a 140-year-old Arizona land lessor incorporated in 1885 and re-domesticated to Delaware in 2023. **[Fact]** The corporation and related entities own roughly 239,000 acres of surface land and 318,000 acres of mineral rights in Navajo and Coconino Counties (`investor-documents/ir-azlcz/2025-12-31_Annual_Report.pdf`, Note 1). **[Fact]** Revenue comes mainly from renewable energy ground leases (solar and wind), grazing leases, access agreements, and related farm operations; the company also holds a 77.5% economic interest in the Apache Railway short line through The Apache Railroad Company, LLC. **[Fact]** Shares trade OTC Pink (AZLCZ) with thin volume; the company is not an SEC registrant and publishes audited tax-basis annual reports via OTC Markets. **[Fact]** At December 31, 2025 the company had 141,666 shares outstanding and tax-basis stockholders' equity of $7.76 million (~$55 per share).

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The stock may look like a tiny book-value oddity while embedding a large, hard-to-replicate land and infrastructure bundle in northeast Arizona. **[Inference]** Tax-basis financials carry land at roughly $13 per acre and assign no balance-sheet value to mineral rights transferred to Aztec Minerals, LLC, so accounting equity understates economic assets (TPL-style GAAP misstatement pattern). **[Inference]** Renewable lease income has become the majority of cash collections, and one solar project entered a 30-year operations term in 2025, yet the market may still price AZLCZ as a legacy ranch rather than a phased renewable landlord. **[Inference]** OTC Pink listing, stapled LLC units, related-party financing, and 40-year Little Colorado water adjudication keep institutional screens away, sustaining a market-structure discount even as the share price has risen sharply.

---

## Executive summary

Aztec is one of Arizona's largest private landowners, now monetizing acreage through wind and solar ground leases while retaining water, mineral, and railway optionality. FY2025 tax-basis owner cash was boosted by land sales and cattle charges; Marvin normalizes on FY2024 run-rate owner cash of about $15 per share. At $2,287 the stock trades near 42 times tax-basis book and about 1.9 times Marvin's probability-weighted economic net asset value estimate (~$1,180 per share).

**Base 7-year return on normalized owner cash: about -21% per year.** Dhando is partial given real assets and related-party concentration risk. Stance: **watch**.

### Mental models in plain English

**Dormant asset / misstated book (Horizon Kinetics):** Surface land and minerals are the economic engine, but tax-basis carrying values are far below development value. Mineral rights transferred to Aztec Minerals carry **no assigned value** in the filing (Note 1).

**Market structure discount (HK):** OTC Pink, illiquid float, stapled Aztec Land Company, LLC units, and K-1-style complexity exclude many yield and index investors.

**Inversion (Munger):** What breaks the thesis? Renewable developers cancel during development terms (permitted without refund), federal failure to fund the 2024 water settlement, or related-party governance extracting value through Page Land & Cattle and Railway transactions.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Paying a full price for optionality already reflected in a multi-hundred-million-dollar market cap on thin float.

- **Renewable development risk:** Lessees may cancel during development without refund (Note 10); majority of leases still pre-operations.
- **Water adjudication:** Little Colorado River case CV-6417 spans 40+ years; 2024 tribal settlement needs federal legislation not yet passed (Note 13).
- **Related-party concentration:** Page Land & Cattle, Railway, and stapled LLC flows create governance and minority-holder inference risk (Notes 5, 15).
- **Illiquidity:** OTC Pink with 32-share daily volume (Stooq 2026-06-04); exit friction for size.

**Lens failure mode (asset / optionality):** If we are wrong that economic land and renewable value exceed price, the name becomes a low-growth rent stream at an extreme multiple with no catalyst to close the gap.
