## What this business is
AbbVie Inc. (NYSE: **ABBV**) is a global biopharmaceutical company built around patented medicines in immunology, oncology, neuroscience, aesthetics, and eye care. **[Fact]** FY2025 net revenues were **$61.2 billion** (up **8.6%** versus **$56.3 billion** in FY2024). **[Fact]** Cash from operations was **$19.0 billion** and capital spending was **$1.2 billion**, yielding about **$10.05** of owner cash per diluted share. **[Fact]** The company reports one operating segment but discloses five therapeutic areas; immunology led by **Skyrizi** and **Rinvoq** is replacing legacy **Humira** revenue lost to biosimilars. **[Inference]** AbbVie is a cash-rich pharma compounder in a deliberate portfolio transition: growth assets are scaling while debt from the Allergan acquisition and a large dividend absorb much of the free cash flow.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
There is **no classic dated mispricing signal** (HK predictive attribute: **none**). **[Inference]** Generalists may still anchor on Humira's collapse and GAAP net income (**$4.2 billion**, diluted earnings per share **$2.36**) without normalizing for intangible amortization, litigation reserves, and non-operating items. **[Fact]** Skyrizi net revenues rose **50%** in FY2025 to roughly **$17.6 billion** (U.S. **$15.2 billion** plus international **$2.4 billion**), and Rinvoq rose **39%** to roughly **$7.5 billion** globally. **[Fact]** Humira net revenues fell **49%** in FY2025 (U.S. **$3.1 billion** versus **$7.1 billion** prior year). **[Inference]** The market may be paying up for visible dividend growth and immunology momentum, leaving little margin of safety at about **$250** per share on **$10** of owner cash. Return here is **normalized owner cash at price**, not a sum-of-the-parts on pipeline optionality alone.

## Executive summary
AbbVie is completing the handoff from Humira to Skyrizi and Rinvoq. FY2025 operating cash flow was **$19.0 billion**; after **$1.2 billion** of capital spending, owner cash is about **$10.05 per share**. Q1 2026 net revenues were **$15.0 billion** (+**12.4%** year over year), with Skyrizi U.S. sales **$3.8 billion** (+**29%**) and Rinvoq U.S. **$1.4 billion** (+**20%**).

At about **$250** (Yahoo Finance, 2026-07-09), the Lawrence base case implies about **7.6% per year** on normalized owner cash, below our **15%** accumulate hurdle. The business has a **stable** durable advantage (Munger moat) on immunology franchises with patent protection, with **partial** asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando): strong cash generation and a **$6.65** annual dividend, but **~$65 billion** of debt limits balance-sheet flexibility. Marvin proposes **watch** until price offers a better return or third-party work is indexed. No approved external sources are in the base case.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** **Immunology growth decelerates** before debt maturities are comfortably refinanced, compressing owner cash just as Humira and Imbruvica erosion continue.

- **Leverage and interest expense:** Net interest expense **$2.6B** FY2025; long-term obligations **~$65B** (10-K).
- **Litigation and antitrust:** Humira pricing lawsuits and product-liability matters can move non-cash reserves and settlement cash (10-K commitments; 10-Q legal proceedings).
- **Patent and biosimilar pressure:** Humira U.S. revenues fell **57%** in FY2025; Imbruvica U.S. **-16%** shows oncology share loss.
- **GAAP versus economic earnings:** Low reported equity (**$3.3B**) and EPS (**$2.36**) understate economic earnings power but can confuse screens.

**Inversion:** If we are wrong, AbbVie is a successful post-Humira transition story priced as a bond-proxy: you collect a growing dividend while immunology compounds, and the market never offers a **15%** entry.

**Lens failure mode:** Operating lens fails if pipeline readouts become the dominant value driver (optionality lens) or if litigation creates a dated liability wall (event lens). Current filings support staying on the operating lens.
