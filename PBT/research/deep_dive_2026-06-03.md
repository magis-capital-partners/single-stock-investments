## What this business is

Permian Basin Royalty Trust is a passive Texas express trust created in 1980 that owns net profits interests in Permian Basin oil and gas properties. **[Fact]** Argent Trust Company is trustee; there are no employees and no operating business. **[Fact]** Unitholders receive monthly cash distributions from two property groups: a **75%** net profits interest on Waddell Ranch (Crane County, operated by Blackbeard Operating) and a **95%** net profits interest on legacy Texas Royalty properties (Riverhill Energy). **[Fact]** **46,608,796** units were outstanding at March 27, 2026 (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260327_rpt20251231_acc0001193125_26_128561.htm`). **[Fact]** The trust is grantor-trust taxed; unitholders receive K-1s. Proved reserves at December 31, 2025 totaled **19.7 million** barrels of oil equivalent on properties underlying the royalties.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The unit price may embed Permian oil optionality and a corporate-conversion narrative while the filing floor is a depleting distribution stream with Waddell Ranch in excess cost. **[Inference]** Horizon Kinetics holds **12.5%** of units (Schedule 13G in 10-K); SoftVest (**13.3%**) won court approval in May 2026 to ease indenture amendments, raising conversion speculation. **[Inference]** At **~$29** the market pays roughly **2.5×** the SEC standardized-measure reserve value (**~$12** per unit at year-end 2025), so yield-focused buyers may overpay if Waddell stays cost-burdened. **[Inference]** K-1 complexity and depletion keep the name off dividend screens, but that cuts both ways: it can also hide overpricing versus engineer-discounted cash flows.

---

## Executive summary

PBT is a depleting Permian royalty trust: Texas Royalty properties still pay, but Waddell Ranch production costs exceeded gross proceeds in April 2026, so the May distribution carried **no** Waddell proceeds. FY2025 distributable income was **$0.31** per unit (including **$4.5 million** Blackbeard settlement); organic run rate was closer to **$0.21**. At **$28.94** (Stooq, June 3, 2026) the equity-yield-curve base case is about **-17.7%** per year over six years. Normalized cash yield on 2024 distributions (**$0.55**/unit) is only **~1.9%**. Reserve-engineering floor (standardized measure) is **~$12** per unit versus price **~$29**. Dhando is partial (no debt, pass-through). Stance: **watch**.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Waddell Ranch remains in excess cost while operator capex stays elevated, compressing distributions as reserves deplete.

- **Depletion:** Proved BOE **19.7M**; trust terminates when royalties end and assets are sold
- **Operator risk:** Blackbeard controls Waddell development and cost reporting (quarterly lag)
- **Governance/tax:** SoftVest-driven indenture changes may lead to entity conversion with K-1 tax uncertainty
- **Lens failure mode:** Treating monthly distributions as safe yield without excess-cost and settlement adjustments

**Inversion:** What breaks the thesis? Sustained sub-SEC commodity prices plus perpetual Waddell excess cost drive distributions toward zero while unit price mean-reverts toward standardized measure (~$12).

2. **Base payoff** = **$9.00** per unit cumulative distributions plus terminal over **6** years.  
3. Total return ratio = 9.00 ÷ 28.94 = **0.311**.
