## What this business is

Singapore Exchange Limited (SGX Group) operates Asia's leading multi-asset market infrastructure: cash equities, equity derivatives, fixed income, currencies and commodities, plus post-trade, data, and index services. **[Fact]** The group earns transaction, clearing, listing, and data fees on handled volume; it does not take directional trading risk on core operations. **[Fact]** FY2025 (year ended 30 June 2025) net revenue was **S$1,298.2 million** (+11.7% year on year), adjusted net profit attributable to shareholders **S$609.5 million**, and adjusted earnings per share **57.0 cents** (IR FY2025 results summary; see Primary sources). **[Fact]** At **S$22.25** per share (Yahoo Finance, 2026-06-11), the stock trades near **43 times** our **S$0.52** normalized owner-cash starting point.

## Why the market might be wrong

There is no dated structural discount. **[Inference]** Investors may capitalize SGX's multi-asset growth (OTC FX, currency derivatives, cash equities rebound) and **57%** operating margins as if mid-teens revenue growth and today's earnings multiple persist without competition or fee pressure. **[Inference]** Our base return uses normalized owner cash below spot adjusted earnings, not a forward run rate from H1 FY2026 until the FY2025 annual PDF is fully extracted on disk. Return is franchise earnings power at price, not a timed mispricing with a known catalyst date.

We propose **watch** and would revisit nearer **S$14–15** where Lawrence base case approaches a low-teens annual return **[Assumption]**.
