## What this business is

Ameren Corporation is a regulated electric and natural gas utility serving roughly 2.5 million customers in Missouri and Illinois through three reportable segments: Ameren Missouri, Ameren Illinois, and Ameren Transmission. **[Fact]** Consolidated operating revenues were **$8.8 billion** in fiscal 2025 (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260218_rpt20251231_acc0001002910_26_000009.htm`). **[Fact]** The company earns allowed returns set by state and federal regulators on invested capital (rate base), not through open-market pricing. **[Fact]** Ameren Missouri contributed about **52%** of 2025 operating revenues (electric), Ameren Illinois electric distribution about **26%**, with natural gas, transmission, and other lines making up the balance. **[Fact]** Management is executing a **$30.5 billion to $33.1 billion** capital plan from 2026 through 2030 to modernize grids, add renewables, and serve rising load including data-center demand.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

Utility investors often treat Ameren as a bond proxy: safe dividend, modest growth, fully priced. **[Inference]** That framing understates the **rate-base growth engine** embedded in the five-year capex plan and Missouri PISA recovery, which can compound earnings faster than the current yield alone implies. **[Inference]** Conversely, the market may be **under-discounting dilution and regulatory lag**: roughly **$4 billion** of equity issuance is planned through 2030, and Illinois MYRP appeals create timing risk on allowed returns. **[Opinion]** There is no obvious mispricing edge at today's price; the stock looks fairly valued for a quality regulated compounder, which is why Marvin lands below our **~15%** return hurdle.

## Executive summary

Ameren is a Midwest regulated utility investing heavily in transmission, distribution, and renewables across Missouri and Illinois. FY2025 diluted earnings were **$5.35** per share on **$8.8 billion** of revenue, and the board raised the annual dividend to **$3.00** per share in February 2026. At today's price, Marvin's base case implies about **7% per year** over ten years on normalized regulated earnings power, below our **~15%** target. The moat is **narrow** (geographic monopoly plus regulation), and dhando is **partial**: allowed-return frameworks cap catastrophic downside, but equity issuance and political affordability risk bound the upside. **Stance: watch** for a cheaper entry or clearer regulatory wins on Illinois MYRP appeals. ## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Regulatory and political pushback slows capex recovery or forces equity-heavy financing, diluting per-share growth below the capital plan.

**Inversion checklist:**

- If Illinois MYRP appeals reduce allowed returns or capital trackers, Illinois earnings stall.
- If Missouri affordability politics cap ROE or disallow PISA investments, Missouri growth slows.
- If credit markets widen, the **$4 billion** equity plan may not offset leverage, pressuring ratings.

**Lens failure mode (operating lens):** Thesis fails if normalized earnings power stalls near **$5** per share while the stock stays at a premium multiple, delivering utility-bond returns without the expected rate-base compounding.

## Terms

- **Allowed ROE:** Return on equity permitted by regulators, not necessarily earned ROE.
