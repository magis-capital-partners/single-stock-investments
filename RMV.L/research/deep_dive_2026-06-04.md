## What this business is
Rightmove plc runs the dominant UK online property portal. **[Fact]** Estate agents and new-home developers pay subscription and product fees to advertise listings; home movers use the site for search, valuations, and research. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **£425.1 million**, up **9%**, with **underlying operating profit** of **£297.7 million** (70% margin). **[Fact]** The platform is asset-light: **£308.0 million** cash generated from operating activities on roughly **772 million** average shares. **[Fact]** Strategic growth areas (commercial property, mortgages, rental services) grew combined revenue **25%** year over year while the core agency membership base grew **1%**.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The market may be anchoring on **FY2026 guidance** for only **3–5%** underlying operating profit growth while the company accelerates AI and product investment. **[Inference]** That near-term margin pause can obscure a toll-booth model with **~89%** consumer time share on property portals (Comscore, cited in FY2025 results) and **ARPA** still rising (**6%** on core packages in FY2025). **[Inference]** At **443 pence** per share (LSE, 4 June 2026), owner cash yields roughly **9%** before growth, so the equity can look “fully priced” on earnings multiples yet still clear a mid-teens annual return on filing-based cash per share if buybacks and product mix persist.

---

## Executive summary
Rightmove is the UK’s default property search utility: high margins, net cash, and no external debt. FY2025 cash generation supports roughly **40 pence** of owner cash per share; the share price near **£4.43** embeds a high single-digit cash yield plus growth from ARPA, adjacent products, and buybacks.
