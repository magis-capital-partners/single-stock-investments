## What this business is

Keweenaw Land Association owns mineral rights on more than one million net acres in Michigan's Upper Peninsula. **[Fact]** Revenue today is mineral lease income, primarily from Highland Copper's Copperwood project leases (~$349,700 in FY2024). **[Fact]** The company is not an operator; it collects rent and royalties if lessees develop mines. **[Fact]** Book value was about $14.3 million (~$12.70 per share) at December 2024 with roughly $4 million in cash and treasuries against ~$160,000 per year operating burn. **[Fact]** In June 2024 management repurchased 51,633 shares at $27 and acquired 667,000 additional acres at roughly $1.52 per acre. The stock trades OTC with very thin volume.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The market may either ignore Copperwood production royalties priced at zero in lease-only cash flow, or overpay after a move from management's $27 repurchase to much higher OTC quotes. **[Inference]** At $55 (Stooq, May 22, 2026), roughly 4.3x book, the dormant asset (Copperwood royalties) is partially in the price. **[Inference]** OTC illiquidity and CEO-prepared statements keep the acreage option opaque to generalists. Horizon Kinetics / SSI framing: base case assumes **zero** production royalties; bull sizes Copperwood at ~$7.7M per year at $4/lb copper.

---

## Executive summary

Keweenaw Land is a Michigan mineral estate with a Copperwood free option on top of modest lease income. FY2024 book value is ~$12.70 per share; burn is ~$160K per year against cash and treasuries. At $55, price sits ~4.3x book, so floor_pass fails and dhando weakens versus the June 2024 $27 repurchase. Optionality overlay: Copperwood option yield ~12.4% (bull) at $4/lb Cu; Lawrence yield-curve base is -8.3% (lease income only). Bear path -17.8% toward book if Copperwood delays. We propose watch: asset math remains interesting but entry no longer clears the 15% bar.
