## What this business is
Keweenaw Land Association owns mineral rights on more than one million net acres in Michigan's Upper Peninsula. **[Fact]** Revenue today is mineral lease income, primarily from Highland Copper's Copperwood project leases (~$349,700 in FY2024). **[Fact]** The company is not an operator; it collects rent and royalties if lessees develop mines. **[Fact]** Book value was about $14.3 million (~$12.70 per share) at December 2024 with roughly $4 million in cash and treasuries against ~$160,000 per year operating burn. **[Fact]** In June 2024 management repurchased 51,633 shares at $27 and acquired 667,000 additional acres at roughly $1.52 per acre. The stock trades OTC with very thin volume.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The market may either ignore Copperwood production royalties priced at zero in lease-only cash flow, or overpay after a move from management's $27 repurchase to much higher OTC quotes. **[Inference]** At $55 (Stooq, May 22, 2026), roughly 4.3x book, the dormant asset (Copperwood royalties) is partially in the price. **[Inference]** OTC illiquidity and CEO-prepared statements keep the acreage option opaque to generalists. SSI framing (May 2024) used **$4/lb copper**; we refresh spot via Stooq COMEX (**~$6.67/lb** as of 2026-06-02) and scale the SSI royalty bridge linearly. Lawrence base still assumes **no production royalties** in operating cash; overlay uses **35% probability** on spot royalty math.

## Executive summary
Keweenaw Land is a Michigan mineral estate with a Copperwood free option on top of modest lease income. We expect **-8.3%** per year on the Lawrence lease-only base at **$55** (stance gate). FY2024 GAAP book is ~$12.70 per share; **economic floor** about **$21 per share**, not GAAP book. Spot copper **~$6.67/lb** (2026-06-02) scales the SSI royalty bridge to ~**$12.8M/yr**; **option yield about 20.7%** at this price. Management expects **cash-flow positive from 2026** at the 2025 annual shareholder meeting **[Management statement]** (unverified vs H1 2025 net loss). **Watch:** price still embeds much of the option despite improved spot Cu.
