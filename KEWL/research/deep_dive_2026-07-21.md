## What this business is
Keweenaw Land Association owns mineral rights on more than 1.3 million gross acres in Michigan's Upper Peninsula and northern Wisconsin. **[Fact]** Revenue today is mineral lease income, primarily from Highland Copper's Copperwood project leases (**$349,700** in FY2024). **[Fact]** The company is not an operator; it collects rent and royalties if lessees develop mines. **[Fact]** Book value was about **$14.3 million** (~**$12.70** per share) at December 2024 with roughly **$4 million** in cash and treasuries against ~**$160,000** per year operating burn. **[Fact]** In June 2024 management repurchased **51,633** shares at **$27** and acquired **667,300** gross mineral acres at roughly **$1.52** per acre. The stock trades OTC with thin volume.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The market may either ignore Copperwood production royalties priced near zero in lease-only cash flow, or overpay after a move from management's **$27** repurchase to much higher OTC quotes. **[Inference]** At **$55**, roughly 4.3× book, the dormant production royalty is partially in the price. **[Inference]** OTC illiquidity and CEO-prepared statements keep the acreage option opaque to generalists. Proof-first component math at base case sums to about **$35.75** per share versus price near **$55**, suggesting the market embeds Copperwood and secondary acreage upside not yet in filing-locked lease cash alone.
