## What this business is
Agilent Technologies is a life-science and chemical-analysis tools company that sells instruments, consumables, software, and lab services to biopharma, academic, diagnostics, and applied-chemical customers. **[Fact]** FY2025 net revenue was **$6.95 billion** on **$1.48 billion** of operating income (~**21%** margin) and **$1.30 billion** of net income (**$4.57** diluted earnings per share). **[Fact]** The company reports three segments after a November 2024 reorganization: **Life Sciences and Diagnostics Markets**, **Agilent CrossLab**, and **Applied Markets**. **[Fact]** Revenue is driven by recurring consumables and CrossLab services on an installed instrument base, plus periodic instrument placements tied to customer capital budgets. **[Inference]** Agilent is a **compounder** in the Danaher/Thermo Fisher peer set, with more exposure to analytical instruments and CDMO services than to full bioprocessing.

## Why the market might be wrong
There is **no clear dated mispricing signal** (predictive attribute: **none**). **[Inference]** The stock may already price a quality franchise at a premium multiple, leaving modest owner-cash yield at today's price. **[Inference]** A **transitory** biopharma funding downturn depressed instrument demand in FY2023–FY2024; H1 FY2026 revenue growth of **8.5%** versus the prior year may signal recovery that the market has only partly credited. Return is **normalized free cash flow per share at price**, not a single catalyst with a payoff date.

## Executive summary
Agilent is a **life-science tools compounder** with a razor-and-blade model across three reorganized segments. FY2025 free cash flow per share is about **$4.04** (cash from operations **$1.56 billion** less capital spending **$407 million**, divided by roughly **285 million** diluted shares). H1 FY2026 shows **$3.63 billion** of revenue (+**8.5%** year over year) and diluted earnings per share of **$2.27** versus **$1.86** in the prior-year period. At about **$134** per share (NYSE close 2026-07-09), our base case implies **4.19% per year** over seven years, below the **15%** hold band. Bear and bull scenarios span **-1.8%** to **7.7%** per year. **Stable** moat on validated workflows and consumables pull-through; **partial** dhando because cyclical end markets and premium valuation cap asymmetry. Marvin proposes **watch**. Primary risk: **multi-year biopharma and academic funding weakness** compressing instrument placements and operating leverage.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** A prolonged biopharma and academic funding downturn reduces instrument placements and operating leverage, forcing margin compression despite consumables stability.

- China and academic budget cuts persist two or more years (invert pillar 2).
- BIOVECTRA integration under-delivers on revenue or margin (acquisition was ~$925M, financed partly with new debt).
- Working-capital build continues to lag reported earnings (H1 FY2026 cash from operations below prior H1).
- Peer price competition from Thermo Fisher or Danaher on large pharma accounts.

**Lens failure mode:** Operating lens fails if consumables recurrence proves less durable than reported (customers delay service contracts or switch platforms after validation expiry).
