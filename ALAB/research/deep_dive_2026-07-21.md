## What this business is
Astera Labs, Inc. (ALAB) designs fabless semiconductor connectivity solutions for cloud and AI infrastructure, including PCIe, CXL, and Ethernet retimers, smart DSPs, and switches. **[Fact]** Shares trade on Nasdaq (ALAB). **[Fact]** FY2025 consolidated revenue was **$852.5 million** (+115% year over year), operating income **$173.4 million**, and net income **$219.1 million** (diluted earnings **$1.22** per share) per `ALAB/investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260220_rpt20251231_acc0001736297_26_000010.htm`. **[Fact]** FY2025 cash was **$167.6 million** plus **$1,021.2 million** in current available-for-sale debt securities against **$31.0 million** of operating lease liabilities; operating cash flow was **$319.3 million**.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock may trade as a must-own AI connectivity winner while customer concentration and normalized owner economics tell a more bounded story. **[Inference]** Revenue grew **115%** to **$853 million**, yet Customer A accounted for **37%** of revenue and the top customer represented **73%** of accounts receivable at year-end. **[Inference]** Net income benefited from a valuation-allowance release; two-year average operating income per share is about **$0.81**, far below the headline earnings pace. **[Inference]** Proof-first component math at base case sums to about **$19** per share of filing-locked economic value versus a price near **$309**, suggesting the market prices design-win scale and AI infrastructure adoption far beyond what conservative components capture today.
