## What this business is
Arch Capital Group Ltd. (NASDAQ: **ACGL**) is a Bermuda-domiciled specialty insurer, reinsurer, and mortgage insurer operating through three segments: Insurance, Reinsurance, and Mortgage. **[Fact]** FY2025 net income available to Arch common shareholders was **$4.4 billion** and book value per share was **$65.11** (up from **$53.11** in FY2024). **[Fact]** After-tax operating income (the company's normalized earnings measure, excluding net realized gains) was **$3.7 billion**, or about **$9.84 per diluted share** on **375.9 million** weighted average diluted shares. **[Fact]** The group wrote **$2.9 billion** of underwriting income across segments in FY2025, supplemented by **$1.6 billion** of net investment income. **[Inference]** Arch is a capital-light compounder in global specialty risk: it earns underwriting margin where expertise creates pricing power, invests float at roughly **4%** pre-tax yield, and returns excess capital through repurchases.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
There is **no classic dated mispricing signal** (HK predictive attribute: **none**). **[Inference]** Generalists may anchor on GAAP net income (**$11.60** diluted earnings per share) that includes realized investment gains and equity-method marks, overstating repeatable owner cash versus after-tax operating income of **$9.84 per share**. **[Fact]** The stock trades near **1.6 times** December 2025 book value (**$65.11**), embedding continued strong reinsurance and mortgage profitability. **[Fact]** Insurance segment combined ratio was **95.2%** in FY2025, near breakeven underwriting, while reinsurance (**80.8%**) and mortgage (**14.6%**) carry the group. **[Inference]** The market may be paying for mid-cycle peak margins in reinsurance and mortgage without fully reserving for property catastrophe clustering, social inflation, or a housing downturn. Return here is **normalized owner cash at price**, not a simple price-to-book trade.

## Executive summary
Arch Capital is a three-engine specialty insurance platform: reinsurance and mortgage insurance produced most of FY2025 underwriting profit, while the insurance segment (expanded by the August 2024 Allianz mid-market acquisition) grows premium base. After-tax operating income was **$3.7 billion** (**$9.84 per share**) in FY2025; book value rose **23%** to **$65.11 per share**.

At about **$102** (Yahoo Finance, 2026-07-20), the proof-first component schedule sums to about **$97 per share** in base economic value, and the Lawrence base case implies about **14.9% per year** on normalized owner cash, just below our fifteen percent accumulate hurdle. The franchise shows a **stable** durable advantage (Munger moat) in specialty underwriting with modest leverage (**$2.7 billion** senior notes), with **partial** asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando): book value growth and repurchases limit downside, but catastrophe and cycle risk cap full dhando. Marvin proposes **watch** until price offers a better return. No approved external sources are in the base case.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Property catastrophe clustering or casualty social inflation forces reserve strengthening and compresses reinsurance and insurance margins simultaneously.

**Inversion:** What would make this fail? Two consecutive years with group combined ratio above **100%**, book value per share declining, and management issuing dilutive equity rather than repurchasing.

**Lens failure mode:** Operating lens assumes normalized after-tax operating income persists; a soft reinsurance market plus mortgage claims spike would break the owner-cash bridge faster than a generic compounder downturn.
