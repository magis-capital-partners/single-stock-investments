## What this business is
DataSection Inc. (3905.T) is a Japanese technology company pivoting from legacy IT services into GPU-backed AI data centers under its Neo Cloud and TAIZA brands. **[Fact]** Customers are cloud and enterprise tenants; revenue comes from colocation, compute rental, hosting, and add-on services tied to installed GPU capacity. **[Fact]** FY2026 guidance (English IR, inventory tier) calls for **¥33,601M** revenue versus **¥2,942M** in FY2025, with parent net profit **¥2,804M** and guided EPS **¥115.57**. **[Fact]** May 7, 2026 disclosure secured **5,080 B300 GPUs** via Compal (~**USD 325M**), with Inzai site operations phased from May to July 2026. Financing mixes customer advances (**¥13.6B**), borrowings (**¥30.6B** planned), and a 23rd stock-acquisition-rights issue (**¥6.7B**).

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The market may be over-penalizing a **transitory problem**: Giga B200 overhang (**577** units, **USD 252M** unpaid) and financing-timing fear ahead of energization, while underweighting a binding hyperscaler-linked ramp if Inzai starts on schedule. **[Inference]** At **¥2,591** (Yahoo, 2026-07-21), the stock trades **below** proof-first component base **¥2,953/sh**, so part of the discount may already reflect execution fear rather than fully priced success. **[Inference]** Conversely, inventory-tier guidance may still embed optimistic utilization; PDF extract failure keeps filing verification open.

---

## Executive summary
DataSection is attempting a step-change from small legacy revenue to hyperscaler-scale AI data-center operations in FY2026. Management has named capacity, capex, and a financing bridge in English IR filings (inventory tier; PDF extract still failed), but durable competitive advantage is unproven and asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) fails on concentrated capex and customer concentration. At **¥2,591** (Yahoo, 2026-07-21), base expected annual return is **13.98%** on normalized **¥90/share** owner earnings (FY2026 guided EPS discounted for ramp risk), with proof-first component base **¥2,953/sh**. Upside requires utilization proof and Giga resolution; downside is financing slip or guide miss, not a franchise floor. We propose **watch** until FY2026 results and FY2027 guidance confirm run-rate margins post depreciation.
