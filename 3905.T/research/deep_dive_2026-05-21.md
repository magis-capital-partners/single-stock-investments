## Executive summary

DataSection is in the middle of a **high-velocity pivot** from legacy AI software/retail-tech services to **AI infrastructure ("Neo Cloud")** — GPU capacity, proprietary cluster software (TAIZA), and owned/operated AI data centres in Japan, Australia, and abroad. FY2025 consolidated revenue was only **¥2.9B**; the revised FY2026 (Mar-26) forecast calls for **¥33.6B** revenue and **¥2.8B** parent net profit — a step-change driven by hyperscaler-linked data centre contracts and GPU server deployments.

**One-line view:** Transformational AI-infrastructure compounder-in-the-making with explosive revenue ramp and strategic GPU/data-centre positioning, but **execution risk** on project timing, capital intensity, and customer concentration.

**Thesis status:** **strengthening** (strategic positioning and revenue inflection) with **execution/valuation risk** (medium-term).

## Business overview

DataSection operates across:

1. **AI Infrastructure (strategic core)** — Neo Cloud: GPU capacity, AI data centre design/operation, TAIZA cluster optimization. Management positions the company as **"Asia's largest Neo Cloud"** within two years of its 2024 reorganization.
2. **Legacy AI / DX services** — Generative AI middleware, retail-tech, South America smart retail (via subsidiary Fupbimx).

### Key operational developments

- **Hyperscaler contracts:** Three large AI data service agreements via partner Now Naw Japan; first project began services **Sep-2025** (`en-ir-20260216001.pdf`, p. 3).
- **GPU procurement:** B200 server purchase (Jul-2025, Giga Computing); additional Rubin-class servers (Dec-2025, Inventec).
- **Data centre delays (Apr-2026 revision):** Japan and Australia phase-1 fit-out largely complete, but **operations commencement postponed** due to materials/equipment delivery delays; depreciation of **¥2.3B** pushed out (`en-ir-20260421001.pdf`).

## Munger lenses

### Circle of competence — borderline

Headline business is explainable in five sentences. Full project economics, partnership structures, and GPU lease/back-to-back arrangements require deeper diligence — **defer sizing until model is built**.

### Moat

- **GPU scarcity / supply chain:** Alliances with Taiwanese server OEMs for NVIDIA GPUs.
- **Software:** TAIZA for large-scale cluster optimization.
- **Customer relationships:** Tier-1 cloud hyperscaler pipeline; high concentration risk (**inference** — exact customer share not disclosed in English materials reviewed).
- **Geographic:** Japan + Asia positioning for sovereign/local AI capacity demand.

### Inversion — what would kill the thesis?

1. **Data centre commissioning slips further** → revenue and EBITDA miss; depreciation cliff when assets go live.
2. **Hyperscaler contract cancellation or repricing** → concentrated revenue at risk.
3. **GPU obsolescence / technology leap** → stranded capex (B200/Rubin cycle risk).
4. **Equity dilution or debt stress** from accelerated buildout.
5. **Governance / related-party complexity** in partnership structures.

### Psychology / incentives

Management narrative emphasizes "second founding" and Neo Cloud leadership — watch for promotional framing vs. audited cash flows. Share-based comp **¥818M** removed from FY2026 forecast after cancellation of 22nd stock acquisition rights (positive for reported earnings, but signals prior incentive complexity).
