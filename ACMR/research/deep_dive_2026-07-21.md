## What this business is
ACM Research, Inc. (ACMR) designs and sells wet cleaning, electroplating, and advanced packaging equipment to semiconductor fabs, with a large China manufacturing and customer base. **[Fact]** Shares trade on Nasdaq (ACMR). **[Fact]** FY2025 consolidated revenue was **$901.3 million**, operating income **$109.4 million**, and net income attributable to common shareholders **$94.1 million** (diluted earnings **$1.37** per share) per `ACMR/investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260302_rpt20251231_acc0001628280_26_013231.htm`. **[Fact]** FY2025 cash and equivalents were **$757.4 million** against total debt of **$214.0 million**; operating cash flow was only **$10.3 million** after a heavy working-capital build, down from **$152.5 million** in FY2024.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock may trade as a secular advanced-packaging winner while near-term cash conversion and margin mix tell a cyclical equipment story. **[Inference]** Revenue grew **15%** year over year to **$901 million**, yet operating income fell **28%** as costs and working capital absorbed growth. **[Inference]** Deferred revenue of **$252 million** at year-end signals a large backlog, but FY2025 operating cash flow of **$10 million** shows the market may overestimate how quickly that backlog converts to distributable owner cash. **[Inference]** Proof-first component math at base case sums to about **$23** per share of filing-locked economic value versus a price near **$83**, suggesting the market prices advanced packaging share gains and semicap recovery beyond what the conservative component schedule captures today.

## Executive summary
ACM Research is a specialized semicap equipment vendor focused on wet clean and electroplating tools for logic and advanced packaging fabs. FY2025 results show top-line momentum (**$901 million** revenue, **+15%** YoY) but compressed margins and a working-capital trough that collapsed operating cash flow to **$10 million**. The business is understandable as a capital-cycle equipment name with a narrow technology niche, but the bear case is only partially bounded at today's multiple.

At about **$82.52** per share, we expect about **-10.33%** per year over seven years on the mechanical synthesis base case. Proof-first component economic value base case is **$22.57** per share versus price **$82.52**, so filing-locked components sit well below the market multiple. Dhando is **partial**. Stance: **watch**.
