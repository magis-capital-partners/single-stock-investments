## What this business is

Cerebras Systems designs and sells wafer-scale AI processors and inference infrastructure. **[Fact]** The company sells on-premises hardware systems and cloud inference services built around its Wafer-Scale Engine 3 chip. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$510.0 million**, up **76%** from **$290.3 million** in FY2024 (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/S-1-A_20260511_acc0001628280_26_033143.htm`). **[Fact]** Hardware was **70%** of revenue (**$358.4 million**); cloud and other services were **30%** (**$151.6 million**). **[Fact]** The company completed its Nasdaq IPO on May 15, 2026 at **$185** per share, selling **34.5 million** Class A shares for **$6.4 billion** gross proceeds (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/8-K_20260515_acc0001628280_26_035605.htm`). **[Fact]** Total Class A and Class B shares outstanding after the offering was approximately **219.7 million**.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The market may treat Cerebras as a scaled AI compounder because GAAP net income turned positive in 2025. **[Inference]** **$237.8 million** of GAAP net income included **$390.7 million** of other income from warrant and forward-contract fair-value marks, not recurring owner cash. **[Inference]** Customer concentration remains extreme: Mohamed bin Zayed University of Artificial Intelligence (MBZUAI) was **62%** of FY2025 revenue and G42 was **24%** (versus **85%** for G42 in 2024). **[Inference]** The stock at **~$220** prices in OpenAI and Amazon Web Services partnerships that are not yet fully reflected in operating cash flow. First-quarter 2026 results are scheduled for June 23, 2026.

---

## Executive summary

Cerebras is a fast-growing AI inference hardware and cloud company that went public in May 2026 after refiling its registration statement with new OpenAI and AWS partnerships. Revenue reached **$510 million** in 2025 with **39%** gross margin, but the company still lost **$145.9 million** from operations and burned cash building inference data-center capacity. GAAP profitability is misleading because warrant accounting drove most of the bottom line. Customer concentration in UAE strategic partners remains the central risk. At **~$220** per share we expect **-2.4% per year** over seven years on our base economic payoff path, below our accumulate hurdle. Moat **unproven**. Dhando **none**. Stance **watch**.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Customer concentration (MBZUAI **62%**, G42 **24%**) plus dependence on OpenAI and AWS partnerships converting to repeatable cash flow.

- OpenAI MRA termination could trigger **$1.0 billion** working-capital loan clawback (S-1/A risk factors)
- Cloud gross margin fell to **39%** from **42%** as data-center costs rose
- Competing GPU ecosystem (NVIDIA) and custom silicon at hyperscalers
- **Lens failure mode:** Partnership announcements do not convert to diversified, positive owner cash

**Inversion:** What would make this work? OpenAI 750MW deploys on schedule, AWS revenue exceeds **20%** of mix, and operating margin turns positive while shares do not dilute further.
