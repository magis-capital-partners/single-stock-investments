## What this business is
Cerebras Systems designs and sells wafer-scale AI processors and runs Cerebras Cloud, a high-speed inference and training service built on its own silicon. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$510.0 million**, up **76%** versus 2024, split between hardware (**$358.4 million**) and cloud services (**$151.6 million**) per the S-1 registration statement (`CBRS/investor-documents/sec-edgar/S-1_20260417_rpt20260417_acc0001628280_26_025762.htm`). **[Fact]** The company completed its Nasdaq IPO on May 14 and May 15, 2026, selling **34.5 million** Class A shares at **$185.00** each (**8-K** May 15, 2026). **[Fact]** In January 2026 Cerebras announced a multi-year agreement with OpenAI valued at more than **$20 billion**, covering deployment of **750 megawatts** of Cerebras compute (S-1 business description).

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The market may treat headline GAAP net income (**$237.8 million** in 2025) as proof of durable owner cash, when **$363.3 million** of that came from a one-time forward-contract extinguishment gain and reported operating cash flow was **negative $10.0 million** (S-1 cash flow statement). **[Inference]** The IPO first-day pop from **$185** to roughly **$311** prices in rapid conversion of the OpenAI master revenue agreement before capacity is built and recognized. **[Inference]** Customer concentration (MBZUAI **62%** and G42 **24%** of FY2025 revenue) means the equity story is a single-customer ramp, not a diversified franchise yet. Predictive attribute: **none** at spot (no dated mispricing catalyst beyond contract execution).
