## What this business is
Indiana Resources Limited (ASX: IDA) is an Australian mineral explorer, formerly IMX Resources, focused on the Gawler Craton in South Australia. The company holds about 5,713 km² of 100%-owned tenements targeting gold, base metals, and rare earths. After settling the Ntaka Hill dispute in Tanzania and receiving settlement cash, the balance sheet was well funded while the listed price traded far below cash per share. Corporate attention in 2025 centered on the Minos gold project on the Lake Labyrinth Shear Zone, with regional REE and titanium work as secondary options.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The market prices IDA near A$0.027 while April 2025 cash implied about A$0.11 per share, so enterprise value at spot was negative. The gap likely reflects legal overhang (Tanzania tax claim, subsidiary distribution dispute), sponsor fatigue after the 2024 special dividend, and no published mineral resource yet on Minos. The predictive attribute is **cash floor plus a dated capital-return catalyst**: if the proposed A$0.05 per share return executes and cash burn stays controlled, the equity stub can re-rate toward partial cash backing without needing a mine.
