## What this business is
Eli Lilly and Company (NYSE: **LLY**) is a global pharmaceutical company focused on cardiometabolic health, oncology, immunology, and neuroscience. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$65.2 billion** (up **44.7%** versus **$45.0 billion** in FY2024). **[Fact]** Cash from operations was **$16.8 billion** and capital spending on property and equipment was **$7.8 billion**, yielding about **$9.98** of owner cash per diluted share. **[Fact]** The tirzepatide franchise (**Mounjaro** for diabetes and **Zepbound** for obesity) is the dominant growth engine: U.S. Mounjaro revenue was **$13.7 billion**, U.S. Zepbound **$13.5 billion**, and international Mounjaro **$9.3 billion** in FY2025. **[Inference]** Lilly is a high-growth pharma compounder investing heavily in manufacturing capacity to meet GLP-1 demand, with quality economics but a valuation that prices much of the visible runway.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
There is **no classic dated mispricing signal** (HK predictive attribute: **none**). **[Inference]** Generalists may anchor on trailing free cash flow (~**$10** per share) without adjusting for a temporary capex super-cycle: FY2025 capital spending rose **55%** to **$7.8 billion** to build injectable and oral capacity. **[Fact]** Q1 2026 revenue was **$19.8 billion** (+**55.5%** year over year) with Mounjaro global **$8.7 billion** and Zepbound **$4.2 billion** in the quarter. **[Fact]** Trulicity U.S. revenue was **$5.4 billion** FY2025, down from prior peaks as patients switch to tirzepatide. **[Inference]** The market may be **right on quality** but **wrong on entry price**: at about **$1,187** per share, investors pay roughly **119 times** normalized owner cash, leaving little margin of safety even if tirzepatide growth persists. Return here is **owner cash at price through the capacity cycle**, not a sum-of-the-parts on pipeline optionality alone.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** **Tirzepatide growth decelerates or margins compress** before the capex cycle converts to normalized free cash flow, leaving investors holding a premium multiple on peak growth expectations.

- **Competition:** Novo Nordisk and oral GLP-1 developers can erode share or force pricing concessions (10-K risk factors).
- **Manufacturing and supply:** Capacity constraints have been a bottleneck; execution risk remains on new site ramp (10-K; capex **$7.8B** FY25).
- **Regulatory pricing:** U.S. Inflation Reduction Act negotiation and international reference pricing can cap net pricing power (10-K).
- **Legacy erosion:** Trulicity U.S. **$5.4B** FY2025 shows how quickly a GLP-1 franchise can fade when superseded.
- **Leverage:** Long-term debt **~$42.5B** is serviceable at current earnings but limits flexibility if growth stalls (10-K balance sheet).

**Inversion:** If we are wrong, Lilly is the winning GLP-1/GIP compounder priced for perfection: you own the best franchise in the largest drug category in history, and normalized owner cash catches up to the multiple over many years.

**Lens failure mode:** Operating lens fails if pipeline readouts (oral GLP-1, retatrutide) become the dominant value driver beyond what Lawrence base growth assumes. Current filings support staying on the operating lens with pipeline upside in bull scenario only.
