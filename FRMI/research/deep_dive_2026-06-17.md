## What this business is

Fermi Inc. (Fermi America) is a Texas-based developer building **Project Matador**, a gigawatt-scale private power and powered-shell artificial intelligence campus in Carson County. **[Fact]** The company elects REIT tax status and expects substantially all future revenue from long-term lease rental income paid by hyperscale tenants once data-center shells are delivered. **[Fact]** As of March 31, 2026, Fermi had **not** commenced revenue-generating activities; the quarter reflected site development, equipment procurement, and commercial marketing. **[Fact]** The campus is planned for roughly **11 gigawatts** of on-demand generation (expandable toward **17 gigawatts**) and about **15 million square feet** of AI-ready space, using on-site gas, solar, battery storage, and longer-dated nuclear baseload. **[Fact]** The company was formed in January 2025 and listed on Nasdaq in September 2025.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The market may treat Fermi either as a failed tenant story after the September 2025 non-binding letter of intent expired, or as a speculative nuclear developer, when the filing picture is closer to a pre-lease infrastructure REIT with **$1.4 billion** of capitalized project assets and permitted gas capacity already on the balance sheet. **[Inference]** Governance noise (CEO termination, proxy contest, securities litigation) can obscure that vertical construction is explicitly gated on a definitive tenant lease, not on speculative science-project timelines. **[Inference]** If hyperscaler power scarcity persists, the predictive attribute is a **dated catalyst**: first powered-shell commercial operations targeted for **2027** once a binding lease triggers vertical build.

---

## Executive summary

Fermi is a pre-revenue powered-shell REIT assembling one of the largest permitted private-power campuses in the United States. The balance sheet already holds about **$1.43 billion** of project equipment and site assets, funded partly by **$421 million** of debt, against **$243 million** of cash and restricted cash and roughly **638 million** shares outstanding. There is no signed tenant lease in the March quarter filing, and first-quarter general and administrative expense was **$166 million**, driven largely by share-based compensation. Governance transition and litigation add execution risk. At about **$5.00** per share (pre live refresh), our base dated-payoff path implies roughly **7% per year** over seven years, below our accumulate hurdle. Moat **unproven**. Dhando **partial** at best. Stance **watch**.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** No binding hyperscaler lease before liquidity and covenant milestones force dilution or asset sales.

- **Governance:** Former CEO proxy contest and securities class action divert management and capital (10-Q Items 1A, 8).
- **Liquidity:** Going-concern language raised and mitigated by Yorkville note and capex sequencing; not risk-free (10-Q Note 2).
- **Execution:** **$192 million** unconditional purchase obligations; MPS unit pick-up begins **2027** regardless of tenant status.
- **Lens failure mode:** Treating pre-lease PP&E as run-rate REIT earnings would overstate owner cash; Lawrence path stays pre-revenue until rent starts.

**Inversion:** What kills the thesis? Tenant walks, equipment becomes stranded, or dilutive financing at distressed prices with governance still contested.
