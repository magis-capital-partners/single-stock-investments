## What this business is

Accenture is the world's largest professional-services firm by revenue, helping Global 2000 companies and governments with strategy, technology integration, and managed operations. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$69.7 billion** (+7% local currency), split roughly half consulting and half managed services. **[Fact]** The company employs about **779,000** people and serves **9,000+** clients, including most of the Fortune Global 100. **[Fact]** Cash generation is strong: operating cash flow **$11.5 billion** minus capital spending **$0.6 billion** in FY2025. **[Inference]** Accenture earns by deploying skilled labor and proprietary methods at scale, with increasing mix shift toward recurring managed-services contracts.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The stock trades at a depressed multiple versus historical quality-compounder levels while filing cash flow remains robust. **[Inference]** The market may be overweighting **generative AI disruption** (clients need less consulting labor) and underweighting Accenture's **$3 billion Gen AI investment** and reinvention positioning. **[Fact]** FY2025 bookings were **$80.6 billion**, down 1% in local currency, but Q2 FY2026 bookings rebounded to **$22.1 billion** (+1% local currency). **[Inference]** No dated HK predictive attribute exists; the edge is **quality franchise at a trough free-cash-flow yield** (~12% on FY2025 owner cash), not a catalyst with a known payoff date.

---

## Executive summary

Accenture is a global IT services compounder with durable scale, a managed-services mix shift, and heavy capital return (**$8.3 billion** returned in FY2025). FY2025 free cash flow per share is about **$17.19**; H1 FY2026 revenue grew **7%** with Q2 bookings stabilizing. At **~$139** (July 2026), Lawrence synthesis implies **26.6%** per year over 7 years on filing-based owner cash. Moat is **stable** (brand, client relationships, delivery scale) but not wide; **partial** dhando because AI could compress future margins even at today's price. Marvin proposes **accumulate** on math; **[HUMAN REVIEW]** whether FY2025 cash flow is peak-cycle before AI labor substitution.

Accenture operates a **platform-scale services franchise**: global footprint, deep hyperscaler partnerships, and cross-industry reinvention capabilities (10-K FY2025 Item 1).

### Mental models in plain English

**Moat durability (Munger):** Does scale protect the franchise? **Partially yes.** Long client relationships (195 of top 200 clients over 10 years per 10-K) and switching costs on multi-year transformations create stickiness, but the business is people-intensive and faces talent competition.

**Moat trajectory (Munger):** Is the advantage widening? **Stable to mixed.** Managed services grew 9% versus consulting 6% in FY2025 (mix improvement), but generative AI could commoditize portions of consulting work over time.

**Deny compounding to others (Stahl):** Can peers replicate? **Partially.** IBM Consulting, Indian IT firms (Infosys, TCS), and Big Four advisory compete on price; Accenture's breadth and brand support premium positioning but not monopoly economics.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** **Generative AI reduces billable hours** faster than Accenture captures new reinvention revenue, compressing margins and free cash flow per share from the FY2025 base.

- **Bookings stagnation:** FY2025 bookings −1% local currency; one quarter of stabilization is not a trend.
- **People business cyclicality:** Enterprise IT budget cuts in recession reduce consulting faster than managed services offset.
- **Business optimization:** Restructuring charges signal management sees efficiency pressure; repeated rounds would erode culture and talent.

**Inversion:** If we are wrong, Accenture is a **high-quality cash compounder mispriced for permanent impairment** while actually gaining AI-era share: you earn mid-20s annual returns as cash flow grows modestly and the market re-rates toward historical multiples.
