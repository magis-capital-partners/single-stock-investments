## What this business is
The Williams Companies owns and operates one of the largest U.S. natural gas infrastructure networks. **[Fact]** Williams gathers, processes, transports, and stores natural gas and natural gas liquids across **24 states** and the Gulf of America, with interests in **more than 32,000 miles** of pipelines, **35** processing plants, **9** NGL fractionators, **423 billion cubic feet** of gas storage, and roughly **23 million barrels** of NGL storage (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260224_rpt20251231_acc0000107263_26_000006.htm`). **[Fact]** The company serves about **800 customers** and earns primarily from fee-based transportation, gathering, and processing contracts rather than commodity price speculation. **[Fact]** Consolidated revenues were **$11.95 billion** in fiscal 2025, up from **$10.50 billion** in 2024. **[Fact]** The Transcontinental Gas Pipe Line (Transco) system connects prolific supply basins to the fastest-growing U.S. demand markets, including the Southeast, Gulf Coast, and LNG export corridors.

## Why the market might be wrong
Income investors often bucket Williams with bond-proxy midstream names: safe dividend, leverage, fully priced. **[Inference]** That framing can understate the **contracted growth backlog** tied to LNG, power generation, and data-center load, especially the Power Innovation projects (1.9 gigawatts in Ohio and Utah under long fixed-price agreements). **[Inference]** Conversely, the market may be **under-discounting leverage and capex execution risk**: long-term debt was **$27.3 billion** at year-end 2025, 2026 growth capital is guided at **$6.1 billion to $6.7 billion**, and interest expense was **$1.44 billion** in 2025. **[Opinion]** There is no obvious dated mispricing edge at today's price; the stock looks fairly valued for a quality midstream toll road, which is why Marvin lands below our **~15%** return hurdle despite solid operating momentum.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Leverage and growth-capex execution: if 2026 spending of **$6.1-6.7 billion** does not convert to contracted cash flows on time, per-share owner cash growth stalls while interest and maintenance burdens persist.

**Inversion checklist:**

- If FERC delays Transco or related expansions, backlog conversion slows and returns on invested capital fall.
- If data-center power demand shifts away from onsite gas generation, Power Innovation returns undershoot plan.
- If credit markets widen, refinancing **$1.3 billion** near-term maturities plus ongoing growth funding pressures equity.

**Lens failure mode (operating lens):** Thesis fails if normalized owner cash stalls near **$3** per share while the stock stays at a midstream premium multiple, delivering bond-like returns without the expected contracted-volume compounding.
