## What this business is
Automatic Data Processing is the largest payroll and human-capital-management processor in the United States, serving employers from small businesses to Fortune 500 companies. **[Fact]** FY2025 service revenue was **$20.6 billion** (+7% versus prior year), excluding **$75.2 billion** of PEO pass-through costs that flow through the balance sheet but are not owner cash. **[Fact]** The company processed payroll for tens of millions of workers globally and generated operating cash flow of **$4.9 billion** minus capital spending of **$0.17 billion** in FY2025. **[Fact]** Two reportable segments dominate: **Employer Services** (payroll, tax filing, time and benefits) and **PEO Services** (co-employment for mid-market clients). **[Inference]** ADP earns recurring fees per worksite employee plus interest spreads on client funds held between collection and disbursement.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock trades at a premium multiple typical of quality compounders while the market may underweight ADP's **client-fund interest spread** and **PEO mix shift**. **[Fact]** Interest on funds held for clients contributed **$489 million** of FY2025 segment revenue; rate cuts compress this high-margin line faster than payroll fees grow. **[Inference]** Conversely, the market may overweight **Paychex and cloud HCM competition** while underweighting payroll switching costs and ADP's compliance moat. **[Fact]** H1 FY2026 service revenue grew **6%** with diluted EPS up **9%**, suggesting retention remains solid. **[Inference]** No dated HK predictive attribute exists; the edge is **durable franchise at a fair price**, not a catalyst with a known payoff date.

---

## Executive summary
ADP is a payroll and HCM compounder with sticky recurring revenue, strong cash conversion, and heavy capital return (**$3.7 billion** returned in FY2025 via dividends and buybacks). FY2025 free cash flow per share is about **$11.67**; at **~$255** (July 2026), total synthesis implies **12.0%** per year over 7 years on filing-based owner cash. Moat is **stable** (switching costs, compliance scale) but not wide; **partial** dhando because net debt (~**$13** per share) and client-fund rate sensitivity cap the bear case. Marvin proposes **watch** on math (below ~15% accumulate hurdle); **[HUMAN REVIEW]** whether FY2025 client-fund spreads are peak-cycle.

ADP operates a **payroll toll booth**: employers pay recurring fees for tax compliance, direct deposit, and HR software; ADP holds client funds briefly and earns interest spreads (10-K FY2025 Item 1).

### Mental models in plain English

**Moat durability (Munger):** Does scale protect the franchise? **Partially yes.** Tax compliance complexity, multi-state payroll rules, and employee record history create switching costs. Clients rarely change payroll vendors mid-year.

**Moat trajectory (Munger):** Is the advantage widening? **Stable.** PEO co-employment grows faster than core payroll, but cloud HCM vendors compete for new logos.

**Deny compounding to others (Stahl):** Can peers replicate? **Partially.** Paychex matches mid-market; Workday/UKG compete on HCM; neither fully replicates ADP's global compliance scale at equal cost.

**Bounded bear (Pabrai):** Filing-locked FCF (~**$11.67/sh**) and net debt (~**$13/sh**) cap downside versus speculative growth stories, but client-fund spreads are not a hard floor if rates fall sharply.

**Time arbitrage (Horizon Kinetics):** No dated predictive attribute; payroll franchises re-rate slowly. Patience matters only if owner cash grows through cycles.

### Thesis pillars

1. **Recurring payroll toll:** Service revenue **$20.56B** FY2025 (+7%) from fees per worksite employee and tax-compliance subscriptions; retention driven by switching costs.
2. **Client-fund spread engine:** Interest on funds held for clients **$489M** FY2025 adds high-margin revenue tied to rate environment (10-K segment note).
3. **PEO mix shift:** PEO services revenue **$1.53B** FY2025 grows faster than core payroll; co-employment expands wallet share with mid-market clients.
4. **Capital return discipline:** **$3.68B** returned FY2025 (**$2.40B** dividends, **$1.28B** buybacks); diluted shares **408.7M** down from **415.7M** three years prior.
