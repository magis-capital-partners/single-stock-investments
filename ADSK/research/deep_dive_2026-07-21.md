## What this business is
Autodesk makes design and engineering software for architects, builders, manufacturers, and media creators. **[Fact]** FY2026 revenue was **$6.74 billion**, up **18%** year over year from **$5.72 billion** in FY2025. **[Fact]** The company has largely completed its shift from perpetual licenses to **subscription and maintenance** revenue. **[Fact]** FY2026 operating cash flow was **$2.45 billion** minus capital spending of **$43 million**, yielding free cash flow of about **$2.41 billion** on **215 million** diluted shares (**~$11.20** per share). **[Fact]** Remaining performance obligation (contract backlog) stood at **$8.3 billion** at January 31, 2026. **[Inference]** Autodesk earns by selling sticky, workflow-embedded tools (AutoCAD, Revit, Fusion, Civil 3D) where file formats, training, and project libraries create switching costs.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock trades near **20 times** FY2026 free cash flow per share while revenue grew **18%** and subscription transition risks have faded. **[Inference]** The market may be overweighting **construction-cycle slowdown** and **open-source competition** while underweighting **RPO conversion** and **construction cloud upsell**. **[Fact]** Starboard Value filed activist proxy materials in March 2025 seeking board engagement on margins and capital allocation (context tier only; not in base IRR). **[Inference]** No dated Horizon Kinetics predictive attribute exists; the edge is **quality subscription compounder at a full but not extreme multiple**, not a catalyst with a known payoff date.

### Mental models in plain English

**Moat durability (Munger):** Does scale protect the franchise? **Partially yes.** Switching costs from file libraries, training, and project workflows are real, but open-source (FreeCAD, Blender) and lower-cost competitors (Bentley, Trimble) exist in niches.

**Moat trajectory (Munger):** Is the advantage widening? **Stable to mixed.** Construction cloud and Forma investments aim to deepen the data platform moat; AI-assisted design could help incumbents or enable new entrants.

**Incentive alignment (Munger):** Does management get paid for owner outcomes? **Mixed.** FY2026 buybacks were **$1.4 billion** but stock-based compensation was **$788 million** (10-K). Proxy ties pay to revenue, EPS, and TSR; Starboard activist materials flag governance as a human review item.

**Circle of competence (Munger):** Can we explain the business simply? **Yes.** Subscription design software for buildings and products; revenue recurs as long as professionals keep projects on Autodesk file formats.

**Deny compounding to others (Stahl):** Can peers replicate? **Partially.** Dassault Systèmes and PTC compete in PLM/manufacturing; Autodesk's AEC dominance is harder to replicate but not impossible over decades.

**Croupier vs operator (Stahl):** Is this a toll-road or a capital recycler? **Operator compounder.** Autodesk reinvests in cloud platform R&D and returns excess cash via buybacks rather than collecting passive tolls.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** **Stock-based compensation and competition compress free cash flow per share** faster than revenue growth, leaving shareholders with modest returns despite a quality franchise.

- **Construction cycle:** AEC revenue tied to building starts; deep recession would slow billings and RPO growth.
- **Open-source / low-cost rivals:** FreeCAD and cloud-native tools attack SMB and education segments.
- **Activist overhang:** Starboard campaign may distract management or force short-term margin cuts that hurt R&D.
- **AI disruption:** Generative design could reduce seat counts or ARPU if clients need fewer human designers.

**Inversion:** If we are wrong, Autodesk is a **mispriced platform compounder** where subscription transition completion and construction cloud monetization drive double-digit FCF growth: you earn low-teens annual returns as cash flow compounds and the market re-rates toward software-peer multiples.
