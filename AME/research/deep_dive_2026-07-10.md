## What this business is

AMETEK manufactures niche electronic instruments and precision electromechanical components sold into process, power, industrial, aerospace, defense, and medical end markets. **[Fact]** FY2025 net sales were a record **$7.40 billion** (+6.6% versus 2024) split between Electronic Instruments Group (**$4.92 billion**) and Electromechanical Group (**$2.48 billion**). **[Fact]** The company converts earnings to cash efficiently: FY2025 free cash flow was **$1.67 billion** on **$1.48 billion** net income. Growth is driven by the **AMETEK Growth Model**: operational excellence, strategic acquisitions, global expansion, and new product development.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

We do not see a dated mispricing catalyst at today's price. **[Inference]** The stock trades at a scarcity premium for a proven serial acquirer with record backlog (**$3.58 billion** at year-end 2025) and mid-20s operating margins. **[Fact]** Q1 2026 sales rose **11%** year over year (roughly **5% organic**, **4% acquisitions**, **2% foreign exchange). The market may be right that quality persists; our work says the **expected annual return at ~$233** is below our ~15% hurdle even if the franchise compounds.

### Mental models in plain English

**Moat durability (Munger):** Niche leadership, specification lock-in, and integration capability across **15 acquisitions since 2021** (~**$1.8 billion** annualized sales) support a durable franchise. R&D spend was **$236 million** in FY2025.

**Deny compounding to others (Stahl):** Fragmented instrument markets and engineering depth make full replication hard; scale in sourcing and M&A integration is the moat extension mechanism.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** **Premium multiple compression** if acquisition pace slows or industrial demand softens while the market still prices perfection.

- **Integration risk** on larger deals (FARO scale) could linger in margins longer than guided.  
- **End-market cyclicality** in aerospace and automation could cut organic growth below mid-single digits.  
- **Capital allocation crowding:** heavy M&A plus buybacks may limit flexibility in a downturn.

**Inversion:** If we are wrong, AMETEK keeps compounding at high-single-digit owner-cash growth and the scarcity premium persists, delivering total returns closer to bull (**~8%** annual) than our base case.

**Lens failure mode:** Operating lens assumes filed free cash flow is the right owner metric; a prolonged acquisition spree funded by debt at rising rates would require revisiting leverage and normalization.
