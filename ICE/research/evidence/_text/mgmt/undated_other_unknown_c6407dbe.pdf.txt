innovation of his was around seeing the value of clearing and particularly clearing and difficult to clear markets 
like the U.S. power market. 
 
You all may recall Enron, when it blew up, it was everybody to one market. There was very little confidence that 
anybody had post that Enron explosion with regards to who their counterparties were. Jeff understood how those 
markets worked and worked to develop a clearing solution, so that those counterparties didn't have to deal with 
each other's balance sheet risk as clearinghouses able to step in and mitigate that risk. And that really allowed 
power trading to return to the level it had prior to the Enron collapse and it was a better market because it was 
more transparent and it was all to all as opposed to all to one. 
 
Another innovation in the market more recently was you guys will remember the financial crisis of 2008. A lot of 
people were worried about a thing nobody heard of called CDS. And the biggest scary part about CDS was that 
actually nobody knew who the exposures were with. Nobody knew how big their exposure was to which 
counterparty. 
 
And so we looked at that as a problem that clearing again could solve because clearing fundamentally matches 
positions, it nets positions down, so if there are many of us that have positions that are offsetting among multiple 
counterparties, those positions could be netted down. So we stepped in and offered a clearing solution for CDS. 
Today, we have well over 90% of all the cleared CDS flows through either ICE Clear Credit or ICE Clear Europe, 
which are two CDS clearing houses. And again, it made the markets better and it allowed people to continue to 
use a risk management tool because it introduced the transparency and the collateralization  of the risks that were 
necessary to bring confidence back into those markets. So those are a couple of examples of what we do, which 
is we look for problems that our customers have and we innovate come up with innovative ways to solve them.
