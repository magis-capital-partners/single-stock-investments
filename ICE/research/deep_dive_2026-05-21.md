## Executive summary

Intercontinental Exchange is a **canonical Stahl croupier**: global exchange, clearing, data, and mortgage-technology infrastructure with minimal principal risk on core operations. FY2025 was the **20th consecutive year of record net revenues** ($9.9B, +7% YoY); Q1 2026 accelerated to **record net revenues of $3.0B** (+20% YoY) with exchange segment margins near **80%**.

**One-line view:** Diversified financial-market toll booth (energy/financial futures, NYSE, fixed income data, mortgage tech) with extraordinary exchange economics and volume leverage — but mortgage-tech GAAP margins remain thin and M&A amortization obscures cash economics.

**Thesis status:** **strengthening**

## Munger lenses

### Circle of competence — pass

ICE is understandable: regulated market infrastructure + SaaS-like mortgage/data layers. Segment economics are disclosed clearly.

### Moat

- **Network / regulatory:** NYSE, ICE Futures, clearing houses — high switching costs for members
- **Data & workflow:** Fixed income analytics + mortgage origination/servicing rails (stickier than pure trading)
- **Scale:** Record volumes in Q1 2026 macro uncertainty — customers " relied on mission-critical markets" (CEO quote)

### Inversion — what would kill the thesis?

1. **Prolonged low volatility** across energy + rates → transaction revenue compression
2. **Regulatory fee caps** or forced interoperability on US equity markets
3. **Mortgage origination slump** persisting → mortgage tech growth stalls; integration costs linger
4. **Competitive share loss** in energy/financial futures (must track **handled volume**, not share alone — Stahl)
5. **Leverage + large M&A** — $20B+ debt; goodwill/intangibles ~$46B

### Psychology / incentives

CEO Jeff Sprecher long tenure; capital return ($848M to stockholders in Q1 2026). Watch incentive alignment on large mortgage-tech integration vs exchange core.
