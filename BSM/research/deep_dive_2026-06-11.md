## What this business is

Black Stone Minerals, L.P. (NYSE: BSM) is a publicly traded Delaware limited partnership that owns oil and natural gas **mineral and royalty interests** across 41 U.S. states, plus smaller **non-operated working interests**. **[Fact]** The partnership receives revenue when third-party operators produce hydrocarbons from its acreage; it does not operate wells. **[Fact]** As of February 20, 2026, **212,333,793** common units and **14,711,219** Series B cumulative convertible preferred units were outstanding (`10-K_20260224_rpt20251231_acc0001621434_26_000018.htm`). **[Fact]** FY2025 total revenue was **$469.9 million**; net income was **$299.9 million** (**$1.28** per common unit basic); cash distributions to common unitholders were **$285.7 million** (**~$1.35** per unit on a weighted-average basis). **[Fact]** The asset base spans **20.3 million** net mineral and royalty acres (**2.6 million** developed, **17.7 million** undeveloped) and **527,366** net working-interest acres as of December 31, 2025 (`same 10-K`, Acreage tables).

---

## Why the market might be wrong

Yield-focused investors may treat BSM as a simple commodity distribution bond and underweight the **17.7 million net undeveloped mineral acres** that require no capital from the partnership when third parties drill. **[Inference]** K-1 partnership tax reporting and a large unit count (~212 million) can exclude the name from dividend screens despite a **~9.7%** trailing distribution yield at **~$13.88** per unit. **[Inference]** FY2025 revenue rose **8.4%** year over year even as oil volumes fell **9.6%**, because natural gas realizations improved and lease bonus income rose **71%**; the market may anchor on peak 2023 distributions (**~$1.90**/unit) rather than the gas-weighted mix today. **[Inference]** Horizon Kinetics held **~151,238** units (~**$2.3 million** 13F value) as of Q1 2026 per the royalty screen; that is **context only**, not an approved third-party IRR input.

## Executive summary

Black Stone Minerals is one of the largest publicly traded mineral-and-royalty partnerships in the U.S.: passive toll on operator production across major basins, with a massive undeveloped acreage inventory and modest working-interest participation. FY2025 distributions were **~$1.35** per common unit on **$470 million** of revenue; Q1 2026 declared **$0.30** per unit (**$63.7 million** paid), down from **$0.375** in Q1 2025. At **~$13.88** per unit and **$1.35** starting owner cash, Marvin's Lawrence base case is **9.4%** per year over seven years, a **watch** stance below our **~15%** bar. Dhando is **partial**: no operating leverage on most royalty cash, but commodity prices, operator concentration (~**13%** Pioneer/XTO revenues), and revolver borrowings (**~$154 million** outstanding at year-end 2025) matter. The undeveloped acreage option is real but not in the Lawrence base path. No approved third-party sources are in base IRR.

### Mental models in plain English

**Permutations & combinations (Stahl):** Cash can rise from commodity price, volumes on existing royalties, lease bonus payments, or conversion of undeveloped acres when operators drill. **[Fact]** FY2025 lease bonus and other income was **$21.4 million**, up **71%** year over year (`10-K` Results of Operations).

**Low risk, high uncertainty (Munger):** Mineral and royalty interests are largely non-cost-bearing; working-interest participation adds capital calls. **[Fact]** Credit facility borrowing base was **$580 million** with **~$154 million** drawn at December 31, 2025 (`10-K` Note on Credit Facility).

**Inversion (Munger):** Thesis fails if distributions fall sustainably below **$1.00**/unit while the partnership funds working-interest capex and acquisitions with debt and unit issuance without per-unit cash follow-through.

**Market structure (HK):** K-1 reporting and MLP structure can persistently misprice large mineral portfolios relative to private-market acreage transactions. **[Inference]** Predictive attribute: **market structure discount** plus **dormant_asset** (undeveloped acres).

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Oil and natural gas price declines reduce revenues, distributable cash flow, and quarterly distributions (`10-K` risk factors).

- **Production decline:** FY2025 oil volumes **-9.6%** and gas **-10.7%** YoY; without replacement drilling, royalty cash falls (`10-K`).
- **Operator dependence:** BSM does not control drilling timing or capital on mineral interests (`10-K`).
- **Working-interest capital:** Acquisitions and WI participation require capital (**~$107M** FY2025 property acquisitions) (`10-K` cash flow).
- **Credit facility:** **~$154M** outstanding; covenant restricts distributions if availability falls below **10%** of borrowing base (`10-K`).
- **Preferred priority:** Series B preferred units (**14.7M**) receive distributions before common (`10-K` Note 12).
- **Derivative volatility:** Mark-to-market commodity hedges distort GAAP income (Q1 2026 **$(64.6)M** derivative loss) (`10-Q`).

**Lens failure mode (asset):** If the market correctly prices BSM as a declining-yield commodity pass-through with no undeveloped-acreage uplift, total return equals distribution yield minus price erosion; the **17.7M** acre option never converts.
