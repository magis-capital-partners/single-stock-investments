## What this business is
Tejon Ranch Co. owns and manages roughly **270,000** contiguous acres in California between Los Angeles and Bakersfield. **[Fact]** The company runs five reporting segments: commercial/industrial real estate, resort/residential (entitled land, pre-revenue in recent periods), mineral resources, farming, and ranch operations. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$49.6 million** (+18% versus **$41.9 million** in FY2024); net income **$0.08 million**; cash from operations **$6.1 million** (down from **$14.3 million** in FY2024); stockholders' equity **$475.2 million** (**~$17.65** per share on **26.9 million** shares). **[Fact]** The stock traded at **~$18.84** on June 3, 2026 (Stooq), near GAAP book. This is a land and water bank with operating tolls, not a commodity producer.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The market may treat TRC as a sleepy farmland REIT at book value, or as a discounted call on Centennial/Grapevine homebuilding without a timeline. **[Inference]** At **~$18.84**, price roughly equals GAAP book while **~270,000** acres and long-dated entitlements are carried at historical cost. **[Fact]** FY2025 investing outflows included **~$46.6 million** for real-estate acquisitions and **~$10.1 million** to develop real-estate assets, while reported cash from operations fell sharply. **[Inference]** Predictive attribute: **land optionality and entitlements** (equity yield curve on dated NAV convergence, not run-rate earnings yield).

---

## Executive summary
Tejon Ranch is a scarce California land bank: **$49.6M** FY2025 revenue, entitlements for master-planned communities (Centennial, Grapevine), an active Tejon Ranch Commerce Center (TRCC) industrial platform, water banking, and farming/mineral cash streams. FY2025 net income was essentially break-even after development spend; cash from operations troughed at **$6.1M**. At **~$18.84** (NYSE, June 3, 2026), our base case is a **7-year dated payoff** to **$28** per share (conservative acre NAV plus run-rate cash, not GAAP book alone), implying **5.8%** per year. GAAP book (**~$17.65**) is not a fair-value floor for **270k** acres. Moat is **stable** (scarcity, conservancy constraints); dhando is **partial** (acre NAV floor vs price); we propose **watch** (below **~15%** accumulate bar on base path).
