# TowerView LLC — shareholder activity at Tejon Ranch Co. (TRC)

**Last updated:** 2026-06-10  
**Scope:** SEC filings in `TRC/investor-documents/sec-edgar/`, management DEFA14A (Apr 2025 proxy fight), and public IR materials.

## Summary

**TowerView LLC** is Daniel R. Tisch’s Tisch-family investment vehicle (predecessor: Mentor Partners). It has been Tejon’s **largest shareholder** for 14+ years. Influence is exercised primarily through **board representation and inside-the-boardroom engagement**, not public opposition proxies or filed activist letters (contrast: Bulldog Investors, 2025).

Proxies document shareholder feedback and management responses in **“What We Heard / How We Responded”** tables; individual holders are not named, but TowerView’s size (~14–19% beneficial) and Tisch’s directorship make it the anchor long-term holder.

## Board seat (March 2012)

- **March 13, 2012:** Tisch elected to the board the same week TowerView disclosed ~16% ownership.
- Company described him as representing **“the second largest shareholder, TowerView LLC.”**
- Original **Schedule 13D** stated shares acquired **for investment** with **no current plans** to change control — but noted **Tisch’s board election** as the triggering event.
- Tisch has been a director **continuously since 2012** and is a **2026 re-election nominee**.

---

## Governance carve-out: “Exempt Purchaser”

**2023 proxy** (certificate excerpt) names **Daniel R. Tisch, TowerView LLC, and DT Four Partners LLC** as **“Exempt Purchasers”** — exempt from certain change-of-control restrictions applicable to other large holders. Treats TowerView as a long-term aligned block, not a hostile accumulator.

## 2025 Bulldog proxy fight — TowerView as management anchor

**Bulldog Investors / Special Opportunities Fund** ran an opposition slate (Apr–May 2025).

Management **DEFA14A** (“Building the Future”, Apr 2025) repeatedly contrasted Bulldog with incumbents:

- *“Our largest shareholder, TowerView, is represented on our Board (Daniel Tisch).”*
- *“Existing board members in aggregate have significantly higher economic interest than Bulldog.”*
- Board refresh attributed to **2024 shareholder engagement** with larger holders — not Bulldog’s nomination process.
- Management claimed Bulldog did not engage constructively after an initial ranch tour.

**Outcome:** Bulldog’s **Andrew Dakos** elected; **Geoffrey Stack** not re-elected. **Tisch remained** and was re-nominated for 2026.
