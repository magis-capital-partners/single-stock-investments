## What this business is
Viatris Inc is a global generic and branded pharmaceuticals company formed from the 2020 combination of Mylan and Pfizer's Upjohn business. **[Fact]** The company sells off-patent medicines, complex generics, and established brands across four geographic segments: Developed Markets, Greater China, Japan / Australia / New Zealand, and Emerging Markets (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260226_rpt20251231_acc0001792044_26_000013.htm`). **[Fact]** FY2025 net revenues were about **$14.3 billion**, down from **$14.7 billion** in FY2024 and **$15.4 billion** in FY2023 (XBRL `Revenues` tag, same 10-K). **[Fact]** The company is mid-way through an enterprise-wide strategic review (EWSR) that sold non-core assets (including India API operations and biosimilars contributed to Biocon Biologics) and targets **$600 to $700 million** of annual cost savings to improve operating cash flow once fully implemented (10-K MD&A). **[Fact]** About **1.15 billion** shares were outstanding at year-end 2025; long-term debt was about **$12.5 billion** non-current plus **$1.9 billion** current portion.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock may trade as a melting ice cube of legacy Mylan leverage while understating durable generic cash generation after portfolio pruning. **[Inference]** GAAP earnings in FY2025 and Q1 2026 are distorted by large gains on divestitures (Q1 2026 diluted EPS **$2.55** vs **$0.15** in the current quarter per `10-Q_20260507`), so screens on trailing P/E mislead. **[Inference]** Operating cash flow near **$2.3 billion** is more stable than reported net income (**$163 million** to common in FY2025 per `filing_facts_2026-06-02.json`). **[Inference]** Complexity and litigation overhang (opioid, antitrust history in filings) keep generalists away, creating a **complexity discount** rather than a pure earnings discount. **[Inference]** David Rosen (Rubric Capital, approved Sohn 2026 panel) argues **residual-volatility** deep value: few buyers when stocks fall, plus underpriced **Idorsia** Phase III assets (**selatogrel**, **cenerimod**) with readouts in roughly six to nine months (`2026-05-27_Sohn_Digging_Deeper_VTRS_excerpt.md`). Filings confirm the collaboration and asset names (`DEF 14A_20241025`); Rosen's mix splits and "double in 12 months" are not verified here.

## Executive summary
Viatris is a post-merger generic pharma operator simplifying its portfolio, paying down debt, and returning cash while **Idorsia Phase III** assets (**selatogrel**, **cenerimod**) offer filing-backed upside. Normalized owner cash blends **50%** Marvin floor (**$1.45/sh** from FY2025 operating cash flow) and **50%** Rosen cash-yield normalization (**$1.55/sh**, approved Sohn 2026). Rosen's literal 12-month double remains a **bull catalyst footnote**, not the base case. Moat **unproven**; dhando **partial**. **Watch**, approaching the 15% hold band pending readouts and deleveraging.
