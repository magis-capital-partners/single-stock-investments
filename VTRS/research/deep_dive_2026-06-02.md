## What this business is
Viatris Inc is a global generic and branded pharmaceuticals company formed from the 2020 combination of Mylan and Pfizer's Upjohn business. **[Fact]** The company sells off-patent medicines, complex generics, and established brands across four geographic segments: Developed Markets, Greater China, Japan / Australia / New Zealand, and Emerging Markets (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260226_rpt20251231_acc0001792044_26_000013.htm`). **[Fact]** FY2025 net revenues were about **$14.3 billion**, down from **$14.7 billion** in FY2024 and **$15.4 billion** in FY2023 (XBRL `Revenues` tag, same 10-K). **[Fact]** The company is mid-way through an enterprise-wide strategic review (EWSR) that sold non-core assets (including India API operations and biosimilars contributed to Biocon Biologics) and targets **$600 to $700 million** of annual cost savings to improve operating cash flow once fully implemented (10-K MD&A). **[Fact]** About **1.15 billion** shares were outstanding at year-end 2025; long-term debt was about **$12.5 billion** non-current plus **$1.9 billion** current portion.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock may trade as a melting ice cube of legacy Mylan leverage while understating durable generic cash generation after portfolio pruning. **[Inference]** GAAP earnings in FY2025 and Q1 2026 are distorted by large gains on divestitures (Q1 2026 diluted EPS **$2.55** vs **$0.15** in the current quarter per `10-Q_20260507`), so screens on trailing P/E mislead. **[Inference]** Operating cash flow near **$2.3 billion** is more stable than reported net income (**$163 million** to common in FY2025 per `filing_facts_2026-06-02.json`). **[Inference]** Complexity and litigation overhang (opioid, antitrust history in filings) keep generalists away, creating a **complexity discount** rather than a pure earnings discount.

---

## Executive summary
Viatris is a post-merger generic pharma operator simplifying its portfolio, paying down debt, and returning cash via dividends while revenue slowly declines. FY2025 operating income rose on portfolio actions, but normalized owner cash is better anchored on **~$2.3 billion** operating cash flow than on GAAP net income. At **$15.69** (Stooq 2026-06-02) the Lawrence base case implies about **9.5%** per year on normalized free cash per share, below our 15% hurdle. Moat is **unproven** after asset sales; dhando is **partial** (cash yield vs leverage). We propose **watch**: interesting cash engine, wrong risk/reward for core until deleveraging and base-business growth are visible in filings.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Net debt near **$14 billion** against equity of about **$3.2 billion** (parent stockholders' equity per 10-K) leaves little room for operational misses.

- Generic price deflation in U.S. and Europe faster than cost program offsets.
- Integration / separation costs from Eye Care and remaining portfolio moves.
- Litigation and regulatory settlements (opioid and antitrust history in 10-K).
- **Inversion:** If operating cash flow falls below **$1.8 billion** for two years while dividends continue, equity is a value trap, not dhando.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** Market prices a clean compounder; business is still a leveraged turnaround with shrinking revenue.
