## What this business is
SoftBank Group Corp. (9984.T) is a **pure holding company** listed on the Tokyo Stock Exchange. **[Fact]** It does not run a standalone operating business at the parent; economics flow through four reportable segments: **holdco investments** (listed and private stakes, including Alibaba marks), **SoftBank Vision Funds (SVF)**, **SoftBank Corp.** (Japanese telecom, majority-owned subsidiary), and **Arm Holdings** (semiconductor IP, listed stake). **[Fact]** FY2025 (ended 2025-03-31) consolidated parent owners equity was **¥11,561,541 million** on **1,452.982 million** average shares, or **¥7,957** book value per share. **[Fact]** Parent net profit was **¥1,153,332 million**, but holdco investment profit alone was **¥3,413,821 million**, driven largely by Alibaba fair-value marks rather than recurring cash. Return to shareholders today is mostly dividends (**¥44** per share full year FY2025) plus mark-driven equity, not steady parent-level free cash flow.

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock at **¥5,918** (Yahoo Finance, 2026-07-24) trades at a deep discount to both consolidated book (**¥7,957** per share) and our proof-backed component base of **¥9,432** per share. **[Inference]** Generalists anchor on volatile investment gains, Vision Fund losses, and headline leverage from **asset-backed financing** (Arm margin **¥1,258.5 billion**, Alibaba forward **¥997.8 billion**, SoftBank Corp margin **¥796 billion** as of FY2025) rather than look-through asset value. **[Inference]** The market may be over-discounting Arm royalties, SoftBank Corp dividend upstream, and eventual Alibaba monetization while under-weighting the OpenAI commitment (up to **$30 billion** net) and financing unwind risk. Mispricing is **asset-lens**: value sits in stakes and catalyst exits, not in normalized owner cash at spot.

## Executive summary
SoftBank Group is a Japanese holdco whose value is the sum of Arm, SoftBank Corp, Vision Fund stakes, and a large investment book. FY2025 book equity is **¥7,957** per share; our four-component proof stack sums to **¥9,432** per share at base case after mark uplift and a financing reserve, versus a **¥5,918** quote. Reported earnings swing with investment marks; we normalize owner cash to **¥250** per share (dividend plus modest recurring investment income, excluding one-time Alibaba gains). Asset-backed loans against Arm, Alibaba, and SoftBank Corp shares create a structural overhang that caps dhando comfort even when price sits below book. Lawrence owner-cash math at today's price implies about **0.8% per year** over seven years on the base case, with bear **-3.6%** and bull **5.4%** in `valuation.json`. That fails our ~15% return bar. Component NAV offers partial downside protection, but financing stress and AI capital calls limit "tails I don't lose much." Proposed stance **watch**: asset discount alone does not earn a Lawrence return at spot; revisit on financing clarity, Alibaba/Arm catalysts, or a lower entry price.
