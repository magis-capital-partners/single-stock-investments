## What this business is
Pure Cycle Corporation is a Colorado water and land developer focused on the southeastern Denver metro area. **[Fact]** The company wholesales water and wastewater services through the Rangeview Metropolitan District on a 24,000-acre Lowry Ranch service area and develops the ~930-acre Sky Ranch master-planned community east of Denver (`investor-documents/ir-pcyo/0001104659-25-110312.pdf`, Business section). **[Fact]** Reportable revenue streams are water and wastewater resource development, land development (finished lots and project fees), and a smaller single-family rental segment. **[Fact]** As of February 28, 2026, the company had delivered 1,169 finished lots at Sky Ranch, was ~78% through Phase 2D and ~91% through Phase 2C, and reported 27 consecutive profitable quarters (`investor-documents/ir-pcyo/2026-04-08_Pure_Cycle_Announces_Financial_Results_For_the_155.pdf`). **[Fact]** Shares trade on NASDAQ (PCYO); ~24.1 million common shares were outstanding in April 2026.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock may trade as a thinly followed small-cap land developer while the economic engine is a long-duration water franchise plus phased real-estate optionality. **[Inference]** GAAP book (~$6 per share at FY2025) embeds water infrastructure at depreciated cost and carries Arkansas mineral interests at **zero** after a fiscal 2020 impairment, so price-to-book alone understates asset backing. **[Inference]** Large related-party receivables from Sky Ranch public-improvement advances (~$56 million at February 28, 2026) are not owner cash today but are balance-sheet value if CAB bond/tax reimbursement timelines hold. **[Inference]** Micro-cap liquidity and housing-cycle headlines can obscure steady tap-fee and lot-delivery economics that management paces to builder absorption.

---

## Executive summary
Pure Cycle combines exclusive Denver-area water service rights with a phased Sky Ranch land business and a nascent rental portfolio. FY2025 net income was $13.1 million on $26.1 million of revenue; H1 FY2026 accelerated lot revenue after a mild winter. At **$10.09** per share the stock trades above GAAP book with a modest earnings yield.

**We expect about 9.8% per year** over seven years (total synthesis) on normalized owner cash of **$0.50** per share, with Sky Ranch and tap-fee growth in the base case. The Lawrence owner-cash path before qualitative adjustment is slightly lower. Dhando is **partial** (related-party repayment and housing-cycle risk). Stance: **watch**.

### Mental models in plain English

**Permutations & combinations (Stahl):** Revenue mixes metered water fees, one-time tap fees, lot sales, project management, and rent. Oil-and-gas industrial water can swing year to year (FY2025 O&G deliveries down 86% versus FY2024 per 10-K).

**Inversion (Munger):** What breaks the thesis? Sky Ranch CAB fails to reimburse advances, housing freeze stops lot absorption, or adverse Water Court rulings constrain new supplies.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Failure to recover Sky Ranch CAB and Rangeview advances on expected timelines, forcing equity issuance or development slowdown.

- Housing affordability and buyer confidence could reduce builder lot take-down pace.
- Water Court denial or delay on remaining Box Elder / reservoir claims (1,635 AF application denied February 2025; settlement added water December 2025 per press release).
- Concentration in Denver metro growth politics and district governance conflicts (officers serve dual roles).
- Single-family rental regulatory uncertainty slowed new unit completions; inventory risk if rental rules tighten further.

**Lens failure mode (operating + asset):** If we are wrong that lot revenue and tap fees compound, the stock re-rates to book and receivable recoveries alone, and Lawrence returns fall toward low single digits.
