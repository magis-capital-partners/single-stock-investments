## What this business is

Bolsas y Mercados Argentinos S.A. (BYMA) is Argentina's integrated capital-markets infrastructure company. **[Fact]** It operates the stock exchange (trading, clearing, and central counterparty), owns **99.97%** of central securities depository Caja de Valores S.A., and runs education subsidiary Instituto Argentino de Mercado de Capitales (IAMC). **[Fact]** The group reports under IAS 29 hyperinflation accounting; FY2025 consolidated service revenues were **ARS 76,394 million** and income from transaction rights **ARS 180,381 million** (amounts in thousands of constant pesos, `investor-documents/official-reports/BYMA-Financial_Statements_2025-12-31.pdf`). **[Fact]** FY2025 operating income was **ARS 220,086 million** and net income attributable to shareholders **ARS 172,419 million**; diluted earnings per share **ARS 28.31** on **6,089,555 thousand** weighted average shares. **[Fact]** Q1 2026 press release (May 8, 2026) shows inflation-adjusted revenues up **24%** year-over-year and operating margin excluding financial income at a record **71.6%** (`investor-documents/presentations-and-media/BYMA-Press_Release_1Q26.pdf`). BYMA is a toll collector on Argentine securities activity (Stahl croupier): fees on custody, trading, and clearing without principal market risk.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The stock at **~ARS 296** trades near **10 times** FY2025 earnings and **~9 times** normalized owner cash per share, despite LTM EBITDA margin near **76%** and operating margin near **69%** (`BYMA-Press_Release_4Q25.pdf`). **[Inference]** Local investors may discount Argentine listed infrastructure for currency and policy risk even when volume and fee yield are rising. **[Inference]** Our base return uses mid-cycle owner cash (**ARS 33** per share) after hair-cutting peak operating cash flow, not the full **140%** fourth-quarter 2025 average daily traded value surge. The mispricing, if any, is jurisdiction and cycle timing, not hidden asset value.

## Executive summary

BYMA is Argentina's exchange and central depository toll road with extraordinary margins and rising custody and trading volumes. FY2025 filings show **ARS 172.4 billion** net income and **ARS 247.0 billion** cash from operations; fourth-quarter 2025 average daily traded value rose **140%** year-over-year and first-quarter 2026 operating margin excluding financial income hit **71.6%**. At **ARS 295.75** per share, we expect about **25.2%** per year over seven years on **ARS 33** normalized owner cash per share, above our fifteen-percent accumulate bar on math alone. The franchise moat is stable, but Pabrai dhando is only **partial** because peso devaluation and regulatory shifts can compress real returns faster than multiples expand. We propose **watch** until human review sizes Argentina exposure; mechanical gate would say accumulate on IRR.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Argentine macro and currency policy reduce real fee growth or equity market activity faster than the stock's low multiple compensates.

- BCRA monetary tightening cut fixed-income and repo ADTV in 1Q26 (`BYMA-Press_Release_1Q26.pdf`)
- IAS 29 restatement and peso volatility complicate cross-border investor comparison
- Quantex integration risk; purchase price and returns not yet in filings
- Concentrated local ownership (BCBA **~30.9%**; foreign **~19.3%** per annual report) limits global re-rating catalyst

**Inversion (what would make us wrong):** Sustained ADTV and AuC growth with stable operating margin above **65%** for eight quarters would support upgrading to accumulate despite jurisdiction label.

**Lens failure mode:** Treating BYMA as a developed-market croupier (ICE, CBOE) without haircutting growth for policy risk would overstate dhando.
