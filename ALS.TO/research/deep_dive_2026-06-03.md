## What this business is
Altius Minerals Corporation is a Canadian royalty and streaming company listed on the TSX (ALS) and OTCQX (ATUSF). **[Fact]** It earns cash from long-life royalties and streams on base and battery metals, potash, iron ore, and renewable electricity, and runs a Project Generation business that seeds exploration partnerships and holds equity stakes. **[Fact]** The company also owns 57% of Altius Renewable Royalties (ARR) and holds a 29% interest in Generation Mining (GBR) through its joint-venture structure. **[Fact]** FY2025 attributable royalty revenue was **CAD 69.9 million** (**CAD 1.51 per share**), up from CAD 64.0 million in 2024 (`official-reports/nr_20260310_Q4_2025.pdf`). **[Fact]** About **46.3 million** common shares were outstanding after the Lithium Royalty Corp. (LRC) transaction (`official-reports/nr_20260310_Q4_2025.pdf`).

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock has more than doubled over the past year while headline net earnings are dominated by a one-time gain on the Arthur Gold royalty sale. **[Inference]** Investors may be paying a scarcity premium for diversified exposure to copper, potash, and Texas renewables without anchoring to **adjusted operating cash flow** of only **CAD 0.59 per share** in FY2025. **[Inference]** Horizon Kinetics (Q3 2025 commentary, **context tier only**) argued that Altius bundles hard-to-hold commodities at a small market cap; at **CAD 59.91** today the equity is far above the **~CAD 1.2 billion** frame in that letter, so the scarcity discount may have closed. **[Inference]** Project Generation equity marks and GBR development options are not fully visible in a single GAAP line, so both bulls and bears can misread the latent pipeline.

---

## Executive summary
Altius is a diversified royalty platform: producing cash from Chapada copper, Saskatchewan potash (Nutrien/Mosaic), Labrador iron ore dividends, and a fast-growing renewable royalty book, plus venture-style upside in Project Generation and GBR. FY2025 adjusted operating cash flow was **CAD 27.5 million** (**CAD 0.59 per share**); the quarterly dividend is **CAD 0.10** (**CAD 0.40 per year**). At **CAD 59.91** (2026-06-02) the Lawrence owner-cash model fails our **~15%** annual return hurdle.

**Base 7-year annual return: -15.2% per year.** Dhando is **partial** (low debt, diversified royalties; price leaves little margin for error). Stance: **watch**.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Paying a full scarcity multiple after a +100% year while owner cash yield is near **1%**.

- **Commodity reversal:** Potash and copper prices normalized lower would cut attributable revenue faster than costs.
- **Iron ore dividend path:** LIORC dividends fell FY2025; prolonged weakness drags consolidated cash.
- **GBR / development impairment:** Q4 2025 JV impairment signals development book may be over-marked internally.
- **Lens failure mode (asset):** If Project Generation and GBR options are worth zero, the stock is a low-yield royalty bond at CAD 60.

**Inversion:** What if adj. operating cash flow grows 8% for five years and the market keeps a 20x terminal multiple on renewables-weighted cash? Bull case in `valuation.json` still struggles to clear 15% from CAD 59.91 without a lower entry price.
