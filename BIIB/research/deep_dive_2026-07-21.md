## What this business is

Biogen Inc. (NASDAQ: BIIB) is a global biopharmaceutical company focused on neuroscience and rare disease. **[Fact]** FY2025 total revenue was **$9.891 billion**, including **$7.119 billion** of product revenue, **$1.861 billion** of anti-CD20 collaboration revenue, and **$732.9 million** of contract manufacturing, royalty, and other revenue. **[Fact]** Multiple sclerosis (MS) product revenue was **$4.039 billion**, down **7.1%** year over year; rare-disease product revenue was **$2.154 billion**, up **8.4%**. **[Fact]** The company generated approximately **$2.1 billion** of free cash flow in 2025 and ended the year with about **$4.2 billion** of cash and **$6.3 billion** of debt before the Apellis closing. **[Fact]** On **May 14, 2026**, Biogen completed the acquisition of Apellis Pharmaceuticals for aggregate consideration of approximately **$5.3 billion**, funded with cash and **$2.0 billion** of newly drawn term facilities, plus contingent value rights (CVRs) tied to SYFOVRE sales milestones. **[Inference]** Owner economics today are a mix of declining legacy MS cash flows, growing rare-disease and launch products, a large clinical pipeline, and newly levered geographic/complement assets from Apellis.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The market may be pricing **pipeline and Alzheimer's optionality** (LEQEMBI, litifilimab, felzartamab) and **Apellis geographic complement** while underweighting **MS patent cliffs** (TECFIDERA European revocation, TYSABRI method patents expiring 2026–2027), **integration and deleveraging risk** after a **$5.3 billion** deal, and **CVR liabilities** of up to roughly **$582 million** if SYFOVRE milestones are achieved. **[Inference]** Proof-first component math at base case sums to about **$145 per share** of economic value versus a price near **$197**, suggesting the equity embeds success probabilities and growth not yet reproduced in filing-locked owner-cash paths. **[Fact]** Insider selling dominated the past twelve months (net sales roughly **$7.6 million** versus negligible open-market buys), which is context, not a base-case input.

## Executive summary

Biogen is a mature biotech transitioning from an eroding MS core toward rare disease, Alzheimer's collaboration revenue, and an Apellis-led geographic expansion. FY2025 still produced strong free cash flow, but legacy products face generic and competitive pressure while growth products and pipeline milestones must offset decline. At approximately **$197** per share, the proof-complete component schedule implies a **negative** annualized return on base economic value over seven years. Moat is **unproven** (patent-dependent franchises). Dhando is **partial** (bounded downside reserve, but acquisition leverage and clinical binary risk). Proposed stance: **watch** (legacy field; human capital authority only).

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** MS franchise erosion accelerates faster than growth products and pipeline can replace, while Apellis integration and leverage constrain flexibility.

**Lens failure mode (biotech / milestone):** Unsupported success probabilities or double-counting pipeline value in both product forecasts and option schedule would overstate equity value; proofs keep pipeline and products in separate overlap keys.

**Inversion checklist:**
- Litifilimab or other Phase 3 programs fail → pipeline component toward low case.  
- SYFOVRE sales miss CVR thresholds → lower Apellis asset value; CVR liability falls but strategic rationale weakens.  
- Post-close 10-Q shows higher integration cost or debt than proof base → senior-claims component repriced.
