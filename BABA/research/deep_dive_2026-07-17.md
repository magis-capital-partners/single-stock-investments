## What this business is
Alibaba Group is a China-based technology holding company operating multi-sided commerce, cloud computing, and international retail platforms. **[Fact]** FY2026 revenue was **RMB 941.2 billion** (approximately **US$148.4 billion**), down **5.5%** versus FY2025. **[Fact]** The group reports four operating segments for FY2026: **Alibaba China E-commerce Group** (Taobao, Tmall, quick commerce), **Cloud Intelligence Group**, **Alibaba International Digital Commerce**, and **All others** (Cainiao, Freshippo, DingTalk, Qwen consumer, media). **[Fact]** One American depositary share represents **eight** ordinary shares. **[Fact]** Cash and equivalents were **RMB 145.5 billion** and equity-method investments **RMB 210.2 billion** at March 31, 2026. **[Fact]** The company uses a **variable interest entity (VIE)** structure so Cayman-listed shareholders do not directly own the operating companies in China.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
**Predictive attribute:** complexity discount (VIE legal structure, China ADS geopolitical overhang, segment restructuring, and a negative reported free cash flow year during an AI capex cycle).

**[Inference]** The market may be treating Alibaba either as a permanently impaired China e-commerce story (ignoring Cloud AI acceleration and Ant optionality) or as a cloud winner (ignoring quick-commerce subsidies that cut China Adjusted EBITA **44%** in FY2026). **[Fact]** Consolidated Adjusted EBITA fell to **RMB 76.4 billion** from **RMB 173.1 billion** while Cloud Adjusted EBITA rose **35%** to **RMB 14.3 billion**. **[Inference]** At **$115.53**, the stock embeds substantial haircuts for regulatory tail risk and capital allocation during the AI investment phase that may be overstated if capex normalizes and Cloud mix rises.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** VIE or PRC regulatory action could impair foreign shareholders' economic interest in operating assets despite contractual arrangements (`20-F_20260520...htm` Item 3.D risk factors).

**Secondary risks:**
- China retail share loss to Pinduoduo and Douyin-commerce persists beyond quick-commerce investment phase (20-F Item 4 competition disclosure).
- AI infrastructure capex remains elevated with delayed return on invested capital (FY2026 FCF **negative RMB 46.6 billion**, 20-F p.137).
- US export controls on advanced GPUs and cloud access restrictions (Remote Access Security Act cited in 20-F risk factors).
- HFCA Act / PCAOB audit issues force ADS delisting or discount persists (20-F ADS delisting discussion).
- Ant Group stake remains illiquid with uncertain monetization path (20-F Item 7 Ant related-party section).

**Inversion:** What would make this fail? A structural break in VIE enforceability, a multi-billion regulatory fine beyond historical antitrust penalty, or Cloud growth stalling while China subsidies continue.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** Using FY2024 peak free cash flow (**RMB 156 billion**) or FY2026 negative FCF without normalization for a one-time AI capex spike.
