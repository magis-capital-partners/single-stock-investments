## What this business is

Hong Kong Exchanges and Clearing (HKEX) operates the Stock Exchange of Hong Kong, its derivatives and commodities markets (including London Metal Exchange ownership), and the central clearing and settlement infrastructure for those venues. **[Fact]** Brokers, issuers, and clearing members pay trading, clearing, listing, data, and depository fees; the group does not run a balance-sheet lending franchise on core exchange operations. **[Fact]** FY2024 revenue and other income was **HK$22.4 billion** (+9% year-over-year) with profit attributable to shareholders **HK$13.1 billion** (+10%) and basic earnings per share **HK$10.32** (HKEX Group FY2024 annual report, IR web mirror pending local download). HKEX is the dominant listing and trading gateway between international capital and Chinese issuers, with Connect programs linking Hong Kong to Shanghai and Shenzhen.

## Why the market might be wrong

No dated mispricing edge at today's price. **[Inference]** FY2024–FY2025 benefited from strong headline average daily turnover (ADT **HK$131.8 billion** in FY2024, +26% year-over-year) and robust derivatives activity; Q1 2026 revenue **HK$8.2 billion** (+20% versus Q1 2025 per HKEX IR headline). **[Inference]** The market may be fairly valuing a peak-to-mid-cycle earnings stream at roughly **26 times** trailing earnings rather than underwriting permanent boom-level volumes. Return is franchise quality at price, not a catalyst stack.

## Executive summary

HKEX is Asia's flagship exchange and clearing croupier with **74%** EBITDA margins and a durable regulatory moat around Hong Kong market infrastructure. FY2024 was a record year: revenue **HK$22.4 billion**, profit **HK$13.1 billion**, EPS **HK$10.32**; FY2025 EPS reached **HK$14.05** with full-year dividend **HK$12.52** per share (HKEX Group IR). We normalize owner earnings to **HK$11** per share, below spot EPS, to adjust for cyclical turnover and margin-fund pass-through. At **HK$383** (2026-06-10 close), the Lawrence base case implies roughly **0%** annualized return over seven years, far below our **15%** hold band. Bull case about **3%** if China-linked volumes persist; bear about **-8%** on flat earnings and multiple compression. Asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) is **partial**: limited balance-sheet risk, but earnings level is cyclical. We propose **watch** at cycle-peak earnings and ~26 times price to earnings.
