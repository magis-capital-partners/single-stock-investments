## Executive summary

Teqnion is a **Swedish industrial serial acquirer** (founded 2006, listed 2019) owning a decentralized portfolio of niche industrial subsidiaries. Growth via **M&A + operational improvement**; 2025 included nine acquisitions and restructuring of an underperforming "tail" of portfolio companies.

**FY2025:** Net sales **1,800 MSEK** (+15%); **EBITA 203.1 MSEK** (+36%, margin **11.3%**); **organic net sales −5%**; EPS **5.74 SEK** (+3%); FCF **+123%** YoY. Board proposed **no dividend** for 2025.

**Q1 2026:** Net sales **474.4 MSEK** (+17%); **EBITA 68.0 MSEK** (+106%, margin **14.3%** record); organic sales **−5%** (deliberate exit of unprofitable business); EPS **2.04 SEK** (−4% on FX/tax).

**One-line view:** Pabrai-style serial acquirer of niche industrials — turnaround gaining traction on margins/cash flow while organic revenue still negative; M&A discipline is the swing factor.

**Thesis status:** **strengthening** — operational turnaround visible in EBITA and FCF; organic growth and M&A integration remain key monitors.

## Munger lenses

### Circle of competence — pass with work

Parent model is simple; each subsidiary is a mini deep-dive. Acceptable if we trust management's decentralized discipline.

### Moat

- **Niche market leadership** in many small subsidiaries (local scale).
- **Not a single corporate moat** — portfolio of micro-moats.
- **Goodwill impairment SEK 73M in Q3 2025** — signals past overpayment or operational failure in at least one asset.

### Inversion — what would kill the thesis?

1. **M&A indiscipline** — overpaying, leverage creep (equity ratio already falling).
2. **Organic deterioration** beyond deliberate exits.
3. **UK/Nordic integration failure** under new Teqnion Nord/Väst structure.
4. **Key person risk** — founder CEO Johan Steene central to culture.
5. **Small-cap liquidity** on First North.
