## What this business is

Evolve Royalties Ltd. is a Canadian junior royalty company listed on the CSE that collects cash from net profits interests and net smelter return royalties on copper, tin, and lithium projects without operating mines. **[Fact]** The portfolio spans 14 assets anchored by a 0.51% net profits interest on Teck's Highland Valley Copper mine, a 5% copper NSR on a portion of Hudbay's Copper Mountain mine, a sliding-scale tin gross-revenue royalty on Namibia's Uis mine, and a C$0.75 per tonne royalty on Eldorado Gold's McIlvenna Bay project (`EVR/presentations-and-media/corporate-presentation-2026-02.pdf`; `EVR/official-reports/news-release-2026-06-01-ytd-royalty-payments.txt`). **[Fact]** The company was formed by the Nomad Royalty team (sold to Sandstorm Gold for US$755 million in 2022) and listed on the CSE on December 17, 2025 after a reverse takeover with Voyageur Minerals Explorers Corp. **[Fact]** From January 1 through June 1, 2026, Evolve received C$2.70 million in royalty payments on roughly 55 million pro-forma shares outstanding.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Commodity price downturn compresses royalty receipts and junior multiples simultaneously, leaving the NAV discount in place.

- Teck's Highland Valley permitted mine life historically cited to 2027 before MLE; extension execution risk remains
- Uis acquisition consumed US$22.5M cash plus 4.2M shares; integration and tin price sensitivity
- Copper Mountain payments deferred until US$10M pre-tax exclusion clears (US$6.7M paid to date)
- CSE liquidity and short operating history limit institutional ownership
- Argentina lithium (LAA) construction timing tied to Tibet Summit capital allocation and lithium prices

**Lens failure mode (asset / optionality):** If development royalties (McIlvenna, Copper Mountain, LAA) slip beyond 2027, the investment becomes a two-asset micro-royalty at a premium to run-rate cash, and the NAV discount thesis fails.
