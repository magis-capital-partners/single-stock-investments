## What this business is
AeroVironment, Inc. (NASDAQ: AVAV) designs and produces unmanned aircraft systems, loitering munitions, and related defense technologies for the U.S. Department of Defense and allied governments. **[Fact]** FY2026 revenue was **$1,976.8 million**, more than double FY2025's **$820.6 million**, primarily because the company closed the **BlueHalo** acquisition on May 1, 2025 and now reports two operating segments: **Autonomous Systems (AxS)** and **Space, Cyber and Directed Energy (SCDE)**. **[Fact]** Flagship products include the Switchblade family of loitering munitions, small UAS, and BlueHalo-derived space, cyber, and missile-defense offerings. **[Fact]** Funded backlog was approximately **$1,183 million** at April 30, 2026, up from **$726.6 million** a year earlier, with management expecting about **85%** of that backlog to convert to revenue in fiscal 2027. **[Inference]** The equity today is a leveraged defense-growth platform whose near-term GAAP earnings are dominated by acquisition accounting, not underlying demand.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The market may be capitalizing a **post-BlueHalo growth narrative** (loitering munitions demand, broader defense-tech portfolio, $1.2B funded backlog) while underweighting **integration-year cash economics**: FY2026 GAAP operating loss of **$311 million**, **negative** operating cash flow of **$78.4 million**, **$204 million** of intangible amortization, a **$241 million** goodwill impairment tied to the terminated SCAR space program, and **$729 million** of long-term debt. **[Inference]** Segment adjusted EBITDA of roughly **$286 million** (AxS **$288.7 million** plus SCDE **-$2.6 million**) shows operating demand, but reported owner cash is still negative and normalized free cash per share is far below the current share price. **[Inference]** Lock-up releases on **~17.4 million** BlueHalo seller shares (40% May 2026, 30% November 2026, remainder May 2027) may also create a supply overhang not visible in backlog headlines.

---

## Executive summary
AVAV is a defense compounder in a BlueHalo integration year. Switchblade and broader AxS demand drove legacy revenue growth before the May 2025 acquisition doubled the revenue base and added space, cyber, and directed-energy capabilities. Funded backlog of **$1.18 billion** and segment adjusted EBITDA near **$286 million** support a normalized owner-cash story, but FY2026 GAAP results were loss-making and cash-consuming after amortization, impairment, and deal costs. At **$142.60** (Yahoo close 2026-07-20), the weighted synthesis base case yields **-4.7%** per year over seven years on **$2.75 per share** normalized owner cash (not the negative spot filing number). Moat is **stable** on Switchblade and DoD relationships; dhando is **partial** because integration, leverage, and program cancellation risk cap the bear case. Proposed stance: **watch**.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** BlueHalo integration fails to deliver SCDE margins while amortization and debt service persist, leaving equity owners with low cash conversion despite backlog growth (`10-K_20260629` Business Segment Results; cash flow statement).

**Inversion bullets:**

- **Program cancellation repeat.** SCAR stop-work and termination led to **$241M** goodwill impairment (`10-K_20260629` risk factors); other space/cyber programs could follow.
- **Budget / appropriation delay.** Funded backlog **$1,183M** requires continued congressional funding (`10-K_20260629` Item 1); delays would push revenue right.
- **Leverage and dilution.** Long-term debt **$729M** and diluted share count **49.1M** vs **28.2M** prior year (`10-K_20260629`; `8-K_20250501` credit agreement) raise the bar for per-share owner cash.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** If we are wrong that segment adjusted EBITDA converts to owner cash, the stock is a story multiple on defense spending without compounder economics.
