## What this business is

AeroVironment, Inc. (NASDAQ: AVAV) designs and produces unmanned aircraft systems, loitering munitions, and related defense technologies for the U.S. Department of Defense and allied governments. **[Fact]** FY2026 revenue was **$1,976.8 million**, more than double FY2025's **$820.6 million**, primarily because the company closed the **BlueHalo** acquisition on May 1, 2025 and now reports two operating segments: **Autonomous Systems (AxS)** and **Space, Cyber and Directed Energy (SCDE)**. **[Fact]** Flagship products include the Switchblade family of loitering munitions, small UAS, and BlueHalo-derived space, cyber, and missile-defense offerings. **[Fact]** Funded backlog was approximately **$1,183 million** at April 30, 2026, up from **$726.6 million** a year earlier, with management expecting about **85%** of that backlog to convert to revenue in fiscal 2027. **[Inference]** The equity today is a leveraged defense-growth platform whose near-term GAAP earnings are dominated by acquisition accounting, not underlying demand.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The market may be capitalizing a **post-BlueHalo growth narrative** (loitering munitions demand, broader defense-tech portfolio, $1.2B funded backlog) while underweighting **integration-year cash economics**: FY2026 GAAP operating loss of **$311 million**, **negative** operating cash flow of **$78.4 million**, **$204 million** of intangible amortization, a **$241 million** goodwill impairment tied to the terminated SCAR space program, and **$729 million** of long-term debt. **[Inference]** Segment adjusted EBITDA of roughly **$286 million** (AxS **$288.7 million** plus SCDE **-$2.6 million**) shows operating demand, but reported owner cash is still negative and normalized free cash per share is far below the current share price. **[Inference]** Lock-up releases on **~17.4 million** BlueHalo seller shares (40% May 2026, 30% November 2026, remainder May 2027) may also create a supply overhang not visible in backlog headlines.

---

## Executive summary

AVAV is a defense compounder in an integration year. Switchblade and broader AxS demand drove legacy revenue growth before BlueHalo doubled the revenue base and added space, cyber, and directed-energy capabilities. Funded backlog of **$1.18 billion** and segment adjusted EBITDA near **$286 million** support a normalized owner-cash story, but FY2026 GAAP results were loss-making and cash-consuming after amortization, impairment, and deal costs. At **$142.60** (Yahoo close 2026-07-20), the base case annual return is **pending mechanical refresh** on approximately **$2.75 per share** normalized owner cash (not the negative spot filing number). Moat is **stable** on Switchblade and DoD relationships; dhando is **partial** because integration, leverage, and program cancellation risk cap the bear case. Proposed stance: **watch**.

### Business mechanics (Hohn)

**Operating snapshot.** FY2026 consolidated revenue **$1,976.8M**; gross profit **$500.6M** (~25% gross margin); GAAP operating loss **-$311.0M**. AxS revenue grew **65%** year over year, including **$253.5M** of product revenue from BlueHalo and ESAero acquisitions plus legacy Switchblade volume. SCDE is entirely acquisition-sourced in FY2026.

**Thesis pillars.**

1. **Backlog conversion.** Funded backlog **$1,183M** (+63% YoY) with **85%** expected in FY2027 provides visible revenue, assuming appropriations hold.
2. **Switchblade franchise.** Legacy AV LMS demand remains the core profit engine (AxS adj EBITDA **$288.7M**).
3. **Platform expansion.** BlueHalo adds space, cyber, and directed-energy programs; upside depends on margin recovery in SCDE.
4. **Integration overhang.** **$204M** amortization, **$241M** impairment, and deal-related SG&A depress GAAP earnings and owner cash in FY2026.

**Growth mechanism.** Near-term growth is driven by funded backlog release and continued Switchblade/UAS production scale. Medium-term growth requires cross-selling BlueHalo capabilities through AV's DoD channels and improving SCDE margins. Ukraine was **38%** of revenue in FY2024 (**$274M**) and **18%** in FY2025 (**$150M**), showing demand can normalize after conflict spikes.

### Moat (Munger)

Switchblade and qualified UAS platforms create **switching costs** and **regulatory barriers** in DoD procurement. However, customer concentration in government budgets and peer competition (Lockheed, RTX, Anduril) limit pricing power outside niche LMS products. Moat rating: **stable**, not widening until SCDE proves durable profitability.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** BlueHalo integration fails to deliver SCDE margins while amortization and debt service persist, leaving equity owners with low cash conversion despite backlog growth.

**Inversion bullets:**

- **Program cancellation repeat.** SCAR stop-work and termination led to **$241M** goodwill impairment; other space/cyber programs could follow.
- **Budget / appropriation delay.** Funded backlog requires continued congressional funding; delays would push revenue right.
- **Leverage and dilution.** Long-term debt **$729M** and higher share count (**49.1M** diluted vs **28.2M** prior year) raise the bar for per-share owner cash.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** If we are wrong that segment adjusted EBITDA converts to owner cash, the stock is a story multiple on defense spending without compounder economics.
