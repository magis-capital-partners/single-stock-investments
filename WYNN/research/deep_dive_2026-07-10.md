## What this business is
Wynn Resorts is a developer and operator of luxury integrated casino resorts. **[Fact]** Through ~72% ownership of Wynn Macau, Limited, the company runs Wynn Palace and Wynn Macau in Macau. **[Fact]** In the U.S., it owns and operates Wynn Las Vegas and Encore on the Strip and Encore Boston Harbor in Massachusetts. **[Fact]** FY2025 consolidated operating revenues were **$7.14 billion** (up **0.1%** year over year). **[Fact]** The company also holds a **40%** equity interest in a joint venture building Wynn Al Marjan Island in the United Arab Emirates, targeted to open in **2027**. **[Inference]** Owner economics are driven by gaming win (especially VIP and premium mass in Macau), hotel occupancy and average daily rate, food and beverage spend, and capital spending to maintain ultra-luxury positioning.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
There is **no classic dated mispricing signal** (HK predictive attribute: **none**). **[Inference]** Generalists often bucket Wynn with highly levered U.S. regional casinos, while filings show a bifurcated portfolio: recovering Macau VIP/mass win (Q1 2026 Macau revenue **+14.2%** year over year) and a stable Las Vegas premium franchise. **[Fact]** FY2025 net income attributable to Wynn Resorts fell **35%** to **$327 million** even though operating revenues were flat, driven by higher income taxes (**+$101 million**) and lower interest income, not operating collapse. **[Inference]** Bears may overweight **$10.5 billion** of long-term debt and thin parent equity (**$275 million**) and underweight **$1.35 billion** of operating cash flow and **$2.22 billion** of Adjusted Property EBITDAR. **[Inference]** Bulls may extrapolate Q1 2026 momentum without modeling **~$660 million** of annual capital spending and UAE pre-opening cash needs.

## Executive summary
Wynn Resorts operates five luxury integrated resorts (two in Macau, two in Las Vegas, one in Boston) plus a **40%** stake in a UAE property opening in **2027**. FY2025 operating revenues were **$7.14 billion**; operating cash flow **$1.35 billion**; owner cash after capital spending about **$6.65 per share** on **104.2 million** diluted shares. Q1 2026 accelerated: revenue **+9.2%**, net income **+65.6%**, led by Wynn Palace (**+23%** revenue).

At about **$100** per share (Yahoo Finance, 2026-07-10), the Lawrence base case implies about **12.5%** per year on normalized owner cash, below our **15%** accumulate hurdle. The business has a **stable** durable advantage (Munger moat) on luxury brand, scarce licenses, and premium locations, with **partial** asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando): strong property-level cash generation, but **$10.5 billion** debt and thin equity cap upside asymmetry. Marvin proposes **watch**. No approved external sources are in the base case.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Refinancing and covenant stress on **$10.5 billion** of long-term debt if Macau volumes stall or interest rates rise on floating-rate tranches.

- Macau regulatory changes could compress VIP table drop and win per turn, as seen in prior crackdown cycles.
