## What this business is
AST SpaceMobile is building a constellation of large low-Earth-orbit satellites that connect directly to unmodified smartphones through partner mobile network operators. **[Fact]** The company designs BlueBird (BB) satellites with phased arrays up to roughly 2,400 square feet and sells service through revenue-sharing agreements with carriers such as Vodafone, Rakuten, AT&T, and Bell Canada rather than selling phones or retail plans itself. **[Fact]** FY2025 total revenue was **$70.9 million** (+1,505% versus **$4.4 million** in FY2024), while net loss attributable to common stockholders was **$341.9 million**. **[Fact]** At March 31, 2026 the company held **$3.03 billion** in cash and **$2.96 billion** of long-term debt, primarily convertible notes. **[Fact]** Q1 2026 revenue was **$14.7 million** versus **$0.7 million** in Q1 2025.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
**Predictive attribute:** complexity discount (pre-profit satellite platform with convertible debt stack, equity raises, and binary commercial ramp).

**[Inference]** The market may be treating ASTS either as a proven direct-to-cell winner (ignoring launch risk, MNO contract timing, and dilution) or as a perpetual cash incinerator (ignoring VoLTE milestones and the step-up from Block 1 to Block 2 satellites). **[Fact]** Management disclosed first VoLTE voice and SMS over satellite with AT&T in July 2025 and Canada's first space-based 4G VoLTE call with Bell Canada in October 2025, but commercial SpaceMobile service revenue remains early. **[Inference]** FY2025 revenue growth was driven heavily by government services and contract timing, not yet by recurring consumer broadband fees at scale.

## Executive summary
