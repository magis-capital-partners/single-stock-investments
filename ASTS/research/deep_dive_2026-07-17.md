## What this business is

AST SpaceMobile is building a constellation of large low-Earth-orbit satellites that connect directly to unmodified smartphones through partner mobile network operators. **[Fact]** The company designs BlueBird (BB) satellites with phased arrays up to roughly 2,400 square feet and sells service through revenue-sharing agreements with carriers such as Vodafone, Rakuten, AT&T, and Bell Canada rather than selling phones or retail plans itself. **[Fact]** FY2025 total revenue was **$70.9 million** (+1,505% versus **$4.4 million** in FY2024), while net loss attributable to common stockholders was **$341.9 million**. **[Fact]** At March 31, 2026 the company held **$3.03 billion** in cash and **$2.96 billion** of long-term debt, primarily convertible notes. **[Fact]** Q1 2026 revenue was **$14.7 million** versus **$0.7 million** in Q1 2025.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

**Predictive attribute:** complexity discount (pre-profit satellite platform with convertible debt stack, equity raises, and binary commercial ramp).

**[Inference]** The market may be treating ASTS either as a proven direct-to-cell winner (ignoring launch risk, MNO contract timing, and dilution) or as a perpetual cash incinerator (ignoring VoLTE milestones and the step-up from Block 1 to Block 2 satellites). **[Fact]** Management disclosed first VoLTE voice and SMS over satellite with AT&T in July 2025 and Canada's first space-based 4G VoLTE call with Bell Canada in October 2025, but commercial SpaceMobile service revenue remains early. **[Inference]** FY2025 revenue growth was driven heavily by government services and contract timing, not yet by recurring consumer broadband fees at scale.

## Executive summary

At **$57.23** (2026-07-17 close), the base scenario yields roughly **-8% per year** over seven years on a partial commercial ramp path. AST SpaceMobile is a direct-to-cell satellite platform with **$3.0 billion** of cash, **$2.96 billion** of convertible debt, and **291 million** diluted shares after repeated equity raises. FY2025 owner cash was deeply negative (**-$3.42 per share**) because capital spending of **$1.06 billion** dwarfed operating cash flow. Technical milestones with AT&T and Bell Canada de-risk the physics, but moat, dhando, and filing-based owner economics remain unproven at this price. Marvin proposes **watch** until either price falls to a mid-teens annual return on conservative scenarios or filings show durable commercial revenue at scale.

#### Thesis pillars

1. **Direct-to-cell technology de-risking (structural if scaled).** **[Fact]** Block 2 BlueBird satellites launched December 23, 2025 with phased arrays up to ~2,400 square feet. **[Fact]** AT&T VoLTE call July 21, 2025 and Bell Canada 4G VoLTE October 2, 2025 used unmodified smartphones on partner spectrum.

2. **MNO distribution without retail capex (structural).** **[Fact]** Agreements with Vodafone, Rakuten, AT&T, Bell Canada and others provide spectrum and customer relationships; ASTS expects majority of future revenue from revenue-sharing with MNOs once SpaceMobile service is commercial. **[Inference]** Pricing and uptake remain negotiated and uncertain per filing language.

3. **Government services bridge (cyclical / contract).** **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue jumped from **$4.4 million** to **$70.9 million**, largely from government and related services rather than mass-market broadband fees. **[Inference]** This pillar funds near-term cash but is not the long-term compounder path.

4. **Constellation deployment cadence (execution).** **[Fact]** Company targets on the order of **90 satellites** for enhanced continuous coverage. **[Assumption]** Launch and integration delays are the primary swing factor for commercial timing.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Launch failure, satellite anomalies, or MNO partners delaying commercial revenue-share after successful demos.

Additional risks:
- Convertible debt stack (**~$2.96 billion**) creates dilution or refinancing overhang if commercial cash flow lags.
- Share count rose from **~154 million** (FY2023 avg diluted) to **~291 million** (Q1 2026 avg diluted).
- Regulatory and spectrum coordination across jurisdictions may slow global rollout.

**Lens failure mode (asset / optionality):** Treating demo calls as proof of recurring revenue; using cash per share alone as a floor while ignoring convertible claims on equity.
