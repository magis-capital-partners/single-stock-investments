## What this business is

XP Inc. is Brazil's leading independent investment platform. **[Fact]** The company connects retail and institutional clients to mutual funds, fixed income, equities, insurance, credit cards, and lending through a network of independent financial advisors and direct digital channels. **[Fact]** FY2025 client assets reached **R$1,491 billion** with **4.7 million** active clients, roughly **12%** of an estimated **R$8 trillion** addressable market. **[Fact]** Revenue comes from advisory and distribution fees on client assets, brokerage commissions, securities placement, and net income on financial instruments the firm intermediates. XP files as a Brazilian broker-dealer and related subsidiaries; it is not a stock exchange.

## Why the market might be wrong

**[Inference]** The ADR trades near **8–9 times** adjusted earnings despite mid-teens earnings growth and a dominant share position in Brazilian retail wealth. **[Inference]** Investors may lump XP with generic LatAm financials or past governance controversies, underweighting the recurring fee stream tied to **R$1.5 trillion** of client assets now confirmed in the Apr 2026 FY2025 20-F. **[Inference]** Headline total revenue and income (**R$18.4 billion**, +8% versus last year) mixes high-margin service fees with mark-to-market financial instrument income, obscuring the steadier retail distribution engine. Post-filing capital return announcements (buyback and dividend) and a planned CFO transition are not yet in our SEC folder; they may tighten the earnings-yield floor if executed, but we do not move them into base owner cash without a filed 6-K.

## Executive summary

This refresh anchors on the **FY2025 20-F** filed **2026-04-29**, the first full annual filing since onboarding. XP remains Brazil's #1 independent investment platform: **R$1.49 trillion** client assets, **4.7 million** active clients, adjusted net income **R$5.2 billion** (+15%, ~28% margin), and net inflows **R$94.3 billion** in FY2025. Retail is the core profit pool; cards (**R$53 billion** TPV) and lending (**R$35 billion** book) are embedded cross-sell levers. The **Other** segment lost **R$2.5 billion** gross in FY2025 (~13% of revenue), a drag we watch but do not yet haircut in base owner cash. News since the 20-F cites Q1 2026 earnings, a **R$1 billion** buyback through May 2027, a **$0.20** dividend, and a CFO transition; no 6-K is in the folder yet, so those items stay **[PENDING FILING]**. At about **$15.26** per ADR (prior close), our base case implies **25.91%** per year over seven years on roughly **$1.78** adjusted owner cash per share. Asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) is **partial**: earnings yield cushions downside, but Brazil rates, regulation, and IFA competition are real tails. We propose **accumulate** pending human review of currency, governance, and whether to model buyback accretion in owner cash.
