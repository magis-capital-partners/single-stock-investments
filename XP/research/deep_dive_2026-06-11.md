## What this business is

XP Inc. is Brazil's leading independent investment platform. **[Fact]** The company connects retail and institutional clients to mutual funds, fixed income, equities, insurance, credit cards, and lending through a network of independent financial advisors (IFAs) and direct digital channels. **[Fact]** FY2025 client assets reached **R$1,491 billion** with **4.7 million** active clients, roughly **12%** of an estimated **R$8 trillion** addressable market. **[Fact]** Revenue comes from advisory and distribution fees on client assets, brokerage commissions, securities placement, and net income on financial instruments the firm intermediates. XP files as a Brazilian broker-dealer (XP CCTVM) and related subsidiaries; it is not a stock exchange.

## Why the market might be wrong

**[Inference]** The ADR trades near **8–9 times** adjusted earnings despite mid-teens earnings growth and a dominant share position in Brazilian retail wealth. **[Inference]** Investors may lump XP with generic LatAm financials or past governance controversies, underweighting the recurring fee stream tied to **R$1.5 trillion** of client assets. **[Inference]** Headline total revenue and income (**R$18.4 billion**, +8% year over year) mixes high-margin service fees with mark-to-market financial instrument income, obscuring the steadier retail distribution engine. Return is operating earnings at a depressed multiple unless Brazil macro or regulation breaks the client-asset flywheel.

## Executive summary

XP is Brazil's #1 independent investment platform: IFA-led distribution plus direct digital, with **R$1.49 trillion** client assets and **4.7 million** active clients at FY2025. The FY2025 20-F shows total revenue and income of **R$18.4 billion** (+8%), adjusted net income of **R$5.2 billion** (+15%, ~28% margin), and net inflows of **R$94 billion**. Retail remains the core profit pool; cards and lending cross-sell are embedded growth levers. At about **$15.24** per ADR, our base case implies **24.5%** per year over seven years on roughly **$1.78** adjusted owner cash per share, above the 15% hold band. Asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) is **partial**: earnings yield cushions downside, but Brazil rates, regulation, and IFA competition are real tails. We propose **accumulate** pending human review of currency and Other-segment losses.

### Mental models in plain English

**Platform toll (Stahl):** Does XP earn fees without manufacturing the underlying products? **Yes.** Distribution, advisory, brokerage, and placement fees on third-party and proprietary products (`20-F_20260429_rpt20251231_acc0002070979_26_000182.htm`).

**Volume vs share (Stahl):** Is the asset base growing? **Yes.** Client assets rose from **R$1,286 billion** (2024) to **R$1,491 billion** (2025); net inflows **R$94 billion** in FY2025 KPI table (same filing).

**Fee model resilience:** Are revenues recurring? **Partially.** Service fees on client assets are recurring; financial-instrument income is more mark- and rate-sensitive (same filing, revenue footnote).

**Cycle normalization (Hohn):** Are we extrapolating one boom year? **No.** Base case uses FY2025 adjusted net income and moderates growth below FY2025's +15% adjusted earnings pace (`XP/research/valuation.json`).
