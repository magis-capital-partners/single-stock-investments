## What this business is
Xylem Inc. (XYL) is a global water technology company that designs, manufactures, and services pumps, treatment equipment, smart meters, analytics software, and outsourced water solutions for utilities and industrial customers. **[Fact]** The company reports four segments: **Water Infrastructure** ($2,636 million revenue in 2025), **Applied Water** ($1,849 million), **Measurement and Control Solutions** ($2,086 million), and **Water Solutions and Services** ($2,464 million). **[Fact]** FY2025 consolidated revenue was **$9.035 billion** (+5.5% versus $8.562 billion in 2024), operating income **$1,223 million** (13.5% margin), and free cash flow approximately **$910 million** (operating cash flow $1,241 million minus capital spending $331 million). **[Fact]** The Evoqua Water Technologies merger closed **May 24, 2023**, creating the WSS segment and roughly doubling the company's services exposure. Xylem sells through direct utility channels, industrial distributors, and recurring service contracts under brands including Flygt, Goulds, Sensus, Wedeco, and Godwin.

## Why the market might be wrong
The market may treat Xylem as a slow-growth industrial and underweight the post-Evoqua margin trajectory and debt paydown. **[Fact]** Adjusted operating income rose to **$1,612 million** (17.8% margin) in 2025 from **$1,373 million** (16.0%) in 2024; Water Infrastructure operating margin expanded to **17.5%** from **13.9%** and WSS to **12.3%** from **9.3%**. **[Fact]** Long-term debt fell to **$1,408 million** from **$1,978 million** year over year while cash rose to **$1,479 million**. **[Inference]** Generalists may anchor on Measurement segment margin compression (11.7% operating margin versus 13.2% prior year) and premium valuation multiples, missing that consolidated owner cash is growing through mix and integration. There is no dated catalyst; the inefficiency is **descriptive** (quality water compounder at a full price), not an equity yield curve setup.

## Executive summary
Xylem is a global water technology leader with durable utility relationships, specification-driven equipment brands, and a growing outsourced services platform built through the Evoqua combination. FY2025 free cash flow was approximately **$3.73** per share on **$910 million** of free cash flow, and Q1 2026 revenue grew **2.7%** to **$2.125 billion** with diluted earnings per share of **$0.79** versus **$0.69** a year earlier. At **$130.00**, base expected annual return is **-0.3%** per year, below our **15%** hurdle. Asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) is **partial**: debt reduction and dividend growth cushion downside, but premium multiple limits upside asymmetry. We propose **watch**.

### Mental models in plain English

**Installed base (Hohn):** Pumps, treatment skids, and meters stay in service for decades; replacement and aftermarket parts favor incumbents. **[Fact]** Water Infrastructure serves utility and industrial end markets through transport (pumps, pipes) and treatment (filtration, disinfection) applications.

**Moat trajectory (Munger):** Is competitive advantage widening? **Stable, not widening.** Brand specification (Flygt, Goulds, Sensus) and utility relationships defend share. **[Fact]** Measurement operating margin fell to **11.7%** in 2025 from **13.2%** in 2024 despite revenue growth, signaling competitive pressure in analytics and metering. **[Inference]** WSS margin expansion (12.3% operating, 17.1% adjusted) shows integration benefits, but consolidated moat is stable.

**Capital allocation (Pabrai/Munger):** Judge management by actions. **[Fact]** Long-term debt declined **$570 million** in 2025; dividends per share paid increased **11%**; Q1 2026 declared dividend **$0.43** per share. Capital spending was **$331 million** versus **$321 million** prior year. Restructuring and realignment costs were **$133 million** in 2025 as integration continues.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Premium valuation embeds continued margin expansion and mid-single-digit owner-cash growth; any Measurement margin relapse or municipal capex pause forces multiple compression.

- **Measurement margin pressure:** Operating margin **11.7%** in 2025 versus **13.2%** in 2024; smart-metering competition may persist (10-K segment note).
- **Integration and restructuring:** **$133 million** restructuring/realignment in 2025; management guides **$40 to $55 million** for full-year 2026. Charges must convert to sustained margin, not recurring drag.
- **Municipal budget cyclicality:** Water Infrastructure tied to utility capex; delayed projects can defer equipment orders.
- **Leverage and rates:** Total debt ~$1.9 billion including current maturities; interest expense sensitivity if rates stay elevated.
- **Inversion (Munger):** What kills the thesis? Two years of flat revenue with Measurement margins below 10% and rising restructuring costs would downgrade moat toward **eroding** and force bear-case growth.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** If municipal capex slows more than services growth offsets, owner cash growth understates bear risk at today's multiple.
