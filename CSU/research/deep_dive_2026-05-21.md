## Executive summary

Constellation Software is a **serial acquirer of vertical market software (VMS)** businesses — a decentralized holding company that deploys **100% of free cash flow** (FCFA2S) into small acquisitions meeting hurdle rates. FY2025 revenue reached **$11.62B** (+15% YoY) with **FCFA2S of $1.68B** (+14%); GAAP net income fell to **$586M** (−24%) amid earnout accrual volatility and mix. Q1 2026 accelerated: revenue **$3.18B** (+20%), **6% organic growth** (2% adjusted), net income **$400M** (+131% YoY).

**One-line view:** Proven capital-allocation machine with recurring-revenue subsidiaries — **Pabrai-style compounder** if acquisition pipeline and returns on invested capital hold; **not** a simple earnings line item due to M&A accounting noise.

**Thesis status:** **strengthening** on revenue/FCF trajectory; **watch** net income volatility and leverage on CSI facility.

## Archetype — Munger moat + Pabrai compounder

| Lens | Assessment |
|------|------------|
| **Munger — moat** | **Per-business micro-moats:** mission-critical VMS with high switching costs; holding company moat is **capital allocation discipline + decentralized ops**, not one product. |

| **Stahl — spinoffs** | Monitor **Topicus / Lumine / Volaris** orbit — CSU has history of spinning growth platforms when structure adds value (Stahl spinoff lens applies to portfolio structure, not core CSI). |

## Business overview

CSI acquires and holds **VMS** companies — software for narrow verticals (government, healthcare niches, transit, etc.). Revenue types:

- **License** (non-recurring)
- **Maintenance / recurring** (largest quality bucket)
- **Professional services**
- **Hardware / other** (pass-through)

Operating model: **decentralized** — Group Presidents run M&A and oversight; BU managers run day-to-day. Capital policy: reinvest **FCFA2S** into acquisitions, not dividends (by design).

**Fact** — Management objective: invest all FCFA2S in acquisitions meeting **hurdle rate** (defined in shareholder letters).  
**Source:** `Q4-2025-Shareholder-Report.pdf` (MD&A definitions)

## Competitive position & capital allocation

- **Acquisition engine:** Thousands of small targets; CSI competes with other VMS roll-ups (Tyler, Roper-style strategies differ in size/vertical).
- **Hurdle rate discipline:** Stated central to culture; verify ROIC on recent large deals (Topicus track, etc.).
- **Leverage:** CSI Facility / leverage table in MD&A — human to confirm net debt / EBITDA vs. covenant headroom (`CSI-MDA-Q1-2026-Final.pdf`).
- **Spinoff ecosystem:** Topicus, Lumine, Volaris listed entities — CSU retains stakes; portfolio structure adds complexity but unlocks growth capital.
