## What this business is
Hims & Hers Health, Inc. operates a consumer telehealth platform connecting patients to licensed clinicians for chronic and stigmatized conditions (sexual health, hair loss, hormone health, weight loss, dermatology, mental health, and lab testing). **[Fact]** The company fulfills prescriptions through owned and partner pharmacies, including a licensed 503B compounding outsourcing facility, a peptide manufacturing facility, and a laboratory testing facility. **[Fact]** Since founding, the platform has facilitated over **fifty million** telehealth consultations in the United States, Canada, the United Kingdom, and parts of the European Union. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$2.35 billion** (up from **$1.48 billion** in FY2024). **[Fact]** FY2025 operating income was **$105.6 million**, net income **$128.4 million**, and net cash from operating activities **$300.0 million**. **[Fact]** At March 31, 2026, cash was **$222.3 million**, convertible senior notes **$974.1 million**, and stockholders equity **$446.2 million**.

## Why the market might be wrong
**Predictive attribute:** GLP-1 regulatory overhang versus brand scale (compounded weight-loss revenue versus FDA scrutiny and slowing growth).

**[Inference]** The market may price Hims & Hers as a durable subscription compounder because revenue more than doubled in two years and the company reports positive net income and strong operating cash flow. **[Fact]** Q1 2026 revenue was **$608.1 million**, up only **~3.8%** year over year versus **$586.0 million** in Q1 2025, a sharp deceleration from prior triple-digit quarterly growth rates. **[Fact]** The FY2025 10-K states the company received FDA warning letters on compounded semaglutide GLP-1 products and was **directly named** in a February 2026 FDA statement on restricting mass-marketed compounded GLP-1 alternatives. **[Inference]** Investors may underweight how much recent growth depended on compounded weight-loss therapies that face regulatory contraction, and how much ongoing customer acquisition spend is required to hold subscribers in a low-switching-cost DTC market.

## Executive summary
At **$32.84**, the base case is **0.7%** per year over seven years on normalized starting owner cash of **$0.80 per share**. Hims & Hers is a scaled DTC telehealth platform with FY2025 revenue **$2.35 billion**, operating income **$105.6 million**, and operating cash flow **$300 million**, but Q1 2026 revenue growth slowed to **~4%** year over year. The company carries **$974 million** of convertible debt and **~$700 million** of investment securities alongside **$222 million** of cash. Compounded GLP-1 semaglutide is an embedded product line under active FDA scrutiny. Moat is **eroding** (regulatory and competitive pressure on weight loss), dhando is **partial** (liquidity floor versus revenue tail risk), and Marvin proposes **watch** until normalized owner cash growth and regulatory clarity justify the multiple.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** FDA and state regulators restrict compounded GLP-1 semaglutide marketing and fulfillment, removing a major recent growth driver and compressing gross margin.

Secondary risks:

- **Customer acquisition cost inflation** as weight-loss competition intensifies (Ro, branded drugs, retail clinics).
- **Convertible dilution** on **$974M** notes if stock re-rates lower or conversion triggers.
- **Integration and lease burden** from **$137M** operating lease ROU assets and facility expansion (10-K).

**Lens failure mode (operating):** Treating FY2025 operating cash flow **$300M** as sustainable owner cash without subtracting recurring marketing and growth capex (**$226M** productive asset spend in FY2025).

**Inversion test:** What would make this fail? A finalized FDA ban on mass-marketed compounded semaglutide combined with subscriber churn exceeding new customer adds for two consecutive quarters would break the base growth assumption.
