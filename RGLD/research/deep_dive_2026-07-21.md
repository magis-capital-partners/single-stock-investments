## What this business is
Royal Gold is a precious-metals royalty and streaming company headquartered in Denver. **[Fact]** It acquires and holds royalties, streams, and similar interests that entitle it to a share of production or revenue from mines operated by third parties, without Royal Gold acting as the mine operator. **[Fact]** Shares trade on Nasdaq (RGLD). **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$1,030 million**, operating income **$638 million**, and net income **$466 million** (diluted earnings **$6.69** per share) per `investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260219_rpt20251231_acc0000085535_26_000008.htm`. **[Fact]** FY2025 cash from operations was **$705 million**; the balance sheet expanded sharply after the Sandstorm Gold combination (goodwill and deferred taxes rose; total assets **$9.54 billion** at year-end 2025 versus **$3.39 billion** at year-end 2024).

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock may trade as levered gold beta at a full multiple after the Sandstorm combination, while the underlying model is a high-margin toll on ounces with limited sustaining capital spending. **[Inference]** FY2025 earnings power benefited from both metal prices and enlarged attributable production; the market can treat peak-year cash flow as permanent. **[Inference]** Goodwill and deferred tax liabilities from purchase accounting make book value a poor anchor, but the economic asset is the contracted cash stream portfolio, not GAAP intangibles. **[Inference]** Proof-first component math at base case sums to about **$152** per share of economic value versus a price near **$217**, suggesting the market embeds development optionality and mid-cycle gold assumptions not yet reproduced in filing-locked owner-cash paths.

---

## Executive summary
Royal Gold is a high-margin precious-metals royalty and streaming franchise that completed a transformative combination in 2025, roughly doubling revenue and scale. Operating margins remain near **62%** of revenue on FY2025 filings, with strong cash conversion and active debt paydown in early 2026. The business is understandable and the bear case is bounded versus operators (no mine operating risk on balance sheet), but returns at today's price are sensitive to gold price and development timing.

At about **$217** per share, we expect **8.06%** per year over seven years on the synthesis base case (Lawrence owner-cash path **6.7%** per year). Proof-first component economic value base case is **~$152** per share. Royal Gold is a high-margin royalty franchise after the 2025 combination, but the stock does not clear our ~15% accumulate bar at this price. Dhando is **partial**. Stance: **watch**.

### Mental models

**Croupier / toll (Stahl):** Royal Gold earns a fee-like share of mine output without bearing mine-level operating cost inflation or closure liability to the same degree as operators. Volume is production on underlying assets; price is metal price and contract terms.

**Low risk, high uncertainty (Munger):** Limited operating leverage on cost side, but revenue is volatile with gold and silver. Portfolio diversification across operators and jurisdictions reduces single-asset risk but not commodity risk.

**Inversion (Munger):** What breaks the thesis? Sustained lower gold prices, major operator failures on key assets, or overpaying for acquisitions that fail to deliver attributable ounces.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Sustained decline in gold and silver prices reducing revenue on price-linked contracts faster than development ounces replace it.

- Operator delays or cancellations on development assets that underpin growth assumptions and the development inventory component.
- Integration or synergy shortfall from the 2025 combination relative to pro forma expectations in filings.
- Goodwill impairment or tax/accounting noise obscuring true owner cash (monitor deferred tax and acquisition adjustments).
- Rising cost of capital if leverage rises again after the recent **$300M** debt repayment in Q1 FY2026.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** If we are wrong that this is a compounder, it is because we treated a cyclical peak cash year as mid-cycle and paid a growth multiple for a commodity toll.
