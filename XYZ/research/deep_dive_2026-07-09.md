## What this business is

Block, Inc. (NYSE: **XYZ**, formerly Square) runs two linked financial ecosystems for consumers and small businesses. **[Fact]** **Cash App** is a consumer wallet for peer-to-peer payments, direct deposit, debit card, stock and bitcoin trading, and buy-now-pay-later via Afterpay. **[Fact]** **Square** (seller) provides payment processing, point-of-sale hardware, payroll, banking, and software for merchants. **[Fact]** FY2025 consolidated revenue was **$11.5 billion** (up **10%** versus last year) and gross profit was **$10.4 billion**. **[Fact]** The company reports two segments and had **59 million** monthly transacting actives on Cash App at year-end 2025. **[Inference]** Owner cash is real (FY2025 operating cash flow **$2.58 billion**), but a large share of headline revenue is low-margin bitcoin activity that contributes only **2%** of gross profit in Q1 2026.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

There is **no classic dated mispricing signal** (HK predictive attribute: **none**). **[Inference]** The market still debates Block as a fintech growth stock with bitcoin optionality, while reported revenue mixes in pass-through bitcoin volumes that overstate economic earnings power. **[Fact]** Excluding bitcoin ecosystem revenue, Cash App net revenue rose **20%** in FY2025 and **37%** in Q1 2026. **[Inference]** Generalists may overweight bitcoin revenue lines and underweight the shift toward higher-margin Cash App financial services and Square software attach. **[Inference]** Bears may underappreciate Square's **$250 billion+** annualized gross payment volume and improving gross profit trajectory (+**27%** consolidated in Q1 2026). Return here is **normalized owner cash at price**, not a sum-of-the-parts on bitcoin holdings alone.

## Executive summary

Block connects **59 million** consumer actives and millions of sellers through Cash App and Square. FY2025 operating cash flow was **$2.58 billion**; after **$155 million** of capital spending, owner cash is about **$3.89 per share**. Q1 2026 gross profit grew **27%** year over year, led by Cash App (+**38%**), while Square gross payment volume rose **13%**.

At about **$77** (Yahoo Finance, 2026-07-09), the Lawrence base case implies a low-single-digit annual return on normalized owner cash. The business has a **stable** durable advantage (Munger moat) on integrated consumer and seller rails, with **partial** asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando): strong gross profit growth, but equity is sensitive to consumer credit losses, bitcoin volatility, and multiple compression. Marvin proposes **watch** until price offers a better return or third-party work is indexed. No approved external sources are in the base case.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** **Consumer credit deterioration** on BNPL and Cash App lending book drives provision spikes that outpace gross profit growth.

- **Bitcoin regulation or engagement fade** compresses a high-visibility revenue line (even if low gross profit).
- **Competitive margin pressure** in merchant acquiring from larger processors and vertical software bundles.
- **GAAP noise** from corporate bitcoin marks and tax items obscures operating trends quarter to quarter.

**Inversion:** If we are wrong, Block is a durable dual-network platform priced for perfection: you earn low-single-digit returns while gross profit grows but the market never pays up for ex-bitcoin quality.

**Lens failure mode:** Operating lens fails if consumer receivables are mispriced on balance sheet (asset lens) or if bitcoin treasury becomes the dominant value driver (optionality lens). Current filings do not support switching the primary lens.
