## What this business is
Yum! Brands, Inc. (YUM) is one of the world's largest quick-service restaurant franchisors, operating four global concepts: **KFC**, **Taco Bell**, **Pizza Hut**, and **Habit Burger & Grill**. **[Fact]** As of December 31, 2025, the system comprised **63,285** restaurants in **155** countries and territories; **97%** of units are franchised. **[Fact]** FY2025 **system sales** were **$68.3 billion** (+4% reported); **reported revenues** (franchise fees, royalties, company-store sales, and advertising contributions) were **$8.21 billion** (+8.8% versus $7.55 billion in FY2024). **[Fact]** FY2025 cash from operations was **$2.01 billion**; capital spending was **$371 million**; free cash flow was approximately **$1.64 billion**, or about **$5.83** per diluted share on **281 million** shares. The company collects royalties and franchise fees on franchisee sales rather than operating most stores directly.

## Why the market might be wrong
There is **no dated mispricing catalyst** (HK predictive attribute: **none**). **[Inference]** Generalists may anchor on GAAP net income (**$1.56 billion**, flat versus $1.49 billion prior year) and elevated leverage (**$11.9 billion** long-term debt) while underweighting the asset-light franchise cash engine: **$68.3 billion** of system sales converting to **$2.01 billion** of operating cash flow with limited capital intensity. **[Fact]** Same-store sales turned positive at **+3%** in FY2025 after **-1%** in FY2024; Taco Bell U.S. momentum and international KFC unit growth are the mix drivers (10-K FY2025). **[Fact]** Q1 2026 revenues rose **15%** year over year to **$2.06 billion** with diluted EPS **$1.55** versus **$0.90** prior year, but lapping and tax timing affect quarterly comparability. **[Inference]** The inefficiency is **descriptive** (quality franchise compounder at a full price with leverage), not a timed equity yield-curve setup.

## Executive summary
Yum! Brands is a global QSR franchisor: **$68.3 billion** of FY2025 system sales across KFC, Taco Bell, Pizza Hut, and Habit, with **97%** franchised units and **$1.64 billion** of FY2025 free cash flow (~**$5.83** per share). Same-store sales were **+3%** in FY2025; system sales grew **+4%** reported. Taco Bell (**$18.4 billion** system sales, **9,030** units) and KFC (**$36.4 billion**, **33,897** units) carry the portfolio; Pizza Hut (**$12.8 billion**, **19,974** units) remains the relative laggard.

At about **$165.13** per share (Yahoo Finance, 2026-07-10), the Lawrence base case implies about **3.52%** per year on normalized owner cash of **$5.83** per share, below our **15%** accumulate hurdle. Durable competitive advantage (Munger moat) is **stable**: global brand scale, franchisee network effects, and advertising cooperatives, but Pizza Hut share pressure prevents moat widening. Asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) is **partial**: strong franchise cash generation and **$2.84** dividend per share bound distress, but **$11.9 billion** of debt and ~28 times free cash flow at spot cap upside. Marvin proposes **watch**.

Yum! is a **multi-sided franchise platform** (Lawrence bucket): the company orchestrates brands, national advertising, supply agreements, and digital infrastructure while franchisees put up store capital and operating labor. Reported revenue is a thin slice of **$68.3 billion** system sales.

### Thesis pillars

1. **Asset-light scale:** **63,285** units, **97%** franchised; FY2025 capital spending only **$371 million** on **$2.01 billion** operating cash flow (10-K FY2025 cash flow statement).
2. **Taco Bell U.S. engine:** **9,030** Taco Bell units with **$18.4 billion** system sales; **86%** of units in the U.S.; company owns **8%** of U.S. units for innovation and training (10-K segment note).
3. **KFC international growth:** **33,897** KFC units (**90%** outside U.S., **99%** franchised); **$36.4 billion** system sales; division operating profit leader (10-K).
4. **Capital returns:** FY2025 repurchased **$552 million** of stock and paid **$789 million** of dividends (**$2.84** per share declared) while carrying **$11.9 billion** long-term debt (10-K).

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Pizza Hut continues losing share to delivery-native competitors, triggering franchisee closures and royalty pressure faster than KFC/Taco Bell growth can offset. **[Fact]** Pizza Hut system sales **$12.8 billion** on **19,974** units versus Taco Bell **$18.4 billion** on only **9,030** units implies weaker unit productivity (10-K FY2025).

**Secondary risks:**
- **Leverage:** **$11.9 billion** long-term debt constrains buybacks in a rate or earnings shock (10-K FY2025).
- **Habit drag:** Company-owned Habit units (**79%** of U.S. Habit) with minimal division profit (~**$13 million** operating profit on **$706 million** system sales) (10-K segment note).
- **Franchisee relations:** Advertising fund disputes or franchisee financial stress could slow unit development (10-K risk factors).

**Inversion (Munger):** What would make this fail? Same-store sales turn negative for four consecutive quarters across divisions, net debt rises above **$13 billion** without EBITDA growth, and Pizza Hut net unit count declines for two years. Monitor same-store sales by division, net unit growth, and net debt each quarter.
