## What this business is
The AES Corporation is a global power company that builds, owns, and operates generation assets and two U.S. regulated utilities. **[Fact]** It reports four strategic units: Renewables, Utilities (AES Indiana and AES Ohio), Energy Infrastructure, and New Energy Technologies (`AES/investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260302_rpt20251231_acc0000874761_26_000063.htm`). **[Fact]** FY2025 consolidated revenue was about **$12.2 billion** and segment Adjusted EBITDA totaled about **$2.9 billion**. **[Fact]** AES signed **4.0 gigawatts** of new renewables contracts in 2025 and ended the year with a **12.0 gigawatt** contracted backlog not yet in operation. **[Fact]** On **March 1, 2026**, AES agreed to be acquired for **$15.00 per share** in cash by a buyer group led by Global Infrastructure Partners and EQT Infrastructure VI (`AES/investor-documents/sec-edgar/8-K_20260302_rpt20260301_acc0001193125_26_084157.htm`).

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock now trades primarily as a **merger arbitrage** spread to **$15.00**, not as a standalone renewables compounder. **[Inference]** Short-horizon holders may overweight regulatory delay risk (Ohio PUCO, FERC, CFIUS, and foreign approvals named in the merger filing) and underweight the board's unanimous recommendation plus committed equity financing not conditioned on debt markets. **[Inference]** Pre-deal investors who owned AES for dividend growth may exit into a take-private, creating transient selling unrelated to deal break probability. **[Opinion]** The edge, if any, is timing and spread discipline, not a hidden standalone re-rating while the merger is pending.

---

## Executive summary
AES is a contracted renewables and U.S. utility platform now subject to a **$15.00** all-cash take-private by GIP and EQT, expected to close in **late 2026 or early 2027**. At today's price near **$14.73** per share, Marvin's base merger-arbitrage path implies about **7.1%** annualized return over roughly nine months, below our **~15%** bar. The **12 gigawatt** backlog and AES Ohio data-center load contracts support the strategic logic of the deal, but regulatory timing and spread compression limit upside. Standalone owner cash remains negative after growth capital spending, so the operating case is secondary while the merger is live. **Stance: watch** pending a wider spread, faster close visibility, or human approval to size arb risk.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Regulatory or political rejection of the GIP/EQT transaction without a higher competing bid, forcing a break and repricing toward standalone fundamentals.

**Inversion checklist:**

- If PUCO or FERC imposes burdensome conditions, closing slips past the **June 1, 2027** outside date and the spread widens.
- If growth capex stays elevated after a broken deal, equity holders fund construction without public-market access.
- If Latin American currency or commodity shocks hit Energy Infrastructure earnings, standalone support for the equity weakens.

**Lens failure mode (event lens):** Thesis fails if the merger does not close and no superior proposal emerges; the equity becomes a negative near-term cash-flow developer story again.
