## What this business is

The AES Corporation is a global power company that builds, owns, and operates generation assets and two U.S. regulated utilities. **[Fact]** It reports four strategic units: Renewables, Utilities (AES Indiana and AES Ohio), Energy Infrastructure, and New Energy Technologies (`AES/investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260302_rpt20251231_acc0000874761_26_000063.htm`). **[Fact]** FY2025 consolidated revenue was about **$12.2 billion** and segment Adjusted EBITDA totaled about **$2.9 billion**. **[Fact]** AES signed **4.0 gigawatts** of new renewables contracts in 2025 and ended the year with a **12.0 gigawatt** contracted backlog not yet in operation. **[Fact]** On **March 1, 2026**, AES agreed to be acquired for **$15.00 per share** in cash by a buyer group led by Global Infrastructure Partners and EQT Infrastructure VI (`AES/investor-documents/sec-edgar/8-K_20260302_rpt20260301_acc0001193125_26_084157.htm`).

## Why the market might be wrong

The stock now trades primarily as a **merger arbitrage** spread to **$15.00**, not as a standalone renewables compounder. **[Inference]** Short-horizon holders may overweight regulatory delay risk (Ohio PUCO, FERC, CFIUS, and foreign approvals named in the merger filing) and underweight the board's unanimous recommendation plus committed equity financing not conditioned on debt markets. **[Inference]** Pre-deal investors who owned AES for dividend growth may exit into a take-private, creating transient selling unrelated to deal break probability. **[Opinion]** The edge, if any, is timing and spread discipline, not a hidden standalone re-rating while the merger is pending.

## Executive summary

AES is a contracted renewables and U.S. utility platform now subject to a **$15.00** all-cash take-private by GIP and EQT, expected to close in **late 2026 or early 2027**. At today's price near **$14.25** per share, Marvin's base merger-arbitrage path implies about **7%** annualized return over roughly nine months, below our **~15%** bar. The **12 gigawatt** backlog and AES Ohio data-center load contracts support the strategic logic of the deal, but regulatory timing and spread compression limit upside. Standalone owner cash remains negative after growth capital spending, so the operating case is secondary while the merger is live. **Stance: watch** pending a wider spread, faster close visibility, or human approval to size arb risk.

## Business & moat

### What (Stahl + Lawrence bucket)

**[Fact]** AES is an **infrastructure** business (Lawrence bucket: regulated utility plus contracted generation): it sells electricity under long-term contracts and regulated tariffs rather than as a merchant trader. **[Fact]** FY2025 segment Adjusted EBITDA was approximately **$932 million** (Renewables), **$863 million** (Utilities), **$1,130 million** (Energy Infrastructure), and **$(35) million** (New Energy Technologies), totaling **$2,890 million**. **[Fact]** Net income attributable to AES was **$910 million** in FY2025 versus **$1,679 million** in FY2024. **[Fact]** Operating cash flow was about **$4.3 billion** while capital expenditures were about **$5.9 billion**, so reported free cash flow was negative after growth spending. **[Fact]** Shares outstanding were about **713.1 million** at March 31, 2026.

### Mental models

- **Regulated utility floor (Munger moat):** AES Indiana and AES Ohio earn allowed returns set by state commissions; this caps upside but supports baseline cash availability.
- **Contracted generation toll (Stahl infrastructure):** Renewables PPAs with corporates and utilities convert power demand into long-duration revenue streams; backlog visibility is the growth engine.
- **Inversion:** What kills the equity? Merger failure, regulatory rejection with no superior bid, or leverage stress if the deal breaks and growth capex continues.

### Business mechanics (Hohn)

**Operating snapshot.** AES develops renewables and gas assets globally, operates U.S. wires-and-generation utilities, and incubates technology bets (Fluence storage JV, Uplight, Maximo). Growth in 2025 came from signing new contracts and converting backlog to operating megawatts, not from spot power price spikes.

**Thesis pillars (standalone, now secondary to merger).**

1. **Backlog conversion:** **12.0 GW** signed but not operating underpins multi-year revenue growth (**[Fact]** 10-K FY2025).
2. **Data-center load:** **2.1 GW** AES Ohio data-center growth plus **310 MW** retail supply contracts signed in 2024 (**[Fact]** 424B5 March 2025).
3. **Corporate PPA leadership:** BloombergNEF ranked AES among the top global sellers of renewable power to corporates (**[Fact]** 10-K FY2025).
4. **Regulated rate relief:** AES Indiana received IURC approval for new base rates and **9.9%** allowed ROE (**[Fact]** 424B5 March 2025).

**Structural vs cyclical.** Backlog and data-center demand are structural; commodity power prices and Latin American macro remain cyclical overlays on Energy Infrastructure earnings.

**Disruption / cycle.** AES is mid-cycle in renewables build-out: heavy capital spending today funds contracted cash flows later. Coal exit program reduces legacy fuel risk but requires replacement capacity spending.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Regulatory or political rejection of the GIP/EQT transaction without a higher competing bid, forcing a break and repricing toward standalone fundamentals.

**Inversion checklist:**

- If PUCO or FERC imposes burdensome conditions, closing slips past the **June 1, 2027** outside date and the spread widens.
- If growth capex stays elevated after a broken deal, equity holders fund construction without public-market access.
- If Latin American currency or commodity shocks hit Energy Infrastructure earnings, standalone support for the equity weakens.

**Lens failure mode (event lens):** Thesis fails if the merger does not close and no superior proposal emerges; the equity becomes a negative near-term cash-flow developer story again.
