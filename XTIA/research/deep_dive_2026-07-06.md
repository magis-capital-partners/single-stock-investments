## What this business is

XTI Aerospace is a micro-cap company pivoting from legacy indoor-positioning software (formerly Inpixon) into unmanned aerial systems. **[Fact]** The company completed a reverse merger with Legacy XTI Aircraft Company in March 2025, acquired Drone Nerds (an enterprise drone distributor) through an 83.4%-owned subsidiary, and discontinued the old Inpixon business. **[Fact]** Today, substantially all revenue comes from Drone Nerds: hardware distribution, training, and repair across wholesale, direct, and retail channels. **[Fact]** Two development divisions, Autonomous Defense Systems and domestic manufacturing, have not generated revenue. **[Fact]** The TriFan 600 VTOL aircraft program is paused while management focuses on nearer-term drone economics.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The stock may still trade like a failed SPAC shell rather than a operating drone distributor with accelerating revenue. **[Inference]** Q1 2026 revenue was **$27.7 million**, up **68%** versus Q1 2025, yet the share price near **$1.59** implies a modest enterprise value once cash is netted against debt and warrant overhang. **[Inference]** GAAP net loss of **$35.3 million** in Q1 2026 includes a **$21.4 million** non-cash warrant fair-value charge, which screens out on simple earnings metrics. **[Inference]** Complexity discount applies: reverse merger history, multiple preferred series, warrant liabilities, and negative stockholders equity keep generalist investors away even as Drone Nerds scales.

## Executive summary

XTI Aerospace is a turnaround pivoting from defunct indoor-positioning software into commercial drone distribution through its 83.4% stake in Drone Nerds, plus pre-revenue defense and manufacturing options. FY2025 revenue was **$22.5 million**; Q1 2026 revenue accelerated to **$27.7 million** (+68% year over year) while the company still reported a **$10.4 million** operating loss and negative stockholders equity. Cash was **$15.2 million** (~**$0.39** per share) at March 31, 2026, against a **$64.9 million** warrant liability that dominates the balance sheet.

At **$1.59** per share (July 6, 2026 close), our base scenario implies about **7% per year** over five years on a **$2.25** equity payoff if Drone Nerds reaches modest profitability and dilution stabilizes. Dhando is **none**: cash per share is far below price and operating burn continues. Moat is **unproven** in a fragmented distributor market. Stance: **watch**. Bear case assumes further dilution toward **$0.35** per share; bull case assumes a defense contract plus distribution scale toward **$5.50** **[Assumption]**.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Warrant liability and continued equity issuance dilute common holders faster than Drone Nerds operating income can compound per-share value.

- Operating losses persist while ADS and manufacturing divisions consume overhead without revenue
- Going-concern language in FY2025 10-K if capital markets close
- Supply-chain concentration on foreign drone OEMs despite NDAA compliance push
- Integration risk from Drone Nerds acquisition and minority Class B unitholders (16.6%)
- Short-term debt **$10.6 million** plus asset-based credit covenants
- Negative stockholders equity limits strategic flexibility

**Inversion:** What would make this fail? Revenue growth stalls, warrants convert at depressed prices, and cash falls below working-capital needs within 12–18 months.

**Lens failure mode:** Treating XTIA as a normalized **compounder** on trailing GAAP earnings misprices the business; it is an event-driven turnaround with a levered capital structure, not steady owner cash.
