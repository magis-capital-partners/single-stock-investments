## What this business is

Trinity Place Holdings Inc. is the legal successor to Syms Corp., the off-price retailer that liquidated more than a decade ago. **[Fact]** Until May 2025 it owned an indirect real estate joint venture in Greenwich, Connecticut and other legacy retail sites. **[Fact]** On May 20, 2025 the company transferred its 95 percent interest in that joint venture (a New Jersey property held through TPHGreenwich Holdings LLC) into a separate liquidating trust for the benefit of the people who held Trinity Place stock on that date. **[Fact]** Today Trinity Place has no operating business. **[Fact]** It owns a handful of dormant retail trademarks (FilenesBasement.com, the Stanley Blacker brand, Running of the Brides, and a slogan) and a very large tax loss carryforward, and it is controlled and financially supported by an affiliate of Steel Partners Holdings L.P. **[Fact]**

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The stock trades on OTC Pink at about two cents a share and a market value near $1.5 million, priced like a dead shell. **[Fact]** The predictive attribute here is a **dormant asset** (Horizon Kinetics lens): the company's charter still protects roughly $330 million of federal and state net operating loss carryforwards against an ownership change, which only makes sense if the controlling holder, Steel Partners, sees future use for them. **[Inference]** No transaction is disclosed as of this writing, so this is a real option on Steel Partners' capital-allocation playbook, not a dated catalyst with a known date. **[Inference]**

---

## Executive summary

Trinity Place is no longer a real estate company. It gave up its remaining property interest to a trust for its former shareholders in May 2025 and is now a bare-bones shell: about $54 thousand of cash, a small IP licensing trickle, and a **$1.4 million** secured loan from a Steel Partners affiliate that keeps the lights on. Book value per share is negative. The only reason this shell has any value at all is a large, protected tax loss carryforward that Steel Partners could someday use if it drops a profitable business into the company. Nothing like that is announced today. Treating this as a long-dated, largely speculative option on Steel Partners' intentions, the base case works out to about **14.4%** per year over five years, but the bear case is closer to a near-total loss, and there is no cash or book-value floor under the common stock. Stance: **watch**. This is a name to track for a catalyst, not to size as a core position.

#### Catalyst path (event-driven)

- Base path: Steel Partners keeps the shell alive, funds it with note draws, and slowly grows the small IP licensing income, with no committed transaction disclosed as of 2026-07-17.
- Bull path: Steel Partners contributes or merges a taxpaying operating business into the shell to use part of the net operating loss carryforward, mirroring what it has done with other holding-company subsidiaries over the years.
- Failure mode: the note keeps growing, dilution or a going-private transaction squeezes out minority holders, and the loss carryforwards eventually go unused.

### Moat (Munger)

There is no moat because there is no business. **[Fact]** The only durable feature is legal and structural: the charter's 4.75 percent ownership-change restriction protects the tax attribute from being destroyed by trading activity, and Steel Partners' control gives it the exclusive practical ability to decide what happens to the shell next. That is a controlling-holder advantage, not a competitive advantage, and it cuts both ways for minority shareholders. **[Inference]**

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Steel Partners never discloses a use for the net operating loss shell, the promissory note keeps growing to fund losses, and minority shareholders are diluted or squeezed out with little to show for the tax asset.

- The company could be taken private or deregistered (it has already signaled intent to pursue deregistration steps per `TPHS/investor-documents/sec-edgar/8-K_20250205_rpt20250205_acc0001104659_25_009671.htm`) with a low-ball minority buyout.
- The Steel Promissory Note is secured by all company assets; if it is not repaid or converted on favorable terms, equity could be wiped out in a formal wind-down.
- Section 382 ownership-change rules limit how much of the $329.9 million federal loss carryforward could actually be used in any given year even if a transaction happens, so the option is worth much less than the face amount suggests.

**Lens failure mode (asset):** If the dormant asset (the loss carryforward) is never activated by a real transaction, the common stock is simply a melting shell funded by growing related-party debt, worth close to zero.

### Optionality overlay

**GAAP vs economic floor:** GAAP book value per share is already negative, and the Steel Promissory Note is secured by all of the company's assets ahead of common equity. That means book value is not a floor at all here; it is already below zero. The only real economic asset the market can be underpricing is the off-balance-sheet net operating loss carryforward, which carries a full valuation allowance and therefore shows up as zero net asset on the balance sheet. `nav_overlay` in `valuation.json` treats that carryforward as a probability-weighted option, not a hard floor, because no transaction is disclosed and Section 382 would cap annual usage well below the $329.9 million face amount even if one occurs.

#### IRR arithmetic (show your work)

How we calculated the annual return:

1. Start with price today **$0.023** per share.
2. Base payoff in year 5: **$0.045** per share (see Assumption 9).
3. Total multiple: 0.045 / 0.023 = 1.957.
4. Annualized: 1.957 to the power of one-fifth, minus one = **14.4%** per year.
5. Bear check: 0.005 / 0.023 over 5 years is about **negative 26.3%** per year.
6. Bull check: 0.120 / 0.023 over 5 years is about **39.2%** per year.
7. There is no soft cash floor: cash of $54 thousand across 64.9 million shares is about **$0.0008** per share, and the secured note ranks ahead of it.

**Upside / downside from price:** Base implies owning a speculative option on Steel Partners' future use of the shell at roughly a two-times payoff over five years; bear is a near-total, uncapped loss because there is no floor beneath the secured note.

**Returns statement:** At today's price, the base case earns **14.4%** per year if a **$0.045** per share risked payoff arrives in five years.

Filings confirm the shell mechanics, the note, and the size of the loss carryforward, but they do not support a committed monetization plan or a firm timeline. Until Steel Partners discloses a transaction, the base case stays at this speculative **14.4%** dated-payoff estimate rather than a filing-backed forecast, and stance stays at **watch** regardless of the headline percentage because the bear case has no floor.

## Terms (this report)

| Term | Meaning here |
|------|----------------|
| Net operating loss (NOL) carryforward | Past tax losses a company can use to offset future taxable income; here, about $330 million of federal and state losses that only have value if Trinity Place (or an acquirer through it) eventually earns taxable profit |
| Section 382 | The tax rule that limits how much of a loss carryforward a company can use each year after a big change in who owns its stock; the reason the charter restricts transfers above 4.75 percent |
