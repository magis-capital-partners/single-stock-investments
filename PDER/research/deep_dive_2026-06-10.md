## What this business is
Pardee Resources Company is a family-controlled natural-resource holding company that owns and manages coal, oil and gas, timber, solar, and agriculture assets across Appalachia, Colorado, California, and Portugal. **[Fact]** The company reports five operating divisions: metallurgical coal royalties, oil and gas royalties, timber and surface leases, alternative energy (solar), and agriculture (California table grapes and Portugal almonds). **[Fact]** At December 31, 2025 Pardee had **651,498** common shares outstanding, **$134.2 million** of shareholders' equity (**$205.44** book value per share), and **$27.9 million** of cash. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenues from operations were **$46.9 million**; net income attributable to Pardee shareholders was **$16.4 million** (**$25.09** earnings per share), boosted by an **$8.9 million** gain on the sale of dormant Pennsylvania oil and gas minerals. **[Fact]** The stock trades OTC under symbol PDER; only **~7%** of shares traded in 2025, and the company cautions that the market is sporadic and thin.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The market may treat PDER as a passive coal royalty trust whose earnings collapsed when metallurgical coal weakened, missing the diversified royalty portfolio and periodic asset monetization that have produced a **14.4%** compounded annual shareholder return over ten years (with dividends reinvested, per the 2025 annual report). **[Fact]** FY2025 EBITDA per share was **$41.49**, far above the **$7.20** regular dividend, and management paid a **$15.00** special dividend in addition to the regular payout. **[Inference]** At **~$274** (June 10, 2026), the stock trades at roughly **33%** above GAAP book (**~$205** per share), so the OTC quote may already embed option value on undeveloped coal reserves, Roanoke solar investments, and retained mineral rights on **47,000** acres of West Virginia hardwood timber that failed to sell in 2025. **[Inference]** Predictive attribute: **dormant asset** monetization plus **market structure discount** (OTC Pink, thin float, no SEC 10-K screen inclusion).

## Executive summary
Pardee Resources is a multi-division natural-resource royalty company: Appalachian met coal and gas, timber and rural land sales, California table grapes, Portugal almonds, and a growing solar portfolio including a **$14.4 million** Roanoke, Virginia school rooftop investment closed in 2025. FY2025 operating cash fell to **$9.2 million** from **$16.4 million** in 2024 as coal and timber markets softened, while a one-time **$8.9 million** mineral sale inflated reported earnings. Q1 2026 earnings per share fell **54%** year over year as a major coal lessee idled operations in February 2026. At **~$274**, our base case annual return is **2.34%** per year (seven-year total synthesis on normalized owner cash of **$16.00** per share; Lawrence filing path **3.6%** before synthesis weights). Book equity provides a partial reference point, but the stock trades above book, limiting dhando. We propose **watch** until coal production stabilizes and normalized distributable cash again supports the premium valuation.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Metallurgical coal royalty collapse if idled mines stay offline and Chinese steel export competition persists.

- **Coal idling (Feb 2026):** Major lessee temporarily idled a mine complex; Q1 2026 coal revenues **$2.5M** vs **$3.3M** prior year.
- **Premium valuation:** At **~$274** vs book **~$205**, the market may already price reserve optionality; downside if specials stop.

- **Agriculture weather:** Portugal almond losses in 2025; California drought or frost can swing grape economics.

**Lens failure mode (asset payoff lens):** Thesis fails if undeveloped coal and timber options never convert to cash royalties and the company becomes a declining dividend stream on depleted reserves.
