## What this business is

A. O. Smith Corporation (AOS) is a global manufacturer of residential and commercial water heaters, boilers, heat pumps, tanks, and water treatment products. **[Fact]** The company reports two segments: **North America** (~78% of 2025 net sales) and **Rest of World** (primarily China, India, and Europe). **[Fact]** FY2025 consolidated net sales were **$3.83 billion** (flat versus $3.82 billion in 2024), net earnings **$546.2 million**, and free cash flow **$546.0 million**. **[Fact]** North America segment earnings were **$727.9 million** on **$2.98 billion** of sales (24.4% margin); Rest of World earned **$76.4 million** on **$880.4 million** of sales (8.7% margin). The company sells through roughly **800** independent wholesale plumbing distributors plus national retail and maintenance channels under brands including A. O. Smith, State, and Lochinvar.

## Why the market might be wrong

The market may treat AOS as a mature, housing-cyclical name and underweight its pricing power and capital return. **[Fact]** Gross margin rose to **38.8%** in 2025 from **38.1%** in 2024 on early-year pricing actions and higher North America boiler mix. **[Fact]** Share count fell from **151.0 million** diluted shares in 2023 to **141.9 million** in 2025 while the company repurchased **5.9 million** shares for **$400.8 million** in 2025. **[Inference]** Generalists may anchor on flat revenue and Q1 2026 earnings softness (diluted EPS **$0.85** versus **$0.95** in Q1 2025) and miss that owner cash is growing through margin, buybacks, and lower capital spending after 2024 capacity projects. There is no dated catalyst; the inefficiency is **descriptive** (quality compounder near fair price), not an equity yield curve setup.

## Executive summary

A. O. Smith is the leading North American water heater manufacturer with durable brands, wholesale distribution, and a high-margin domestic franchise offset by slower Rest of World growth. FY2025 free cash flow was **$3.85** per share on **$546.0 million** of free cash flow, and management guides 2026 consolidated sales up **2–5%** with free cash flow of **$525–575 million**. At **$59.02** (2026-07-10), base expected annual return at today's price is **13.9%** per year on filed owner cash, below our **15%** bar. Durable competitive advantage (Munger moat) is **stable**: scale, brand, and channel relationships in North America; China remains competitive and lower margin. Asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) is **partial**: housing-cycle risk bounds upside if volumes stall, but buybacks and dividends cushion downside. We propose **watch**.

### Mental models in plain English

**Replacement cycle (Hohn):** Water heaters fail on a predictable life cycle; new construction adds volume but replacement is the larger steady base. **[Fact]** The 10-K describes North America as the largest manufacturer and marketer of water heaters with a leading share in residential and commercial markets.

**Moat trajectory (Munger):** Is competitive advantage widening? **Stable in North America.** Brand, distribution breadth (~800 wholesalers), and product breadth (Lochinvar boilers, heat pumps, water treatment) defend share. **[Inference]** Rest of World is more fragmented; 2024 China restructuring ($11.3 million severance) shows margin pressure is real.

**Capital allocation (Pabrai/Munger):** Judge management by actions. **[Fact]** 2025 repurchases **$400.8 million**, dividends **$1.38** per share (six percent increase in Q4 2025), capital spending **$70.8 million** down from **$108.0 million** in 2024 after Juarez, McBee, and Lebanon projects.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** U.S. residential construction and replacement volumes soften while the stock still prices in mid-single-digit owner-cash growth.

- **Input cost inflation:** Steel and copper spikes without timely price pass-through compress North America margin (10-K risk factors).
- **China competition:** Rest of World sales fell to **$880.4 million** in 2025 from **$918.6 million** in 2024; margin recovery may stall.
- **Regulatory product transitions:** DOE efficiency rules create 2026 buy-ahead benefit then normalization risk.
- **Inversion (Munger):** What kills the thesis? Two years of declining North America unit volumes plus inability to raise price would force bear-case growth and erode moat toward **eroding**.

**Lens failure mode (operating):** If housing-linked volumes weaken more than replacement demand supports, owner cash growth understates bear risk at today's multiple.
