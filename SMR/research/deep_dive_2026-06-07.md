## What this business is
NuScale Power develops small modular reactor (SMR) nuclear technology and licenses its NuScale Power Module design. **[Fact]** The company has **one** operating segment (10-K FY2025). **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$23.9 million**, up from **$4.2 million** in FY2024, primarily from related-party engineering services to Fluor (**76.0%** of revenue in 2025). **[Fact]** Operating loss was approximately **$690 million** and cash was **$836 million** at December 31, 2025 (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260226_rpt20251231_acc0001822966_26_000018.htm`). **[Fact]** Class A shares outstanding were approximately **318.5 million** at year-end. **[Fact]** The company is pre-revenue on reactor electricity sales; it earns licensing and engineering fees while pursuing deployment partnerships.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The market may treat SMR as a proven nuclear utility rather than a pre-deployment licensor with concentrated customer risk. **[Inference]** Positive GAAP net income (**$356 million**) reflects warrant and derivative accounting, not sustainable owner cash. **[Inference]** ENTRA1's non-binding **72 NPM** agreement and Romania's Doicesti approval are milestones, not contracted reactor cash flows yet. **[Inference]** **$339 million** of contractual obligations due in 2026, including **$260 million** PMA milestone contribution, may require dilution if not funded by partners.

---

## Executive summary
NuScale is a pre-revenue SMR licensor with the only NRC-certified modular reactor design at commercial scale and **$836 million** of cash on the balance sheet. Revenue today is engineering fees to Fluor, not electricity sales. The stock is a deployment option on international partnerships such as ENTRA1 and Romania Doicesti, not a utility dividend. At **~$15** per share we expect **2.2% per year** over seven years on our base payoff path, below our accumulate hurdle. Moat **unproven**. Dhando **none**. Stance **watch**.
