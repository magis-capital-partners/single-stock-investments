## What this business is
Bolsa Mexicana de Valores (Grupo BMV) is Mexico's dominant market infrastructure operator. **[Fact]** The listed parent earns toll-like fees across cash equity trading and clearing, derivatives (MexDer and Asigna), over-the-counter brokerage (SIF ICAP), listing and maintenance, central securities depository custody (Indeval), and market data (Valmer). **[Fact]** FY2025 consolidated revenue was **MXN 4,470 million** (+7% versus 2024), operating income **MXN 2,252 million** (+4%), EBITDA **MXN 2,512 million** (56% margin), and net income **MXN 1,602 million** (-2%) (`investor-documents/ir-bmv/quarterly_report_4q2025.pdf`). **[Fact]** Q1 2026 revenue was **MXN 1,212 million** (+7% year over year) with EBITDA margin **56.5%** (`investor-documents/ir-bmv/press_release_1q2026.pdf`). The business puts little principal capital at risk on core exchange operations; revenue scales with trading volume, listings, custody balances, and data subscriptions.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
No dated mispricing catalyst. **[Inference]** Nearshoring and a deeper local capital markets pool could extend volume and listing growth beyond what a mid-teens multiple implies, especially in Indeval custody and information services (+12% revenue in 2025). **[Inference]** Conversely, Q4 2025 operating expenses rose **15%** on personnel, technology, and depreciation tied to the Digital Evolution program, which may compress margins if revenue growth slows. **Predictive attribute:** none (franchise earnings at price unless expense cycle or Asigna margin-deposit revenue mean-reverts).

## Executive summary
Grupo BMV is a Stahl croupier on Mexican market infrastructure with ~80% cash equity market share and high recurring economics in custody and data. FY2025 filings show **MXN 4,470 million** revenue (+7%), **56%** EBITDA margin, and **MXN 2.88** earnings per share on **556 million** shares in circulation. We normalize starting owner cash to **MXN 2.85** per share from operating cash flow less capital spending. At **MXN 36.36** (2026-06-11), Lawrence base case implies **18.4%** per year over 7 years (total synthesis **18.4%**), above the 15% hold band but with partial dhando because clearing collateral and rate-sensitive financial income are cyclical. Bear case ~**10.8%** on flat volumes; bull ~**21.9%** if listings and derivatives volumes persist. We propose **hold** pending human review of expense ramp and first-dive evidence completeness.
