## What this business is
Air Products and Chemicals, Inc. (APD) is a global industrial gases company founded in 1940. **[Fact]** It sells atmospheric and process gases through three supply modes: on-site plants (take-or-pay contracts), merchant bulk delivery, and packaged cylinders. **[Fact]** FY2025 sales were **$12.0 billion** (down 1% versus FY2024), with geographic segments Americas, Asia, Europe, and Middle East and India. **[Fact]** The company also builds and operates large energy-transition projects, including the NEOM Green Hydrogen joint venture in Saudi Arabia and equity interests such as the 55% JIGPC joint venture. **[Fact]** CEO Seifi Ghasemi has led the company since July 2014.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The market may treat APD as a damaged cyclical chemicals name after **$3.7 billion** of FY2025 project-exit charges and a pending **$2.9 billion** pre-tax charge on further clean-energy exits (June 2026). **[Inference]** Generalists may anchor on GAAP losses and negative reported free cash flow during the NEOM build-out, underweighting the durable core: Americas, Asia, and Europe together generated roughly **$6.2 billion** of segment operating income in FY2025 before corporate drag. **[Fact]** Q2 FY2026 adjusted earnings per share rose to **$3.20**, up **19%** versus the prior-year quarter, suggesting core operations are recovering while growth-project capital is being cut. There is no dated predictive attribute; the setup is **descriptive** (impairment overhang + oligopoly core) rather than a timed equity yield curve.

## Executive summary
Air Products is a global industrial gases oligopoly with long-lived on-site contracts and merchant pricing power, now resetting capital allocation after expensive clean-energy project write-downs. At **$296.75** (NYSE, 2026-07-10), base expected annual return at today's price is **1%** per year on normalized maintenance-level owner cash (**$8.62** per share), far below our **15%** bar. Durable competitive advantage (Munger moat) is **stable** in core gases but **tested** by repeated project impairments. Asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) is **partial**: the **$7.14** annual dividend (43-year increase streak) and investment-grade balance sheet limit distress, but equity can still de-rate on further charges. We propose **watch** until capital discipline proves out and owner cash growth re-accelerates without new multi-billion write-downs.
