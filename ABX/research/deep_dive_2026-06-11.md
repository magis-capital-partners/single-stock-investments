## What this business is

Abacus Global Management (NYSE: ABX; notes: ABXL) is an alternative-asset and life-settlement platform. **[Fact]** The company originates and brokers purchases of life insurance policies between policy sellers and institutional buyers, services those policies until mortality, and earns spread plus fees. **[Fact]** It also runs asset-management sleeves (including free-cash-flow-themed strategies via FCF Advisors, acquired December 2024) and a small technology services line (Abacus Intel mortality verification, launched late 2024). **[Fact]** Revenue is reported in three segments: Life Solutions, Asset Management, and Technology Services. **[Fact]** The company renamed from Abacus Life, Inc. in February 2025 and lists on the NYSE with 9.875% senior notes due 2028 (ABXL).

---

## Why the market might be wrong

Life-settlement economics are opaque: GAAP net income can look modest while adjusted earnings and policy-portfolio marks tell a stronger story. **[Inference]** FY2025 revenue rose to $235 million (+110% year over year) and adjusted EBITDA reached $133 million, yet FY2025 cash from operations was only $26 million because policy purchases and working capital absorb cash before mortality payoffs arrive. **[Inference]** The stock near $9.25 (~2.2 times GAAP book) may screen expensive on trailing GAAP free cash flow while underpricing a rollup that doubled revenue through Carlisle and FCF Advisors. **[Inference]** Complexity (fair-value policy portfolio, securitized funding, dual equity/note structure) may keep generalist investors away even when servicing fees and securitization are repeatable.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Mortality/longevity assumptions prove too optimistic on acquired policies, compressing spreads while debt service remains fixed.

- Funding market disruption or higher spreads on warehouse and ABXL notes
- Integration failure across Carlisle, FCF Advisors, and legacy Abacus cultures
- Regulatory scrutiny of life-settlement marketing to retail policyholders
- Share dilution from 2026 LTIP and shelf registrations

**Inversion:** What would make this fail? Sustained GAAP cash conversion near zero, debt covenants tighten, and adjusted metrics converge down to GAAP losses.

**Lens failure mode:** Treating ABX as a Stahl **croupier** misprices the business; it is a balance-sheet-intensive alt-asset platform, not a toll on exchange volume.
