## What this business is

Rayonier Inc. is a timberland real estate investment trust that owns and manages forests, sells logs and wood products, and monetizes higher-and-better-use (HBU) land. **[Fact]** On January 30, 2026 Rayonier completed a merger-of-equals with PotlatchDeltic Corporation, creating a combined company with approximately **4.1 million acres** of timberlands in the U.S. South (**3.2 million acres**) and U.S. Northwest (**930 thousand acres**), plus six sawmills and an expanded real estate development platform. **[Fact]** The company reports four segments: Southern Timber, Pacific Northwest Timber, Wood Products, and Real Estate. **[Fact]** Q1 2026 revenue was **$276.8 million**; total Adjusted EBITDA was **$94.1 million**; Cash Available for Distribution (CAD) was **$90.2 million** and Adjusted CAD (after **$27.5 million** mandatory debt repayments) was **$62.7 million**. **[Fact]** At March 31, 2026 the balance sheet showed **$7.75 billion** in total assets, **$5.87 billion** in timber and timberlands (net), **$1.86 billion** in long-term debt, **$682 million** in cash, and stockholders equity of **$5.33 billion** (**~301.7 million** common shares outstanding).

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The market may still price RYN as pre-merger standalone Rayonier (~2.8 million acres, timber-only) rather than as the larger integrated platform that now combines PotlatchDeltic sawmills, Chenal Valley real estate, and **4.1 million acres** of geographically diversified timberland. **[Fact]** Q1 2026 Real Estate segment Adjusted EBITDA was **$46.2 million** on **7,695 acres** sold (including a **2,226-acre** solar developer parcel at **$10,100** per acre), versus **$2.0 million** in the prior-year quarter on **1,031 acres**. **[Inference]** At **~$19.82** (May 2026 insider purchase price), the stock trades slightly above GAAP book equity of **~$17.66** per share, not at a deep land discount, yet the embedded HBU, carbon-capture, and solar lease options on Southern acreage may be under-modeled in run-rate earnings screens. **[Inference]** Predictive attribute: **timberland acreage REIT** plus **post-merger integration catalyst** (synergy realization on overhead, wood products, and capital allocation).

---

## Executive summary

Rayonier is now the largest pure-play timber REIT in the United States after merging with PotlatchDeltic: **4.1 million acres**, four operating segments, and a quarterly dividend of **$0.26** per share (**$1.04** annualized). Q1 2026 Adjusted CAD of **$62.7 million** (after mandatory debt repayments) was boosted by lumpy real estate sales; we normalize owner cash to **~$0.58** per share for the Lawrence base case. Leverage rose to **~$1.86 billion** long-term debt post-merger, partially offset by **$682 million** cash. At **~$19.82**, our base case annual return is pending mechanical refresh from normalized CAD (**~$0.58**/sh, 7-year model, **13×** exit multiple). Book equity provides a partial floor, but price trades above GAAP book and integration risk prevents full dhando. We propose **watch** pending proof that merger synergies lift normalized CAD without relying on one-off HBU sales.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Post-merger leverage (**$1.86 billion** long-term debt) plus cyclical Wood Products exposure compress CAD just as the dividend (**$1.04** per year) sets shareholder return expectations, forcing dilutive equity issuance or asset sales.

- **Inversion:** If merger synergies reduce overhead by disclosed targets and Southern CCS/solar contracts convert to recurring lease revenue, normalized CAD could exceed **$0.70**/share while debt amortizes via mandatory repayments (Q1: **$27.5 million**), supporting the dividend without new equity.
- Lumber price collapse would hit Wood Products and log realizations simultaneously, a correlation risk unique to the integrated model versus pure timberland peers.
- Integration failure (culture, systems, harvest planning across **4.1M** acres) could destroy synergy value before the market reprices.
- Rising rates on variable debt (covenant: EBITDA/interest **9.5×** actual vs **2.5×** required) remain manageable but tighten if EBITDA falls.

**Lens failure mode (asset payoff):** If GAAP timberland book (**~$1,463**/acre) overstates recoverable value in a prolonged lumber downturn (unlike Q1 transaction comps at **$6,457+**/acre), the partial book floor is illusory and the asset lens collapses to a low-return yield trap at **~5%** dividend yield.
