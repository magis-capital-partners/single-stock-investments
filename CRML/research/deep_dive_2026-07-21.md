## What this business is
Critical Metals Corp. is a Nasdaq-listed mining exploration and development company focused on critical minerals for electrification and Western supply-chain security. **[Fact]** Its flagship asset is the Tanbreez rare earth project in southern Greenland, where the company holds a **42%** equity interest in Tanbreez Mining Greenland A/S with a contracted path to **92.5%** subject to Greenland government approval. **[Fact]** The company also owns the Wolfsberg lithium project in Austria (BMW offtake and **$15 million** advance payment) and minority stakes in Weinebene and Eastern Alps lithium projects. **[Fact]** FY2025 net loss was **$51.9 million** on **104.9 million** ordinary shares; cash was **$7.3 million** at June 30, 2025 with a going-concern warning. **[Fact]** The company has declared mineral resources but has not declared reserves or begun production.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
**Predictive attribute:** complexity discount (Greenland jurisdiction, pre-reserve resource, related-party Tanbreez history, and headline PEA net present value versus thin cash floor).

**[Inference]** The market may treat CRML as a scaled Western rare earth producer because of Tanbreez size headlines (4.7 billion metric ton host rock, company PEA citing ~**$3 billion** pre-tax project net present value) and policy tailwinds for non-China REE supply. **[Fact]** The filed resource estimate is **44.87 million metric tons** at **0.38%** total rare earth oxides, much of it **inferred**, and the company explicitly warns that inferred resources cannot support economic viability on their own. **[Inference]** Investors may underweight GEM Global arbitration risk, European Lithium (EUR) voting control, repeated equity issuance needs, and the gap between project-level net present value and per-share owner payoff after financing and dilution.
