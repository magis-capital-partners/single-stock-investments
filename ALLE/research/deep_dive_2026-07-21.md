## What this business is
Allegion plc is a global provider of security products for commercial and residential buildings. **[Fact]** FY2025 net revenue was **$4.07 billion**, up **7.9%** from **$3.77 billion** in FY2024. **[Fact]** The company sells mechanical locks, exit devices, electronic access control, and software under brands including Schlage, Von Duprin, and CISA. **[Fact]** Two reportable segments dominate: **Americas** (**$3.22 billion**, 27.9% operating margin) and **International** (**$849 million**, 9.0% margin). **[Fact]** FY2025 operating cash flow was **$783.8 million** minus capital spending of **$69.1 million**, yielding free cash flow of about **$714.7 million** on **86.6 million** diluted shares (**~$8.25** per share). **[Inference]** Allegion earns by specifying products into new construction and retrofit projects where architect and installer relationships create switching costs.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock trades near **17 times** FY2025 free cash flow per share while Americas revenue grew mid-single digits and electronic mix is rising. **[Inference]** The market may overweight **non-residential construction slowdown** and **import competition** while underweighting **specification-grade mechanical share** and **electronic access upsell**. **[Fact]** Q1 2026 revenue was **$1.03 billion** (+9.7% year over year) with Americas at **$810 million**, suggesting near-term demand remains solid. **[Fact]** Total debt is **$1.98 billion** against cash of **$356 million**; a **$191 million** revolver draw adds variable-rate sensitivity. **[Inference]** No dated Horizon Kinetics predictive attribute exists; the edge is **durable franchise at a fair price**, not a catalyst with a known payoff date.

### Mental models in plain English

**Moat durability (Munger):** Does scale protect the franchise? **Partially yes.** Code compliance, fire-life-safety specifications, and installer training create switching costs, but commodity residential locks face import pressure.

**Moat trajectory (Munger):** Is the advantage widening? **Stable to mixed.** Electronic access deepens the platform moat; EMEA price competition limits international margin expansion.

**Incentive alignment (Munger):** Does management get paid for owner outcomes? **Mixed.** FY2025 dividends were **$175.5 million** ($2.04 per share) and share repurchases continued, but leverage rose with revolver usage (10-K).

**Circle of competence (Munger):** Can we explain the business simply? **Yes.** Sell locks and access control into buildings; earn on specification, retrofit, and electronic upsell.

**Deny compounding to others (Stahl):** Can peers replicate? **Partially.** Assa Abloy and dormakaba compete globally; Allegion's Americas specification position is harder to displace locally.

**Croupier vs operator (Stahl):** Is this a toll-road or a capital recycler? **Operator compounder.** Allegion reinvests in product development and tuck-in M&A while returning cash via dividends and buybacks.
