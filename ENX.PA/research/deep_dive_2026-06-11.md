## What this business is

Euronext N.V. is the leading pan-European market infrastructure group: regulated equity, fixed-income, and derivatives venues, central counterparty clearing, securities services, market data, and primary listings across Amsterdam, Paris, Brussels, Lisbon, Milan, Oslo, Dublin, and Athens. **[Fact]** FY2025 underlying revenue and income was **€1,823.2 million** (+12.1% versus 2024), adjusted EBITDA reached about **€1.1 billion**, and free cash flow was **€682.3 million** (`investor-documents/ir-euronext/urd_2025.pdf`, §5.2.4). **[Fact]** Q1 2026 underlying revenue and income was **€528.5 million** (+15.3% versus Q1 2025), with adjusted EBITDA **€343.2 million** and margin **64.9%** (`investor-documents/ir-euronext/q1_2026_press_release.pdf`). The company earns toll-like fees on trading, clearing, listing, custody, and data subscriptions with minimal principal risk on core operations (Stahl croupier).

## Why the market might be wrong

There is no dated structural discount or yield-curve payoff. **[Inference]** Q1 2026 is the eighth consecutive quarter of double-digit revenue growth on elevated market volatility; the market may treat that run rate as durable. **[Inference]** Our base case uses FY2025 free cash flow per share (**€6.73**), not Q1 2026 peak volumes or the **€2.13** adjusted earnings per share quarter. Return is franchise quality at price, not a mispricing with a known catalyst date. **Predictive attribute:** none.

### Mental models in plain English

**Croupier toll (Stahl):** Does the company earn fees on activity without taking principal risk? **Yes.** Trading, clearing, and listing revenue with high margins (`q1_2026_press_release.pdf`; `urd_2025.pdf` §1.3).

**Volume vs share (Stahl):** Is handled volume growing? **Yes.** Underlying revenue **€1,823.2M** in FY2025 (+12.1%); Q1 2026 **€528.5M** (+15.3% YoY). Cash equity market share on Euronext-listed names about **64%** in 2025 (`urd_2025.pdf` §1.3).

**Fee model resilience (Stahl):** Does recurring revenue cushion cyclicality? **Partially.** Non-volume-related revenue was **56%** of total in Q1 2026 and covered **159%** of underlying operating expenses excluding depreciation (`q1_2026_press_release.pdf`). FICC and equity markets still drive upside in volatile quarters.

**Cycle normalization (Hohn):** Are we underwriting peak activity as normal? **No.** Base free cash flow **€6.73** per share from FY2025, explicitly below the Q1 2026 revenue run rate (`valuation.json`).

**Primary risk:** **Peak earnings trap** if Q1 2026 volatility normalizes and the market reapplies a lower multiple on transaction revenue.
