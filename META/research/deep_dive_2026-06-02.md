## What this business is
Meta Platforms Inc. runs the world's largest social advertising business through Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, and Messenger (Family of Apps), plus Reality Labs for VR/AR hardware and software. **[Fact]** Advertisers pay for targeted impressions and engagement; users get free apps that drive daily active people and ad inventory. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was **$201.0B** (+22%); Q1 2026 revenue **$56.3B** (+33%). **[Fact]** Reportable segments are **Family of Apps** and **Reality Labs**. **[Fact]** FY2025 consolidated operating income **$83.3B**; operating cash flow **$115.8B**; capital expenditures **$69.7B**; owner free cash flow **~$46.1B** (~**$17.91/sh**). **[Fact]** Management guides **$125–145B** capital expenditures in 2026 for AI infrastructure and core business. **[Inference]** Owner economics depend on whether FoA ad pricing and volume growth outrun the capex step-up and persistent Reality Labs losses.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
There is **no clear dated mispricing signal** (HK predictive attribute: **none**). **[Inference]** At **~$633**, the stock embeds FoA compounding and AI infrastructure optionality; it is not a holdco-discount or dormant-asset story. **[Inference]** A single consolidated free cash flow growth rate hides **two engines**: Family of Apps throws off most cash today while Reality Labs and AI capex absorb capital. **[Inference]** Lawrence 10-year math on **FY2025 free cash flow per share ($17.91)** may understate medium-term earnings if 2026–2027 capex normalizes; the segment build makes that tension explicit.
