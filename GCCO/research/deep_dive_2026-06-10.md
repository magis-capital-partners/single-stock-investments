## What this business is
The Garden City Company is a closely held farmland and royalty company headquartered in Garden City, Kansas, with a secondary office in Colorado Springs. **[Fact]** The company owns farmland in southwest Kansas that it leases to tenant farmers on an annual **crop-share** basis: revenue is recognized when crops are harvested and inventoried, and grain inventory on the balance sheet represents the company's share of unsold crops. **[Fact]** It also collects **oil and gas royalties** from wells operated by third parties on its land. **[Fact]** As of December 31, 2024, **34,865** shares were outstanding; stockholders' equity was **$14.2 million** (**~$407** per share). **[Fact]** The company carries **no bank debt**, invests surplus cash in U.S. Treasury and equity securities (**$8.1 million** fair value at year-end 2024), and pays a large annual cash dividend (**$40** per share in 2024, **$45** in 2023). **[Fact]** Leadership includes President and CEO R. Thayer Tutt, Jr. and a board chaired by William J. Hybl; the stock trades OTC Pink (GCCO) with audited GAAP financials posted via OTC Markets.

## Why the market might be wrong
The market may treat GCCO as an illiquid family dividend stub, either ignoring the **embedded liquid-asset and farmland bundle** or overpaying after a multi-year move above book value. **[Fact]** At an indicative **~$1,284** per share (December 2024 stock option exercise price), the stock trades roughly **3.2 times** December 2024 GAAP book (**~$407** per share) and about **4 times** mark-to-market liquid assets (cash, securities, and grain inventory, **~$317** per share combined). **[Inference]** Predictive attribute: **family-controlled farmland OTC** plus **market structure discount** (no exchange listing, thin volume, dividend screens only). **[Inference]** Land and water rights are carried at **historical cost** (**$2.93 million** on the balance sheet); supplemental acreage schedules in older PDFs were not text-extracted in this workspace, so economic farmland value may exceed GAAP but is **[Assumption]** until verified. **[Inference]** FY2024 farm rental and oil/gas revenues fell versus 2023 on crop and commodity prices, so trailing earnings understate mid-cycle cash generation but also show cyclical risk.

## Executive summary
Garden City is a debt-free Kansas farmland and royalty vehicle that pays most of its operating cash back as dividends. FY2024 normalized owner cash (operating cash flow minus capital spending) was about **$53** per share on **$6.4 million** of revenue, down from stronger 2022 crop and oil prices. At **~$1,284** per share **[Assumption]**, our base case annual return is **-1.4%** per year on filing-based owner cash with modest growth and a **14 times** year-10 cash-flow exit multiple (seven-year Lawrence model). Liquid assets near **$317** per share and GAAP book **~$407** per share cap downside versus a zero, but the quote sits well above both, so **floor_pass** is false. Dhando is **partial**: no debt and tangible assets help, but premium pricing and family control limit minority upside. We propose **watch** until a verified live quote, FY2025 OCR, or supplemental acreage marks justify accumulate.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** Paying **~3 times** book and **~4 times** liquid assets for a cyclical crop-share and royalty stream while dividends (**$40/sh**) consume most normalized owner cash (**~$53/sh**), leaving little retained capital if farm rental and oil royalties stay at FY2024 trough levels.

- **Inversion:** A return to FY2022 revenue (**$8.9M**) plus securities gains could support dividends and re-rate equity toward higher book-plus-land marks without new debt.
- Postretirement health obligation (**$1.28M**, unfunded) grows with actuarial losses and healthcare inflation (Note 7).
- Southwest Kansas water regulation and drought risk sit in "water issues" expenses (**$1.1M** G&A FY2024).
- OTC illiquidity may prevent exit at quoted prices; option plan uses **~$1,284** strike as best filing anchor for value.
- Investment portfolio marks (**$364K** unrealized gain FY2024) can reverse and swing reported earnings.

**Lens failure mode (asset payoff):** If supplemental acreage shows modest acreage or heavy irrigation capital needs, the land remark in our overlay is too high and the name is purely a low-growth dividend bond priced for yield, not NAV.
