# Economic value and comparable valuation audit

**Date:** 2026-07-15

**Securities:** TPL, LB, WBI, AZLCZ

## Conclusion

The models do not use GAAP book value as intrinsic value, but the prior pricing layer did not make the economic-value bridge sufficiently explicit. The audit therefore separates three concepts:

1. **Accounting reference:** useful for cash, debt, and proof that old land or royalties are understated, but not the valuation floor.
2. **Comparable economic value:** observable value for the same economic claim, adjusted for location, production, contract quality, and asset mix.
3. **Risked present value to the common owner:** economic value after completion, timing, leverage, control, liquidity, tax, and realization discounts.

Uncontracted option success values remain separate. They are not added at 100% merely because powered land, water, or royalty acreage is scarce.

### WaterBridge

WaterBridge is not a capital-free royalty vehicle. Its economic value comes from connected infrastructure, long-term dedications, density, permits, and brownfield returns, offset by debt and continuing capital requirements.

- Gravity Water was acquired at approximately 5.5x run-rate EBITDA before synergies.
- WaterBridge is valued at 8x/10x/12x normalized EBITDA, a premium justified only by greater scale, contracts, and network effects.
- Replacement cost remains an embedded cross-check, not an additive asset.
- Speedway and Devon value remains conditional on incremental after-tax ROIC.

**Result:** component value remains **$9.12/$26.51/$46.10** per economic unit. The audit did not support raising it.

## Investor-wisdom controls now made explicit

- **Horizon Kinetics:** dormant assets and capital-free royalties can be worth far more than current income or book value; third-party capital can expand reserves and cash flow.
- **Groundbreaker:** segment heterogeneous land; capitalize contracted tolls; treat local water and interconnection as scarce; do not transplant water prices across watersheds.
- **Klarman:** keep current cash flow, hard-asset NAV, and catalyst/options as separate layers.
- **Marathon / Capital Returns:** capital-intensive infrastructure creates value only above its cost of capital.
- **Marks / Oaktree:** debt, refinancing, control, and realization risk belong ahead of common equity.

## Remaining work

The largest unresolved evidence gaps are TPL's tract-level producing/undeveloped royalty split, LB's Alpha Digital lease economics, WBI's cohort-level growth-capex returns, and AZLCZ's complete contracts, water adjudication, and stapled-LLC ownership linkage. These gaps widen ranges; they no longer force an implicit zero or disappear inside a GAAP number.
