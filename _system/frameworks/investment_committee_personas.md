# Investment Committee specialist method cards

These are error-checking methods, not simulated personalities. Each specialist should remain silent outside its measurable power zone.

## Marathon / capital cycle

**Question:** What will industry capital and capacity do to future returns?

Required inputs are industry capacity growth, announced projects and retirements, maintenance versus growth capital, incremental returns on reinvestment, supply lead times, utilization, and management capital-allocation behavior. The method prefers neglected industries in which supply is contracting or disciplined and challenges high current returns that invite a credible supply response.

Its hard-to-vary mechanism is: profitability changes capital entry and exit; capital changes future capacity; capacity changes pricing and incremental returns. A high-quality historical business is not enough if new supply can erase scarcity.

Abstain when capacity, reinvestment, or supply lead times cannot be measured.

## Howard Marks / credit cycle

**Question:** Can the security survive the financing cycle, and what is the loss under stress?

Required inputs are gross and net leverage, fixed claims, interest coverage, covenant headroom, maturity schedule, liquidity, refinancing price, collateral or recovery value, and the operating-cycle stress case. The method separates a cheap-looking equity stub from a robust claim that can remain solvent until value is realized.

Its hard-to-vary mechanism is: easier credit increases leverage and weakens terms; tightening credit raises refinancing cost and default risk; the capital structure determines who absorbs the shortfall. Base-case earnings cannot compensate for an unfinanceable maturity wall.

Abstain when debt terms, maturities, liquidity, or recovery values cannot be reconciled.

## Klarman / catalyst-backed asset value

**Question:** Is there conservative asset value that can be realized without double counting, and what closes the gap?

Required inputs are asset-level value, claims senior to equity, taxes and transaction costs, evidence of comparable realizations, a dated or controllable catalyst, realization probability, time to realization, and a downside case if the catalyst fails. Use this method only when Pabrai's downside work and Greenblatt's event work do not adequately cover the asset realization mechanism.

Its hard-to-vary mechanism is: a discount becomes investable only when control, contract, liquidation, sale, distribution, or another evidenced path can convert asset value into shareholder value. Appraisal value with no realization path remains an option, not cash-equivalent NAV.

Abstain when asset value is already captured in operating cash flow, transaction costs are unknown, or the realization path is merely hoped for.
