### Mental models & latticework
- [PROPOSED MUNGER] Build a latticework of mental models from multiple disciplines before specializing in stock picking; isolated facts without models produce "confusion and folly." — `munger/Munger-1994-Elementary-Worldly-Wisdom.pdf`

### Psychology of misjudgment
- [PROPOSED MUNGER] Maintain a checklist of ~25 standard causes of human misjudgment; watch for **lollapalooza** effects when several biases combine. — `munger/Psychology-of-Human-Misjudgment.pdf`
- [PROPOSED MUNGER] Incentive-caused bias is among the most reliable forces in human behavior — always map who gets paid for what before trusting management narrative. — `munger/Psychology-of-Human-Misjudgment.pdf`

### Inversion & failure avoidance
- [PROPOSED MUNGER] Before asking "why is this a good investment," ask "what would make this fail?" and whether we can survive being wrong. — `munger/Munger-1994-Elementary-Worldly-Wisdom.pdf`

### Circle of competence & simplicity
- [PROPOSED MUNGER] If we cannot explain the business in plain language within five sentences, we are outside circle of competence — pass or defer. — `munger/Munger-1994-Elementary-Worldly-Wisdom.pdf`

### Moats & competitive advantage
- [PROPOSED MUNGER] Prefer businesses where rational managers can defend pricing power over long periods; moat quality matters more than near-term EPS noise. — `munger/Munger-1994-Elementary-Worldly-Wisdom.pdf`

**Status: PROMOTED 2026-05-21** → `_system/memory/MEMORY.md` (all accepted by human)

*Source: `_system/reference/investment-wisdom/pabrai/`*

### Dhando & asymmetric payoffs
- [PROPOSED PABRAI] Seek **heads I win, tails I don't lose much** — downside protection matters more than upside precision. — Dhando framework; letters 2005–2020 series

### Bet sizing & concentration
- [PROPOSED PABRAI] Run a concentrated portfolio of high-conviction ideas; redemptions are a feature when partners leave capital with fewer, better ideas. — `pabrai/Pabrai-Letter-l_010112.pdf` (2012 redemptions discipline)
- [PROPOSED PABRAI] When subscriptions lag redemptions but idea quality is high, treat cash scarcity as a signal to prioritize — not to dilute into marginal names. — `pabrai/Pabrai-Letter-l_010124.pdf` (Jan 2024: "more good investment ideas than available cash")

### Business selection
- [PROPOSED PABRAI] Invest only in simple, understandable businesses with durable economics and honest management — skip complex stories requiring heroic assumptions. — partner letters (recurring theme 2005–2025)

### Portfolio discipline
- [PROPOSED PABRAI] Wait for fat pitches; annual redemption windows and fund capacity limits are constraints to respect, not obstacles to force trades. — `pabrai/Pabrai-Letter-l_010125.pdf` (Jan 2025 fund structure)

### Cloning & learning
- [PROPOSED PABRAI] Cloning great investors is valid starting point — but every cloned idea still requires independent primary-source verification before capital. — Dhando / letter practice

## [PROPOSED STAHL]

**Status: PROMOTED 2026-05-21** → `_system/memory/MEMORY.md` (all accepted by human)

*Source: `_system/reference/investment-wisdom/stahl/`*

### Croupier business model
- [PROPOSED STAHL] **Croupiers** facilitate pecuniary transactions (exchanges, custodians, processors) without putting principal capital at risk — distinct from balance-sheet financials like banks. — `stahl/Stahl-Croupier-Business-Model-2008.pdf`
- [PROPOSED STAHL] Modern regulation pushes OTC/exotic instruments toward organized exchanges and clearing — expanding croupier TAM over time. — `stahl/Stahl-Croupier-Business-Model-2008.pdf`
- [PROPOSED STAHL] Liquidity demand can **rise** in crises (ETF turnover, futures hedging) even when credit markets freeze — do not assume croupier volumes collapse with recession. — `stahl/Stahl-Croupier-Business-Model-2008.pdf`

### Exchanges & market infrastructure
- [PROPOSED STAHL] For exchanges, analyze **handled volume** alongside market share — share loss and volume growth are not mutually exclusive. — `stahl/Stahl-Exchanges-Less-Talk-More-Figures-2009.pdf`
- [PROPOSED STAHL] Exchange operating margins can remain extraordinarily high (20–75%+) through severe market stress — durability matters more than one-year EPS dips. — `stahl/Stahl-Exchanges-Less-Talk-More-Figures-2009.pdf`
- [PROPOSED STAHL] Fee model matters: ad valorem pricing ties revenue to market levels; per-trade/volume pricing can be more resilient in drawdowns. — `stahl/Stahl-Exchanges-Less-Talk-More-Figures-2009.pdf`

### Real diversification
- [PROPOSED STAHL] ~50% of S&P 500 names are statistically irrelevant to index performance (≤7 bp weight each); index "diversification" is narrower than it appears. — `stahl/Stahl-Achievement-of-Diversification-2020.pdf`
- [PROPOSED STAHL] Low index weight ≠ low economic importance — compare employees, revenue, and real-economy footprint, not cap-weight alone. — `stahl/Stahl-Achievement-of-Diversification-2020.pdf`

### Spinoffs & corporate simplification
- [PROPOSED STAHL] Spinoffs and separations can unlock value when conglomerate complexity hides segment economics — screen for forced simplification events. — `stahl/Stahl-Spinoffs-Going-Separate-Ways-2015.pdf`

### Portfolio context (Stahl lens)
