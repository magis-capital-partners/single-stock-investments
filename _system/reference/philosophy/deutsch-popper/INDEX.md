**Key Popper ideas for valuation:**

- Scientific theories are **bold conjectures** tested by attempted **refutation**
- **Falsifiability** = demarcation criterion (not confirmation count)
- **Risky predictions** — corroboration counts only after genuine test that could fail
- **Ad hoc rescue** — adjusting theory to escape refutation destroys explanatory power
- **Problem-situation first** — science starts with problems, not bare observation

**Books (not hosted — purchase/library):**

- *The Beginning of Infinity* (2011) — explanations, optimism, fallibilism, testability
- *The Fabric of Reality* (1997) — four strands: quantum, epistemology, evolution, computation

**Key Deutsch ideas for valuation:**

- **Good explanations** are **hard to vary** (details matter; easy-to-vary = bad)
- Knowledge grows by **conjecture + criticism**, not induction from data alone
- **Fallibilism** — we may always be mistaken; seek error correction
- **Reach** — good theories explain beyond the data used to invent them
- Reject **instrumentalism** — predictions without explanatory mechanism are insufficient
