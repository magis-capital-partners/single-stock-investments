# Popper — falsification & growth assumptions (Marvin extract)

Sources:
- Popper-Science-Conjectures-and-Refutations-essay.pdf
- Popper-Science-as-Falsification-1963.html
- plato.stanford.edu/entries/popper/ (2022 revision)

## Demarcation

A theory is scientific if it forbids some observable state of affairs — if observation X occurs, the theory is **false**.

Growth rates in valuation are mini-theories. "11% FCF growth" must forbid something observable (e.g. segment revenue below threshold).

## Conjecture and refutation

Science proceeds: problem → bold conjecture → deductive consequences → **attempted falsification**.

Marvin: Do not treat the assumption ledger as induction from past CAGR. State the conjecture (mechanism), then what would refute it.

## Risky predictions

Confirming instances do not support a theory unless they result from a **risky** test that could have failed.

Marvin: Each growth theory needs ≥1 near-term quantitative prediction derived from the mechanism (not "stock will do well").

## Ad hoc rescue (forbidden without new theory)

Adjusting auxiliary assumptions to protect a favored theory from refutation lowers scientific status.

Marvin examples to flag:
- Raising exit multiple when growth misses
- Cutting FCF₀ normalization when capex disappoints without filing bridge
- "Temporary headwinds" with no dated falsifier

## Corroboration ≠ verification

Surviving tests increases **corrovisional** status only. All knowledge is provisional.

Marvin: Lawrence base IRR is provisional; falsifiers belong in the dive.

## Problem-situation first

Theories start from **problems**, not neutral observation.

Marvin: Frame as "Why is IRR X% at price P?" then build growth theory to explain gap to 15% target — not default spreadsheet CAGR.
