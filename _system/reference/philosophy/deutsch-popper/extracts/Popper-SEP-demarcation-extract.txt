# Popper — demarcation & basic statements (SEP extract, abridged)

Source: Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, "Karl Popper" (revision Mon Sep 12, 2022)
URL: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/popper/

## Falsifiability as demarcation

Popper replaces verification with falsifiability: a universal law is tested by searching for genuine counter-instances. Methodologically, a single counter-instance may not suffice in practice (measurement error, auxiliary hypotheses), but logically asymmetry holds: verification of universals is impossible; falsification is decisive.

## Theory-laden observation

There are no pure theory-free observations. All observation-statements are selective and theory-laden.

Marvin implication: FCF₀ normalization is already theory-laden — document the normalization theory in the growth stress test, not only the number.

## Basic statements and convention

Testing stops at basic statements the research community accepts provisionally — like a jury verdict. They are not justified by experience alone; they are conventional, corrigible decisions.

Marvin implication: "Normalized FCF" and "mid-cycle earnings" are community judgments — state them explicitly and what would overturn them.

## Scientific progress

Theories are not inductively inferred. Science grows by proposing bold theories with high informative content (improbable, highly testable), then attempting to refute them.

Marvin implication: Prefer **specific, risky** growth mechanisms over smooth CAGR that fits any outcome.

## Verisimilitude

Better theories have higher truth-likeness — more true consequences, fewer false ones.

Marvin implication: Compare growth **theories** across bear/base/bull — which has better explanatory reach, not just which hits IRR target.

## Ad hoc degeneration (Marxism / psychoanalysis example)

Theories saved from falsification by ad hoc adjustments become pseudo-science.

Marvin implication: Document **ad hoc rescue watch** in every growth stress test.
