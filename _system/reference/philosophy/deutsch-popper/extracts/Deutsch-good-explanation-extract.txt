# Deutsch — good explanations & growth assumptions (Marvin extract)

Sources:
- Deutsch-Constructor-Theory-2012.pdf (author site / arXiv)
- Deutsch-Physics-Philosophy-Quantum-Technology.pdf
- The Beginning of Infinity (2011) — paraphrase for workflow; read full book for depth

## Good vs bad explanations

A **good explanation** is hard to vary: if you change the details, the explanation stops working.

Bad: "GOOGL grows 11% because it is a quality compounder." (Works for MSFT, CPRT, anything.)

Good: "Consolidated owner cash grows 11% because Cloud OI share rises from 9% to 14% of consolidated OI while Services OI grows 8% on Search query volume + modest ARPU, with corp capex flat as % of OI after 2027." (Specific; break any piece and the rate fails.)

## Conjecture, not induction

Theories are created by conjecture and criticism — not read off from data.

Marvin: FY2025 growth history informs the conjecture but does not **prove** forward growth. The ledger must show mechanism, not extrapolation alone.

## Fallibilism

We may always be mistaken. Acknowledge what would change your mind.

Marvin: Falsifiers table is mandatory, not optional rhetoric.

## Reach

Good explanations have **unlimited reach** — they solve problems beyond those they were invented for.

Marvin: A Cloud backlog theory should also imply margin, capex intensity, or hiring — testable elsewhere in the dive.

## Instrumentalism (reject)

Treating theories as mere prediction tools without caring if they are **true** explanations.

Marvin: "Reverse DCF says we need 14% growth" is instrumental unless tied to operating mechanism in segment build.

## Optimism / error correction

Progress is unbounded where error correction exists.

Marvin: When theory fails (falsifier hits), **revise theory and growth** — do not hide behind watch stance without updating assumptions.

## Constructor theory (optional depth)

Science expresses what transformations are possible vs impossible.

Investment analog: Which **transformations** of the business are physically/contractually possible (backlog → revenue, acreage → royalties) vs impossible (instant 50× FCF without capex)?
