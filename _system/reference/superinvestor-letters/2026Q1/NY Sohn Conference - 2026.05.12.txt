Kevin Salimian, Founder and Portfolio Manager of Voxel Capital: 
Thanks, Brian. Good morning, everyone. It is a privilege to be here with all of you at the 
Sohn Investment Conference. I am Kevin Salimian, and I am the founder of Voxel Capital, a 
concentrated long-only investment firm launching in Q3 of this year. Today, I am excited to 
discuss Infineon Technologies AG (IFX GR), a company that we believe is at the early stages 
of a multi-year growth and margin transformation. 
 
Infineon Technologies AG (IFX GR) is the number one provider of power semiconductors to 
the automotive and AI markets. The company's chips are used to manage electricity 
efficiently and enable electric mobility. The power seat controls in your car very likely were 
made by Infineon Technologies AG (IFX GR), and the company is also the number one 
provider of semiconductors used to regulate power within servers, including AI servers. 
 
Infineon Technologies AG (IFX GR) was spun off from Siemens AG (SIE GR) 27 years ago and 
is led by talented executive Jochen Hanebeck, who worked his way up through the 
company over the last 32 years. The company's rich corporate history in the power 
semiconductor industry gives it a unique R&D and go-to-market position. Infineon 
Technologies AG (IFX GR) has been the leading provider of power semiconductors for its 
key end markets, including autos, industrials, and data centers, for over two decades. 
 
Our thesis on Infineon Technologies AG (IFX GR) has three key elements: 
 
We project structurally inflecting growth in the AI power business. 
 
We forecast a return to secular growth in the automotive segment. 
 
We believe Infineon Technologies AG (IFX GR) will see a rapid rise in profit margins over the 
coming years. 
 
Let's start with the inflecting growth in AI power. Infineon Technologies AG (IFX GR) as a 
company has long been a key player in the entire AI power value chain. The company's 
semiconductors are used across multiple steps to manage, convert, and deliver power to 
AI processors in data centers. However, Infineon Technologies AG (IFX GR) as a stock 
historically has not been considered an AI winner, and for a good reason. AI-related 
revenues historically were a very small percentage of consolidated Infineon Technologies 
AG (IFX GR) revenues, including only 5% of 2025 revenues. 
 
We believe we are at the beginning of a structural inflection in the importance of power 
semiconductors as power delivery becomes increasingly strategic, putting Infineon 
Technologies AG (IFX GR) in an excellent position going forward. Prospectively, there are 
three key reasons for power semiconductors to see significant dollar content growth. First, 
power draw for GPUs is nearly doubling generation over generation, increasing the need for 
power semiconductors that can regulate all that additional power. Second, the shift to 
higher voltages, including the upcoming transition from 400V to 800V , carries higher 
complexity and requires more advanced materials, leading to additional power 
semiconductor content. And third, new form factors in AI racks, including vertical power 
and power sidecars, will drive additional power semiconductor content in the coming 
years. 
 
Broadly, we expect that as the power draw for GPUs doubles in each prospective chip
