Dear Partner, 
We continue to fare well in this highly turbulent year.  Usually, market participants talk about ‘volatility’ or the expectation of it as a 
euphemism for a period of market weakness, but in the Trump 2.0 presidency, it seems to describe a more classic form.  The last several 
months have brought extreme volatility to equity markets, as the conflicting forces of the AI infrastructure built-out cycle are met with 
uncertainty and commodity/trade dislocations from the Iran conflict.  Even though we consider ourselves primarily long and short stock 
pickers, and specialists in tech and smaller capitalization opportunities, we do find ourselves riveted to oil prices, the ten-year treasury 
and the VIX to understand the context.   In addition, the dispersion within technology subsectors is muddying the waters as Big Tech 
and the entire market races to build AI infrastructure readiness, while investors debate whether this comes at the expense of software 
and service spending.  Our operating mindset is that this stew of conflicting tech forces, along with a troubled Mideast and higher oil 
prices, will be the context all year long.  Adding to this, we continue to believe that consumer spending is more pressured than widely 
discussed, and hence a focus of our short book, and that a higher degree of political gamesmanship and election ugliness will further 
color the environment in the second half of the year.  Accordingly, upside and downside volatility does not seem to be abating any time 
soon.  Seatbelts on! 
Since our last letter, we weathered the market weakness in February and March and captured some of the broad-based improvement in 
April.  Our exposure levels throughout this period have been relatively consistent, averaging approximately 40% net long with 180% or 
so of gross exposure.   Our winning technology longs have continued to include some carry-over names from last year and a few new 
ones in data center adjacent and networking infrastructure, but not really software yet (although early in May there are signs that this 
might be changing).   Through much of 2026, the daily game is to watch the relationship between semiconductor indices vs. software 
(IGV) to observe the bifurcation.  As we wrote last time, we think the negativity in software is too severe and our 13Fs will likely 
continue to show more long exposure in software than what we carried throughout last year.  Across the board, software investing will 
not likely be easier because there does seem to be a changing of the guard, and whole new set of ‘token’ based economics that is being 
foisted on the market with advances in code writing and development coming from Anthropic and OpenAI.  The most interesting recent 
development in the perception of software were the recent results of Atlassian (TEAM).  They delivered strong growth numbers, 
exceeding expectations on all fronts, and for the time being upsetting the persistent narrative of the software spend cycle being 
broken.  We have no position in TEAM, but their results are an interesting marker that perhaps upcoming results of a variety thoughtfully 
run enterprise software companies may turn out to be stronger than the market expects and provide important proof points of resilience 
in a category that has been heavily pressured for the last six months. 
The AI spend cycle has been a fascinating dynamic to watch as it both recalls memories of the Internet Bubble of 2000, promises to be 
even more than that in terms of worldwide impact, but is inflating some segments of the market to overearning levels well beyond 
longer term run rates. In addition, there is just so much information to digest, from industry participants jockeying with one 
another, their suppliers, and rotating issues of which product/energy bottleneck is slowing down deployments, that each day seems 
to bring a new investment consideration upsetting how it is all being perceived (again, read more volatility).  Stepping back, from 
an intellectual challenge perspective, this period is proving to be fascinating; however, with the amount of rapid change and big dollar 
bets going on industry wide, we are expecting even more dispersion, not just from small companies and suppliers, but from the Mag 7 
mega caps as well.  The game for the technology mega caps has dramatically changed from where they have been for the last decade; 
most are now spending the vast amount of their free cash flow on cap ex and simultaneously investing huge sums in Anthropic and 
OpenAI that are also their largest new customers.  We strongly believe that there is strong potential for some missteps even with the 
largest public names in technology such that typical index investing in technology is more problematic (to be clear, there’s more 
downside risk in the QQQs over the next few years than there has been in a long time).  In addition, with the SpaceX IPO, and then 
Anthropic and OpenAI needing to tap the public markets, there are potential traffic jams to contemplate later 2026 and into 2027. 
With all this said, we continue to think that this is an optimal time for long/short directional investing in technology, so we believe the 
onus remains on us to deliver strong performance. Our book over the next several months will likely feature more software longs, and 
a cross section of AI winners and losers.  The consumer tailwinds seem tricky, and oil issues could get even more challenging, so that 
is reflected in a larger exposure to travel and consumer technology-related shorts.  We are open on June 1 for new investors.
