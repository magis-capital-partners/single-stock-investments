OUTLOOK 
 
Our last two Assessments expressed increasing confidence that the tide is 
turning in favor of the high-quality, predictable mid cap growth companies 
that we typically own in SEG Partners. The first quarter validated that 
thesis before testing it. Through the end of February, the broadening-out 
narrative was unfolding in full force: The Russell Midcap Index rose 
approximately +7.0% while the S&P 500 Index returned less than +1.0%, 
the clearest evidence yet that the environment was beginning to shift.  
 
March changed the complexion of the quarter. The military conflict with 
Iran delivered a macro shock that hit cyclicals and industrials 
disproportionately hard. As oil prices spiked, consumer confidence 
deteriorated and the risk of broader regional destabilization introduced 
significant uncertainty into equity markets. Our portfolio—which reflects 
our more optimistic outlook for the US consumer and broader economy—
faced pressure. 
 
The Iran Conflict and the Decision to Remain Invested 
 
We have been through similar disruption in the recent past. Cautious 
positioning due to the April 2025 “Liberation Day” tariff announcements 
was intended to protect the portfolio from policy-related volatility. Our 
reduced net exposure, however, caused us to miss a significant portion of 
the subsequent rally. We discussed this experience candidly in our Q1 
2025 Assessment and committed to learning from it. 
 
When the Iran conflict escalated in February and March, we were faced 
with a similar decision. The risks were real: disruptions to the energy 
supply, the potential escalation to a broader regional conflict and 
uncertainty about the duration and scope of military operations. This time 
we chose not to meaningfully reduce exposure, as the timing and duration 
of geopolitical crises are inherently unpredictable.  
 
Rather than significantly reposition our portfolio, we maintained a fairly 
conservative, steady net exposure for much of March, ranging from 
roughly 67% to 71% through the first three weeks of the Iranian conflict. 
While we modestly added to our short positions to better hedge our core 
longs, our large net exposures in Industrials (33% net as of quarter-end, 
47% long and 13% short) and Consumer Discretionary (18% net, 34% long 
and 16% short) drove the bulk of our March underperformance. As the 
market sell-off deepened late in the month, we began lifting our net 
exposure by adding to core longs and reducing beaten-down shorts, with 
our net exposure reaching 78% by April 1
st. With the announcement of the

planned ceasefire on the evening of April 7th, we have seen a full reversal 
of the March drawdown.  
 
Our Industrials positions are anchored by businesses that generate 
revenue predominantly in the US and are tied to secular capital 
expenditure themes: grid modernization, data center power infrastructure, 
environmental services and reshoring. Our Consumer Discretionary 
positions are concentrated in higher-end and experiential categories, 
including live entertainment, premium sleep and luxury cruises—areas in 
which our companies are market leaders with durable pricing power. On a 
fundamental basis, we believe the earnings growth of our high-quality long 
portfolio will be resilient over time, with an ability to weather 
unpredictable geopolitical events and short-term news flow. With little 
changed in the underlying fundamentals and an easing of geopolitical 
tensions, our portfolio has seen a rapid recovery so far in the second 
quarter. 
 
The Mid Cap Opportunity Persists 
 
Our conviction in the mid cap opportunity described in our prior 
Assessments has strengthened. As of quarter-end, our long portfolio 
trades at a weighted-average P/E of approximately 20x our forward 
estimates, a modest premium to the S&P 500 Index and the Russell 
Midcap Index. On a free-cash-flow (FCF) basis (our preferred valuation 
metric), our long portfolio trades at a 5.4% FCF yield —a sizable discount 
to the S&P 500’s 3.5%  and the Russell Midcap’s 4.5% . We expect our long 
portfolio to grow cash earnings at 17% in 2026, a modest premium to 
growth expected by both the S&P 500 and Russell Midcap. 
 
Over the last two decades, our long portfolio has typically traded at an 
average premium of more than 20% to the broader indices, a premium 
justified by the higher quality and durable growth characteristics of our 
companies. As referenced above, that premium has now dissipated. While 
trying to pinpoint the timing of mean reversion would be an exercise in 
false precision, the most powerful catalyst for a broadening of market 
returns may be the one we discuss later in greater detail below: the 
diffusion of artificial intelligence (AI) into the broader economy. 
Enterprise adoption of AI tools has accelerated meaningfully since late 
2025; as these productivity gains filter through the economy, the market’s 
definition of “AI winners” should broaden well beyond the firms providing 
picks and shovels for the AI infrastructure buildout.

AI Productivity: From Promise to Proof 
 
In our last Assessment, we discussed our expectation that 2026 would 
mark a turning point in the transition from AI investment to measurable 
productivity gains. Over three months into the year, the evidence 
supporting this thesis continues to accumulate. 
 
A few examples across our portfolio: 
• Labcorp is using AI automation in pathology and microbiology to 
improve throughput, while its AI-powered “Test Finder” is helping 
physicians identify the right test in seconds. 
• EMCOR’s revenue is growing two to three times faster than 
headcount as the Company scales its data center and 
infrastructure operations across 17 electrical markets nationally.  
• C.H. Robinson has deployed AI agents across its freight 
brokerage operations that have increased shipments per person 
per day by more than 40% since the end of 2022. A freight quote 
that previously required 17 to 20 minutes of manual work and 
reached only 60% of custome r requests is now completed in less 
than 32 seconds with 100% coverage. The Company’s headcount 
is down 13% while it continues to gain market share, 
outperforming the Cass Freight Shipment Index by more than 
1,000 bps.  
 
The acceleration in enterprise AI adoption is now visible not just in our 
portfolio companies’ operations but also in the financial results of the AI 
tools they are deploying. Revenue at Anthropic, the developer of the 
Claude AI assistant, has more than tripled on an annualized basis since the 
start of 2025.  
 
The accompanying acceleration in spending is a leading indicator of 
broader productivity gains. As these tools continue to improve in 
capability and decline in cost, we expect the productivity benefits to 
compound, with each successive generation of AI models unlocking use 
cases that were previously uneconomical. 
 
The implications extend well beyond individual companies. If productivity 
growth can sustain the acceleration we have observed over the past two 
years, from approximately 1.5% annually in the 2010s to roughly 2.5% more 
recently, the macroeconomic consequences will be substantial. Higher 
productivity growth typically supports real wage gains without inflationary 
pressure, improves corporate margins without requiring price increases 
and could meaningfully strengthen the federal government’s fiscal

believe this acceleration is increasingly likely.

The Impact of AI on our Current Portfolio 
 
It is useful to frame our long portfolio’s exposure to this transformation 
across three broad categories. 
 
The first category, and a growing area of the portfolio, comprises our direct 
exposure to AI infrastructure. Companies including EMCOR, Hubbell, 
Amphenol and Quanta Services are enabling the physical buildout 
required to support AI, from connecting data centers to the power grid to 
manufacturing the electrical products needed to upgrade it. These are 
businesses that have operated for years or decades in their respective 
domains and are now benefiting from an acceleration in demand.  
 
The second category consists of companies that are direct beneficiaries of 
AI-driven productivity gains. As we detailed above, companies such as 
Labcorp, EMCOR and C.H. Robinson—as well as Corpay —are using AI 
not to sell technology but to compound the operational advantages they 
already possess. Whether manifested in margin improvements or 
incremental share gains versus peers, we expect these improvements to 
show in financial results.

portfolio, consists of businesses with durable competitive advantages that 
are largely insulated from AI disruption. These are companies whose

Looking Forward: Our Outlook for 2026 
 
We enter the remainder of 2026 with a sober recognition that the 
macroeconomic and geopolitical backdrop remains uncertain. Tariff policy 
continues to evolve; the Federal Reserve faces a delicate balancing act 
between supporting growth and containing inflation; and geopolitical risks 
remain. Even with a resolution to the conflict, there will be lingering 
economic consequences: The significant destruction of infrastructure 
around the Persian Gulf means that oil and gas volumes through the Strait 
of Hormuz may not fully recover for months, and oil prices sustained at or 
near current levels will place upward pressure on inflation and interest 
rates while higher gasoline prices weigh on disposable incomes and 
consumer spending. That said, the underlying resilience of the US 
economy, repeatedly demonstrated through this decade’s shocks, should 
prevent the economy from stalling out, creating a climate in which well-
managed businesses that can make their own luck continue to perform 
well. 
 
Against this backdrop, we take comfort in the quality and resilience of our 
portfolio. Our companies generate predictable cash earnings, possess

durable competitive advantages and are managed by proven leadership 
teams. On a fundamental basis, we expect our long portfolio to grow 
earnings at a mid-teens rate and for valuations to remain attractive on 
both an absolute and relative basis. 
 
We believe the current environment, characterized by elevated volatility, a 
narrowing of mega cap dominance and a renewed focus on fundamentals, 
is one that should ultimately favor our approach. We remain patient, 
disciplined and focused on the long term. 
 
Our Long Portfolio 
 
We focus on researching what we believe are competitively advantaged,

Live Nation Entertainment (LYV) is the global leader in live music 
promotion, event management and ticketing. With approximately 50% -
60% share in the US and roughly 30% worldwide, the Company dominates 
a market we expect to grow at a high-single-digit rate, supported by 
strong, lasting demand from both fans and artists. Ticketmaster processes 
roughly 70% of major venue tickets in North America. The integrated 
model connecting promotion, ticketing, venues and sponsorship creates a 
self-reinforcing flywheel. Venue expansion is the key driver of profit 
growth, as profit per fan from owned venues runs approximately 2.5x 
higher than from third-party venues. Depending on the pace of execution, 
we believe earnings growth could accelerate from the low-double digits to 
the mid-teens. In its most recent quarter, Live Nation delivered record 
adjusted operating income in its Concerts segment, with 30% growth for 
the full year. In a recent development, a federal jury found that Live 
Nation and Ticketmaster maintained monopoly power in the ticketing 
market. The Company has stated that pending motions and potential 
appeals will determine whether the verdict stands, and it remains 
confident that the ultimate outcome will not be materially different from 
the terms of the Department of Justice settlement reached earlier in the 
trial. Worst case, in a forced breakup, we believe that the Company trades 
at a discount to the values of Live Nation and Ticketmaster as 
independent entities. Based on our forward estimates, Live Nation trades 
at an adjusted FCF yield of 5% (ex cluding growth capital expenditures), 
and we believe that it will be able to grow FCF per share by more than 10% 
per year in each of the next three years. 
 
ITT (ITT) is a diversified industrial manufacturer with leading global 
positions in automotive brake pads and rail products (approximately 39%

applications (22%). The Company produces highly engineered, mission -

cost of failure vastly exceeds the cost of the component. Once a 
component is specified into a process plant or aircraft platform, the 
replacement cycle is measured in decades. This, combined with growing 
aftermarket revenue, creates durable switching costs and a widening 
competitive moat. ITT has gained meaningful market share through 
innovation and service excellence. A transformative development is ITT’s

expects to generate $80 million of cost savings by year three, with

will be able to grow FCF per share at a mid-teens rate in each of the next 
three years. 
 
Somnigroup (SGI), formerly known as Tempur Sealy, is the largest US 
mattress company, controlling more than 40% market share of the broad 
US market and over 50% of the premium segment (mattresses priced 
above $2,000). The Mattress Firm acquisition created the first v ertically 
integrated mattress manufacturer and retailer at scale, with approximately 
2,300 retail locations —a distribution advantage no competitor can 
replicate. Tempur-Pedic’s proprietary foam formulations remain the gold 
standard in premium sleep, and the integrated model provides pricing 
intelligence, inventory optimization and end-to-end control of the 
consumer experience. As industry volumes fell from 24 million to 17 
million units with rising interest rates, Somnigroup has been a cyclical 
thriver, expanding organic market share by more than 5% during the 
downturn and positioning itself to outperform as demand rebounds. In 
fact, we believe Somnigroup’s share gains are likely to accelerate over the 
next two years. Most recently, the Company reported strong Q4 results 
with 20% EPS growth and issued 2026 guidance calling for 19% EPS 
growth, driven almost entirely by operational improvements rather than an 
industry recovery. With incremental margins of 35% -40%, a normalization 
in industry volumes would drive substantial additional earnings growth. 
Based on our forward estimates, Somnigroup trades at a FCF yield of 5% , 
and we believe it will be able to grow FCF per share by more than 20% per 
year in each of the next three years. 
 
Martin Marietta Materials (MLM) is the second-largest aggregates 
producer in the US, with leading positions in over 90% of its markets, 
primarily in high-growth regions such as Texas, the Carolinas and Georgia. 
Aggregates is a business defined by local monopoly economics: Crushed

meaning the nearest quarry has an unassailable cost advantage. Permitting

time. The Company’s reserves, measured in decades of production, are 
among the most valuable and underappreciated assets on any balance

per year in each of the next three years. 
 
Tractor Supply (TSCO) is the largest US rural lifestyle retailer, generating 
nearly $15 billion in annual sales across more than 2,500 stores. The 
Company is the only farm and ranch retailer with true national scale in a 
highly fragmented market in which no direct national competitor exists. 
The Company serves recreational farmers, ranchers and landowners. 
Approximately 85% of its sales come from non -discretionary products, 
with over half of those from consumable, usable and edible (CUE) items, 
such as pet food and livestock feed, which drive repeat traffic. With 
roughly 15% market share, the Company occupies a niche too specialized 
for Home Depot or Walmart to address and too scaled for local 
independents to match. Tractor Supply’s Neighbor’s Club loyalty program 
counts over 35 million members and drives approximately 80% of sales. 
FY 2026 guidance calls for approximately 6% EPS growth, and we expect 
further share gains as Tractor Supply’s scale and sourcing power help it 
navigate the current environment better than smaller peers. Based on our 
forward estimates, Tractor Supply trades at an adjusted FCF yield of 5% 
(excluding growth capital expenditures), and we believe it will be able to 
grow FCF per share by an average of more than 10% per year over the next 
three years. 
 
Labcorp (LH) is a leading clinical laboratory company processing over 
600 million tests per year. Labcorp and Quest Diagnostics together 
control roughly 50% of the US independent clinical laboratory market, 
serving as a duopoly with massive scale advantages in logistics, testing 
automation and payer negotiation. The competitive advantages 
compound over time: As Labcorp processes more volume, its cost per test 
declines, enabling reinvestment in higher-complexity specialty tests for 
which reimbursement is more favorable and competition thinner. The

Viking Holdings (VIK) is the leading operator of high-end river and ocean 
cruises, catering primarily to English-speaking retirees. In river cruises 
across Europe, Viking commands approximately 52% market share, over 
three times its nearest rival. In luxury ocean cruises, it holds roughly 26% 
share. The competitive advantages are structural and compounding: 
Approximately 70% of bookings come from repeat guests or their referrals, 
giving Viking the lowest customer acquisition cost in the industry. Unlike 
the vast majority of peers, Viking’s no-kids, no-casinos policy creates 
differentiated appeal to higher-end customers aged 55 and older. These 
guests typically pay three times mainstream cruise fares to sail with Viking. 
Guests book approximately 11 months ahead with payments made roughly 
seven months before departure, supporting strong working capital

the existing customer base, and the purpose-built fleet (designed to last

90% of 2026 capacity already sold, short -term market volatility can be 
absorbed without pricing concessions. In its most recent quarter, Viking 
reported strong results with 2026 advance bookings of $6 billion, up 13% 
year over year (YoY), providing visibility for low- to mid-teens top-line 
growth. Based on our forward estimates, Viking trades at an adjusted FCF 
yield of 8% (ex cluding growth capital expenditures), and we believe it will 
be able to grow FCF per share at a high-teens rate in each of the next 
three years.

Wabtec (WAB) is the global leader in freight rail technology and services,

embodied in our Core Principles, which serve to shape our identity as a 
Firm and to inform our actions. 
 
Originality 
We generate our own ideas and never deploy common practice without 
skepticism. We strive to avoid the herd. 
 
We do our own research and never exchange views with other managers. As a 
result, we tend to have differentiated portfolios. 
 
Innate Curiosity 
We ask dumb questions. We challenge universally accepted beliefs and 
seek new levels of understanding. 
 
We conduct original, continuous and rigorous research supported by our 
dedicated Qualitative Field Research and Data Analytics Teams. 
 
Continuous Improvement 
We constantly reassess our investments, policies and processes. We 
embrace a culture of intellectual integrity, learning, mentorship, self-
reflection, entrepreneurship and growth. 
 
Complacency is the enemy. We learn little from our successes; we must own 
and learn from our mistakes. 
 
Charity

The Shares of SEG Partners Offshore, Ltd. (the “SEG Fund”) may not be offered for a public offering 
in Japan unless a securities registration statement pursuant to Article 4, Paragraph 1 of the Financial 
Instruments and Exchange Act of Japan (including any amendments or successor laws, the “FIEA”) 
has been filed with the Director of the Kanto Local Finance Bureau of the Ministry of Finance of 
Japan. 
 
No securities registration statement for a public offering has been filed or will be filed with respect 
to the solicitation for the purchase of the Shares in Japan or to a resident of Japan as the Shares will 
be offered in Japan as a “private placement for qualified institutional investors only” (tekikaku kikan 
toushika muke shibo) under Article 2, Paragraph 3, Items 2(a) of the FIEA. 
 
The SEG Fund has filed a notification with the Commissioner of the Financial Services Agency of 
Japan (the “FSA”) pursuant to the Act on Investment Trusts and Investment Corporations of Japan 
in connection with the private placement of the Shares in Japan. A report with respect to the 
placement and redemption of the Shares may be filed by the SEG Fund with the Ministry of Finance 
of Japan, as required, in accordance with the terms and conditions of the Foreign Exchange and 
Foreign Trade Law of Japan. 
 
For the purpose of complying with the FIEA and any other applicable law of Japan, any offering of the 
Shares of the SEG Fund shall be conducted as a “self-offering” (jikoboshu) by Ruth Kyle, solely in her 
capacity as the Marketing Officer of the SEG Fund. Furthermore, for the avoidance of doubt, to the 
extent that any information of the SEG Fund has been delivered or distributed by an officer or 
employee of an affiliated entity of the SEG Fund, such information has been delivered or distributed 
on behalf of Ruth Kyle, in her capacity as a Marketing Officer of the SEG Fund.
