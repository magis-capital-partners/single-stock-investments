Market Context and Performance Contribution
March was a more challenging month for the portfolio as markets remained choppy
and driven by Trump’s tweets. We saw a mix of geopolitical shock, inflation pressure
and sharp sector rotation, but not the broad sell-off that might have been expected.
Our process stayed the same: cut exposure, stay disciplined and focus on
opportunities with attractive asymmetric risk reward.
 
Key Performance Contributors Included:
Copper Smelters: Short Boliden AB and Aurubis AG (STO: BOL, ETR: NDA)
Our thesis that copper smelters would face both cost and revenue pressure has
contributed positively to our performance this month. We expected economic
uncertainty to put pressure on copper demand, and rising energy prices to squeeze
margins. Those with good memories may recall that we made this trade in 2022,
which also contributed to our performance amid uncertainty after the War in Ukraine
began.
 
Gold Hedge: Short Gold Futures (MGCJ6) 
The gold hedge helped offset part of the weakness in junior gold exposure, which
remained under pressure as the gold price corrected. It was a useful way to reduce

AI Structural Losers: Shot WPP and Publicis (LSE: WPP, Euronext Paris: PUB)
The short in advertising agencies continued to contribute as the AI disruption theme
unfolded. Traditional agency models are still facing pressure from automation,
pricing compression and a weaker structural growth outlook. February’s move was
not a one-off, and March reinforced the idea that this remains a long-term structural
loser theme rather than a short-term trade.
 
Detractors:
Junior Gold Miners: Long GDXJ ETF
The junior gold mining ETF detracted as gold prices corrected and the sector gave
back part of its recent gains. Our short gold hedge helped offset some of the loss,
but the move still triggered our stop-loss discipline in parts of the book in the
beginning of March.
 
Defence Exposure: Kratos (NASDAQ: KTOS) and Babcock (LSE: BAB) 
 Defence names moved against us even though the original thesis had been
supported by rising conflict risk and the prospect of prolonged and greater defence
spending. These positions sit in our dynamic profit growth bucket, where we like the
long-term structural setup but remain willing to exit if the market disagrees in the
short term. In this case, the move lower was sharp enough to hit our risk controls.
 
Shipping: Long DHT (NYSE: DHT)
Our long position in DHT Holdings, a crude oil tanker company, detracted from our
return in March as sharp sentiment-driven swings amid U.S.-Iran war uncertainty
triggered our stop-loss. We remain constructive as the business still sits in an
attractive part of the cycle, with supportive shipping rates and a relatively cheap
valuation, but the position was not rewarded in the near term.
