Dear Partner, 
 
The 1st quarter for ACK was a tale of two environments. January and February were 
marked by increasing evidence that the long-awaited recovery in non-residential and 
residential construction is in the early stages.  Spending on infrastructure for data 
centers continued having a halo effect in a number of tangential areas. These trends 
resulted in outsized performance for the Fund in several investments and we were 
rewarded through February. However, following the U.S. attack on Iran the playbook 
from 2022 reoccurred: ‘sell anything that can be impacted by crude oil and crude oil 
derivative inputs’, as the fear of margin compression and slowing sales growth 
permeated investors’ reactions. “Goes to show you have to play it slow,” once again 
took root in our investment approach as March proved to be a difficult month. 
While we don’t let macro factors influence positioning at the portfolio level, we very 
much respond to the potential impact that these factors have on our individual 
companies in terms of the cost of energy and its derivatives. Petrochemicals can, at 
times, be a meaningful input for the industrial sub-sectors that constitute a meaningful 
part of our investment universe. The commencement of the U.S. war in Iran caused us to 
re-examine the exposure to the price of oil of each of the companies that we follow.  
2 
 
Many of these companies would be perceived to have earnings exposure to higher 
costs of oil and consequently sold off significantly more than the market.   
We looked at our growth assumptions and re-evaluated them based on higher oil 
prices, and, for the most part, we did not make significant changes to our forecasts. In 
instances where we have determined that elevated oil prices could have a substantive 
cost increase and a company did not have the ability to re-price its products and 
services in this environment, we sold those investments.  We also had a few investments 
reach our price targets in the first two months of the year, and as the consensus 
migrated towards our view we chose to either exit or trim our exposure.  We expect the 
current investment environment to continue to be dynamic, as demand could 
fluctuate in the near term based on energy costs and its impact on consumer 
sentiment. 
Despite the near-term macro noise, we have been gaining more confidence in a 
rebound in private construction and the continued expansion of the domestic industrial 
economy, as mentioned previously. We are now seeing tangible evidence of positive 
impacts from nearshoring and then the lower cost of capital that is conveyed by tax 
incentives from accelerated depreciation. These changes could have major positive 
impacts on non-residential construction, industrial production, machinery, and major 
areas of infrastructure.  
Additionally, a new theme has emerged as we have identified more industrial 
companies that have significant growth potential from capitalizing on the emerging 
800v architecture used in data centers and electrical infrastructure. We recognized this 
space in the fall of 2025 as a revolutionary change in components but understood that 
implementation would be in the future.  Evidence of orders and design wins are 
beginning to be reported by several of our investments and we anticipate a material 
impact heading into 2027.  We will discuss this further below.  We expect to continue to 
find more investment opportunities across various suppliers for this new architecture. 
Littelfuse ("LFUS") is a diversified industrial technology company that designs, 
manufactures, and sells circuit protection, power control, and sensing products across 
three segments: Electronics, Transportation, and Industrial.  
Electronics is the crown jewel of the company: it is the largest segment with the highest 
margins and houses the passive protection franchise where LFUS holds top two global 
market share in virtually every product category. Electronics and Industrial have 
significant overlap in end markets (selling into datacenters, industrial controls, and 
energy storage as examples), while Transportation sells into passenger and commercial 
vehicles. The company aims for long-term organic growth of 5-7% per year, with goals 
for another 5%-7% from M&A. We think these are conservative targets with real upside 
3 
 
from providing content into datacenters, which, in our view, the market is materially 
underpricing. 
The transition to 800 volt DC architecture is a fundamental change in how power is 
delivered inside a datacenter. Today's power chain runs through 5-6 conversion stages 
with end-to-end efficiency of only ~78%, meaning 22% of all electricity purchased is 
wasted as heat before a single computation happens. An 800 volt DC architecture 
collapses this to 2-3 stages, pushing efficiency to 85%+. Assuming ~50GWs of U.S. 800 
volt datacenter installations by 2030, the efficiency gains from the transition equate to 
3-4GWs of power saved.  
Every component that remains in this simplified, higher-voltage chain requires more 
robust protection - that is LFUS's core competency. Our research indicates that 
datacenter customers place a significant premium on the breadth of product offering, 
an established track record, and quality of existing customer references when 
qualifying protection and power control suppliers. LFUS checks all three boxes and is 
among the strongest positioned suppliers in the space, in our view. 
LFUS’ datacenter design wins have more than doubled in 2025, while datacenter 
revenues have moved from < 10% of revenue to low double-digits over just the last two 
years. Management has recently detailed a 2x-4x content uplift in the new 800 volt DC 
architectures from the existing architectures. What we believe is not fully appreciated 
by the market is that the multiplier is on a per-kilowatt basis, meaning that it compounds 
with the simultaneous increase in rack power densities. In other words, as we move from 
~45kW in traditional enterprise racks toward 1MW in next-generation AI deployments, 
we estimate that LFUS content per rack scales disproportionately from roughly $150 
today all the way up to potentially ~$5,000+ if the higher power densities are 
implemented.  
Management is also reorganizing its go-to-market strategy by consolidating sales teams 
and shifting from selling individual components to selling an integrated solution across 
segments. Instead of having separate salespeople calling on the same customer for 
different product lines, the new model has one account manager selling all three 
segments to a customer, which increases wallet share per engagement while reducing 
the headcount needed to deliver it. We believe this reorganization is still in its early 
innings and will drive both share gains and margin expansion.  
Our forecast is for revenue to grow at least low-teens organically over the next few 
years with significant operating leverage translating to >25% EBITDA margins in 2027E, 
up from today’s 21%. Our near-term price target for LFUS is 15x 2027E EV/EBITDA, 
implying a $525 price target, up ~35%. Our medium-term target implies upside to $800 
or 100% based on multiple expansion to 20x on our 2028E EBITDA estimate. The

Montana Aerospace (“AERO”) Along with our successful investment and exit of 
Astronics we think we have identified another undiscovered low multiple opportunity in 
the aerospace area. AERO is a vertically integrated manufacturer of aerostructures for 
commercial and defense aircraft. We believe AERO is entering a multi-year commercial 
aerospace OEM upcycle, and that management's actions to simplify the portfolio and 
broaden the shareholder base position the stock to re-rate accordingly. 
The company is a direct supplier of structural components to both Boeing and Airbus 
across major commercial programs. AERO casts its own aluminum billets from ~70% 
recycled aluminum scrap and continues up the value chain by extruding aluminum into 
profiles, machining into finished parts, applying surface treatment, and completing the 
final assembly.  Its end-to-end integration capabilities are a competitive advantage as 
it eliminates intermediate shipping and handling and captures margin that would 
otherwise be paid to intermediary suppliers. We estimate 40% of the cost of a finished 
aerospace component is merely just value chain transportation, which we believe gives 
AERO pricing flexibility that subscale competitors do not have. Additionally, AERO 
extends its capabilities in aluminum to titanium and hard metal machining.  
AERO's Romanian footprint also delivers aerostructures capacity at labor costs well 
below Western European peers. This is an advantage that cannot be replicated quickly, 
as qualifying a new plant on existing platforms like the A320 or A350 is a multi-year year 
process. AERO is also one of the few major suppliers with spare capacity and the 
willingness to expand into the narrowbody ramp which enables it to win share today at 
high teens EBITDA margins. 
AERO’s revenue is largely tied to OEM production rates. We believe the current rate 
trajectory implies meaningful revenue growth of in excess of 15% even before any share 
gains or new program wins. Both Airbus and Boeing are intending to ramp narrowbody 
production to record levels: Airbus is targeting an increase in monthly A320 production 
of 40% by the end of 2027 and Boeing is planning to increase monthly 737 MAX 
production by 20+% by the end of 2026. Widebody production, where AERO also has 
significant content per shipset, is also expected to increase meaningfully over the next 
12-24 months. We estimate that every narrowbody plane manufactured by Boeing and 
Airbus carries ~$500k-$1mil. of AERO content, while every widebody carries $1-$2 mil.

Over the past 18 months management has divested two business segments, making the 
company into a pureplay aerostructures provider. We believe the diversified structure 
obscured the core aerospace asset, causing the stock to trade on a conglomerate 
multiple rather than a higher aerospace multiple.  
The closest Aerospace & Defense comparables that are tied predominantly to the OEM 
build cycle (ATRO, DCO, HXL) all trade at 15x+ 2026E EV/EBITDA.  We believe AERO, 
which has margins at or higher than these comps, should trade at least 15x our 2026E 
EBITDA estimate, which would be over 100% increase in stock price in the near term.

environment of companies whose multiples are being masked by fears of real 
slowdown or no growth.  If we are right and “play it slow”, we will be rewarded by

Remember investing is a marathon not a sprint.
