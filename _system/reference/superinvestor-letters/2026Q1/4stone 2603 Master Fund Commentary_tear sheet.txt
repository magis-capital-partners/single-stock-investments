March 2026 Performance Commentary Fourthstone Master Opportunity Fund Ltd | 1
The fund outperformed in March thanks to positive contribution from its short book, idiosyncratic 
positive news for several fund holdings and stock selection more broadly.  The positive momentum 
has continued this April for both the fund and banks with the NASDAQ Bank Index up approx. 7.5% 
this April.
First quarter earnings season is drawing to a close, and we are pleased to report that bank 
fundamentals remain strong, and profitability continues to improve.  The first quarter is typically 
a slower period for growth, but Fed data and our recent conversations suggest that loan growth 
has remained seasonally strong for many banks relative to prior years.   Loan growth at US banks 
continued to accelerate through the end of 1Q with YoY loan growth increasing from 5.4% on Dec. 
31, 2025 to 6.6% on April 1, 2026.  Commercial and Industrial lending lead the way but commercial 
real estate lending also advanced.
Margins continue to benefit from loan and securities portfolios repricing, with incremental 
leverage still available on the deposit side. Although the yield curve flattened somewhat in recent 
weeks following the Iran war, it remains near levels of the past year and meaningfully steeper than 
a 2023/2024, providing an ongoing earnings tailwind.
Share buybacks are trending higher than anticipated by the Street in 1Q, and forward guides were 
good, implying a generally favorable 2026 outlook.
Mike Cricenti
We expect another solid quarter for asset quality across the banking sector, consistent with a 
gradual normalization in credit costs. Our conversations with bank management teams point to 
limited credit pressures that are borrower- or situation-specific rather than driven by broader 
stress.  
In addition to this constructive view of credit quality, we think some investors under appreciate 
banks’ ability to generate earnings to protect themselves in the event that unforeseen credit 
costs arise. It is true that new risks have emerged on the geopolitical landscape, private credit 
concerns, and inflation, but banks have superb capacity to cover additional credit losses and/or 
build reserves this year.  PPNR is pre-tax pre-provision net revenue or the operating cash flow 
for banks. We think investors should take comfort that the coverage of PPNR to expected loan 
losses in 2026 is 5.3x for large-cap banks and 11.4x for SMID-cap banks, implying a significant 
margin of safety for any surprise deterioration in credit.

Investor concerns surrounding loans to non-depository financial institutions (NDFIs), and private 
credit in particular, have been one of several factors weighing on bank stocks in recent months. 
In response to these concerns, many large US banks provided investors with additional details 
on their NDFI portfolios during first quarter earnings calls.  PNC Financial Services Group Inc. 
(PNC) CFO Robert Reilly said loans to NDFIs are the company’s lowest risk loans, with roughly 
90% being investment grade or equivalent.  PNC has experienced “virtually no losses” in the last 
25 years of lending to NFDIs, and the firm expects zero losses going forward, Reilly said.
PNC CEO Bill Demchak further clarified, “ While we recognize that there are many market 
concerns out there from energy prices to AI to private credit, we are not seeing anything that 
suggests these issues are broadly impacting our customers or our credit quality in the near term.  
Specifically in regard to the increased attention on banks’ exposure to non-depository financial 
institutions…the soundbite you ought to walk away with here is that we don’t see any loss content 
in this book and certainly don’t see any exposure to a systemic event…”

BarclayHedge web page below are the estimated number of advisors surveyed for each of the awards. The % of advisors that received that ranking is a calculated % derived

was paid to participate in the survey. Preqin Hedge Fund Online created and conducts the survey based upon net return information provided by the fund manager. Fund 
managers must report a full 36-month track record to be eligible. No fees are paid to participate in the survey and the rankings are not indicative of future performance. The 
Net Returns of Top Performing Hedge Funds, January 2015 – December 2017 presented are based on a ranking by net returns over the period 2015-2017 as per the Preqin 
Special Report: Top Performing Hedge Funds 2017. Based on information provided in this report, the estimated number of advisors surveyed for the Net Returns of Top 
Performing Hedge Funds, January 2015 – December 2017 was 5,804 and 0.017% were ranked 9th. The % of advisors that received that ranking is a calculated % derived by 
dividing 1 by the number of estimated number of advisors surveyed for each of the awards per the Preqin Special Report: HFM Top Performing Hedge Funds 2017 and HFM 
Global Equity Under $1BN.  HFMWeek US awards are open to any fund with its management team based in North America, a minimum AUM of $25m, and a three-year track 
record with monthly performance data. Annual performance periods are between July 1 and June 30. The awards were decided by a judging panel with each member of the 
panel having an equal weight in choosing the winners in each category they are assigned.  No fee was paid to participate in any of the above award surveys and these ratings
