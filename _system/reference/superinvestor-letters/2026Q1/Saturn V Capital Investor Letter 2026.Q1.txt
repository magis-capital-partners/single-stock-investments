The year, thus far, has largely been defined by elevated macro -driven volatility, with U.S. equities 
reacting to escalating Middle East conflict, sharp increases in energy prices, and a rotation away from 
technology—particularly AI-impacted software. This is evidenced by the S&P 500 Index closing the 
quarter down -4.4%. While this backdrop may have overshadowed the constructive setup for biotech 
entering the year, the sector remains fundamentally insulated from day-to-day headline risk.  
Biotech and pharma have  historically demonstrated relative resilience during periods of 
macroeconomic stress. Healthcare demand is largely non -discretionary, and the space has often 
outperformed broader markets during past downturns, supported by stable end-markets, innovation-
driven growth, and limited direct sensitivity to economic cycles.

on par with , or modestly exceeding , therapies from Takeda and Johnson & Johnson. The stock 
subsequently appreciated to ~$23 on the news, near our pre-data target of $25. The next key catalyst

is in systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE), a~$1.5B market with less competitive intensity.  Data is

Disc Medicine (Ticker: IRON), which we’ve discussed previously, is a biopharmaceutical company 
developing therapies for rare hematologic diseases. Its lead program, bitopertin, targets Erythropoietic 
Protoporphyria (EPP), a genetic disorder causing extreme sensitivity to sunlight, with  severe cases 
resulting in blistering after only brief sun exposure. The program received an expedited review pathway 
under the FDA’s Commissioner’s National Priority Voucher program; however, in February 2026, the 
FDA declined accelerated approval, citing the need for additional clinical data to support efficacy. In 
our view, the decision reflects the current regulatory posture —particularly under Vinay Prasad—and 
may have varied under different timing or context. While the outcome delays the timeline and weighed 
on the stock, which is where we were wrong, we remain constructive on the underlying biology and 
clinical path with Phase 3 data expected by year-end. 
Ocular Thereapeutix (Ticker: OCUL) focuses on ophthalmic diseases, with its lead asset, Axpaxli , 
currently in the Phase 3 SOL -1 trial for wet age -related macular degeneration (wAMD), a >$10B 
market dominated by anti -VEGF therapies that require frequent injections. Our thesis centered on 
the potential to deliver comparable efficacy with far less treatment, addressing a key unmet need. In 
February 2026, the company reported that SOL -1 met its primary endpoint, demonstrating clinically 
meaningful efficacy at n ine months. However, the magnitude of benefit was more modest than we 
had anticipated. Combined with concerns around trial design and ultimate commercial potential, we 
exited our small (~2%) position for a loss in favor of higher-conviction opportunities.
