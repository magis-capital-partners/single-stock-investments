Market and Portfolio Review 
 
February was another strong month for the Fund, extending 
the constructive tone that began in January. Equity markets 
continued to reward momentum and AI -linked bottleneck 
names. A sharp turn in geopolitics into early March has  
introduced greater volatility and led us to adopt a more tactical 
approach. 
 
Overall, AI disruption risk became more front -and-center in 
February with increasing skepticism in almost all businesses 
that are asset light, service heavy and primary white collar 
worker jobs. There was also a rising crowdedness into  
perceived AI winners – many related to AI infrastructure. This 
trend began to show up as episodic selloffs in areas such as 
software, while capital rotated toward areas perceived as  
harder to disrupt – including energy, industrials, materials, and 
infrastructure.  
 
Closer to home, Japan continued to deliver strong performance 
following the Takaichi -led LDP landslide victory, although  
debate around Bank of Japan forward action remains. In  
Korea, the KOSDAQ 3000 initiative continued with reports in 
early February highlighting the potential for pension funds to 
increase exposure to KOSDAQ and launches of new actively 
managed KOSDAQ ETFs. In general, Korea has seen  
significant inflow globally as evidenced by many of the levered 
ETF products emerging in both US and Hong Kong exchanges, 
exposing it to significant volatility in recent weeks.  
 
As we write this, markets have experienced a sharp increase in 
volatility following the re -emergence of geopolitical risk. The 
unfortunate escalation of the Iran conflict spiked energy prices 
and risk sentiment. Oil moved sharply higher amid fears of 
disruption around the Strait of Hormuz, reviving inflation  
concerns and tightening financial conditions at the margin.  
Energy shocks tend to matter disproportionately for Asia given 
the region’s net oil import exposure, which also contributed to 
the unwind of the EM vs US trade that has been popular this 
year. From a flows perspective, the market action has been 
consistent with a forced de -grossing and momentum unwind. 
Year-to-date winners began to underperform relative to  
laggards, while trend -following and systematic positioning  
appeared to shift rapidly as volatility rose.  
  
 
AI Update  
 
On the AI front, the “frontier lab” cohort dominated headlines, 
with AI capital raising and funding momentum continuing 
through the month. OpenAI announced a blockbuster $110bn 
funding round, with headline valuation figures reported around 
~$840bn, while Anthropic also raised $30bn at a $380bn post 
money valuation. In addition, AI applications and models are 
increasingly moving beyond the generic chatbot and into higher 
value enterprise workflows. It is reported that as of early March 
2026, OpenAI’s Annual Recovering Revenue (ARR) hit $25bn 
with Anthropic hitting $19bn, implying that both have roughly 
doubled since December 2025. The viral adoption of 
OpenClaw provides another tangible evidence that agentic 
platforms with local tool access, are not theoretical, but  
represent an emerging usage pattern with real -world  
adoption.  
 
This trend is also observed in China, as AI platforms like  
MiniMax and Zhipu also saw solid price momentum in  
February, standing out against broader tech weakness.  
 
Outlook  
 
While the situation remains fluid amid the recent escalation in 
geopolitical risk, our base case is that the Trump administration 
has strong incentives to limit the duration and severity of the 
conflict, given the inflation sensitivity of a US mid -term election 
year. However, this does not prevent significant volatility and 
upward momentum in energy prices in the next few months. 
Against this backdrop, we have reduced risk somewhat while 
maintaining the flexibility to add exposure opportunistically if 
conditions stabilize and/or there is sufficient margin of safety in 
market prices. Our focus remains on owning bottleneck  
beneficiaries where fundamentals are supported by visible  
demand and constrained supply but staying nimble and mindful 
of potential demand shocks and cost increases due to current 
geopolitical situation. 
   
In particular, we need to monitor the developments in Middle 
East, and its impact on the trajectory of AI adoption and AI 
Capex spending. We continue to take the view that a  
disciplined and selective approach, combined with active risk 
management, remains the appropriate approach.
