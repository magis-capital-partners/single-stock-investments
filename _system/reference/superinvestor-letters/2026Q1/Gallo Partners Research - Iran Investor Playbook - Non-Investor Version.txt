What is the Capacity of the U.S. SPR?
19
The U.S. SPR is a series of salt caverns designed to hold a total of ~725 MMBbls of crude oil.
• Created by the Energy Policy and Conservation Act of 1975 as a direct response to the 1973-74 Arab oil embargo
• Minimum level is 150 MMBbls, lower volumes risk damage to the caverns
• Four underground sites along the Gulf Coast with a max drawdown rate of 4.4 MMBBld (90 days to empty from full)
• Sources: RBN Energy, IEA, FERC, EIA
