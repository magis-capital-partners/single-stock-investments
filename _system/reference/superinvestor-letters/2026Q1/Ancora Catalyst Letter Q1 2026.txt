Warner Bros. Discovery, Inc. (NASDAQ: WBD) – Profile  
The saga over who would own Warner Bros. was something that originally unfolded through the fall of 2025 as WBD entered 
a strategic review after receiving acquisition offers for the Company. This process culminated with Warner Bros. entering into 
a merger agreement with Netflix in early December for $27.75 per share plus the value of the Discovery Global Networks 
spinoff. However, this was just the beginning of a sequence of fireworks to decide who would ultimately end up owning this 
legendary collection of assets.  
There was plenty of heartburn with shareholders when Warner Bros. entered the merger agreement with Netflix in December 
2025. This revolved around the widespread belief that the WBD Board chose an inferior offer from Netflix while Paramount 
Skydance (PSKY) submitted a $30 cash offer that valued Warner Bros. at a higher consideration than the Netflix merger 
consideration. At the time, the Warner Bros. management team and Board defended the decision to merge with Netflix by 
highlighting concerns around Paramount’s ability to finance the transaction and questioning Paramount’s ability to navigate 
the regulatory review required to close the transaction.  
As the calendar turned to 2026, the media firestorm over Warner Bros. reached a fever pitch as Netflix rushed to gather 
shareholder support ahead of the shareholder vote for the transaction, while Paramount used a media blitz to highlight why 
its bid for WBD was superior. The shareholder consternation caused from the complicated Netflix merger consideration, 
whether the WBD Board would reengage with a higher bidder, and the market volatility in January and February manifested 
in Warner Bros. stock trading at a discount to the Netflix offer price, setting the stage for a compelling opportunity for an 
activist to hold the WBD Board accountable for not engaging in what was presumably a superior offer for all WBD 
shareholders.  
Our team had been familiar with the Warner Bros. / Netflix / Paramount situation for months, given how much airtime the 
media had given the saga of the merger. Exiting 2025, the Fund was sitting on a significant amount of cash after monetizing 
our positions in Norfolk Southern and Sealed Air during the fourth quarter. As such, we viewed the Warner Bros. situation as 
a compelling opportunity to deploy dry powder into the merger arbitrage opportunity as the WBD stock price remained below 
the original Netflix transaction price. We also believed there was a high likelihood that Paramount would raise its offer to 
ultimately win shareholder support. Additionally, we viewed this situation as one where activism could be used to compel the 
  
5 
WBD Board to do right by shareholders and re-engage with another buyer whose offer to acquire the Company was higher 
than the bid they originally took.  
The setup enabled us to structure a trade that used a combination of common equity and a ladder call strategy that would 
significantly increase in value should Warner Bros. re-engage with Paramount and ultimately decide to take the higher bid. 
After putting on our position in late January / early February, we published a detailed presentation disclosing our intention to 
vote against the Warner Bros.-Netflix merger (Ancora's Case For Voting No - WBD/Netflix). There were several reasons we 
were opposed to the transaction, which included:  
1. Our belief that WBD’s Board ran a flawed strategic review process and may have neglected its fiduciary duties 
to shareholders in the process. 
2. Our belief that WBD’s Board entered an inferior transaction at the expense of shareholders.  
3. Our belief that WBD’s Board was asking shareholders to vote on a transaction in which their shareholders did 
not know the final consideration of their shares due to the unknown value of the Discovery Global spinoff.  
4. Our belief that WBD and Netflix were misleading investors about the ease of getting regulators to approve the 
transaction.  
5. Our belief that WBD’s Board was not willing to engage with Paramount despite the Company having a higher 
all-cash offer in addition to a stated willingness to increase its offer further.  
Our efforts were well received by shareholders who shared the same sentiment about the WBD-Netflix merger consideration 
being inadequate. Our presentation was further enhanced with a CNBC interview with Ancora Alternatives President and lead 
Portfolio Manager, James Chadwick, where he outlined our thesis, highlighted the need for the WBD Board to do right by 
shareholders and left the WBD Board with a reminder that the nominating window for directors was coming up (Ancora's 
James Chadwick CNBC Interview - WBD/Netflix). Ancora’s public voice put the spotlight on WBD’s Board to hopefully re-
engage with Paramount or seek a higher offer from Netflix. Less than two weeks after we published our presentation, WBD 
reopened the process to allow Paramount to submit a best and final offer for the Company. After several days of back-and-
forth political theater, Paramount raised its offer to an all-cash $31 per share. WBD’s Board deliberated on the new offer and 
deemed the all-cash $31 offer to be superior to the Netflix offer. Netflix ultimately decided not to match Paramount’s new 
offer, enabling Paramount to win the auction for Warner Bros. 
Americold Realty Trust, Inc. (NYSE: COLD) “Project Frosty” – Update  
We highlighted Americold in our fourth quarter letter, unveiling a two-part activist thesis predicated on non-core asset sales, 
a significant cost rationalization opportunity and a condensed North American cold storage footprint over time. We believe 
these items, coupled with a modest recovery in cold storage fundamentals, should drive substantial increases in occupancy, 
translating into much higher EBITDA dollars and margins over the next 12-18 months. In February, COLD reported relatively 
in-line fourth quarter results and provided a less-worse-than-feared initial 2026 outlook of 2026 adjusted funds from 
operations (AFFO) of $1.20–$1.30 per share (vs. consensus of $1.22 at the time). The guidance assumed no improvement in 
consumer demand, economic occupancy flat to -300 basis points, 100–200 basis points pressure on revenue per pallet, $30M 
of annualized cost savings and no benefit from unannounced asset sales or deleveraging transactions. The stock rallied nicely 
on earnings, given overly negative sentiment/positioning and the following two items, in our view: 
› CEO Rob Chambers called the bottom in physical occupancy, stating that volumes have stabilized and honestly 
trended better than expected to end 2025 and 1Q26 to-date. Further, customer inventory levels are at such low 
levels that any modest uptick in demand will likely force material inventory restocking, which he believes is 
likely the case as we move through 2026. That said, this impact is NOT reflected in his 2026 outlook.  
 
› 2026 economic occupancy was provided as flat to -300 basis points year-over-year (vs. prior commentary of -
200 to -300 basis points) with the change reflecting the better-than-expected sequential occupancy 
performance in 4Q25 relative to expectations (+290 basis points quarter-over-quarter vs. internal expectations 
for 100-125 basis points uplift) resulting in a better starting point exiting 2025 and early green shoots on 
customer renewals year-to-date.

Despite the large rally at earnings, COLD has proceeded to underperform the market since then, given its high correlation 
with interest rates as the 10-year treasury rate surged from 3.96% on February 27th (prior to the Iran War) to 4.44% on 
March 27th, before settling in the 4.25-4.30% range over the past three weeks. We believe this underperformance is 
unwarranted and largely transitory, considering our view that cold storage fundamentals are transitioning from stabilizing to 
now early improvements, and when combined with the prudent 2026 guide, should set COLD up for a beat and raise cadence 
throughout the year. The improvement in underlying fundamentals is also corroborated by the third-party alternative data 
that we track, which has shown a deceleration in the year-over-year decline in volumes and revenue beginning last 
November and improving on a seasonally adjusted basis throughout 1Q26. Further, on the strategic / capital allocation front, 
there was a meaningful change in tone by CEO Rob Chambers on the fourth quarter earnings call, who committed to all 
options being on the table for de-leveraging, including all international assets, while also putting a firm timeline of 
announcing a transaction or transactions in the first half of the year, in order to get below 6x net leverage. This tone was 
different from the third quarter earnings call, where Rob highlighted the strategic value and synergies across each of 
Americold’s geographies. We were pleased with the public commitment to move quickly and believe the Board has no 
shortage of potential options to choose from, given the level of inbound interest from both private equity and strategics. 
More recently, there was a significant development in the Americold story with another shareholder, Sieve Capital, publicly 
announcing a Withhold Campaign targeting Chairman Mark Patterson and fellow Director, Andrew Powers, at the annual 
meeting in May (Sieve Capital Withhold Campaign at Americold). It appears that Sieve’s goal is to convince fellow 
shareholders to vote against the renomination of both directors, which, if successful, would require both directors to submit 
their resignations to the Board, given Americold’s majority vote standard for uncontested elections, and immediately 
commence a full strategic alternatives process to sell the Company. We believe this enhanced shareholder engagement can 
significantly increase pressure on the current Board to move swiftly with evaluating all possible avenues to accelerate 
shareholder value creation. Our due diligence indicates that interest in parts or a potential sale of the entire Company has 
markedly increased post the following sequence of events in December 2025: Ancora’s COLD presentation at the Bloomberg 
Activist Forum, subsequent Ancora – Americold settlement and publicly-announced private equity interest in the Company’s 
international assets. We believe the most likely scenario going forward is multiple asset sales or a whole company take-
private transaction within the next 6-9 months. Ultimately, our thesis remains firmly intact, and we continue to see a positive 
asymmetrical risk-reward skew with fundamental fair value around $21.50, while a take-private transaction could fetch $22-
$24 given the likelihood of a bidding war for COLD. 
Vestis Corporation (NYSE: VSTS) – Update 
We profiled Vestis in last quarter’s letter and indicated that new CEO Jim Barber was in the early stages of transforming the 
business following a disastrous two years as a public company post the spin-off from Aramark in October 2023. The stock has 
been one of the Fund’s best-performing positions year to-date and has outperformed the market in all three phases of the 
market volatility that we have experienced so far this year. VSTS outperformed the market in the January/February period 
before the onset of the Iran War, sold off less than the market during the sharp move lower in March and has significantly 
outpaced the market during the surge higher in April. We believe there is considerable technical momentum in the name that 
can continue to augment the fundamental inflection in profitability that we expect to see over the next few quarters, which 
should support material upside in the stock over the next 6-9 months. 
We continue to believe Jim Barber’s relentless focus on operational excellence will drive improved operational KPIs, 
translating into a positive flywheel effect enabling customer retention, wallet share penetration and eventual pricing power. 
Ancora estimates a combination of higher-than-expected cost savings, targeted pricing actions and mix optimization will 
support a beat and raise cadence throughout FY 2026. Current guidance at the mid-point calls for $300M of annual EBITDA 
with sequential EBITDA improvement of ~5% each quarter. The first quarter’s reported EBITDA was $70M, which would imply 
$73.5M in the second quarter, $77M in the third quarter and $81M in the fourth quarter. We believe there is significant upside 
to these numbers based on greater sequential improvement cadence than consensus expectations and expect VSTS to report 
second quarter EBITDA in the $77M-$79M range, ~$85M in the third quarter and then ~$95M in the fourth quarter. Further, 
during the first quarter, VSTS’s largest competitor (Cintas) announced a transaction to acquire the third-largest player 
(UniFirst) in a $5.5B deal. We believe this is a significant positive development for the industry, as it will provide a pricing 
umbrella for the entire industry as Cintas looks to raise UniFirst prices to match its own. Given a roughly 12-month regulatory 
review followed by at least 12 months of business integration, we believe this gives Vestis a two-year-plus period to surgically 
take share from both Cintas and UniFirst, which should support a major inflection in top-line revenue growth.

LKQ Corporation (NASDAQ: LKQ) “Project Achilles” – Update 
There is no doubt our investment in LKQ has been frustrating. We described the situation in our fourth quarter letter with the 
following statement,  
“If the current management team is not capable of executing on these initiatives, then we estimate the Board will be 
left with one of two decisions in short order: either 1) replacing the management team, or 2) running a strategic review 
process that encompasses a full evaluation of each of the remaining business segments and/or the entire business.”  
Soon after that letter was distributed in mid-January, LKQ announced the Company was initiating a strategic review of 
alternatives to enhance shareholder value (LKQ Announces Strategic Review). We were pleased to see this announcement as 
it sets the stage for a very favorable event path over the next 6-12 months. As we alluded to above, we believe the 
management team is either going to execute its strategy, hit its numbers and return a tremendous amount of capital to 
shareholders, or the business is going to be sold or its pieces broken up.  
With the stock currently trading at meaningfully depressed levels (7.4x 2026 EBITDA, +10% 2026 free cash flow yield, and 
10.0x 2026 earnings per share), a good first quarter earnings release, in conjunction with the announcement of the sale of 
the Specialty business, could begin to rerate the stock. Diligence checks suggest the Specialty sale process is going well and is 
in the final inning, while market checks continue to reinforce the ongoing recovery in the repairable claims environment in 
North America. LKQ should be in a position to repurchase a significant amount of stock during the remainder of the year as 
asset sale proceeds and annual free cash flow generation point to being able to retire ~15-20% of the shares outstanding at 
current trading levels.  
We estimate that the combination of an active sale process, the ability to repurchase significant amounts of stock and the 
repairable claims environment improving supports a much higher stock price over the next 6-12 months. We estimate a sale 
of the entire business could yield a low/mid-$40 stock price, while staying public and selling the European business could 
yield a much higher stock price over time.  
RB Global, Inc. (NYSE: RBA) – Update 
RBA was not immune to the volatility of the market during the first quarter as the AI threat contagion spread from the 
SaaS/Software sector to other adjacent parts of the market. This created a meaningful selloff in RBA shares as the market 
took a shoot-first, ask questions later, approach to managing risk. However, the selloff created an opportunity to add 
incremental capital to our position in the company. As such, RBA remains one of our largest positions due to the quality of its 
business, along with the continued execution of the transformation strategy from the IAA acquisition three years ago.  
The management team has done a tremendous job integrating IAA and RB Global so far, but the elusive piece of the strategy 
has been market share gains on the salvage side of the house. We believe this is happening in real time, evidenced by 
winning an exclusive relationship with Progressive Insurance at the end of last year. This resulted in RBA taking the 
remaining 10-20% of Progressive’s salvage volumes from Copart over the last several months. This is a monumental 
achievement considering Progressive is the fastest growing auto insurer in the country, making this relationship all the more 
important. We estimate the Progressive win is the first market share domino to fall while also positioning RBA with multiple 
shots on goal over the next several years. The significance of incremental volume to RBA is massive, considering these units 
carry much higher incremental margins than the current salvage volumes.  
We estimate 2026 should be the first “clean” year of numbers since the acquisition of IAA in March 2023. This is anchored by 
the integration being largely complete, RBA winning market share on the salvage side of the house and the legacy RBA 
business (asset disposition) inflecting after two years of lackluster volumes. The output of these factors coming together 
should be a powerful EBITDA, earnings and free cash flow-compounding engine over the next several years. Additionally, we 
have been pushing the Company to augment its capital allocation strategy with a repurchase authorization for the last two 
years, so the Company could opportunistically repurchase stock. RBA announced a $500 million buyback authorization in

CONCLUSION  
Looking ahead to the remainder of 2026, we expect the dominant market drivers to be inflation-related volatility, interest 
rate expectations and geopolitical developments. Additionally, AI-disruption will likely remain a major market mover against 
the backdrop of what is expected to be a highly contentious November election cycle. The Administration will likely be 
aggressive in trying to win votes with consumer / “main street” driven initiatives going into the midterms.  
Overall, perhaps this first quarter has set a cautious tone for the year, but not an irreversible one. Even as we write this 
update, April 2026 has already seen a sharp market reversal with Technology snapping back dramatically and overall markets 
in positive territory for the year. This could all be fleeting if the Iran War were to reignite in the coming weeks/months. We 
are not macro specialists, and we recognize there are a lot of moving parts at play in an election year, with global instability 
perhaps representing the biggest risk to the global economy. As a result, we will prudently deploy hedges over the course of 
the year, particularly as we increase long exposure through new investments. 
We believe that we enter the second quarter with a legacy portfolio poised to experience several catalyst events in the 
coming months. Combined with new positions added over the year, we are cautiously optimistic that the portfolio is well-
positioned to outperform in 2026. 
As we have stated in previous letters, ACF is managed with a focus on risk-adjusted returns and alpha creation. Alpha is the 
excess return earned above a benchmark (the S&P 500 Index) for the risk employed. Our strategy aims to deliver strong, 
long-term risk-adjusted returns and protect our Limited Partners from suffering real capital loss, regardless of the market 
environment. Ancora’s hedged products are not “swing for the fences” short-term strategies. 
We feel our performance since inception in 2014 validates our thesis that an activist strategy with select and discrete hedging 
can deliver attractive returns while minimizing volatility and beta to the broader market. ACF’s portfolio managers greatly 
appreciate the opportunity to invest on your behalf. As always, feel free to contact us with any questions or concerns.
