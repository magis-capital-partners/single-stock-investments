In Q1 2026, Blue Eagle Capital Partners, LP (the "Fund") gained 2.08% net while maintaining an 
average net market exposure of approximately 27%. The S&P 500 fell -4.33%, the Nasdaq 
declined -6.96%, and the Russell 2000 gained 0.89%. 
 
For the past few years, AI has been the defining theme of the market. In this kind of environment, 
portfolio construction matters as much as  it ever has . What generates outsized gains one 
moment can just as easily turn against you without discipline around entry, exit, and sizing. 
Our approach in markets like these is to participate in the themes where fundamentals are 
genuinely being transformed, like AI, while staying disciplined about how we do it. Within AI, we 
consolidate around a handful of structural compounders that we believe will continue to 
deepen their earnings power regardless of where the cycle sits.  Alongside those core positions, 
we pick our spots in chokepoint trades, where the market is mispricing the magnitude of a near-
term inflection in free cash flow per share. We enter when the mispricing is wide and exit when 
it has closed. 
Much of our portfolio sits outside of AI, and we continue to hunt for setups where a meaningful 
inflection is underappreciated by the market. Our recent investment in Motorola Solutions 
(MSI), which we write about later in the letter, fits this bill. 
The same approach extends to the short book, which generated almost 8% alpha2 this quarter. 
AI has created an obvious set of winners, but it has also created a clear set of losers underneath 
– businesses whose competitive position, pricing power, and growth algorithms are under real 
pressure. Short alpha has compounded at approximately 6% annually over the life of the fund, 
and we continue to view it as one of the more durable edges in our process.

Portfolio Update 
As discussed above, our approach in a thematic market is built around three things: 
consolidating around structural compounders, playing chokepoints opportunistically, and 
hunting for setups outside of AI where a fundamental inflection is underappreciated. We want 
to walk through how each of these has shown up in the portfolio recently. 
On the compounder side, we have concentrated our exposure in several businesses whose 
earnings power we believe compounds well beyond the current cycle. Taiwan Semiconductor 
(TSMC) is the monopoly building block of the global AI supply chain – every chip, every model, 
every inference run goes through them. ASML Holding (ASML) sits one layer behind, making the 
machines that make those chips. Comfort Systems (FIX) is the company building the data 
center infrastructure, leveraging both an extensive skilled lab or force and a differentiated 
modular construction capability that compresses build times for hyperscalers. Siemens Energy 
(ENR GY) is one of three global suppliers of gas turbines at a moment when electricity demand 
is structurally accelerating , driven by both AI infrastructure and a global wave of grid 
modernization. These are the types of positions we  are happy to own provided the market 
continues to underappreciate the durability of their respective earnings power. 
Alongside compounders, we play chokepoints opportunistically, taking advantage of shorter -
cycle dislocations where the market is mispricing a specific bottleneck, with clear views on 
entry and exit. This quarter, we exited two chokepoint positions and initiated a new one. Vertiv 
(VRT), which provides critical power and cooling infrastructure for data centers, generated an 
approximately 22x return over a six -year holding period. Coherent (COHR), which makes

Our current expression of this bottleneck playbook is ProPetro (PUMP), which most investors 
still view as an oilfield services company. What is being missed is its rapidly scaling behind-the-
meter power business, which delivers modular natural gas generation to oilfield and hyperscale 
data center customers. The company built this business out ahead of the data center cycle and 
as a result has secured near-term capacity at a moment when turbine lead times are stretching 
beyond 2030. This week’s announcement of a strategic framework agreement with Caterpillar 
further extends its capacity advantage and puts the power business on a path to be two to three 
times the size of the legacy oilfield services business over the coming years. 
Motorola Solutions (MSI) 
Motorola Solutions is one of our newest positions , and is a clear example of the kind of 
underappreciated inflection we look for outside of AI . The company is best known for selling 
radios to police departments , a solid, predictable, premium -multiple franchis e that we are 
happy to own. That said, the radio business is not what got us interested. 
The real opportunity is what is hidden inside. Last year, Motorola acquired Silvus Technologies 
for $4.4 billion. Silvus builds the communications backbone for modern drone warfare – mesh 
networking systems that allow drone swarms to find, connect, and coordinate in active 
combat, with anti-jamming capabilities widely considered best-in-class. Its systems have been 
deployed extensively in Ukraine for several years, and that battlefield credibility has translated 
into a rapidly growing order book from the U. S. Department of War, the U.S. Army, Israel, and 
several NATO members. Demand is outpacing supply. 
The numbers are inflecting as we speak. Silvus was generating several hundred million dollars 
of revenue when it was acquired, and we believe it is on path to nearly $2 billion in revenue over 
the coming years as it lands large defense orders and capacity scales to meet demand. What 
makes this particularly powerful for Motorola is that Silvus’ margins are nearly double those of 
the core business, meaning every incremental dollar of Silvus revenue has an outsized impact 
on consolidated earnings. 
The result is a step change in the company’s financial profile. Motorola is moving from mid -
single-digit revenue growth to low -double-digit growth, and from high -single-digit EPS growth 
to over 20%, all while the stock still trades below 20x earnings. This is a pace of organic growth 
Motorola has not seen in two decades. 
Drone communications is one of the fastest-growing segments in defense today, and Motorola 
now owns one of the most valuable assets in the space. Almost none of this is yet reflected in 
consensus estimates. As it does, we believe the stock has significant room to re -rate from a 
multiple that still reflects the old, slower-growth Motorola.

Short Portfolio Update 
On the short side, software continues to be one of the highest alpha areas of the book. The 
market is in the early innings of a prolonged repricing of enterprise software, as AI reshapes the 
growth, pricing, and margin assumptions that underpinned the space for the past decade. We 
have written about this theme in prior letters, and it continues to be a rich hunting ground. But 
we want to be clear about how we are participating: we are not basket-shorting software. Every 
position is underwritten on its own f undamentals, with a specific view of where estimates are 
too high and where the multiple doesn’t reflect the risks. This quarter, a number of the short 
theses we had written about previously played out, and we monetized those shorts. We have 
since rotated capital into a set of software shorts earlier in their thesis, where fundamentals 
are softening and the stocks have yet to reflect it.
