Meat of the Matter – How and When Incumbents Win
30
1) Competitive Advantage: Incumbent must offer better value than disruptor
➢ Retail: Walmart, Costco, and Home Depot offered better all-in prices and quality than online; JC 
Penney, Macy’s, and Sears were already share-losers
➢ Software: Microsoft, Adobe, Intuit, Autodesk, and Constellation Software offered compatibility, user 
familiarity, and industry standard network effects that competitors could not match
2) Switching Costs: Incumbents are stronger where switching costs are generally high
➢ B2C: B2C businesses like newspapers, yellow pages, traditional media, radio, VCRs, DVDs, and 
retail all had relatively low switching costs, which increases risk of disruption
➢ B2B: B2B businesses like on-premise software and mainframes had much higher switching costs, 
which gave incumbents more time to adapt
3) Exploiting Disruption: Incumbent must strategically adopt disruptive tech
➢ Retail: Walmart, Costco, and Home Depot built e-commerce businesses
➢ Software: Microsoft, Adobe, Intuit, Autodesk, and Constellation moved to the cloud, SaaS, and 
mobile
