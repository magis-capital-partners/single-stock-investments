Unity Software (U) 
 
Following an absurd >50% drawdown in 1Q, we made Unity our largest holding .  Unity operates the 
largest gaming platform globally, powering 27% of all games and >50% share in mobile and VR games.  
Unity Create is the industry standard for building games that can run across all hardware sets and gaming 
systems.  It has the largest marketplace of pre-built scripts, 3D models, UI frameworks, and audio, creating 
network effec ts that help studios reach the broadest possible user base with ease.  Unity charges 
subscriptions to studios but derives 65% of revenue from its ad business (“Grow”), matching players with 
games where they’re likely to spend money.  After falling way behind AppLovin (APP) since 2022, Unity’s 
ad business was overhauled by new CEO Matt Bromberg and is finally firing on all cylinders.  
 
Historically, mobile advertising was deterministic because buyers could track and bid on gamers using 
identifiers (IDFAs).  In 2021, Apple changed the game by disabling tracking unless users opted -in, which 
hurt ROAS across the board and shifted the industry toward predictive math based on fragmented 
contextual signals.  AppLovin pivoted their ad engine quickly and delivered much better ROAS than Unity 
ads, leading to consecutive years of rapid growth with very high incremental margins.   
 
Unity has finally caught up with its Vector ad engine, now growing 15% qoq since launch (70% yoy) and 
just now achieved enough scale for them to shutter the legacy Iron Source platform.  In their 1Q26 results, 
Unity showed the remaining strategic lines of business (85% of prior rev) were up 34% yoy across Grow 
and Create, with Vector driving the acceleration at high incremental margins.  Even better, in 2Q26 Unity 
will begin feeding its runtime data into Vector, which is guaranteed to drive the flywheel of its AI predictive 
engine, delivering even better ROAS to studios.  1Q26 was the first time in years that Unity Grow outpaced 
AppLovin in gaming ads revenue growth and the trend seems very likely to continue. 
 
Unity’s engine is a cemented pillar of the global gaming industry, and new mgmt. has just repaired their 
ability to deliver ROAS on par with the competition.  We think Unity is set to deliver rapid organic growth 
and margin expansion thanks to Vector , and its Create business is following Figma’s footsteps of URL-
based design, greatly expanding its potential user base  (in contrast to seat compression fears  elsewhere).  
We can’t fully explain why the stock fell so far, but we’re pleased to buy it with a >7% FCF yield and likely 
over 20% revenue growth (with faster EBITDA growth).  The prospective Carry is 25-45% and the valuation 
should expand significantly toward 8-12x revenue if Vector can sustain its momentum.

Intapp (INTA) 
 
Intapp supplies purpose-built software for professional partnerships like legal, accounting, investment 
banking, and private capital firms.  It has gained share through a unified product suite for deal sourcing & 
pipeline mgmt. (DealCloud), conflict of interest checking , ethical wall enforcement, automated time 
capture & billing, and automated data orchestration. These solutions are daily revenue -generating and 
compliance tools, resulting in 95% gross retention and a large cross-sell opportunities (driving 124% cloud 
NRR).  Despite its strong KPIs, Intapp’s stock fell 44% in 1Q26 due to fears that its users would be replaced 
by Anthropic’s legal plugin and specialized AI like Harvey. There’s some truth in this: AI is improving the 
productivity of lawyers & analysts, slowing seat growth and stepping toward automation.  Except Intapp  
is a key enabler & beneficiary of this trend by providing governed & structured context (ontology). 
 
In February 2026, Intapp unveiled Celeste, its AI ontology & platform aiding orchestration of agentic work 
(task automation, actionable insights ) while strictly adhering to the complex security and regulatory 
demands.  Crucially, Intapp has partnered with Harvey and Microsoft  (Copilot) to embed "Intapp Walls 
for AI" into their agent platforms, separating duties to ensure governance in these high-stakes settings. As 
Harvey and Copilot make legal work more efficient, Celeste offers Firm-level AI with context that simplifies 
and helps automate administration, compliance, deal flow, time keeping and resource allocation. 
 
The long-term potential from Celeste is high: junior associate s cost these firms $150-200k per year  and 
spend 7 0% of their time on billable tasks (legal research, contract drafting) , with the remainder on 
administrative tasks like conflict checks, deal intake, timekeeping, etc.  If context-enabled AI can automate 
30% of this work by 2028, Intapp and Harvey create $45k of value.  If Intapp can charge a third of its value-
add, their revenue potential per professional jumps from $400 today toward $ 4,000-$6,000 in the form of 
work delivered (which aligns with the $12-15k per seat  cost of Harvey). 2  In our view, Intapp is in pole 
position to help unlock the labor budget in this lucrative niche, and their partners are helping to prove how 
transformative AI can be, both for professional partnerships and their modern suppliers.  
 
We think the collapse in Intapp’s stock completely misses the mark.  We’ve been aggressive buyers of INTA 
at a 9.5% FCF yield and we expect ~20% ARR growth  in our base case .  If Celeste ’s ontology  becomes 
standard across its customer base, the stock could set all-time highs by 2028 (+240% to 6-7x that revenue).

Netskope (NTSK) 
 
Netskope is a modern network security provider swept up in the SaaS selloff despite clear AI tailwinds. 
The company provides zero trust network access (ZTNA) and inline data loss prevention (DLP) that secure 
employee access to specific applications based on identity  and context.  Experts think this SASE market 
will grow 23% annually to 2030 as legacy VPNs and firewalls  are displaced, and Netskope ’s recent 31% 
ARR growth shows it is  gaining share with data-sensitive customers (ensuring sensitive data cannot be 
exfiltrated via personal accounts or into LLMs ).  While we see a next-generation architecture in a fast -
growing segment, the stock has become a broken IPO where technical selling pressure has exacerbated the 
stock’s drawdown (insider lockups expire six months after the listing).  
 
We think the market is worried that Netskope prices its suite per employee, suggesting they will struggle 
to capture new TAM in the agentic era.  But we expect sensitive network traffic to increase dramatically in 
the coming years, continuing to broaden the range of enterprises that must secure against leaks in real -
time.  First, human -agent interactions are likely to increase significantly as enterprise agents gain greater 
context, lower latency and more authority, suggesting durable growth for Netskope’s DLP (securing chat 
prompts).  Second, the shift toward working agents implies a big increase in n orth-south API traffic that 
will need to be governed due to sensitivity  & regulations.  If the pandemic drove the first wave of SASE 
adoption as workers moved out of the office, the advent of agent automations should multiply the volume, 
speed and risk of network traffic, increasing the burden on security infrastructure.  
 
Netskope is ideally positioned to capture this growth, and they just launched a suite of products for this 
nascent TAM: One Agentic Broker, AI Guardrails, AI Gateway, and AI Red Teaming.  These features have 
transaction-based pricing, laying the plumbing to meter the flow of secure data required by autonomous 
agents. Their modern network security infrastructure remains critical for enterprises and is not at risk of 
being left behind (or of being unable to charge for the traffic it secures). 
 
We expect Netskope will  continue gaining share in its rapidly expanding market.  The company is 
efficiently acquiring new customers and showing high operating leverage ( 40% incremental EBITDA 
margins) as it scales the traffic flowing through its private NewEdge global network.  While we’d like to 
see a healthier balance sheet, NTSK offers 2 4-32% Carry via organic growth and we think AI product 
traction plus a beat & raise cadence will restore confidence, lifting its valuation multiple back toward 6-10x 
revenue.  Like many software companies, Netskope’s share of revenue from AI SKUs is very small today 
but likely to progress materially over the next three years.

MongoDB (MDB) 
 
We wrote up MongoDB a year ago and it became our largest winner in 2025.  Despite excellent KPIs and 
clear AI tailwinds, MDB’s stock is down 45% year-to-date because it sells software. As a reminder, Mongo 
supplies a leading operational database for unstructured data, which directly benefits from the increased 
innovation, product velocity, and data -centricity of the AI era.  When we see headlines like Anthropic’s 
recent crossing of $30bn in ARR (up 230% in three months) due to the success of Claude Code, and GitHub’s 
disclosure that code commits are increasing over 14x yoy, we can be confident that many new features and 
applications (like ElevenLabs) are being built on Mongo’s AI -friendly platform, with native vector search 
and tools like Voyage AI that help improve the quality and trustworthiness of agents.  
 
MongoDB’s flagship Atlas grew 29% in 4Q25, lapping 27% growth from the year earlier  and accelerating 
in both customers acquired and avg. spend per customer.  We think the tidal wave of investment in AI 
datacenters is already showing up in the results of software infrastructure like MDB, DDOG, FROG, SNOW 
and others, providing a large source of new demand for the next decade .  From today’s price , we think 
Mongo offers a 3.5% FCF yield and growth near or exceeding 20%, plus healthy Kicker potential toward 
the 10x revenue multiple it often garners following beat & raise results.
