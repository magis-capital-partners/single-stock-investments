Our	net	exposure	averaged	59%.		Despite	companies	in	our	portfolio	guiding	to	around	seven	potentially	major	new	data	releases	in	the	first	quarter	of	2026,	none	were	in	January.		Without	data	in	large	positions	to	prove	our	theses	correct	or	incorrect,	we	view	our	performance	as	reflecting	general	biotech	market	volatility.		This	is	a	key	point	about	how	the	biotech	markets	work:	periods	of	outperformance	can	be	punctuated	with	periods	of	market	inertia	and	occasional	drawdowns.		We	remain	focused	on	the	fundamentals	by	identifying	scientific	and	medical	advances	that	will	drive	returns	for	our	investors	when	they	occur.			Relatedly,	for	our	first	2026	update	we	would	like	to	discuss	a	fundamental	scientific	thesis,	our	approach	to	obesity	treatments	and	how	we	think	about	our	investments	in	that	sector.		The	GLP-1s	are	revolutionary	yet	imperfect	treatments	with	limitations	including	tolerability	(e.g.,	gastrointestinal	symptoms),	suboptimal	administration	(historically,	once	weekly	injections)	and	critically,	unwanted	muscle	loss.		With	our	launch	in	August	2025,	we	viewed	the	GLP-1	market	in	and	of	itself	as	largely	played	out.		Small	increases	in	dosing	convenience	(e.g.,	monthly	rather	than	weekly)	seemed	overvalued,	particularly	with	established	players	bringing	oral	versions	of	their	blockbusters	to	market.		Rather	than	focusing	on	building	a	better	GLP-1	mousetrap,	we	decided	to	look	for	new,	fundamental	biological	innovations.	GLP-1-like	drugs	work	by	mimicking	hormones	that	signal	satiety,	thereby	reducing	caloric	consumption.		However,	reducing	energy	intake	is	only	half	of	the	metabolic	equation;	the	other	half	is	energy	utilization.		So	we	asked	ourselves	the	question,	which	naturally	occurring	genetic	variations	improve	health	independent	of	caloric	restriction?
