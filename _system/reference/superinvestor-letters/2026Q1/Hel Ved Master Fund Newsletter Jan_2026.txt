Market and Portfolio Review 
 
January opened the year in a very risk -on tone and  
represented a very volatile first month of the year, though a 
very productive one for our Fund. Broadening was the theme 
for January, with small caps, cyclicals including commodities, 
and Emerging Markets performing well.  
 
Macro wise, dollar debasement trades were front and center, 
with the USD weakening and hard assets like Gold, Silver, Oil 
in a strong rally. Overall, for US macro, inflation remains  
benign, whilst growth and forward -looking indicators look firm. 
There is a continued belief that the Trump Administration wants 
to run the economy 'hot' as we approach the Midterms. 
 
China was also very firm with small caps breaking out as well 
early in the year. Themes like Optical, Semis, AI Software and 
our favorite industrial export ideas all performed well. However, 
towards the month -end, we started to see National -team-
related entities selling into strength as they looked to cool the 
market and maintain the 'slow bull' trend. The IPO market was 
also active and robust, with many A -share companies seeking 
to raise offshore capital in Hong Kong. Although activity usually 
tapers off as the Lunar New Year approaches, the general 
trend has stayed quite strong.  
 
Japan's LDP, led by Prime Minister Takaichi, unexpectedly 
won a two -thirds majority in the Lower House elections. While 
a coalition victory was anticipated, the LDP's supermajority 
surprised markets, resulting in outperformance of stocks tied to 
Takaichi's policies (such as defense and semiconductors) and 
easing some pressure on JPY depreciation.  
 
In Korea, the large -cap KOSPI index has seen strong  
performance, particularly due to the memory trade. The  
government is now taking steps to revive the SMID cap index 
through its "KOSDAQ 3000" initiative. This plan aims to  
value-up KOSDAQ -listed stocks by removing 'zombie' firms 
and encouraging local pension funds to diversify their equity 
allocations into more domestic small-cap companies.  
 
Globally, beta and momentum were very strong with many 
things that worked last year, continuing to work and do well in 
January.  
 
Sector Review  
 
AI Hardware 
 
AI-related hardware names continued to perform very well. Our 
memory-related ideas continued to benefit from the tight supply
-demand landscape leading to sharply higher prices and  
enabling suppliers to have outsized bargaining power. This has 
benefited our positions in Kioxia, Micron, Sandisk and  
 
 
Samsung. Beyond Memory, there are also other semi -related 
names including Networking/Optical and Semicap equipment 
that performs well. Goldman Sachs noted that the consensus 
for 2026 AI CAPEX has been marked up to ~$600B+, roughly 
50% up year on year based on recent hyperscaler earnings 
announcements.  
 
Defense Electronics 
 
Another theme that was particularly topical for January was 
Defense. Following the developments of Venezuela, as well as 
potential geopolitical uncertainty in Greenland and Iran,  
Defense stocks have outperformed this year, benefitting our 
Defense electronics names including Leonardo, Indra Sistemas 
and Korea Aerospace. 
 
China Industrial Tech Export 
 
This continues to be our favorite China theme, where  
manufacturing technology leadership combined with most  
comprehensive and cost competitive supply chain in those tight 
supply/demand industrial markets is driving increasing market 
share for many Chinese industrial leaders. This trend  
represents significant EPS tailwind and warrants further  
re-rating. This has been a strong contributing theme in 2026.  
 
Outlook 
 
Overall, market momentum was very firm throughout January. 
However, there was a bit of volatility towards the month -end  
following the announcement of Kevin Warsh as Fed Chair. This 
came as a surprise to markets as Warsh had more on the 
hawkish stance though his appointment could be seen as  
favorable in terms of maintaining Fed independence. In  
addition, the crowdedness in positioning and the speed at 
which some of the AI winners moved also contributed to a  
minor drawdown towards the month -end. At present, market 
trend continues to look intact, but we remain cautious of any 
potential momentum and beta drawdowns. 
 
In China, we are most interested in Industrial Tech Export.
