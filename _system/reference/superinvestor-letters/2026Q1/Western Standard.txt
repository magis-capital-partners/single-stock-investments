  Investment Highlights  
Innospec Inc. 
Innospec Inc. (“IOSP”) is a leading specialty chemicals manufacturer with a focus on surfactants, operating 
through three primary segments: Fuel Specialties, Performance Chemicals, and Oilfield Services.  IOSP 
differentiates itself through its emphasis on specialty applications where performance, product design, and 
quality are critical.  Surfactants (surface-active agents) are chemicals that reduce surface tension and influence 
how substances interact—whether between liquids or between a liquid and a solid.  They are essential to product 
performance across IOSP’s end markets, determining how shampoo interacts with hair, lotion with skin, or fuel 
with engine components.  Although surfactants represent a small portion of a product’s volume, they are often a 
key driver of performance.  Because they are typically embedded in branded formulations, changes can materially 
affect product characteristics, resulting in long-standing customer relationships. 
 
The Fuel Specialties segment (57% of LTM EBITDA) develops, blends, and supplies additives used in diesel, jet, 
marine, and other fuels.  IOSP maintains a strong position in heavy-duty diesel additives, with limited exposure to 
gasoline.  Its products enhance engine cleanliness and efficiency while enabling cleaner combustion and longer 
 
3 
 
engine life.  This segment generates modest top-line growth (low single digits) but benefits from high margins 
(19–21% EBIT margins) and a capital-light model, resulting in strong and consistent free cash flow generation. 
 
The Performance Chemicals segment (32% of LTM EBITDA) provides specialty chemical solutions across personal 
care, home care, construction, mining, and other industrial markets. Personal care accounts for over 70% of 
segment earnings, where IOSP is recognized for its leadership in high-performance, sulfate- and 1,4-dioxane-free 
surfactants that serve as the “chassis” for many clean and natural beauty products.  Historically, this segment has 
delivered low-teens margins and mid-single-digit revenue growth.  However, in 2025, margins fell below 10% and 
earnings declined by approximately 30%, contributing to a 36% decline in IOSP’s share price. 
 
The earnings pressure was primarily driven by a sharp increase in key raw material costs, notably coconut and 
palm oil.  While IOSP’s contracts allow for cost pass-through, there is typically a 2–3 quarter lag before full 
recovery.  This lag was extended by a weaker consumer spending environment.  We expect earnings to recover 
over the coming quarters as raw material costs have stabilized and demand for IOSP’s personal care products 
remains strong. 
 
The Oilfield Services segment (11% of LTM EBITDA) provides chemical solutions for drilling, completion, 
production, and drag reduction in oil and gas applications.  This business has faced cyclical headwinds due to 
reduced customer spending in the U.S. and Latin America.  However, we believe results have stabilized near 
trough levels, with potential upside driven by renewed demand from a large Mexican customer that had 
previously paused purchases while dealing with internal issues. 
 
The partnership initiated a position in IOSP in September 2025 and has since increased its ownership.  Shares have 
been under pressure this year as the main Performance Chemicals plant in North Carolina delayed deliveries 
during an adverse weather event and investor concerns about specialty chemical supplies due to the ongoing 
US/Iran conflict.  We believe the decline in Performance Chemicals earnings is temporary and expect greater than 
30% earnings growth as cost pass-throughs are realized and IOSP’s plant gets back to full production.  
Additionally, management has used the downtime to implement operational efficiency initiatives that should 
support further margin expansion.   
 
IOSP maintains a strong balance sheet, with $11.70/share in cash and no debt, and consistently generates free 
cash flow.  The company has a track record of returning capital to shareholders through regular dividend increases 
and opportunistic share repurchases.  At a quarter-end price of $73.02/share, and adjusting for cash, IOSP trades 
at 10.5x our estimate of normalized earnings.  Assuming the shares return to the historic average multiple of 
16.3x, upside is 45%, inclusive of the cash on the balance sheet.  
 
Compass Diversified Holdings 
Compass Diversified Holdings (“CODI”) is a publicly traded holding company that owns a diversified portfolio of 
niche consumer and industrial businesses.  The company acquires controlling stakes in high-quality businesses, 
provides growth capital and operational support, and compounds value through active ownership, disciplined 
capital allocation, and selective monetization.  Unlike traditional private equity funds, CODI is a permanent capital 
vehicle with no fixed life, allowing it to hold businesses indefinitely, recycle capital, and opportunistically buy & 
sell assets.  The portfolio is externally managed by a team at Compass Group Management (CGM), led by CEO 
Elias Sabo, and is paid a 2% annual management fee on adjusted net assets plus a 20% incentive fee on 
distributable cash above a hurdle.  Today, CODI owns eight operating companies: 5.11 Tactical, BOA Technology, 
PrimaLoft, Velocity Outdoor, The Honey Pot, Altor Solutions, Arnold Magnetic Technologies, and Sterno Group. 
 
 
4 
 
In May 2025, CODI experienced a major setback when Lugano Diamonds, its largest and fastest-growing 
subsidiary, was found to have engaged in founder-led fraud. CODI acquired a 60% stake in Lugano in 2021 for 
$265 million and extended over $600 million in intercompany loans, resulting in balance sheet exposure that 
significantly exceeded its initial investment.  This fraud led to financial restatements, liquidity pressure, lender 
forbearance agreements, dividend suspension, and the loss of investor confidence.  Shares declined more than 
70%, driven by both the financial impact and the negative sentiment created by weak internal controls. 
 
Importantly, the issues were isolated to Lugano as the remaining portfolio continues to perform, generating high-
single-digit EBITDA growth and strong free cash flow.  We believe this event will force a strategic reset focused on 
deleveraging and portfolio simplification.  CODI has already begun to monetize assets, including the recent $292 
million partial sale of Sterno, reducing leverage to approximately 5x EBITDA, with additional divestments expected 
over the next 6 months.  The Honey Pot, a fast-growing feminine care brand, was acquired by CODI in 2024 for 
$380 million and is a likely sale candidate.  We believe it could fetch roughly double that today due to its rapid 
growth, and its monetization could reduce leverage into the mid-3s, opening the door for share repurchases at 
prices below net asset value. 
 
At the same time, we believe the situation has intensified board and shareholder scrutiny of CGM’s fee structure, 
which previously exceeded $70 million annually.  We expect a significant reduction in fees in the near term, with 
each $10 million decrease accreting over $1.00/share of equity value.  Activist investor ADW Capital recently filed 
a 13D disclosing a 14% ownership stake and is pushing for asset sales, cost reductions, and changes to CGM’s 
incentives, further increasing the likelihood of strategic and governance reform. 
 
At current levels, CODI represents a dislocated sum-of-the-parts opportunity, where a single subsidiary failure has 
driven a broad mispricing of otherwise healthy underlying businesses.  With ongoing asset sales, activist 
involvement, and increasing pressure on governance and capital allocation, we see a clear path toward valuation 
normalization and improved shareholder returns.  We estimate NAV of $17.86/share, 127% above Q1’s closing 
price, driven by asset monetization, normalized portfolio performance, and anticipated reductions in CGM fees. 
 
Green Dot Corporation 
The partnership has followed Green Dot Corporation (“GDOT”) since a short sale position in 2010 following the 
company’s IPO.  At that time, GDOT was a provider of reloadable, prepaid debit cards.  Through the acquisition of 
a bank and several other businesses, it transformed itself into a diversified fintech platform.  Today it offers a 
wide array of services including prepaid cards, a digital bank to low-income consumers, banking-as-a-service, tax 
refund processing, earned age access, and money movement services.  After years of poor execution, the Board 
initiated a strategic review of the company, which culminated in an announced sale in November 2025.   
 
The sale will split GDOT into two businesses: the fintech business and Green Dot Bank.  Smith Ventures will 
acquire the fintech business and CommerceOne Financial Corporation (“COB”) will acquire Green Dot Bank.  Smith 
Ventures is the private investment vehicle for Bill Smith, a serial entrepreneur who built several technology-
focused businesses.  CommerceOne is a one-branch bank in Birmingham, Alabama founded by former executives 
at First Partners Bank, which was sold to Progress Bank in 2017.  Interestingly, Bill Smith sits on the Board of 
CommerceOne and is one of its original backers. 
 
Once the transaction closes, the fintech and COB will enter a seven-year agreement for COB to act as the exclusive 
bank sponsor for the fintech business.  We expect the deal to close in June 2026 at which time the partnership will 
receive $8.11/sh in cash and 0.2215 shares of COB for each GDOT share. 
 
 
5 
 
At GDOT’s Q1 closing price of $11.22/share, the implied price-to-book ratio for COB is 51%, a price the partnership 
finds extremely attractive.  CommerceOne has historically been well run with an efficiency ratio of 40%, 
generating ROEs of 15% with minimal credit losses.  While those metrics will be diluted once the transaction 
closes as 85% of pro forma assets will be cash and securities, we believe that over time the company will be able 
to reposition its balance sheet into higher yielding securities, ramp its loan book by hiring origination teams, and 
scale the business to again generate mid-teens ROEs.   
 
The partnership believes COB should, at a minimum, trade at 1x book value, or +97% from the current implied 
value of the stub.  As comparable banks trade at 2x (or more) of book value, we believe COB’s depressed 
valuation makes it an acquisition target.   
 
Baltic Classified Group 
The partnership initially wrote up Baltic Classifieds Group ("BCG") in its Q3 2024 letter.  BCG operates fourteen 
online classified portals across Lithuania, Estonia, and Latvia, holding dominant positions in autos, real estate, 
jobs, and generalist categories with market share roughly 10x its nearest competitor.  Given its dominant market 
position and that it had yet to fully monetize its services relative to European peers, we believed that BCG would 
compound earnings at a high-teens rate for many years as it closed the monetization gap to European peers such 
as Rightmove, Auto Trader, FINN, and Trade Me. 
 
Since that write-up, the shares declined 52% from their July 2025 peak to Q1’s closing price of GBP 1.83/share, 
near the 2021 IPO price despite EBITDA having more than doubled since listing.  The stock now trades at 16x 
forward earnings, a steep discount to the post-IPO average of 28x.  The decline in the shares can be attributed to 
two issues, one that we believe is temporary and the second we believe is overstated.   
 
First, Estonia introduced a new vehicle tax effective January 1, 2025 that raised the cost of owning a car by 
approximately 10%.  This pulled forward purchases into late 2024 and left Estonian auto transactions down 
roughly 50% year-over-year through most of 2025, dragging consolidated revenue growth from 15% in FY 2024 to 
8% in FY 2025.  Initially, the company believed the Estonian auto market would recover after a few months.  
Unfortunately, the negative market impact of the new tax caused the government to question if it should be 
removed, further delaying the auto recovery due to increased uncertainty.  This caused the company to cut its 
revenue guidance on the Estonian auto segment.  Furthermore, tough year-over-year comparisons in November 
and December 2025 due to the pull forward of demand in late 2024, delayed the return to double digit topline 
growth and disappointed investors. 
 
Second, European classified stocks sold off on AI-related concerns after Rightmove, a UK classifieds business cut 
their medium-term margin targets.  The concern is that AI-powered search and agentic tools will disintermediate 
classified portals, with buyers shifting discovery to LLMs, such as ChatGPT, and bypassing classifieds entirely.  We 
believe the threat is overstated.  90% of total traffic to BCG’s portals is direct and organic (i.e., unpaid search) as 
people in the Baltics visit a BCG site 9 times per month, on average.  This is because BCG operates two-sided 
networks where traffic comes to their sites because they have the most relevant inventory, and sellers list on their 
sites because that is where the traffic is.  That is hard to disrupt, as shown by many new entrants trying & failing 
over the past twenty years.  Additionally, investors are concerned that AI agents will handle purchases 
automatically, eliminating the need for classified marketplaces.  It’s hard for us to envision a future where two of 
the largest purchases by a consumer in their lives (i.e., home and car) will be made with little or no input from the 
buyer.  Perhaps consumers use an agent to curate options for themselves.  To be effective, these agents will need 
access to clean data, the data that BCG has standardized, de-duplicated, moderated, vetted, and enriched.  BCG 
has no plans and no need to share their data with LLMs or agents given customers interact with their websites 
directly.   
 
6 
 
 
Another concern is that defending against the threat of AI requires a step-up in investment that will compress 
margins.  On BCG’s December investor call, they framed the magnitude of investment needed, which is projected 
to cause EBITDA margins to compress from 77.8% in FY 2025 to 75% as the company increases investment 
(primarily people).  While seeing margins compress is not great, this is a very manageable amount, and we feel 
management may be conservative given their margin guidance assumes subdued revenue growth.  As the 
company moves past the Estonian auto tax issue, we believe margins may normalize at a higher rate than 
investors are expecting.  In addition, the investments the company is making into AI tools such as semantic 
search, AI-assisted listing creation, call tracking, automated property valuation, fraud screening, etc., improve the 
customer experience and allow the continued increase in monetization of their platform.   
 
At the current levels, the partnership believes the shares are undervalued, and management and the board agree. 
Since shares moved lower in November 2025, BCG has repurchased 28 million shares for cancellation, returning 
EUR 52 million to shareholders through buybacks.  Management has indicated the board is considering taking on 
debt to accelerate repurchases, noting that BCG's own stock now looks more attractive than any acquisition 
target.  In addition, two Board members, the ex-CEO of Autotrader and the founder of Rightmove, personally 
purchased GBP 1.3 million worth of stock in the last 5 months.  As the partnership expects FCF to grow at a mid-
teens rate for many years, our price target of 2.78/share, 52% above Q1’s close, offers an attractive return given 
the company’s unrivaled competitive position, 75% EBITDA margins, and capital-light business model.
