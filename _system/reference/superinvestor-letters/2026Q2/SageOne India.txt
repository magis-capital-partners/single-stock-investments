Investment Strategy 
SageOne India Growth Master Fund (“SOIGMF”) is an India 
dedicated long -only equities fund domiciled in the 
Cayman Islands. The investment process in stock 
selection involves an in -depth, fundamental bottom -up 
research into companies and emerging trends to identify 
and invest in companies with long-term structural growth, 
high profitability and quality management. The team 
implements a buy -and-hold discipline based on a multi -
year view hence low turnover and no leverage. The 
portfolio would typically consist  of 12-20 high conviction 
stocks across multiple sectors with a focus on the mid-cap 
space, majority of the portfolio comprising of stocks in the 
$1bn-$8bn cap range. The portfolio is constructed with the 
objective of achieving strong risk -adjusted returns with 
minimal downside through low/no exposure to cyclicals, 
policy risks, macro risks, irrational competition risks, 
corporate governance risks & unproven business models.

Performance Commentary 
 
 
Indian equities endured their most challenging month since March 2020, dominated by the escalation 
of the US-Iran conflict and its cascading effects on global energy markets. Joint US -Israeli airstrikes 
on February 28 led to the effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz, through which roughly one-fifth of 
global oil supply transits, sending Brent crude surging from around $60 per barrel in mid -February to 
above $100, and briefly approaching $120 in early March. The Nifty 50 fell 11.3% during the month, the 
BSE 500 declined 11.4% with virtually no sector spared. The rupee came under severe pressure, falling 
to a record low of Rs 93.74 by March 20 as surging oil import costs and foreign capital flight weighed 
on the currency. FIIs recorded their highest-ever monthly equity outflow of approximately $12.3 billion, 
with financial services bearing the brunt at over $6.3 billion in sectoral outflows alone. The cumulative 
FII outflow for calendar year 2026 reached approximately $15.9 billion, nearly matching the total 
withdrawal for the entire year of 2025. 
Domestic Institutional Investors, however, emerged as the market’s anchor, making net purchases of 
approximately $14.9 billion during the month and absorbing the bulk of foreign selling. Equity mutual 
fund inflows surged 38% to $4.3 billion, the highest since July 2025, as domestic investors treated the 
correction as a buying opportunity rather than a reason to exit. Monthly SIP flows remained robust, and 
the structural shift in India’s market ownership, with DII holdings now exceeding FII holdings, meant 
that the correction was contained relative to what would have occurred in a prior era of foreign -flow 
dependence. 
 
The MSCI India Midcap USD Index and the MSCI India SmallCap USD Index delivered returns of -14.31% 
and -13.78%, respectively, while the Fund returned -12.40% for the month. 
We have carefully assessed the direct and indirect impact of the ongoing conflict on our portfolio 
companies. The primary transmission channel is through oil prices and, consequently, input costs, 
particularly for companies in the industrial and manufactur ing spaces. However, any damage to oil 
infrastructure and the resulting price spikes are likely to be transient in nature , global production 
capacity outside the affected region, including from the US, Russia, and other Middle Eastern 
producers, is sufficient to compensate for short -term supply shortfalls once the situation stabilises. 
None of our portfolio companies have direct exposure to the conflict zone. Where there are indirect 
effects through higher energy or raw material costs, we believe these are manageable and do not 
impair the long-term earnings trajectory of our holdings. 
We continued to actively rebalance the portfolio during the month. We exited positions in a housing 
finance company and a consumer -facing business where, in our assessment, the risk -reward had 
become less compelling relative to opportunities elsewhere in our investable universe. We initiated  two 
new positions in the  solar energy sector . Our conviction in this space has strengthened further , the 
government has mandated that all equipment used in solar power projects must be manufactured 
domestically by FY2029, creating a significant multi-year tailwind for domestic solar module and cell 
manufacturers. We also increased our allocation to a leading player in the spirits and beverages space, 
where valuations had corrected to levels we found attractive. The company’s operational trajectory 
remains intact, and we see the recent pullback as a buying opportunity.

The March quarter earnings season is still in its early stages, with results delayed by approximately 
two weeks owing to the financial year -end reporting cycle. Early reports from the IT services sector 
have come in broadly in line with expectations. However, select companies in the industrial space have 
delivered phenomenal earnings growth, providing early signals that the underlying earnings trajectory 
for quality mid and small-cap businesses may be stronger than what the market is currently pricing in. 
We expect the broader earnings picture to become clearer over the coming weeks and will provide a 
more detailed update in our next commentary. 
The worst of the market correction appears to be behind us. The bulk of the sell -off occurred in 
February and March, and the conflict , while deeply concerning , has not fundamentally altered the 
structural growth story for India. Markets have already begun to show signs of recovery in early April, 
with the Nifty rebounding approximately 10%, the BSE 500 up 12%, mid -caps up 14%, and small-caps 
up 16% from their Mar ch lows. India’s currency depreciation of approximately 20% over the past 
several months has made Indian assets meaningfully cheaper in dollar terms, which is likely to prove a 
powerful draw for foreign capital once geopolitical uncertainty subsides. However, elevated crude 
prices, a hawkish US Federal Reserve signalling “higher for longer” interest rates to combat war -
induced inflation, and continued FII risk aversion towards emerging markets may temper the pace of 
recovery. With domestic capex continuing to rebound, private investment picking up, and corporate 
earnings showing early signs of recovery, the underlying growth story remains firmly intact for patient 
investors willing to look through near-term geopolitical volatility. 
We thank our investors for their continued patience and confidence during this period of heightened 
volatility. We remain firmly committed to our investment process and are optimistic about the 
opportunities that lie ahead.
