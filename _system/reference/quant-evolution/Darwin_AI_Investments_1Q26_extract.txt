Why DES Thrives in noisy markets? 
Continuous Adaptation
• Learning Over Time: Repeated iterations allow DES to adapt to new data and changing 
environments
• Avoiding Overfitting: Since DES is always testing strategies against actual performance, it's less 
likely to overfit to random noise
Natural Selection Mechanism
• Survival of the Fittest: Only the strategies that perform well survive to the next generation
• Less Reliance on Exact Patterns: DES doesn't need precise patterns to function, it focuses on 
overall performance
Resilience Through Diversity
• Multiple Solutions: By evaluating many strategies at once, DES doesn't rely on a single strategy 
that might be affected by noise
• Adaptability: If some strategies perform poorly due to random noise, others may still perform well
