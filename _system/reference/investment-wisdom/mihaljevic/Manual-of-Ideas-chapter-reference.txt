CHAPTER 1 — A Highly Personal Endeavor
--------------------------------------
- Cast yourself as capital allocator / owner, not trader or copycat.
- Stock selection framework: flexible yet concrete; all companies → equity value vs market value.
- Asset bounds: replacement value (upper), liquidation value (lower).
- Earnings power: normalized earnings yield on EV; return on capital employed + reinvestment runway.
- Key: losses compound against long-term appreciation; size limits on mammals and companies (Pabrai).

CHAPTER 2 — Deep Value (Graham / net-net)
-----------------------------------------
- Start with price; tangible bargain metrics first.
- Holy grail rare: asset protection + high ROC unless near-term profit collapse.
- Misuses: overestimated liquidation in distress; time erodes asset-rich low-ROC; creative destruction;
  concentration risk in cigar butts.
- Augment screens: buybacks, insider buying, working capital shrinkage.
- Balance sheet-only value can erode over time.

CHAPTER 3 — Sum-of-the-Parts / Hidden Assets
--------------------------------------------
- Value distinct businesses/assets separately; market misprices non-core assets.
- Strategic flexibility: divest, repurchase, reinvest in high-return segment.
- Misuses: over-slicing; value trap without catalyst; crowding eliminates discount;
  "buy-one-get-one-free" vs "buy-ten-get-one-free" discount magnitude.
- Question: how will hidden assets cease to be hidden?

CHAPTER 4 — Greenblatt Magic Formula (Good + Cheap)
---------------------------------------------------
- Quality: operating income / capital employed (net working capital + net fixed assets).
- Cheap: operating income / enterprise value (leverage-neutral).
- Misuses: transitory high ROC (fads); no reinvestment runway; subjective filters that drop
  highest-prospect names (stomach-churn candidates); cyclicality; M&A rollups; insider selling.
- Tweaks that help: forward earnings; international universe; ROC hurdle before ranking cheapness.
- High ROC on existing capital meaningless without reinvestment at similar returns.

CHAPTER 5 — Jockey Stocks (Great Managers)
------------------------------------------
- Buffett: great business first; great manager second.
- Find underappreciated CEOs: humble, conservative, non-promotional.
- Invert to eliminate bad actors; compensation acid test.
- Proxies: stock ownership, insider buying; ROCE, capital employed growth, margins, capex trends.
- Blank-check question: what would management do with $1B / $10M / $1M? Judge actions not words.
- Separate business performance from stock price.

CHAPTER 6 — Follow the Leaders (Superinvestors)
-----------------------------------------------
- Do your own work; copying can work with context.
- Misuses: hero worship; macro/theme basket ≠ stock endorsement; stale 13F; turnover lag.
- Conviction signals: position size, new/increased, >5% / >10% of issuer, letter commentary,
  style congruence with your approach.
- Track investors matching your philosophy; prefer their letters for process learning.

CHAPTER 7 — Small / Micro Cap
-----------------------------
- Investability screen before analysis (size, revenue, liquidity, insider ownership).
- Misuses: illiquidity; screen passed for wrong reason; non-recurring items buried in reports.
- Hidden inflection: legacy decline + profitable growth segment in same company.
- Qualitative work beyond screens — few professionals work A→Z on small caps.

CHAPTER 8 — Special Situations / Event-Driven
---------------------------------------------
- Return often independent of market near term; situations end — capital recycles.
- Inefficiency sources: index deletion, dividend cancellation, tax selling, spinoff, rights offering,
  growth disappointment, distressed seller, fear/greed, complexity.
- Three questions: source of inefficiency; margin of safety; path to value creation.
- Time component: annualized return matters as much as absolute return.
- Without identifiable inefficiency, higher chance valuation has a flaw.
- Investment diary: log passes and decisions for deliberate practice.

CHAPTER 9 — Equity Stubs (Leveraged Equity)
-------------------------------------------
- High judgment; wide dispersion; win rate may be <50% with lopsided payoffs.
- Prefer industry-wide selloffs over idiosyncratic failure.
- Non-recourse debt sometimes ignored by market.
- Who owns the debt; management vested in common; range of outcomes not point estimate.
- Misuses: overreach after success; overconfidence.

CHAPTER 10 — International Value
--------------------------------
- How global is the business? (especially Europe)
- Exclude worst jurisdictions on downside; ride regional experts.
- FT and local screens; currency/governance risks.
