## What this business is

West Pharmaceutical Services is a global supplier of injectable drug containment and delivery components. **[Fact]** FY2025 net sales were **$3.07 billion** (+6.2% versus $2.89 billion in 2024). **[Fact]** The company reports two segments: **Proprietary Products** (**$2.49 billion**, 81% of sales) and **Contract-Manufactured Products** (**$582 million**). **[Fact]** Proprietary Products sells elastomer stoppers, seals, vial and cartridge components, self-injection devices, and analytical lab services to biologic, generic, and pharmaceutical customers. **[Fact]** Contract-Manufactured Products assembles customer-owned components into finished injectable and diagnostic devices. **[Fact]** Cash from operations was **$754.8 million** and capital spending **$285.9 million**, yielding about **$6.45** of free cash flow per diluted share on **72.7 million** shares. **[Inference]** WST is a high-quality packaging compounder whose economics depend on validated component demand from injectable therapies, especially biologics and GLP-1 obesity drugs, with meaningful cyclicality from customer inventory management.

## Risks & inversion

**Primary risk:** Oral GLP-1 adoption and customer inventory destocking reduce injectable component orders faster than capacity additions convert to cash.

- **Inversion (Munger):** What kills the thesis? Major pharma customers shift obesity share to oral pills; Proprietary Products organic growth falls below 3% for four quarters; elastomer impairments repeat.
- **CEO transition:** Eric M. Green retiring; Michel Lagarde became CEO June 2026. Track record at scale is **[HUMAN REVIEW]**.
- **Competition:** Datwyler and Aptar on components; Phillips Medisize and regional firms on contract manufacturing press price.
- **Raw materials:** Synthetic elastomers tied to petroleum; company hedges crude oil (184,075 barrels contracted through June 2027 at ~$72.94/barrel strike per 10-K).

**Lens failure mode:** Operating lens fails if injectable format share shifts structurally to oral delivery and WST's validated elastomer franchise becomes a slower-growth utility at a premium multiple.
