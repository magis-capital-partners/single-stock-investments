## What this business is
American Airlines Group is the holding company for American Airlines, a major U.S. network carrier operating a hub-and-spoke domestic and international passenger and cargo network. **[Fact]** Shares trade on Nasdaq (AAL). **[Fact]** FY2025 total operating revenue was **$54.6 billion**, operating income **$1.5 billion**, and net income **$111 million** (diluted earnings **$0.17** per share) per `investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260218_rpt20251231_acc0000006201_26_000014.htm`. **[Fact]** FY2025 cash from operations was **$3.1 billion** while capital spending was **$3.8 billion**, and total debt and finance lease obligations were approximately **$29.0 billion** against cash of **$902 million** at year-end 2025.

## Why the market might be wrong
The stock may trade as a distressed equity stub on a highly levered network, while normalized demand and loyalty economics still generate substantial operating cash flow. **[Inference]** FY2025 operating income fell to **$1.5 billion** from **$2.6 billion** in FY2024, so generalists may extrapolate margin compression without crediting ongoing debt paydown from **$3.1 billion** of operating cash flow. **[Inference]** The AAdvantage loyalty program and co-brand partnerships produce high-margin cash that is not separately capitalized on the balance sheet; the market may overweight GAAP net income (**$111 million**) versus mid-cycle owner cash per share near **$4.70** from operating cash flow. **[Inference]** Proof-first component math at base case sums to about **−$9.79** per share of filing-locked economic value versus a price near **$15**, suggesting the market prices deleveraging and cycle recovery beyond what the conservative component schedule captures today.

## Executive summary
American Airlines is the largest U.S. network airline by several traffic measures, operating a consolidated hub system with a material loyalty franchise. FY2025 results show revenue stability near **$54.6 billion** but margin pressure: operating income fell sharply versus FY2024 while debt remained near **$29 billion**. The business is understandable as a capital-cycle airline, but the bear case is not tightly bounded at the equity layer given leverage, and we see no durable moat.

At about **$15.14** per share, we expect **28.48%** per year over seven years on the synthesis base case (Lawrence owner-cash path **27.0%** per year). Proof-first component economic value base case is **−$9.79** per share, so mechanical return math and filing-locked component sum diverge; the levered stub embeds recovery beyond conservative proofs. Dhando is **none**. Stance: **watch**.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** A simultaneous earnings trough and credit-market tightening could force dilutive refinancing or covenant stress before operating cash flow pays down the **~$29 billion** debt stack.

- **Debt wall:** **~$29B** total debt and leases with **$902M** cash; refinancing risk if earnings trough deepens (`10-K_20260218`).
- **Fuel and labor:** Operating income fell **44%** YoY FY2025; cost inflation can outrun fare increases.
- **Fare competition:** Ultra-low-cost carriers and legacy rivalry cap pricing power in downturns.
- **Capex trap:** **$3.8B** capital spending exceeded operating cash flow after debt service needs; fleet renewal is mandatory **[Assumption]**.

**Lens failure mode (levered):** If we are wrong, it is because we treated equity as a cheap option on mid-cycle cash flow while debt maturities and recession coincide, wiping out the stub.
