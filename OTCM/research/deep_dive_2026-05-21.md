## Executive summary

OTC Markets Group operates regulated U.S. OTC equity markets (OTCQX, OTCQB, OTCID, Pink Limited) and SEC-regulated alternative trading systems (OTC Link ATS/ECN/NQB, OTC Overnight, MOON ATS). Revenue is predominantly **recurring subscription and market-data fees** tied to listed/traded securities — a **Stahl croupier** model with limited principal risk on core operations.

FY2025 net revenues **~$107.9M** (+~6% vs prior year); Q1 2026 net revenues **$33.8M** (+15% YoY). Diluted EPS **$2.58** in 2025 (+14%). Management launched **OTCID** (July 2025), extended-hours infrastructure, and international "List Local, Trade Global" initiatives.

**One-line view:** Niche U.S. OTC market infrastructure toll-booth with high recurring revenue and expanding product surface — earnings scale with issuer count and trading activity, not balance-sheet risk.

**Thesis status:** **strengthening** — execution on disclosure tiers and trading infrastructure; monitor valuation vs growth and regulatory shifts.

## Business overview

Three revenue lines (from Q1 2026 MD&A structure): **OTC Link** (ATS/trading), **Market Data Licensing**, **Corporate Services** (disclosure, compliance, issuer programs). OTCID launched July 2025 as a Basic Reporting Market — companies meeting disclosure standards saw increased investor engagement per management.

## Munger lenses

### Circle of competence — pass

Business is understandable: regulated market operator + data/subscription fees on OTC securities. Five-sentence summary feasible.

### Moat

- **Network / standards:** OTCQX/OTCQB tiering creates disclosure ladder; OTCID adds basic tier.
- **Infrastructure:** First-mover extended-hours ATS (OTC Overnight, MOON ATS); major exchanges followed in 2025 per letter.
- **Switching costs:** Issuers and broker-dealers integrated into OTC Link ecosystem.

### Inversion — what would kill the thesis?

1. SEC/regulatory mandate forcing OTC issuers to national exchanges only.
2. Prolonged decline in OTC issuer count or retail OTC trading.
3. Pricing pressure on market-data or disclosure subscriptions.
4. Competitive ATS undercutting on fees or hours.
5. Governance/capital misallocation (insider control ~4.2M shares per proxy disclosures).

### Psychology / incentives

Officers/directors hold substantial stock; recurring subscription model aligns with long-term issuer retention vs one-time fees.
