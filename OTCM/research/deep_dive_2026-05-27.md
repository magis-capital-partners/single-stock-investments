## What this business is

OTC Markets Group operates regulated U.S. over-the-counter equity markets (OTCQX, OTCQB, OTCID, Pink) and FINRA-member ATS infrastructure (OTC Link, extended-hours trading). **[Fact]** Issuers pay listing and disclosure subscription fees; brokers pay ATS and market-data fees. Revenue is subscription- and market-data-heavy with limited principal risk on core operations. **[Fact]** FY2025 net revenues were **$107.882M** (+12% YoY); diluted EPS **$2.58** (+14%). Q1 2026 net revenues were **$33.842M** (+15% YoY) with diluted EPS **$0.59** (+18%). Roughly **11.86M** diluted shares outstanding.

## Why the market might be wrong

No dated payoff or structural discount. **[Inference]** Niche croupiers rarely re-rate to 15% IRR while growing; at ~$52 the stock trades near ~22× FY25 earnings. **[Inference]** Headline net revenue growth understates core fee growth when transaction pass-through grosses up revenue and expense lines. Return is franchise earnings at price unless regulatory or volume scares create a cyclical entry.

## Executive summary

OTC Markets is U.S. OTC listing and ATS infrastructure: subscription-heavy fees on disclosure tiers (including new OTCID tier) and extended-hours trading. Full-tier FY2025 and Q1 2026 filings confirm acceleration: net revenues +12% FY25 and +15% in Q1, with transaction revenue +38% on higher OTC Link activity. FY2025 mix was **81%** subscription / **19%** transaction; Q1 2026 shifted to **78%** / **22%** as volume picked up. OTCID contributed positive revenue in Q1. At ~$52, Lawrence base case implies **9.7%** annualized return over ten years on ~$2.35 mid-cycle owner cash per share, below the 15% hold band. Bull case ~14.6% if OTCID and international listings compound faster. Asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) is partial: low capex and recurring mix help, but regulatory and volume tails exist. We propose **watch**; illustrative entry ~$42–45 needed for ~15% base IRR.
