## What this business is
ASX Limited operates Australia's dominant market infrastructure: listings, cash-market trading, futures and over-the-counter clearing, settlement, registry, and market-data services. **[Fact]** Brokers, issuers, and clearing participants pay fees on activity; ASX does not take proprietary trading risk on its licensed market operations. **[Fact]** FY2025 (year ended 30 June 2025) operating revenue was **$1,107.2 million** (+7.0% versus **$1,034.3 million** in FY2024); underlying net profit after tax **$510.0 million** (+7.5%); underlying earnings per share **262.9 cents** (`investor-documents/official-reports/asx-2025-annual-report.pdf`). **[Fact]** Four reportable segments generated revenue: Listings **$208.0 million**, Markets **$349.2 million**, Technology & Data **$275.6 million**, and Securities & Payments **$274.4 million** (`investor-documents/official-reports/fy2025-results-market-announcement.pdf`). The group holds monopoly-style licences for Australia's primary exchange, clearing, and settlement facilities.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
There is no dated payoff or structural discount. **[Inference]** FY2025 benefited from solid growth across Markets (+10.7%), Technology & Data (+8.0%), and Securities & Payments (+7.4%), plus **$86.8 million** net interest income (+13.2%) on participant margin balances. **[Inference]** The market may correctly price regulatory and technology risk after the October 2024 CHESS batch settlement incident and the multi-year CHESS replacement program (**$77.7 million** of FY2025 capital spending). Return is franchise earnings power at price, not a mispricing with a known catalyst date.

## Executive summary
ASX Limited is Australia's exchange and clearing toll booth with **62.8%** EBITDA margin and a durable regulatory franchise. FY2025 filings confirm **$1,107.2 million** operating revenue, **$646.9 million** EBIT, and underlying net profit after tax **$510.0 million**. We normalize owner cash to **$2.05** per share, below underlying earnings per share **262.9 cents**, to adjust for elevated CHESS modernisation capital spending and partly cyclical net interest income. At **$48.82** per share (2026-06-11), the Lawrence base case implies about **6.87%** per year over 7 years, below our about-fifteen-percent-per-year accumulate bar. Bull case about **9.1%** if volumes and margins persist; bear about **2.2%** on activity mean-reversion. Asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) is **partial**: no operating net debt concern and a **4.6%** dividend yield, but earnings are cyclical and technology execution risk is live. We propose **watch**.

## Risks & inversion
**Primary risk:** **CHESS technology failure or delayed modernisation** undermines settlement reliability, invites regulatory intervention, and prolongs elevated capital spending without margin relief.

- **Activity mean-reversion** in cash equities and derivatives compresses Markets revenue after FY2025 growth.
- **Net interest income normalization** as participant margin balances or investment spreads fall.
- **Sympli losses** persist beyond FY2025 (**$11.0 million** ASX share) with no path to profit.
- **Regulatory fee caps** or competitive pressure from alternative trading venues.

**Inversion:** If we are wrong, ASX remains Australia's licensed market infrastructure monopoly returning mid-single-digit annual returns with a **4.6%** dividend yield, not a broken franchise.
