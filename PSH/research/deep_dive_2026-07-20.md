## What this business is

PSH is Ackman's LSE-listed closed-ended vehicle: concentrated large-cap longs, weekly NAV, persistent double-digit discount, active share repurchases. It is tagged as a fund NAV-discount idea, not an operating compounder.

## Why the market might be wrong

The shares trade at -34.5% vs reported NAV. The inefficiency is market-structure and/or accounting marks, not a hidden factory.

## Executive summary

Base case about **8.3% per year** from discount-close scenarios over five years, with liquid or reported NAV as the floor. Not a filing free-cash-flow IRR.

## Risks & inversion

- Discount never closes; expense ratio compounds against you.
- NAV marks are wrong the other way (private overstatement; Russia permanent zero).
- Liquidity: wide spreads on small CEFs / Class A lines.

**Lens failure mode:** value trap where the wrapper discount is structural and buybacks are too small to matter.
