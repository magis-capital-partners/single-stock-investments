## What this business is
WaterBridge Infrastructure gathers, treats, and disposes of produced water for E&P operators in the Delaware Basin. **[Fact]** The network spans roughly 2,500 pipeline miles and 197 facilities with more than 4.5 million barrels per day of capacity under long-term fee contracts with acreage dedications and minimum volume commitments. **[Fact]** Q1 2026 revenue was $201.0 million with adjusted EBITDA of $102.9 million on about 2.5 million barrels per day handled. **[Fact]** 2026 guidance calls for $425 million to $465 million of adjusted EBITDA and $430 million to $490 million of growth capital spending. **[Fact]** March 2026 debt principal was about $1.475 billion against $50.7 million of cash; valuation uses 123.456 million economic units (47.016 million Class A shares plus 76.440 million OpCo units). This is merchant midstream infrastructure, not a regulated utility rate base.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The market may extrapolate post-IPO combination growth as durable or, conversely, treat sponsor overhang and heavy 2026 capex as fatal. **[Inference]** Filings show strong operating proof in Q1 2026, but public disclosure still lacks project-level return on capital and a contracted-versus-merchant cash-flow split. **[Inference]** At $34.46, the stock trades above the $26.51 base component value, so the market is paying for successful execution of Speedway, Devon-supporting projects, and deleveraging that proofs bound but do not yet verify cohort by cohort.
