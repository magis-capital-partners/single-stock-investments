### Investor-wisdom rules applied

- Marathon/Capital Returns: a large build program creates value only when incremental returns exceed the cost of capital.
- Marks/Oaktree: leverage and refinancing claims are deducted before attributing network optionality to common units.
- Hohn: density, long-term contracts, and brownfield expansion can create a moat, but maintenance and growth capital must be separated.
- Groundbreaker context: local water scarcity supports terminal relevance; it does not convert a capital-intensive operator into a capital-free royalty company.

### Limitations

- Public cohort-level capex and EBITDA data are insufficient to verify management's incremental return claims.
- The Gravity Water transaction is smaller and less contracted than WaterBridge, while public midstream peers have different commodity, basin, and contract mixes.
- Replacement cost is not liquidation value and can exceed economic value when capacity is underutilized.

## Decision explanation

WaterBridge has valuable scale and contracted growth, but $34.46 is above the $26.51 base equity value and assumes successful execution of a $430M-$490M capital program with limited cost overruns. A base-case 15% entry price is materially lower; the current quote is a watch rather than an approval.

**Strongest counter-explanation:** The network may compound faster and with less risk than the model assumes because long-term dedications, MVCs, water intensity, and contiguous scale allow high-return brownfield expansion and rapid deleveraging after the 2026 build cycle.

**Committee routing:** round_one_open — hohn, marathon_capital_cycle, marks_credit_cycle

**Falsifiers:**

- Maintenance capital is demonstrated below $80M while 2026 EBITDA reaches or exceeds $465M.
- Net leverage falls below 2.5x without reducing the contracted growth program.
- Speedway and Devon cohorts earn incremental after-tax ROIC above 18% on disclosed capital.

## Economic claim

Each Class A share corresponds economically to one OpCo unit. Valuation uses 47.016 million Class A plus 76.440 million Class B/OpCo units. The prior Class-A-only denominator materially overstated consolidated free cash flow per listed share.
