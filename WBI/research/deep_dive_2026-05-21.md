## Executive summary

WaterBridge Infrastructure operates **produced-water gathering, disposal, and recycling infrastructure** in the Delaware Basin (Permian), primarily under long-term contracts with E&P operators. Listed via **IPO September 2025** following combination of legacy water entities — **not a Stahl croupier**; asset-heavy midstream with customer concentration and sponsor control.

**FY2025:** Total revenues **$525.6M** (+66% vs $316.3M 2024); **Adjusted EBITDA $254.0M** (+68%); **operating income $78.9M**. ~**2,500 miles** of water pipelines; significant **Five Point** (sponsor) voting control and board designation rights.

**Q1 2026:** 10-Q filed May 2026 — review for quarter cadence post-IPO.

**One-line view:** High-growth Permian water infrastructure toll-road with strong Adj. EBITDA — thesis hinges on Delaware Basin activity, contract durability, sponsor alignment, and GAAP earnings vs adjusted metrics post-combination.

**Thesis status:** **unclear** — operational momentum is strong but recent corporate complexity, sponsor control, and GAAP net loss prevent confident **intact/strengthening** without more normalized history.

## Munger lenses

### Circle of competence — pass with caution

Model is clear (barrels handled × fee); **pro forma and sponsor structure** add complexity.

### Moat

- **Dedicated acreage and long-term contracts** with E&P operators.
- **Pipeline network density** (~2,500 miles) and surface access (LandBridge, TPL AMI).
- **Threats:** Permian drilling slowdown, customer consolidation, sponsor related-party transactions.

### Inversion — what would kill the thesis?

1. **Oilfield activity collapse** in Delaware Basin → volume cliff.
2. **Customer concentration** — loss of one major producer (10-K risk factor).
3. **Five Point conflict** — board control while sponsor owns competing interests.
4. **Leverage / rate exposure** post-IPO capital structure.
5. **Regulatory/environmental** restrictions on disposal/recycling.

### Psychology / incentives

Sponsor incentive units and IPO structure — map **who gets paid on growth vs distributions**.
