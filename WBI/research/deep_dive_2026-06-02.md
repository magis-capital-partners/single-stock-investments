## What this business is
WaterBridge Infrastructure gathers, disposes of, and recycles produced water for E&P operators in the Delaware Basin. **[Fact]** The company operates roughly 2,500 miles of pipeline under long-term fee contracts with dedications and minimum volume commitments. **[Fact]** FY2025 revenue was $525.6 million (+66%) with adjusted EBITDA of $254.0 million (+68%); Q1 2026 revenue was $201.0 million (+105%) with adjusted EBITDA of $102.9 million (+105%). **[Fact]** Produced-water volumes averaged 2,460 MBbl/d in Q1 2026 (+105% YoY), largely from post-IPO combination scale and the Kraken project. **[Fact]** Total debt was $1.46 billion at year-end 2025; Five Point Energy retains significant sponsor control post-September 2025 IPO. This is merchant midstream infrastructure, not a regulated utility.

---

## Why the market might be wrong
The market may either extrapolate Q1 2026 +105% growth as sustainable or dismiss the story due to IPO/combination accounting noise and sponsor conflict. **[Inference]** FY2025 GAAP net loss of $(58.1) million and heavy growth capex ($278.6 million vs $159.7 million OCF) obscure maintenance free cash flow per share (~$2.13 in our model). **[Inference]** At ~$30, imputed EV/EBITDA near 11x on FY2025 EBITDA implies ~9% EBITDA cap rate for merchant Permian water midstream, roughly fair without cycle normalization. No clear dated payoff or dormant asset; return is mid-cycle franchise earnings at price.

WaterBridge is Delaware Basin produced-water infrastructure with strong post-combination operating proof: Q1 2026 adjusted EBITDA $102.9 million on 2,460 MBbl/d volumes.
